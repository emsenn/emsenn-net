# Code of Conduct

## Context

This repository is a research endeavor — the emsemioverse — conducted
by emsenn. It is not a community platform or open-source project in the
conventional sense. Contributions are welcome, but emsenn is the
steward and final authority on what enters the repository.

## Expectations

- **Respect the subject matter.** The research engages with Indigenous
  epistemologies, particularly Lakota. Do not appropriate, trivialize,
  or misrepresent this material.
- **Engage honestly.** If you do not understand something, say so.
  Pretending to understand does more harm than asking.
- **Contribute constructively.** Contributions should produce artifacts
  (terms, texts, specs, code) — not just commentary.
- **Follow the method.** The repository has conventions (see
  `AGENTS.md`). Follow them. If you think a convention is wrong,
  propose a change through a decision record, not by ignoring it.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Misrepresenting Indigenous knowledge or claiming authority over it
- Submitting AI-generated content without review or revision
- Deliberate disruption of the repository's structure or conventions
- Harassment of any kind

## Contact

Open an issue on the repository or reach emsenn through the channels
listed at [emsenn.net](https://emsenn.net).
