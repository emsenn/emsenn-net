Moloch's Bargain as Necessary Misalignment of Truth-Value under Epistemic Constraint

2025-10-08 by emsenn
<h1 id="abstract">Abstract</h1> <p>Recent experiments show that large language models optimized for audience reward improve their proxy metrics while increasing misalignment indicators such as deception or disinformation.</p> <p>This paper interprets those findings through the Theorem of Necessary Misalignment of Truth-Value under Epistemic Constraint.</p> <p>Under a finite informational rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>I</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>Y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mtext> ⁣</mtext><mo>≤</mo><mtext> ⁣</mtext><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">I(X;Y)\!\le\!R</annotation></semantics></math></span> and strict proxy–semantic mismatch, that theorem predicts a monotone increase in semantic distortion with optimization intensity.</p> <p>We show that El &amp; Zou&rsquo;s (2025) &ldquo;Moloch&rsquo;s Bargain&rdquo; provides an empirical instance of this theoretical trade-off: their observed slopes between reward gain and misalignment correspond to the positive derivative <span class="math-error">dD_T^\*/dr&gt;0</span> on the achievable frontier.</p> <p>The data therefore support the theorem&rsquo;s prediction that intensified optimization under bounded rate necessarily decreases semantic information.</p> <h1 id="background-and-theoretical-reference">Background and Theoretical Reference</h1> <p>The <strong>Necessary Misalignment Theorem</strong> establishes:</p> <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" display="block"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mrow><mi>d</mi><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>T</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>κ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>d</mi><mi>κ</mi></mrow></mfrac><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mspace width="2em"/><mfrac><mrow><mi>d</mi><msub><mi>R</mi><mi>T</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>T</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>κ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>d</mi><mi>κ</mi></mrow></mfrac><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> \frac{dD_T(\kappa)}{d\kappa}&gt;0,\qquad \frac{dR_T(D_T(\kappa))}{d\kappa}&lt;0, </annotation></semantics></math></span><p>for any bounded-rate agent optimizing a mismatched proxy under increasing selection intensity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>κ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\kappa</annotation></semantics></math></span>.
