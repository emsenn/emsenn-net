Relational System Locale

What this is

A RelationsSystemLocale is a GenerativeUniverseClosureLocale within a RelationsSystem.

It is a subscope of the relational universe RR that has itself satisfied all seven U_G closure conditions, making it Sh(Tℓ,J∣Tℓ)\mathbf{Sh}(T_\ell, J|_{T_\ell}) — a complete relational universe at scope ℓ\ell. The subscope Tℓ⊆TT_\ell \subseteq T is the history category restricted to this locale’s scope. A RelationsSystemLocale MUST have unique teleology and unique method — the identifying conditions of any GenerativeUniverseClosureLocale.

What closure means at the scope level

The seven conditions, applied to a subscope:

Condition What it requires within the locale
1. X ⊆ U_G(X) Any entity in the locale’s scope is in the locale
2. Isomorphism closure Canonical identity for each entity within the locale
3. Limits and colimits Complex entities within the scope are colimits of basic entities
4. Exponentials Relations within the locale are themselves entities in the locale
5. Subobject classification Valid sub-configurations within the scope are classified
6. Structural operations Generative operations are named objects within the scope
7. Sheaf gluing Compatible concurrent sections assemble within the scope

An entity satisfying all seven conditions within its scope IS a complete relational universe at that scope. This is not a metaphor — it is Sh(Tℓ,J∣Tℓ)\mathbf{Sh}(T_\ell, J|_{T_\ell}).

Distinction from an entity

A RelationalEntity is a node in RR, present by condition 1. A RelationsSystemLocale is a subscope of RR that has satisfied all seven conditions — it is itself a complete relational universe, not just a node within one. A RelationsSystemLocale MUST satisfy all seven U_G closure conditions within its scope.

The distinction is between being in the system and being a local instance of the system.
