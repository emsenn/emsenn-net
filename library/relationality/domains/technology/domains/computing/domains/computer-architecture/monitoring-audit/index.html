Monitoring and audit System

Monitoring and Audit System Architecture

Collects telemetry from every subsystem, enforces invariants, and provides auditable/traceable dashboards for operators.

Dependencies

  • Introspection Simulation: sampling rules and local instrumentation hooks.
  • Audit Simulation: immutable logs and provenance reconstruction.
  • Measurement Algebra Simulation: expectation/integration for observables.
  • Policy Simulation: governs dashboard/API access.
  • Measurement Fabric Simulation: supplies the canonical telemetry bus.
  • Traceability & Knowledge Service: provides trace snapshots so alerts/actions can cite earned acts/specs.
  • Topology Integration Simulation: supplies placement metadata so dashboards can segment telemetry by stratum/region.

Components

  1. Fabric Ingestion Layer.
    Subscribes to measurement-fabric channels plus bespoke introspection hooks; validates schema IDs and stores normalized events.

  2. Invariant Evaluator.
    Uses measurement algebra/introspection specs to compute expectations, thresholds, and trigger alerts when invariants are violated.

  3. Audit & Trace Browser.
    Lets operators review audit logs, correlate them with telemetry, and inspect the traceability nodes related to an alert or change.

  4. Policy & Notification Gate.
    Ensures only authorized users receive alerts/logs; routes notifications to operations control or incident response.

Invariants

  • Telemetry is immutable/tamper evident; schema mismatches trigger rejection.
  • Alerts cite the precise traceability nodes/acts whose invariants failed.
  • Dashboards operate under policy controls; unauthorized access attempts are logged for audit and measurement-fabric reporting.
