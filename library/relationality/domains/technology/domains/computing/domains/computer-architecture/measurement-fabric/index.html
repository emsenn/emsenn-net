Measurement Fabric

Measurement Fabric Architecture

Operates the measurement bus that captures observables from runtime modules, normalizes them via the measurement algebra, and distributes aggregates to monitoring, policy, and resource services.

Dependencies

  • Measurement Fabric Simulation: ingestion/aggregation/routing mechanics.
  • Measurement Algebra Simulation: expectation and normalization routines.
  • Introspection Simulation: sampling directives, policy hooks, and retention requirements for observability data.
  • Topology Integration Simulation: constrains where telemetry channels live and how they align with strata/regions.
  • Traceability & Knowledge Service: records which acts/specs justify every measurement schema and routing rule.

Components

  1. Ingress Agents.
    Run alongside runtime/storage/network modules to capture trace metrics, convert them to registered schemas, and push them onto the bus.

  2. Aggregation Core.
    Executes measurement-algebra operations (expectations, entropy, equivalence) and introspection stack sampling policies.

  3. Routing Layer.
    Maintains the channel graph: forwards aggregates to monitoring/audit, resource manager, policy console, etc., while honoring topology constraints (e.g., keeping data in-region). Guarantees ordering for temporal analyses and emits traceability metadata for configuration changes.

  4. Retention & Audit Store.
    Archives summaries plus digests of raw samples for the audit stack; enforces policy on who can view raw vs aggregated measurements.

Invariants

  • Every emitted observable maps to a registered schema and respects declared bounds.
  • Aggregations are reproducible via measurement-algebra semantics; discrepancies trigger alerts.
  • Routing changes are policy-approved, topology-compliant, and logged with traceability references for audit/compliance review.
