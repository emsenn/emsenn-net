Markov analytics Service

Markov Analytics Service Architecture

Provides flow/kernel analytics, stationary distribution insights, and entropy tracking based on the Markov, measurement, and spectral specs.

Dependencies

  • Markov Dynamics Simulation: kernel, entropy, mixing procedures.
  • Measurement Algebra Simulation: expectation/integration for presenting metrics.
  • Spectral Simulation: extracts harmonic bases and spectral projections for observation kernels.
  • Measurement Fabric Simulation: transports analytics telemetry (mixing, entropy drift) to monitoring/resource systems.
  • Traceability & Knowledge Service: ensures every analytic report references the theorems/specs backing it.
  • Topology Integration Simulation: supplies placement/strata context so diagnostics can highlight localized anomalies.

Components

  1. Kernel Inspector.
    Computes stationary distributions, checks detailed balance.

  2. Entropy Dashboard.
    Tracks entropy trajectories and alerts when monotonicity is violated.

  3. Reachability Analyzer.
    Uses Markov spec to report reachable sets and mixing times.

  4. Spectral Decomposition Engine.
    Applies spectral spec to decompose kernels/observables, providing harmonic modes for downstream diagnostics.

  5. Telemetry, Trace & Topology Adapter.
    Publishes diagnostics via the measurement fabric, attaches traceability IDs, and annotates reports with topology metadata (strata/regions affected).

Outputs

  • Stationary distributions, entropy trends, mixing diagnostics for operators and other modules.
  • Spectral summaries (eigenvalues, projections) feeding reasoning/policy tools.
  • Measurement fabric streams (lag, residuals) and trace references for audit.
