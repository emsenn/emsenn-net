Trace renewal Coordinator

Trace Renewal Coordinator Architecture

Maintains the convergent trace tails required by the baseline-renewal spec and ensures renewed contexts are durably stored and policy-audited.

Dependencies

  • Baseline Renewal Simulation: defines how to monitor trace tails, collapse residues, and trigger renewal actions.
  • Storage Simulation: persists baseline estimates and renewed contexts.
  • Policy Simulation: governs who may inspect/reset baseline states.
  • Measurement Fabric Simulation: standardizes telemetry (drift, convergence, renewal latency) that this service emits.
  • Topology Integration Simulation: ensures renewed traces remain compatible with placement/order constraints.
  • Traceability & Knowledge Service: records which acts/specs justify each renewal event.

Components

  1. Tail Monitor.
    Streams trace outputs from runtime modules, applies the baseline spec’s convergence tests, and raises alerts when tails drift.

  2. Renewal Executor.
    Applies renewal triggers (restep/renew/rehead) and rebuilds context stacks per the spec, ensuring the stabilized suffix is preserved.

  3. Baseline Store.
    Writes baseline estimates and context frames to the storage service so other modules (runtime, analytics) can resume from a converged state.

  4. Policy, Audit, Trace & Telemetry Hooks.
    Authenticates renewal requests, records baseline revisions, attaches traceability IDs, and streams convergence metrics (with topology scope) through the measurement fabric to monitoring/audit modules.

Invariants

  • Renewals only modify the head of traces; stabilized tails remain identical.
  • Stored baselines round-trip through the storage service without precision loss.
  • Every renewal event is policy-authorized, auditable, respects topology constraints, and is reported via the measurement fabric with traceability references.
