Relational Information Physics Computer

This document describes the Relational Information Physics Computer: a relational information engine over the quasicrystalline hypertensor sheaf 𝒮 (specifications/information-engine.md), together with the services that expose it to users and external systems.

At the core, the computer realises:

  • the information engine pipeline (intake → measurement → towers/flow → sheaf sections → dynamics),
  • the measurement fabric and Markov dynamics stacks that track and evolve observables,
  • the quasicrystalline hypertensor sheaf and its dynamics acting as the global information state 𝒮,
  • the knowledge and traceability graphs that expose recognitions and their dependencies,
  • and the storage, network, and policy stacks that make the system robust and multi-tenant.

1. Core engine

The core engine is specified by the Relational Information Engine (specifications/information-engine.md):

  • Intake/validation modules encode external payloads as recognitions and attach earned acts.
  • Measurement and profile modules interpret recognitions as observables and distributions.
  • Tower and flow–nucleus modules assemble and evolve profile towers over fibres.
  • Sheaf/hypertensor modules represent finite sections of 𝒮 as (window, towers) carriers and apply Markov/measurement dynamics locally.

Architecture texts such as Relational Runtime, Measurement Fabric, Hypertensor Analytics, and Traceability & Knowledge Service describe runtime components that invoke these specifications.

2. Surrounding services

Around the core engine, the computer includes:

  • Relational Runtime – executes parametric programs and runbooks, routing all state changes through earned acts and the information engine.
  • Measurement Fabric – aggregates observables and telemetry across modules, enforcing measurement invariants.
  • Hypertensor Analytics Service – provides analysis and exploration of hypertensor/sheaf sections.
  • Traceability & Knowledge Service – presents recognitions, edges, and sheaf sections as a navigable knowledge graph.
  • Relational Storage and Network Services – persist recognitions and manage distributed deployment, using the storage/network specs.
  • Policy & Audit Console – applies policy specs to recognitions and runbooks, recording complete audit trails.

Each of these services is defined in its own architecture document under texts/computer-architecture/ and depends on the relevant specifications listed above.

3. Invariants

Across the whole computer, the following invariants must hold:

  • All persistent state is represented as recognitions and sections of 𝒮 (towers, sheaf sections, or their views).
  • Every state change arises from executing runbooks built from earned acts, respecting the measurement, Markov, and flow–nucleus invariants.
  • All architecture components reference specifications, which in turn realise theorems over acts, ensuring the entire stack is mathematically traceable.

This architecture document serves as the top-level node: lower-level architecture modules, specifications, theorems, and acts must all eventually be used, directly or indirectly, by the Relational Information Physics Computer.
