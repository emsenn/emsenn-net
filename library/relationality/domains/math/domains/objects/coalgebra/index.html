Coalgebra

Formal definition

A Coalgebra for a comonad (W,ε,δ)(W, \varepsilon, \delta) on a category C\mathcal{C} is a pair (A,ρ)(A, \rho):

Coalgebra=(A,  ρ:A→W(A))\text{Coalgebra} = (A,\; \rho : A \to W(A))

satisfying two laws:

εA∘ρ=idA(counit law)\varepsilon_A \circ \rho = \mathrm{id}_A \qquad\text{(counit law)}

δA∘ρ=W(ρ)∘ρ(coassociativity)\delta_A \circ \rho = W(\rho) \circ \rho \qquad\text{(coassociativity)}

A morphism of coalgebras f:(A,ρA)→(B,ρB)f : (A, \rho_A) \to (B, \rho_B) satisfies W(f)∘ρA=ρB∘fW(f) \circ \rho_A = \rho_B \circ f.

What this is

A Coalgebra for a comonad (W,ε,δ)(W, \varepsilon, \delta) on a category C is an object A together with a structure map ρ: A → W(A) satisfying two laws:

  • Counit law: ε_A ∘ ρ = id_A — the structured embedding is reversed by the counit extraction. This is the section condition: ρ is a section of ε_A.
  • Coassociativity: δ_A ∘ ρ = W(ρ) ∘ ρ — occupancy is stable across iterated context steps. The agent does not merely occupy a position instantaneously but maintains that occupancy coherently through time and context change.

The counit law is the section condition for ε_A: every coalgebra is a section, and coassociativity is the additional structure that makes the section dynamically coherent. A Section captures placement; a coalgebra captures sustained, step-coherent placement.

CoKleisli composition

For a coKleisli morphism f: W(A) → B, the coextension f* = W(f) ∘ ρ: A → W(B) composes sequential context-dependent computations. This is the mathematical model of sequential service: each step reads context, produces output, and advances to a new position. The coKleisli category of W has the same objects as C and morphisms A → B given by W(A) → B — every morphism is context-reading. A coalgebra (A, ρ) makes A into an object that can feed into this composition coherently.

Eilenberg-Moore category

The Eilenberg-Moore category C^W of coalgebras for W is the category of all objects equipped with stable context-occupancy. A morphism f: (A, ρ_A) → (B, ρ_B) of coalgebras satisfies W(f) ∘ ρ_A = ρ_B ∘ f — the structure maps commute. This is the category of objects that participate in the comonad’s context structure stably: maps between coalgebras preserve the occupancy.

The comonad W is typically generated by an adjunction L ⊣ R (W = LR). The coalgebra structure is the canonical way of participating in that adjunction stably over time. The forgetful functor C^W → C forgets the structure map; the cofree coalgebra functor sends A to (W(A), δ_A) — the canonical coalgebra on W(A) with structure map given by the comultiplication.

In this system

The directed comonad G_s on the relational universe has coalgebras (X, γ: X ⇒ G_s X) — sheaves equipped with a stepping map. The counit law ε_X ∘ γ = id_X says stepping forward then restricting back is the identity: the server faithfully occupies its current position while advancing. The coassociativity law says two-step service composes correctly: applying the stepping map twice and applying it once then iterating agree.

An agent operating in the FARS with a role that persists across multiple conversation turns bears coalgebra structure relative to the ambient comonad: each turn is a context step, the agent’s state map ρ embeds its current state into the context-extended sheaf, and the counit law ensures the agent remains correctly positioned throughout.

Relation to this system

The nuclear adjunction (σ ⊣ inclusion): Fix(σ) ↪ H_t gives rise to a comonad on H_t whose coalgebras are precisely the propositions P satisfying σ(P) = P — the saturation-fixed points. The coalgebra structure map is the unique map P → σ(P) = P, which trivially satisfies the counit law since σ(P) = P. Coassociativity holds by idempotence: σ(σ(P)) = σ(P). So the coalgebras for the saturation comonad are exactly the saturation-stable propositions — the objects that have settled into their correct fiber position and stay there.

Open questions

  • Whether the directed comonad G_s coalgebra structure is sufficient to formalize serving in this system, or whether additional constraints from the nuclear adjunction are required.
  • Whether the Eilenberg-Moore category of coalgebras for the saturation comonad has a clean characterization in terms of the site topology.
  • The relationship between the cofree coalgebra (W(A), δ_A) and the nuclear normalization map: the cofree coalgebra over A is the canonical way of equipping A with context structure; the nuclear normalization map σ ∘ Δ is the canonical way of equipping a proposition with doubly-quiescent structure. Are these the same construction at different levels?
