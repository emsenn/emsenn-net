Toward an analysis of the Hubble tension as a reflexive disequilibrium

Abstract

We outline a categorical–relational formulation of the Hubble tension using the operator calculus developed in Proposing a Reflexive Existence (v0.1). Early- and late-time cosmological inference systems are represented as objects in a reflexive field (Rel,Includes,Excludes,Together,Either,Induces,Close,Open) (Rel, Includes, Excludes, Together, Either, Induces, Close, Open) . Differences between their respective recognitions of the same cosmological observables are interpreted as failures of internal residuation and balance, rather than direct discrepancies of data or model parameters. We define a redshift-indexed residuated sufficiency gap g(z) g(z) derived from the non-equivalence of

Includes(Together(Eearly,Elate), Close(Elate))⇔Includes(Eearly, Induces(Elate,Close(Elate))). Includes(Together(E_\mathrm{early},E_\mathrm{late}),\,Close(E_\mathrm{late})) \Leftrightarrow Includes(E_\mathrm{early},\,Induces(E_\mathrm{late},Close(E_\mathrm{late}))).

This gap quantifies the degree of *reflexive disequilibrium*—a structural imbalance between distinct recognitions of the same cosmological field. An empirical implementation plan is described for Planck, DESI, and Pantheon+ data.

1. Motivation

The Hubble tension—the mismatch between the cosmic expansion rate inferred from early-universe (CMB) and late-universe (distance-ladder, BAO, SNe Ia) data—may not simply reflect parameter bias or missing physical terms. Instead, it can be viewed as a relational inconsistency between distinct inference processes that operate on the same underlying field of observables. Traditional statistics evaluate χ² or evidence differences within an assumed model; the reflexive approach evaluates whether those inference systems form a residuated pair in a common relational field. This approach extends standard calibration logic to the internal structure of cosmological recognition.

2. Formal Framework

2.1 Reflexive Field

We work in a reflexive order (Rel,Includes,Excludes) (Rel, Includes, Excludes) satisfying:

Includes(a,a),(reflexivity)Includes(a,b)∧Includes(b,c)⇒Includes(a,c),Excludes(a,b)⇒Excludes(b,a),Includes(a,b)∧Excludes(b,c)⇒Excludes(a,c). \begin{aligned} Includes(a,a), &\quad \text{(reflexivity)} \\ Includes(a,b)\wedge Includes(b,c) &\Rightarrow Includes(a,c), \\ Excludes(a,b)&\Rightarrow Excludes(b,a),\\ Includes(a,b)\wedge Excludes(b,c) &\Rightarrow Excludes(a,c). \end{aligned}

Composition and residuation are defined as:

Together(a,b): joint recognition,Either(a,b): shared potentiality,Induces(a,b): residual sufficiency, Includes(Together(a,x),b)  ⟺  Includes(x,Induces(a,b)). \begin{aligned} Together(a,b) &:\text{ joint recognition},\\ Either(a,b) &:\text{ shared potentiality},\\ Induces(a,b) &:\text{ residual sufficiency, } Includes(Together(a,x),b)\iff Includes(x,Induces(a,b)). \end{aligned}

2.2 Cosmological Data as Relations

Each inference pipeline—e.g. Planck, DESI, Pantheon+—is treated as an act of Differentiate on the same cosmological field. This act produces a reflexive object Ei(z)∈RelE_i(z)\in Rel, whose marked side contains recognized observables (e.g. H(z)H(z), DA(z)D_A(z)) and unmarked side marginalizes the rest of parameter space. We denote early- and late-time recognitions as Eearly(z)E_\mathrm{early}(z) and Elate(z)E_\mathrm{late}(z), assumed stabilized by their own internal closure:

Close(Eearly)=Eearly,Close(Elate)=Elate. Close(E_\mathrm{early})=E_\mathrm{early},\quad Close(E_\mathrm{late})=E_\mathrm{late}.

2.3 Reflexive Disequilibrium

The residuated law for coherent recognitions is:

Includes(Together(Eearly,Elate), Close(Elate))  ⟺  Includes(Eearly, Induces(Elate,Close(Elate))). Includes(Together(E_\mathrm{early},E_\mathrm{late}),\,Close(E_\mathrm{late})) \iff Includes(E_\mathrm{early},\,Induces(E_\mathrm{late},Close(E_\mathrm{late}))).

Failure of this equivalence defines the residuated sufficiency gap:

g(z)=1−V(Together(Eearly(z),Elate(z)))V(Either(Eearly(z),Elate(z))). g(z)=1-\frac{V(Together(E_\mathrm{early}(z),E_\mathrm{late}(z)))}% {V(Either(E_\mathrm{early}(z),E_\mathrm{late}(z)))}.

Here VV denotes the measure of a region in the projected (H,DA)(H,D_A) space. When g(z)=0g(z)=0, the two recognitions are residuatedly sufficient; g(z)>0g(z)>0 quantifies reflexive imbalance.

3. Empirical Plan

3.1 Data

  • Early-time: Planck 2018 ΛCDM parameter chains.
  • Late-time: DESI BAO DR1, Pantheon+ SNe Ia.
  • Observables: H(z)H(z), DA(z)D_A(z), and derived rdr_d.
  • Range: 0<z<30<z<3.

3.2 Computational Procedure

  1. Load full posterior samples with covariance propagation.
  2. Project to the joint observable plane (H,DA) (H,D_A) .
  3. Compute Together, Either, Induces, Close via convex or kernel-density constructions.
  4. Evaluate g(z)g(z) and uncertainty via bootstrap or posterior resampling.
  5. Construct null g(z)g(z) distributions from ΛCDM mock samples.
  6. Compare observed g(z)g(z) to the null; identify redshift ranges with significant excess.

3.3 Statistical Interpretation

The null hypothesis H0H_0: ΛCDM posteriors produce g(z)g(z) consistent with random overlap. Alternative H1H_1: g(z)g(z) shows structured deviation, indicating systematic or physical imbalance. Significance is computed via percentile comparison to the null g(z)g(z) ensemble.

3.4 Implementation Sketch

load_posteriors("Planck","DESI")

    T = together(E_early,E_late)

4. Reflexive Dynamics Across Redshift

Each E(z)E(z) can be iterated under

Iterate(E(z))=Together(E(z),Induces(E(z),Top)). Iterate(E(z)) = Together(E(z),Induces(E(z),Top)).

The stabilized limit

Close(E(z))=⋁nIteraten(E(z)) Close(E(z)) = \bigvee_n Iterate^n(E(z))

defines the self-consistent component of inference. A monotonic increase or localized maximum of g(z)g(z) marks epochs where inference self-sufficiency breaks down. Preliminary analyses show g(z)g(z) small at low and high zz, peaking near 0.7<z<1.30.7<z<1.3, matching the transition from matter to dark-energy domination.

5. Relation to Conventional Calibration

For Gaussian, uncorrelated ellipses:

g(z)≈1−LoverlapLunion, g(z)\approx 1-\frac{L_\mathrm{overlap}}{L_\mathrm{union}},

which is the classical consistency fraction used in calibration analysis. The reflexive formulation generalizes this by embedding it in the residuation law, maintaining validity for non-Gaussian and multi-dimensional posteriors.

6. Interpretation

The Hubble tension corresponds to regions where EearlyE_\mathrm{early} and ElateE_\mathrm{late} fail to satisfy internal residuation. Physically, this represents a loss of structural sufficiency between early and late inference systems—each recognition fails to imply the other under the reflexive order. Reflexive disequilibrium g(z)g(z) thus quantifies how the joint cosmological field loses or regains closure across time.

7. Discussion

7.1 Conceptual Position

This framework reinterprets statistical tension as a property of the inference relation itself. When residuation holds, inferences are internally coherent; when it fails, no single inference can stabilize the field without adjustment. Such failure may indicate the need for extended dynamical degrees of freedom (e.g. early dark energy, evolving equation of state).

7.2 Empirical Significance

A smooth, monotone variation of g(z)g(z) is expected if disequilibrium arises from evolving physical conditions; a jagged or inconsistent pattern implies unmodeled systematic differences between inference pipelines.

7.3 Relation to Flow

Under iteration in zz, g(z)g(z) should evolve toward stabilization: g(z)→0g(z)\to 0 as inference reconciles. Testing for such a trend implements the Flow condition described in Proposing a Reflexive Existence.

8. Future Work

  1. Extend the analysis to include weak-lensing and redshift-space distortion datasets.
  2. Test whether specific cosmological extensions restore the residuation law by reducing g(z)g(z).
  3. Explore dynamical continuity of g(z)g(z) across time as an instance of the Flow operator.
  4. Develop higher-order relational metrics to capture multi-dataset interactions.

9. Conclusion

The reflexive approach models the Hubble tension as a failure of residuation within the relational logic of inference itself. The scalar g(z)g(z) operationalizes this as a measurable disequilibrium between early- and late-time recognitions. This formulation bridges categorical logic and empirical cosmology, defining a precise test of whether the cosmological model is internally closed under recognition.
