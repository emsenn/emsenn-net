2026-03-21 A SurfaceEnergyBalance triple (E, t, ell) — a surface energy state E in the surface fiber at history t over locale ell — has shadow class determined by two independent directions of surface energy partitioning: the net radiation history operator (saturation nucleus) and the turbulent transport commitment operator (transfer nucleus). E in E*_t (doubly settled: net radiation history backward-recognizes E as the maximum surface energy state consistent with the accumulated solar and longwave radiation restriction profile — the Penman energy balance term Delta times (R_n minus G) fully realized — AND turbulent transport forward-commits E through every independent boundary layer perturbation — the sensible and latent heat fluxes H and LE are committed through every independent wind gust, stability fluctuation, and humidity pulse; active daytime evapotranspiration from a moist vegetated surface in full sun with adequate wind), E in SatShadow_t (radiation-rich but transport-limited: net radiation history is deeply imprinted — high R_n, warm surface, large vapor pressure deficit — but turbulent transport is absent; the calm sunny day over a wet surface with no wind; the surface has energy to evaporate but the still air cannot carry the vapor away; Bowen ratio approaches infinity for the sensible channel and the latent heat flux is suppressed), E in TrShadow_t (transport-committed but radiation-poor: turbulent mixing is vigorous and committed through every independent wind-gust perturbation — the aerodynamic resistance r_a is very low — but the net radiation restriction profile is weak; the cloudy windy day over a wet surface where R_n is small, H is near zero, and the potential evaporation is limited by available energy rather than transport), E in FreeShadow_t (neither: the stable nocturnal surface, the frozen tundra in polar night, or the bare desert in the absence of both radiation and wind; no net radiation and no turbulent transport). The fundamental surface energy balance equation is R_net = H + LE + G (net radiation = sensible heat flux + latent heat flux + ground heat flux; Trenberth, Fasullo, and Kiehl 2009, BAMS 90, 311-323 give global mean values: R_n at surface approximately 163 W/m², LE approximately 80-88 W/m², H approximately 17-24 W/m², G approximately 0.3 W/m²). Bowen ratio beta = H divided by LE is the partition quality: desert beta approximately 10 (almost all energy to sensible heat), ocean beta approximately 0.1 (almost all to latent heat), moist farmland beta approximately 0.2 (Bowen 1926, Physical Review 27, 779-787). The Penman-Monteith equation (Penman 1948, Proceedings of the Royal Society A 193, 120-145; Monteith 1965, Symposia of the Society for Experimental Biology 19, 205-234) is the saturation nucleus restriction profile made precise: lambda times E = [Delta times (R_n minus G) plus rho_a times C_p times (e_s minus e_a) divided by r_a] divided by [Delta plus gamma times (1 plus r_s divided by r_a)], where Delta is the slope of the saturation vapor pressure curve, gamma is the psychrometric constant, e_s minus e_a is the vapor pressure deficit (the restriction-profile driving quantity), r_a is aerodynamic resistance (the transfer topology parameter), and r_s is surface resistance (the canopy stomatal resistance that determines whether the transfer step is independent).

What this is

The surface energy balance is the first-principles accounting of all energy flows at the Earth’s surface: how much radiative energy arrives from the sun and from the atmosphere, how much is reflected and re-radiated, and how the rest is partitioned between warming the air (sensible heat), evaporating water (latent heat), and warming the soil (ground heat). It is the foundation on which the entire atmospheric water cycle rests: every gram of water that eventually precipitates somewhere was first evaporated from a surface using energy quantified by the surface energy balance.

The nuclear reading makes precise the two-directional character of surface energy exchange. The radiation direction is backward-looking: what energy has accumulated in the restriction maps? The net radiation RnR_n is the restriction profile quantity — it records the history of solar angles, cloud cover, surface temperature, and atmospheric back-radiation that has built up the available energy. The turbulence direction is forward-committing: can the surface carry its current energy state through every independent atmospheric perturbation? The turbulent transport is committed when aerodynamic resistance is low enough that no independent wind gust or humidity fluctuation can prevent the energy from being moved into the atmosphere.

The Penman and Penman-Monteith equations

Howard Penman (1948, Proceedings of the Royal Society A 193, 120–145) derived the first equation combining both the energy balance and aerodynamic approaches to estimate evaporation from a saturated surface:

λE  =  Δ(Rn−G)+γEaΔ+γ\lambda E \;=\; \frac{\Delta (R_n - G) + \gamma E_a}{\Delta + \gamma}

where Δ=des/dT\Delta = de_s/dT is the slope of the saturation vapor pressure curve at air temperature, γ≈66 Pa/K\gamma \approx 66\,\mathrm{Pa/K} is the psychrometric constant (ratio of specific heat to latent heat, modified by pressure), and Ea=f(u)⋅(es−ea)E_a = f(u) \cdot (e_s - e_a) is the aerodynamic evaporation term (a function of wind speed uu and the vapor pressure deficit es−eae_s - e_a). The Penman equation is the saturation nucleus restriction profile for an open water surface: λEPenman\lambda E_\mathrm{Penman} is the maximum evaporation rate consistent with the accumulated RnR_n and vapor pressure deficit history.

John Monteith (1965, Symposia of the Society for Experimental Biology 19, 205–234) extended Penman to vegetated surfaces by introducing two resistance terms:

λE  =  Δ(Rn−G)+ρacp(es−ea)/raΔ+γ(1+rs/ra)\lambda E \;=\; \frac{\Delta (R_n - G) + \rho_a c_p (e_s - e_a)/r_a}{\Delta + \gamma(1 + r_s/r_a)}

where ra=ln⁡(zm−d)/z0)2/(k2u)r_a = \ln(z_m - d)/z_0)^2 / (k^2 u) is the aerodynamic resistance (how efficiently the atmosphere transports vapor, a function of wind speed uu and roughness length z0z_0) and rsr_s is the bulk surface (canopy) resistance (how easily water vapor exits the stomata of leaves). The Penman-Monteith equation is the saturation nucleus restriction profile for vegetated surfaces: λEPM\lambda E_\mathrm{PM} is the maximum evapotranspiration rate consistent with the accumulated radiation, vapor pressure deficit, and stomatal state history. E∈Fix(σt)E \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) iff the actual evapotranspiration equals EPME_\mathrm{PM} — the surface is evaporating at its potential rate.

The aerodynamic resistance rar_a is the transfer topology parameter: low rar_a (high wind, rough canopy) means the turbulent transport steps are genuinely independent (committed through every independent wind event); high rar_a (calm, smooth surface) means the transport steps are not independent (the still air prevents vapor from escaping). The surface resistance rsr_s is the internal holograph topology parameter: low rsr_s (open stomata, moist soil) means the internal projection gap is small; high rsr_s (drought stress, closed stomata) means the internal projection gap is large.

The diurnal energy cycle

The day-night cycle is the canonical restriction-profile oscillation of the saturation nucleus. At a temperate locale:

Dawn (RnR_n rising from zero): the saturation nucleus restriction profile begins building; σt(E)>E\sigma_t(E) > E (the restriction profile is recognizing an increasingly energetic state as RnR_n rises); the surface warms and the vapor pressure deficit opens.

Morning (positive RnR_n, increasing HH and LE\mathrm{LE}): the surface is in SatShadowtE\mathrm{SatShadow}^{\mathcal{E}}_t if the air is calm (radiation potential building, turbulent transport limited) or in Et∗\mathcal{E}^*_t if wind is sufficient. The boundary layer grows as HH drives convective mixing upward from the surface.

Midday (peak RnR_n): the saturation nucleus is at maximum; if the turbulent transport is committed (sufficient wind, rough canopy), the surface is in Et∗\mathcal{E}^*_t at peak evapotranspiration. The sensible heat flux HH is at its daily maximum; the boundary layer has grown to its maximum depth ($1–\3,\mathrm{km}$); CAPE is building in the atmospheric column above.

Afternoon (declining RnR_n): the saturation nucleus restriction profile begins declining; σt(E)\sigma_t(E) decreases; HH and LE\mathrm{LE} decrease even if wind is constant.

Dusk (Rn→0R_n \to 0): the daytime flux partition reverses; GG drives a small upward flux from the warm soil; the surface enters FreeShadowtE\mathrm{FreeShadow}^{\mathcal{E}}_t as RnR_n becomes negative (surface radiating to cold sky).

Night (negative RnR_n): the nocturnal surface is cooling by longwave emission; the boundary layer becomes stably stratified; turbulence is suppressed; the stable nocturnal inversion is the SatShadowJ\mathrm{SatShadow}^J-to-FreeShadow transition in the boundary layer. The restriction profile is now accumulating nighttime cooling, and the saturation nucleus backward-recognizes a cold, stable surface state.

Drought as projection-gap accumulation

Drought is the progressive increase of the internal projection gap λsstomate\lambda_s^\mathrm{stomate}: as soil moisture falls below field capacity, stomata partially close (increasing rsr_s), the Penman-Monteith potential evapotranspiration (EPME_\mathrm{PM}) is not achieved, the actual evapotranspiration falls below potential, and the surface moves from Et∗\mathcal{E}^*_t toward SatShadowtE\mathrm{SatShadow}^{\mathcal{E}}_t.

The soil moisture restriction profile H(it0,t)(E)H(i_{t_0,t})(E) records the prior rainfall and evapotranspiration history. The saturation nucleus σt(E)\sigma_t(E) backward-recognizes the maximum evapotranspiration state consistent with the accumulated soil moisture: as soil moisture falls, σt(E)\sigma_t(E) recognizes progressively lower potential evapotranspiration rates — the saturation condition shifts toward a drier steady state. The restriction profile of drought is self-reinforcing: dry soil increases surface temperature (Bowen ratio rises as LE\mathrm{LE} falls and HH rises), which increases TsT_s, which increases the outgoing longwave, which reduces RnR_n, which reduces the energy available for the σt\sigma_t ceiling.

The Penman potential evapotranspiration (EPenmanE_\mathrm{Penman} computed with rs=0r_s = 0, i.e., no stomatal resistance) is the saturation nucleus ceiling for the energy balance: no evapotranspiration can exceed Penman potential under the current RnR_n and VPD restriction profile. The deficit EPenman−EactualE_\mathrm{Penman} - E_\mathrm{actual} is a direct measure of λE\lambda_E — how far the surface is from its Penman-Monteith equilibrium.

SurfaceEnergyBalance
description
A SurfaceEnergyBalance triple (E, t, ell) — a surface energy state E in the surface fiber at history t over locale ell — has shadow class determined by two independent directions of surface energy partitioning: the net radiation history operator (saturation nucleus) and the turbulent transport commitment operator (transfer nucleus). E in E*_t (doubly settled: net radiation history backward-recognizes E as the maximum surface energy state consistent with the accumulated solar and longwave radiation restriction profile — the Penman energy balance term Delta times (R_n minus G) fully realized — AND turbulent transport forward-commits E through every independent boundary layer perturbation — the sensible and latent heat fluxes H and LE are committed through every independent wind gust, stability fluctuation, and humidity pulse; active daytime evapotranspiration from a moist vegetated surface in full sun with adequate wind), E in SatShadow_t (radiation-rich but transport-limited: net radiation history is deeply imprinted — high R_n, warm surface, large vapor pressure deficit — but turbulent transport is absent; the calm sunny day over a wet surface with no wind; the surface has energy to evaporate but the still air cannot carry the vapor away; Bowen ratio approaches infinity for the sensible channel and the latent heat flux is suppressed), E in TrShadow_t (transport-committed but radiation-poor: turbulent mixing is vigorous and committed through every independent wind-gust perturbation — the aerodynamic resistance r_a is very low — but the net radiation restriction profile is weak; the cloudy windy day over a wet surface where R_n is small, H is near zero, and the potential evaporation is limited by available energy rather than transport), E in FreeShadow_t (neither: the stable nocturnal surface, the frozen tundra in polar night, or the bare desert in the absence of both radiation and wind; no net radiation and no turbulent transport). The fundamental surface energy balance equation is R_net = H + LE + G (net radiation = sensible heat flux + latent heat flux + ground heat flux; Trenberth, Fasullo, and Kiehl 2009, BAMS 90, 311-323 give global mean values: R_n at surface approximately 163 W/m², LE approximately 80-88 W/m², H approximately 17-24 W/m², G approximately 0.3 W/m²). Bowen ratio beta = H divided by LE is the partition quality: desert beta approximately 10 (almost all energy to sensible heat), ocean beta approximately 0.1 (almost all to latent heat), moist farmland beta approximately 0.2 (Bowen 1926, Physical Review 27, 779-787). The Penman-Monteith equation (Penman 1948, Proceedings of the Royal Society A 193, 120-145; Monteith 1965, Symposia of the Society for Experimental Biology 19, 205-234) is the saturation nucleus restriction profile made precise: lambda times E = [Delta times (R_n minus G) plus rho_a times C_p times (e_s minus e_a) divided by r_a] divided by [Delta plus gamma times (1 plus r_s divided by r_a)], where Delta is the slope of the saturation vapor pressure curve, gamma is the psychrometric constant, e_s minus e_a is the vapor pressure deficit (the restriction-profile driving quantity), r_a is aerodynamic resistance (the transfer topology parameter), and r_s is surface resistance (the canopy stomatal resistance that determines whether the transfer step is independent).
