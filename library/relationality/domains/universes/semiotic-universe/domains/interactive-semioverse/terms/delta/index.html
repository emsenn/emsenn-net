Delta

2025-12-31
Defines Delta, Δ

Delta (Δ\Delta) is the semiotic difference operation in the Interactive Semioverse, defined at three levels. Signs become distinguishable through relational encounter — what one handle engages that another does not. Delta formalizes this relational differentiation at increasing depth. At the interaction level, Δix(A,B)=(A∖B)∪(B∖A)\Delta_{\mathrm{ix}}(A, B) = (A \setminus B) \cup (B \setminus A) is the symmetric difference of two interaction surfaces. At the fragment level, Δfrag(F,G)\Delta_{\mathrm{frag}}(F, G) is the least fragment containing (F∪G)∖(F∩G)(F \cup G) \setminus (F \cap G). At the footprint level, Δfoot(τ,τ′;X)=SX(Δfrag(Fτ,X,Fτ′,X))\Delta_{\mathrm{foot}}(\tau, \tau'; X) = \mathcal{S}_X(\Delta_{\mathrm{frag}}(F_{\tau,X}, F_{\tau',X})) — the closure of the fragment delta.

Each level captures a different kind of semiotic difference. The interaction delta is syntactic: which interaction terms does one handle have that the other does not? The fragment delta is semantic but local: what semantic values are in one handle fragment but not both? The footprint delta is semantic and closed: what difference remains after the full closure operator stabilizes the comparison?

The footprint delta can be quantified using the evaluation valuation: mu(Delta_foot(tau, tau’; X)) measures the magnitude of the difference between two handles. This is compiled into an interaction operator by the Compare interface action: [Compare(tau, tau’)](Compare(tau, tau’))_iface computes and may materialize the footprint delta as comparison artifacts within the semioverse.
