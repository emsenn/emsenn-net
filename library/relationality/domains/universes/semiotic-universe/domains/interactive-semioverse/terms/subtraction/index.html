Subtraction

2025-12-31

Subtraction in the Interactive Semioverse is a disciplined operation for semiotic forgetting, built from the Heyting implication. Sign systems do not only grow — they also forget. The Heyting pseudocomplement provides the right notion of removal in an intuitionistic setting: at the element level, a⊖b=a∧(b⇒⊥)a \ominus b = a \wedge (b \Rightarrow \bot) — the meet of aa with the pseudo-complement of bb. This is the largest element cc such that c≤ac \le a and c∧b≤⊥c \wedge b \le \bot.

At the fragment level, fragment subtraction F⊖GF \ominus G is the least fragment containing {a⊖s(G):a∈F}\{a \ominus s(G) : a \in F\}, where s(G)=⋁x∈Gxs(G) = \bigvee_{x \in G} x is the fragment’s support (the join of all its elements). At the state level, subtracting a handle τ\tau from state XX removes the semantic support of τ\tau’s handle fragment from every element of the carrier, then re-fragments.

Subtraction is distinct from forgetting. Forgetting (X∖τX \setminus \tau) removes the handle’s semantic seed and all operators mentioning the handle — a syntactic deletion. Subtraction (X⊖τX \ominus \tau) removes semantic support but preserves operator vocabulary. Subtraction is always at least as conservative as forgetting: S(X⊖τ)≤S(X∖τ)\mathcal{S}(X \ominus \tau) \le \mathcal{S}(X \setminus \tau). Both operations are compiled into interaction operators via the interface algebra.
