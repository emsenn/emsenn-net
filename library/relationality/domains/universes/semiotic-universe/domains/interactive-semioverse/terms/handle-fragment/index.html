Handle Fragment

2025-12-31

The handle fragment Fτ,XF_{\tau,X} of a handle τ\tau in state XX is the least fragment containing the semantic interpretations of τ\tau’s interaction surface: Fτ,XF_{\tau,X} = the least fragment containing {⟦t⟧:t∈IxX(τ)}\{\llbracket t\rrbracket : t \in \mathrm{Ix}_X(\tau)\}. It is the smallest modal–trace Heyting subalgebra of HH generated by the handle’s interaction denotations — the bounded interpretive context generated by a handle’s semiotic encounters.

The handle fragment captures everything that can be inferred locally from the handle’s interactions, before applying the full closure operator. It is monotone in the interaction surface: adding interaction terms can only enlarge the handle fragment. The handle fragment sits inside H and is subject to all the fragment-level reasoning tools – fragmentwise equality, hereditary extensionality, and the sheaf condition.

Closing the handle fragment under the state’s operators produces the footprint: Foot_X(tau) = S_X(F_{tau,X}). The handle fragment is the raw local data; the footprint is what remains after closure stabilizes it. The footprint is always bounded by the core semioverse state X*: nothing escapes the least fixed point.
