Semantic and Semiotic Context

The interactive semioverse draws on both semantic and semiotic traditions, specifically those that align with a relational ontology — where relations are prior to entities. The mathematical structures correspond to concepts in Peircean semiotics: indexicality, habit, unlimited semiosis, and the pragmatic maxim.

Semantics

The Heyting algebra HH provides the semantic domain — a constructive logic of meanings. The implication a⇒ba \Rightarrow b captures constructive entailment; the modal closure jj formalizes habit-formation; the trace GG formalizes semiotic provenance. These are the semantic structures that the interactive layer operates on.

Semiotics

The interactive layer formalizes relational engagement through semiotic concepts: handles as Peircean indices (signs connected by existential relation), interaction surfaces as relational boundaries, failure as Peirce’s doubt (interruption of habit), provenance as unlimited semiosis, evaluation as the pragmatic maxim (meaning as practical consequences), and subtraction as disciplined removal of relational influence.

Application

Handles, interaction terms, and footprints formalize the triadic structure of semiosis in an interactive context: the handle is the representamen (sign vehicle), the interaction term is the act of interpretation, and the footprint is the interpretant (meaning produced) — closed under the sign system’s full discipline.
