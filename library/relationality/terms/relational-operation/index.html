Relational Operation

What this is

A RelationalOperation is an Operation whose carrier is (a part of) the relational universe R = Sh(T, J). It maps R, or a fiber of R, or a class of objects of R, within itself.

The canonical instances are the three acts and the two nuclei:

Operation Carrier Arity Source
Nucleus σ_t Fiber H_t 1 relational-history-saturation-nucleus
Nucleus Δ_t Fiber H_t 1 relational-history-transfer-nucleus
Sequential act G_s R 1 sequential-act
Concurrent act ⊛ R × R 2 concurrent-act
Generative act U_G P(Obj(R)) 1 generative-act

Each of these maps a carrier within itself. None of them are morphisms between different objects — they are all endofunctions on their respective carriers.

Condition 6

The RelationsSystem closure condition 6 requires that generative operations are named objects in the universe — the system can reason about its own operations. This means RelationalOperations are themselves entities in R: they have identity, they are referenceable, and they can be composed and inspected.
