Relational State

What this is

A RelationalState is the State type of a RelationalMachine. It is a presheaf H:M(Σ,I)op→HAnuclH: M(\Sigma, I)^{op} \to \mathbf{HA}_\mathrm{nucl}: a family of fiber Heyting algebras HtH_t indexed by histories t∈M(Σ,I)t \in M(\Sigma, I), with restriction maps ρ:Ht′→Ht\rho: H_{t'} \to H_t for t≤t′t \leq t'.

A RelationalState is not a single fiber. It is the entire indexed family — all fibers simultaneously present, connected by the restriction maps.

Sections and fibers

A section of the RelationalState at history tt is HtH_t — the fiber Heyting algebra at that history. HtH_t is what the RelationalMachine holds “in focus” at history tt — formally, a Focus (H,t)(H, t).

The settled sub-state at tt is Ht∗=Fix(σt)∩Fix(Δt)H^*_t = \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) \cap \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) — the image of the composite retraction πt=σt∘Δt\pi_t = \sigma_t \circ \Delta_t. Ht∗H^*_t is the portion of HtH_t with stable truth values under both nuclei.

Mathematical grounding

The fiber Heyting algebra structure is established in the math entitys for the relational machine. The presheaf axioms (identity and composition of restriction maps) are part of the accord condition — accord. The two commuting nuclei σt\sigma_t and Δt\Delta_t are established in nucleus and the fiber algebra math entitys. A RelationalState MUST be a presheaf over M(Σ,I)opM(\Sigma, I)^{op} valued in Heyting algebras with nuclei. Its restriction maps MUST be Heyting algebra homomorphisms — accord. Each fiber MUST carry two commuting nuclei σt\sigma_t, Δt\Delta_t — nucleus. σt∘Δt=Δt∘σt\sigma_t \circ \Delta_t = \Delta_t \circ \sigma_t MUST hold — commutativity, proved in the math layer.

Relation to flat State

A flat State is a set element — structureless. A RelationalState is algebraically rich: each fiber is a Heyting algebra, the fibers are connected by Heyting algebra homomorphisms (the restriction maps), and each fiber carries two commuting nuclei. The flat-set projection of a RelationalState is just the carrier set of the current fiber HtH_t — it loses the algebraic structure, the nuclei, and the restriction maps.

Open questions

  • Whether the RelationalState must be a sheaf (not just a presheaf) — the sheaf condition corresponds to the accord/gluing condition and is what makes global sections well-defined. Currently treated as a sheaf; the exact statement is in accord.
