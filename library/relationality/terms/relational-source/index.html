2026-03-18 The history from which a prefix morphism departs in T. Source is a T-level concept, distinct from domain, which is R-level.

Relational Source

What this is

The source of a morphism f:t→t′f : t \to t' in the history category TT is tt — the shorter history from which the prefix map departs.

Source is a concept at the level of TT, the history category. It is not a concept at the level of RR, the relational universe.

This distinction matters because the fiber doctrine H:Top→HAnuclH : T^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{HA_{nucl}} is contravariant (Functor). For f:t→t′f : t \to t' in TT, the induced map is H(f):Ht′→HtH(f) : H_{t'} \to H_t — direction reversed. The source of ff in TT is tt, but the codomain of H(f)H(f) in RR also lands at HtH_t. Source and codomain share a location, but they are not the same concept.

As a frontmatter relation

source: on a spec or skill names the T-level origin of the step or operation. It MUST name a history type or a named position in the history poset. It MUST NOT be used as a synonym for RelationalDomain.

Relationship to other concepts

  • RelationalTarget — the history the morphism arrives at in TT; t′t' for f:t→t′f : t \to t'.
  • RelationalDomain — the R-level input of the induced map H(f)H(f); lives at t′t' (the target), not tt (the source), due to contravariance.
  • RelationalCodomain — the R-level output of H(f)H(f); lives at tt (the source).
