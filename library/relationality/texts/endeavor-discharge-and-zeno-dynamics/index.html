Endeavor Discharge and Zeno Dynamics

2026-03-29 The nuclear reading of endeavor discharge — joint retraction, two-stage paths, aligned vs. misaligned endeavors, and the Hurwitz residual of Zeno endeavors.

Discharge condition

At history t0t_0, an endeavor has aim g∈Ht0g \in H_{t_0} with g∉Ht0∗g \notin H^*_{t_0} — the aim is present but unsettled. The saturation gap [g,σt0(g)][g, \sigma_{t_0}(g)] is the backward deficit (the past has not fully constituted gg). The transfer gap [g,Δt0(g)][g, \Delta_{t_0}(g)] is the forward deficit (gg is not yet carried by every forward extension).

The endeavor is discharged at terminal history t1t_1 iff g∈Ht1∗=Fix(σt1)∩Fix(Δt1)g \in H^*_{t_1} = \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_{t_1}) \cap \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_{t_1}): both gaps closed.

The agent’s striving does not modify the nuclei. σt\sigma_t and Δt\Delta_t at any history are structural features of the presheaf HH. What the agent controls is which steps to take, traversing the path t0→t1t_0 \to t_1. The nuclei at each history along the path are determined by the presheaf, not by the agent.

Joint retraction and two-stage discharge

The joint retraction πt=σt∘Δt:Ht↠Ht∗\pi_t = \sigma_t \circ \Delta_t : H_t \twoheadrightarrow H^*_t is a surjective Heyting algebra homomorphism. The discharge condition g∈Ht1∗g \in H^*_{t_1} is equivalent to πt1(g)=g\pi_{t_1}(g) = g.

The retraction factors through either intermediate stable subalgebra:

Execution-first (transfer then saturation):

Ht→ΔtFix(Δt)→σtHt∗H_t \xrightarrow{\Delta_t} \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) \xrightarrow{\sigma_t} H^*_t

Secure the behavioral fact first, then let the evaluative structure respond.

Meaning-first (saturation then transfer):

Ht→σtFix(σt)→ΔtHt∗H_t \xrightarrow{\sigma_t} \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) \xrightarrow{\Delta_t} H^*_t

Establish the evaluative claim first, then build the behavioral track record.

For an aligned endeavor (nuclei commute), both paths yield the same result. For a misaligned endeavor, the paths diverge — the order in which meaning-settlement and execution-settlement are pursued changes the outcome.

Zeno endeavors

A Zeno endeavor is one where the aim is never fully discharged at any finite tower level: g∉Ht(k)∗g \notin H^*_{t^{(k)}} for all finite kk, yet progress is monotone — the shadow count grows without bound and the nuclear gap decreases at each step.

The exact progress measure at tower level kk is the Hurwitz identity:

Fk⋅∣φ−Fk+1Fk∣=φ−kF_k \cdot \left|\varphi - \frac{F_{k+1}}{F_k}\right| = \varphi^{-k}

where FkF_k is the shadow resolution count and φ\varphi is the golden ratio eigenvalue of the nuclear transfer matrix. The residual φ−k\varphi^{-k} decays at each level. The nuclear lead alternates: saturation leads at even levels, transfer leads at odd levels.

The total long-run residual converges not to zero but to the Hurwitz constant — the irreducible measure of discharge-friction in the asymptotic limit. No finite tower-level effort eliminates it. A Zeno endeavor makes perpetual progress without reaching full discharge.

Rational abandonment: an agent in a Zeno endeavor faces a principled question. Continuing is a commitment to the limit, not to a finite discharge. The Hurwitz constant is the lower bound on what any finite striving can achieve.
