Posets and Categories in the Semiotic Universe

2026-03-29

The semiotic universe’s history object (H, ≤) is a thin category — a partial order viewed as a category. A morphism from a to b is the unique witness that a ≤ b. There is at most one morphism between any two objects, and its existence expresses the ordering relation.

The Heyting algebra H is a complete lattice with the Heyting implication — the algebraic structure underlying the semiotic universe. Its elements are semantic values and its ordering expresses entailment: a ≤ b means “a entails b.”
