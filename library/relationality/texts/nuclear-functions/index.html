Nuclear Functions

2026-03-29 How functions behave in the relational universe — the naturality condition, nuclear intertwining, and the distinction between functions that preserve settlement and those that do not.

In the relational universe R=Sh(T,J)R = \mathbf{Sh}(T, J), a function between presheaves f:A⇒Bf : A \Rightarrow B is a natural transformation — a family of fiber-level maps ft:A(t)→B(t)f_t : A(t) \to B(t) indexed by histories t∈Tt \in T, satisfying the naturality condition: for every morphism g:t′→tg : t' \to t in TT,

ft′∘A(g)=B(g)∘ftf_{t'} \circ A(g) = B(g) \circ f_t

Restricting an element from tt to t′t' and then applying ff gives the same result as applying ff first and then restricting. A family of maps that fails naturality is path-dependent — it depends on which history is used to observe the element. Such a family is not a function between presheaves; it is an inconsistent collection of local maps.

Nuclear functions

A function f:A⇒Bf : A \Rightarrow B is a nuclear function (a morphism in HAnucl\mathbf{HA}_\mathrm{nucl}) iff it intertwines with both nuclei at every fiber:

ft∘σtA=σtB∘ftandft∘ΔtA=ΔtB∘ftf_t \circ \sigma_t^A = \sigma_t^B \circ f_t \qquad \text{and} \qquad f_t \circ \Delta_t^A = \Delta_t^B \circ f_t

Nuclear functions preserve the doubly-stable fiber: if a∈Ht∗(A)a \in H^*_t(A), then ft(a)∈Ht∗(B)f_t(a) \in H^*_t(B). General functions do not have this property — a non-nuclear function may send a settled element to an unsettled one.

The restriction maps H(g):Ht→Ht′H(g) : H_t \to H_{t'} are nuclear by construction. They are morphisms in HAnucl\mathbf{HA}_\mathrm{nucl} because the fiber doctrine H:Top→HAnuclH : T^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{HA}_\mathrm{nucl} lands in the category of nuclear Heyting algebras by definition.

The telos map as universal nuclear function

The telos map πtA:A(t)→Ht∗(A)\pi_t^A : A(t) \to H^*_t(A) defined by πtA(a)=σtA(ΔtA(a))\pi_t^A(a) = \sigma_t^A(\Delta_t^A(a)) is a nuclear function. For any nuclear function ft:A(t)→B(t)f_t : A(t) \to B(t) and any a∈A(t)a \in A(t):

πtB(ft(a))=ft(πtA(a))\pi_t^B(f_t(a)) = f_t(\pi_t^A(a))

Nuclear functions commute with telos maps. Non-nuclear functions break this commutation — they do not respect the settlement structure.

Composition

The composition of two nuclear functions is nuclear. Given nuclear f:A⇒Bf : A \Rightarrow B and g:B⇒Cg : B \Rightarrow C, the composition (g∘f)(g \circ f) intertwines with both nuclei because each component does:

(g∘f)∘σtA=g∘(σtB∘f)=(σtC∘g)∘f=σtC∘(g∘f)(g \circ f) \circ \sigma_t^A = g \circ (\sigma_t^B \circ f) = (\sigma_t^C \circ g) \circ f = \sigma_t^C \circ (g \circ f)

Open questions

  • Whether every function in RR can be factored through a nuclear function via a canonical nuclear approximation ftnucl(a)=πtB(ft(a))f^{\mathrm{nucl}}_t(a) = \pi_t^B(f_t(a)).
  • Whether the category of nuclear functions on RR is a full subcategory closed under limits, colimits, and exponentials.
  • Whether there is a meaningful notion of “gap-preserving function” strictly weaker than nuclearity.
  • Whether σ\sigma and Δ\Delta can correct a non-natural family of fiber maps into a natural transformation.
  • Whether the nuclear functions form a sub-presheaf [A,B]nucl↪BA[A, B]_{\mathrm{nucl}} \hookrightarrow B^A in a well-behaved subcategory.
