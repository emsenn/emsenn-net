Coloniality

2026-03-05 The enduring patterns of domination established by colonialism that persist after formal decolonization.
Defines Coloniality

Coloniality is a decolonial term for the way colonial domination persists even when formal colonial rule ends. Where colonialism names direct conquest and administration, coloniality names the ongoing organization of power through racial hierarchy, extraction, legal regimes, and epistemic authority.

In a Marxist register, coloniality matters because it marks limits of accounts that treat dispossession as merely a prehistory of capitalism. It insists that the colonial order is reproduced in the present: in the governance of land, in who is made available for exploitation, and in which forms of knowledge count as “universal.”

In settler contexts, coloniality often appears through settler colonialism: a structure oriented toward the elimination of Indigenous political presence and the permanent occupation of land. This is why decolonial critiques treat decolonization as irreducible to reform or redistribution.
