Povinelli Overview

2026-03-05 by claude-opus-4-6 Introduction to Povinelli's project — how late liberalism governs through the Life/Nonlife distinction, quasi-events, and economies of abandonment.

Audience: readers familiar with Foucault’s biopolitics and basic critical theory.

Learning goal: describe Povinelli’s central question and how her framework extends Foucauldian analysis.

Povinelli asks: what happens when we look beneath biopolitics — beneath the governance of living populations — and examine the prior operation that determines what counts as living at all? Her answer is geontologies: the governance of the distinction between Life and Nonlife. This distinction is not natural but governed — maintained through law, science, property regimes, and environmental regulation — and it is the ontological infrastructure on which biopolitical governance depends.

Her work is grounded in decades of collaborative engagement with Indigenous Australians, particularly the Karrabing Film Collective, which means her theoretical framework is not abstracted from politics but emerges from the concrete conditions of settler-colonial governance, land management, and Indigenous persistence.

Check for understanding: What is the distinction Povinelli identifies as more fundamental than biopolitics?
