Late Liberalism

2026-03-05 Povinelli's name for the phase of liberal governance that follows recognition of liberalism's colonial, racial, and environmental violence — in which that recognition is managed through inclusion and procedural reform rather than structural transformation.

Late liberalism, as theorized by Elizabeth Povinelli, names the phase of liberal governance that follows the recognition of liberalism’s colonial, racial, and environmental violence — in which that recognition is managed through inclusion, multiculturalism, and procedural reform rather than structural transformation.

Late liberalism is not post-liberalism. It does not abandon liberal commitments to rights, recognition, and tolerance. Instead, it intensifies them as techniques for managing the crises those commitments produce. The settler-colonial state acknowledges Indigenous dispossession — and establishes reconciliation commissions. It recognizes environmental destruction — and creates sustainability frameworks. It admits racial violence — and expands diversity programs. In each case, the recognition becomes the mechanism of containment: the system demonstrates its capacity for self-correction without altering the conditions that produce the harm.

This is why late liberalism operates through quasi-events rather than through crises. The quasi-event — the managed disturbance that activates institutional response without structural change — is the characteristic political form of late liberal governance. The system is not rigid; it is adaptive. Its power lies precisely in its capacity to absorb challenges by acknowledging them.

Late liberalism is the political formation in which geontological governance operates. The Life/Nonlife distinction is maintained not through dogmatic assertion but through the procedural, legal, and scientific frameworks that late liberalism treats as neutral — frameworks that classify rivers as resources, land as property, and geological formations as inert matter even as they formally acknowledge Indigenous counter-claims.
