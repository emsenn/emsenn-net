Self-Determination

2026-03-08 by claude The capacity of individuals and communities impacted by disaster to make their own decisions about survival, recovery, and long-term resilience without outside coercion.
Defines self-determination
<p>Self-determination is the capacity of individuals and communities impacted by disaster to make their own decisions about survival, recovery, and long-term resilience without outside coercion [@madrcorevalues2019; @madrmission2019].</p> <p>Within emergent disaster response, the term matters because the central political struggle is often over who gets to define need, set priorities, and shape recovery. Mutual Aid Disaster Relief&rsquo;s core values and mission make self-determination explicit by treating outside aid as support for survivor-led decision-making rather than a substitute for it [@madrcorevalues2019; @madrmission2019].</p> <p>Self-determination is therefore more specific than goodwill. It is a governance principle that opposes paternalism, forced recovery models, and external control over community survival.</p> <h2 id="related-terms">Related terms</h2> <ul> <li><a href="./accountability-to-impacted-communities.md" class="link-internal">Accountability to Impacted Communities</a> - the governance principle that keeps response answerable to those most affected</li> <li><a href="./self-recovery.md" class="link-internal">Self-Recovery</a> - one recovery framework that protects decision-making power close to survivors</li> <li><a href="../../../../../schools/anarchism/terms/autonomy.md" class="link-internal">Autonomy</a> - a broader political concept that informs disaster self-determination</li> </ul>
