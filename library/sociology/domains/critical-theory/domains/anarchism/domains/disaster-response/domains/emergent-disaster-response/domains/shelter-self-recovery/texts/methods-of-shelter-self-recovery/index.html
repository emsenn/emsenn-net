Methods of Shelter Self-Recovery

2026-03-08
Abstract

A research text on the methods used in shelter self-recovery approaches.

Shelter self-recovery uses a distinctive set of methods that try to support rebuilding without displacing household and community agency. The main methods are accompaniment, technical guidance, owner-driven incremental repair, local focal points, and simple risk-reduction messages that people can use within real resource constraints [@pathways2022; @twigg2021; @ahmedparrack2022].

Accompaniment

Pathways Home describes support for self-recovery as accompanying a process rather than delivering a finished solution [pathways2022]. This matters because outside actors do not control the whole recovery pathway. They support people who are already making decisions under conditions of loss, scarcity, and urgency.

Incremental owner-driven repair

A key method in this school is staged, owner-driven rebuilding. Ahmed and Parrack show that in Vanuatu people moved through their own repair pathways using different combinations of labor, materials, assistance, and technical advice [ahmedparrack2022]. This method treats recovery as a sequence of household decisions rather than a single external delivery event.

Technical guidance and build back safer messages

Technical guidance is another core method. The Shelter Cluster’s build back safer key messages were explicitly designed for owner-driven incremental recovery, giving households simple accessible advice rather than assuming an ideal but unaffordable reconstruction model [buildbacksafer2019].
