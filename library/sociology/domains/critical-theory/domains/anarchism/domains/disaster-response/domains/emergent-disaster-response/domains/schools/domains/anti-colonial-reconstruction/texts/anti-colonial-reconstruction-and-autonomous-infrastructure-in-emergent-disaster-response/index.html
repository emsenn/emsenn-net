Anti-Colonial Reconstruction and Autonomous Infrastructure in Emergent Disaster Response

2026-03-08 by claude
Abstract

A research text on the current within emergent disaster response that links rebuilding to anti-colonial struggle and autonomous infrastructure.

An explicit current within emergent disaster response treats rebuilding as anti-colonial struggle. In this current, water systems, solar systems, and community spaces are built as forms of survival and as steps toward reducing dependency on colonial infrastructures and externally imposed recovery [@resistancecaribbean2017; @onsiegessolar2024].

Reconstruction against dependency

The Puerto Rico and Caribbean rebuild campaign states the issue clearly: modular water and energy systems are meant to provide immediate relief and long-term independence and autonomy for marginalized communities [resistancecaribbean2017]. This moves reconstruction beyond repair. It turns rebuilding into a contest over dependency itself.

Colonial bottlenecks as disaster infrastructure

On Sieges, Solidarity, and Solar shows that colonial relations are not background context. They actively shape what infrastructure can be built and when [onsiegessolar2024]. Ports, import restrictions, and the wider colonial economic order become bottlenecks that structure disaster response.

Autonomous infrastructure

The Solar Power to the People account and later Puerto Rico materials show autonomous infrastructure as a recurring answer: solar systems, water systems, and shared facilities maintained close to communities [@solarpower2018; @onsiegessolar2024]. These are not only resilient technologies. They are political technologies of self-determination.

Reconstruction in colonized time

The Powerful Shake places earthquake response in southern Puerto Rico inside a longer history of conquest, debt, and unelected austerity rule [thepowerfulshake2020]. That framing matters because it prevents reconstruction from being imagined as a neutral return to normal. The normal itself is part of what has to be contested.

Significance

This current matters because it gives emergent disaster response a more explicit account of colonial power. It shows that reconstruction can be organized as anti-colonial institution building rather than only as post-crisis repair.
