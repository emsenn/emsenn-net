Self-Recovery

2026-03-08 by claude A disaster-recovery term for affected households repairing or rebuilding through their own choices, labor, and resources, sometimes with outside support.
<p>Self-recovery is the process by which disaster-affected households repair, build, or rebuild through their own choices, labor, and resources, sometimes with outside support [@twigg2021; @ahmedparrack2022].</p> <p>The term matters because it centers household and community agency in recovery instead of treating reconstruction as something wholly delivered from outside. In shelter practice, support for self-recovery usually means helping people help themselves while preserving choice and local control <a href="#ref-pathways2022" class="citation">[pathways2022]</a>.</p> <h2 id="related-terms">Related terms</h2> <ul> <li><a href="./solidarity-not-charity.md" class="link-internal">Solidarity Not Charity</a> - a principle that aligns with supporting people to shape their own recovery</li> <li><a href="../texts/post-disaster-redevelopment-and-grassroots-recovery.md" class="link-internal">Post-Disaster Redevelopment and Grassroots Recovery</a> - a text on conflicts over the terms of return and rebuilding</li> <li><a href="../schools/shelter-self-recovery/index.md" class="link-internal">Shelter Self-Recovery</a> - a school page on the approach built around this term</li> </ul>
