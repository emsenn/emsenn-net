Autonomous Direct Action

2026-03-08 by claude A disaster-response term for direct action carried out through community initiative rather than state or nonprofit command.
<p>Autonomous direct action is direct action carried out through community initiative, shared principles, and voluntary coordination rather than through state or nonprofit command <a href="#ref-madrprinciples2020" class="citation">[madrprinciples2020]</a>.</p> <p>Within emergent disaster response, the term matters because it names a method of acting without waiting for permission while still orienting that action toward collective survival and accountability to impacted people [@madrmission2019; @madrprinciples2020].</p> <h2 id="related-terms">Related terms</h2> <ul> <li><a href="../../../../../terms/direct-action.md" class="link-internal">Direct action</a> - the broader practice of meeting needs without waiting for authorization</li> <li><a href="./solidarity-not-charity.md" class="link-internal">Solidarity Not Charity</a> - the political principle that often guides autonomous direct action in disaster work</li> <li><a href="../schools/mutual-aid-disaster-relief/index.md" class="link-internal">Mutual Aid Disaster Relief</a> - a school that explicitly centers this method</li> </ul>
