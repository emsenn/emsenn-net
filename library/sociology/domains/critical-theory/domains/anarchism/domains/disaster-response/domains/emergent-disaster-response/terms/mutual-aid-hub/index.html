Mutual Aid Hub

2026-03-08 by claude A decentralized coordination site where volunteers, supplies, information, and care practices are gathered, matched, and redistributed.
<p>A mutual aid hub is a decentralized coordination site where volunteers, supplies, information, and care practices are gathered, matched, and redistributed [@landau2022; @ambinder2013].</p> <p>Within emergent disaster response, hubs matter because they turn convergence into usable collective capacity. They can function at once as intake points, kitchens, sorting sites, canvassing bases, dispatch centers, and places where local assessments are turned into action [@watters2014; @landau2022].</p> <p>A hub is not defined only by storage or administration. It is also a relational form. It concentrates trust, attention, and coordination without requiring a fully centralized command structure.</p> <h2 id="related-terms">Related terms</h2> <ul> <li><a href="./community-kitchen.md" class="link-internal">Community Kitchen</a> - one recurring function that hubs often host</li> <li><a href="./local-needs-assessment.md" class="link-internal">Local Needs Assessment</a> - information that hubs collect and route</li> <li><a href="../texts/organizational-forms-in-emergent-disaster-response.md" class="link-internal">Organizational Forms in Emergent Disaster Response</a> - a research text situating hubs among other recurrent forms</li> </ul>
