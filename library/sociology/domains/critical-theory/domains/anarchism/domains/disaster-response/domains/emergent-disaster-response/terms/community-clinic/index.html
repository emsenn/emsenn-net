Community Clinic

2026-03-08 by claude A grassroots health-care site created by or with a community to provide accessible care under conditions where ordinary systems are absent, inaccessible, or untrusted.
Defines community clinic
<p>A community clinic is a grassroots health-care site created by or with a community to provide accessible care under conditions where ordinary systems are absent, inaccessible, or untrusted <a href="#ref-commonground2024" class="citation">[commonground2024]</a>.</p> <p>Within emergent disaster response, the term matters because care often has to be rebuilt locally rather than awaited from distant systems. Common Ground&rsquo;s clinic and later community-based disability response show that a clinic can function as both immediate health support and as infrastructure for continued participation in survival and recovery [@commonground2024; @kennedy2021].</p> <h2 id="related-terms">Related terms</h2> <ul> <li><a href="../texts/care-infrastructure-in-emergent-disaster-response.md" class="link-internal">Care Infrastructure in Emergent Disaster Response</a> - a research text on clinics, kitchens, and wellness centers</li> <li><a href="./wellness-center.md" class="link-internal">Wellness Center</a> - a related care form that can extend beyond medical treatment into broader support</li> <li><a href="../texts/disability-inclusive-grassroots-disaster-response.md" class="link-internal">Disability-Inclusive Grassroots Disaster Response</a> - a text on care and survival beyond able-bodied norms</li> </ul>
