Debrief

2026-03-08 by claude A reflective and informational practice in which participants return observations, lessons, problems, and needs from action back into collective learning and coordination.
<p>A debrief is a reflective and informational practice in which participants return observations, lessons, problems, and needs from action back into collective learning and coordination [@occupysandyorientation2012; @relieftoolkit2022].</p> <p>Within emergent disaster response, the term matters because response knowledge is often generated in the field and would otherwise remain fragmented. Occupy Sandy&rsquo;s field orientation required report-backs after actions, while Mutual Aid Disaster Relief&rsquo;s Relief Toolkit is explicitly framed as a way to facilitate sharing what disaster efforts learn across sites and events [@occupysandyorientation2012; @relieftoolkit2022].</p> <p>Debriefing is therefore not only reflection. It is one of the main ways emergent response turns lived experience into transmissible method.</p> <h2 id="related-terms">Related terms</h2> <ul> <li><a href="./distributed-sensemaking.md" class="link-internal">Distributed Sensemaking</a> - the wider process to which debriefs contribute</li> <li><a href="./lightweight-protocol.md" class="link-internal">Lightweight Protocol</a> - a procedural form that often requires report-backs</li> <li><a href="./skill-sharing.md" class="link-internal">Skill-Sharing</a> - a training practice that often draws on what debriefs produce</li> </ul>
