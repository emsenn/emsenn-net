Community Kitchen

2026-03-08 by claude A grassroots food-preparation and distribution site that turns donations, labor, and local knowledge into collective nourishment.
<p>A community kitchen is a grassroots food-preparation and distribution site that turns donations, labor, and local knowledge into collective nourishment [@landau2022; @watters2014].</p> <p>Within emergent disaster response, the term matters because food aid is not only a matter of handing out goods. Community kitchens transform raw supplies into usable meals, gather people into a shared social space, and make it easier to adapt provision to changing local need [@madrprograms2024; @watters2014].</p> <p>Community kitchens often operate inside hubs, churches, community centers, or temporary mutual-aid sites. They show how disaster response can shift from distribution alone toward collective reproduction.</p> <h2 id="related-terms">Related terms</h2> <ul> <li><a href="../texts/care-infrastructure-in-emergent-disaster-response.md" class="link-internal">Care Infrastructure in Emergent Disaster Response</a> - the wider field of care that kitchens support</li> <li><a href="./mutual-aid-hub.md" class="link-internal">Mutual Aid Hub</a> - a frequent host form for kitchens and meal distribution</li> <li><a href="./survival-programs.md" class="link-internal">Survival Programs</a> - a wider mutual-aid framework for meeting immediate needs while building political capacity</li> </ul>
