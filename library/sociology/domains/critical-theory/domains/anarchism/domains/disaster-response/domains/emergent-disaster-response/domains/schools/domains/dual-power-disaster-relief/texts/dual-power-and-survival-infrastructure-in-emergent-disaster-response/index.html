Dual Power and Survival Infrastructure in Emergent Disaster Response

2026-03-08 by claude A research text on the current within emergent disaster response that builds infrastructure for survival as a step toward durable alternatives.
<p>One explicit current within emergent disaster response treats disaster relief as part of building alternative infrastructure. In this current, food distribution, clinics, wellness spaces, popular education, and energy systems are not only temporary emergency measures. They are part of a <a href="../../../../../../../terms/dual-power.md" class="link-internal">dual power</a> strategy of building capacities people can continue to use beyond the immediate crisis [@madrabout2025; @madrprograms2024].</p> <h2 id="survival-infrastructure">Survival infrastructure</h2> <p>Mutual Aid Disaster Relief&rsquo;s language of <a href="../../../terms/survival-programs.md" class="link-internal">survival programs</a> makes this current explicit. Immediate aid is linked to long-term power, consciousness, and community capacity rather than being treated as neutral service <a href="#ref-madrprograms2024" class="citation">[madrprograms2024]</a>. This is one of the clearest ways that disaster response becomes a site of institution building.</p> <h2 id="relief-tied-to-liberation-struggles">Relief tied to liberation struggles</h2> <p>MADR&rsquo;s core values connect disaster work to the interconnectedness of liberation struggles and the need to dismantle harmful systems while creating what people need to live <a href="#ref-madrcorevalues2019" class="citation">[madrcorevalues2019]</a>. This gives the current a clearer political content than generic resilience language. Infrastructure is not built simply to bounce back. It is built to reduce dependence on oppressive institutions.</p> <h2 id="beyond-the-event">Beyond the event</h2> <p>The <code>Solar Power to the People</code> account shows how this current moves from immediate response toward durable autonomous infrastructure <a href="#ref-solarpower2018" class="citation">[solarpower2018]</a>. Energy systems, water systems, and shared facilities matter because they extend disaster response into ongoing material self-governance.</p> <h2 id="significance">Significance</h2> <p>This current matters because it makes the political horizon of emergent disaster response more explicit. It asks not only how people survive the crisis, but what infrastructures of survival can become the basis for a less dependent and more collectively self-determined life afterward.</p> <h2 id="sources">Sources</h2> <ul> <li><a href="https://mutualaiddisasterrelief.org/about/" class="link-external">Mutual Aid Disaster Relief: <em>About</em></a></li> <li><a href="https://mutualaiddisasterrelief.org/core-values/" class="link-external">Mutual Aid Disaster Relief: <em>Core Values</em></a></li> <li><a href="https://mutualaiddisasterrelief.org/programs/" class="link-external">Mutual Aid Disaster Relief: <em>Survival Programs</em></a></li> <li><a href="https://mutualaiddisasterrelief.org/solar-power-to-the-people/" class="link-external">Mutual Aid Disaster Relief: <em>(Solar) Power to the People</em></a></li> </ul>
