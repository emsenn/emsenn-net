Improvisation

2026-03-08 by claude A disaster-response term for adaptive action created under novel conditions when plans and routines are insufficient.
Defines improvisation
<p>Improvisation is adaptive action created under novel conditions when plans, routines, or formal structures are insufficient <a href="#ref-kendrawachtendorf2006" class="citation">[kendrawachtendorf2006]</a>.</p> <p>Within emergent disaster response, the term matters because disasters regularly generate needs and relations that cannot be fully anticipated. Improvisation is therefore not a sign that response has failed. It is a core capacity through which people and organizations discover what needs to be done under changing conditions [@kendrawachtendorf2006; @quarantelli1995].</p> <h2 id="related-terms">Related terms</h2> <ul> <li><a href="./problem-solving-model.md" class="link-internal">Problem-Solving Model</a> - the method frame that treats improvisation as necessary rather than deviant</li> <li><a href="./distributed-sensemaking.md" class="link-internal">Distributed Sensemaking</a> - the process through which actors collectively determine what improvisation should do</li> <li><a href="../texts/improvisation-and-resourcefulness-in-emergent-disaster-response.md" class="link-internal">Improvisation and Resourcefulness in Emergent Disaster Response</a> - a research text on this capacity</li> </ul>
