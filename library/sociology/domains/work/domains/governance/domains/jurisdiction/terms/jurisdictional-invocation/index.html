2026-03-20 A JurisdictionalInvocation is a triple (G, F, φ) — an invoker G with standing in F, a forum F with jurisdiction J = (A, T_J, H_J, V), and a matter φ — such that G's act introduces φ into F's nucleus domain H^J_t, enabling σ^J_t to evaluate JV(A, φ, t). Before invocation, φ exists as a fiber element in H_t but is not within F's active matter queue. After invocation, the forum must either fix JV(A, φ, t) ∈ H*_t (proceed) or return it to ⊥ (dismiss for want of jurisdiction).

JurisdictionalInvocation

Formal definition

An JurisdictionalInvocation is a triple (G,F,φ)(G, F, \varphi):

Inv=(G:Person,  F:Forum,  φ∈Ht)\mathcal{Inv} = (G : \mathrm{Person},\; F : \mathrm{Forum},\; \varphi \in H_t)

where:

  • GG is the invoker — the party introducing the matter; GG must hold assertive Standing in FF for the act-class Φ\Phi that includes introducing matters of the type φ\varphi
  • FF is the forum with jurisdiction J=(A,TJ,HJ,V)\mathcal{J} = (A, T_J, H_J, V) — the normative system whose nucleus σtJ\sigma^{\mathcal{J}}_t is being requested to operate on φ\varphi
  • φ∈Ht\varphi \in H_t is the matter — the fiber element being introduced; it exists in HtH_t prior to invocation but is not in FF’s sub-fiber HtJH^J_t until GG’s act places it there

The invocation act. GG’s invocation is a fiber element δinv∈Htinv\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in H^{\mathrm{inv}}_t — an element in the invocation sub-fiber — that satisfies:

δinv=Intro(G,F,φ,t)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} = \mathrm{Intro}(G, F, \varphi, t)

where Intro\mathrm{Intro} is the constitutive rule “invoker GG presenting matter φ\varphi to forum FF at tt counts as φ\varphi entering FF’s active-matter queue HtJH^J_t.” JurisdictionalInvocation is an act in the sense of Act: it is authored by GG, occurs at tt, and produces a new settled element — φ\varphi is now a pending matter within HtJH^J_t.

Effect on the forum’s nucleus. After invocation, the forum’s saturation nucleus σtJ\sigma^{\mathcal{J}}_t is obligated to evaluate JV(A,φ,t)=RJ(pos(A,t),φ,V(t,φ))\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) = R_{\mathcal{J}}(\mathrm{pos}(A, t), \varphi, V(t, \varphi)). The nucleus must produce one of two outcomes:

  1. Proceed: all four jurisdiction conditions hold — t∈TJt \in T_J, φ∈HJ\varphi \in H_J, V(t,φ)=⊤V(t, \varphi) = \top, pos(A,t)∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) — so JV(A,φ,t)∈Ht∗J\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \in H^{*\mathcal{J}}_t. The matter is valid before the forum.
  2. Dismiss: at least one condition fails. JV(A,φ,t)∉Ht∗J\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \notin H^{*\mathcal{J}}_t. The matter is ultra vires; FF has no normative basis to act on it. The forum’s nucleus fixes ⊥\bot in the relevant sub-fiber.

What invocation is not. JurisdictionalInvocation does not enlarge TJT_J, HJH_J, or VV. It does not create jurisdiction. It activates the evaluative machinery on a specific φ\varphi. The jurisdiction either covers φ\varphi or it does not; invocation forces the determination.

Nuclear reading

The invocation act introduces a matter into the forum’s active domain. At the nuclear level, the relevant object is the invocation proposition δinv∈Ht\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in H_t and its relationship to Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t).

Definition (invocation act as a fiber element). δinv∈Ht\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in H_t is the proposition “invoker GG has presented matter φ\varphi to forum FF at tt.” The condition δinv∈Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) means the invocation is past-saturated: the act has been genuinely performed and is not merely asserted. This is a definition.

Proposition 1 (invocation and jurisdictional validity are independent fiber elements). The elements δinv\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} and JV(A,φ,t)=pos(A,t)∧φ\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) = \mathrm{pos}(A, t) \wedge \varphi are distinct elements of HtH_t. Their relationship — specifically, whether δinv∈Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) implies JV(A,φ,t)∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) — is not a consequence of the nuclear axioms alone.

Proof. The nuclear axioms (extensiveness, idempotence, meet-preservation) apply uniformly to all elements of HtH_t and say nothing about implications between distinct elements. Whether δinv≤JV(A,φ,t)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \leq \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) holds in the Heyting algebra, or whether δinv\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} implies JV(A,φ,t)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) as a Heyting implication, depends on the normative content encoded in HtH_t, not on the nuclear axioms. □\square

Proposition 2 (settled invocation and settled jurisdictional validity are jointly settled). If δinv∈Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) and JV(A,φ,t)∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t), then δinv∧JV(A,φ,t)∈Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t).

Proof. By meet-preservation of σt\sigma_t (see Meet Preservation):

σt(δinv∧JV(A,φ,t))=σt(δinv)∧σt(JV(A,φ,t))=δinv∧JV(A,φ,t).□\sigma_t(\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t)) = \sigma_t(\delta_{\mathrm{inv}}) \wedge \sigma_t(\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t)) = \delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t). \quad \square

When both the invocation is properly made and the jurisdictional conditions are met, the joint proposition is settled. This is the nuclear content of “invocation accepted and jurisdiction confirmed” being a stable settled fact.

Proposition 3 (the meet of invocation with dismissed matter). If δinv∈Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) and JV(A,φ,t)∉Fix(σt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) (jurisdiction is not established), then δinv∧JV(A,φ,t)∉Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t).

Proof. By meet-preservation: σt(δinv∧JV(A,φ,t))=δinv∧σt(JV(A,φ,t))\sigma_t(\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t)) = \delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \sigma_t(\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t)). Since JV(A,φ,t)∉Fix(σt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t), we have σt(JV(A,φ,t))>JV(A,φ,t)\sigma_t(\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t)) > \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) (by extensiveness, see Saturation Nucleus). Therefore δinv∧σt(JV(A,φ,t))>δinv∧JV(A,φ,t)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \sigma_t(\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t)) > \delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t), so δinv∧JV(A,φ,t)∉Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \wedge \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t). □\square

This is the nuclear basis of dismissal: a proper invocation of a matter outside the forum’s jurisdiction does not produce a settled acceptance. The invocation is settled (δinv∈Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t)) but the jurisdictional validity proposition is not, and their meet is accordingly not settled.

Proposition 4 (idempotence of invocation act). σt(σt(δinv))=σt(δinv)\sigma_t(\sigma_t(\delta_{\mathrm{inv}})) = \sigma_t(\delta_{\mathrm{inv}}).

Proof. Idempotence of σt\sigma_t (see Idempotence). □\square

A valid invocation does not become “more invoked” under further saturation. The act either enters Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) or it does not; there is no intermediate state.

What is not derivable from the nuclear axioms alone. The claim that invocation cannot be un-received — that once δinv∈Fix(σt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) the forum is obligated to evaluate jurisdiction — is not a consequence of the nuclear axioms. It requires an additional norm specifying that forums must respond to properly made invocations. Similarly, the claim that δinv∈Fix(Δt)\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} \in \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) (that the invocation is transfer-stable, persisting into all forward extensions) requires the extension-structure condition defining Fix(Δt)\mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) (see Transfer Nucleus), which is not derivable from the nuclear axioms alone. Commutation of σt\sigma_t and Δt\Delta_t (see Commutation) ensures compatibility but does not establish the transfer-stability of δinv\delta_{\mathrm{inv}} in particular.

JurisdictionalInvocation and multiple forums

A single matter φ\varphi may be invoked before multiple forums F1,F2F_1, F_2 simultaneously — generating invocation acts δinv1\delta^1_{\mathrm{inv}} and δinv2\delta^2_{\mathrm{inv}} in separate sub-fibers. The resulting JV(A1,φ,t)\mathrm{JV}(A_1, \varphi, t) and JV(A2,φ,t)\mathrm{JV}(A_2, \varphi, t) may both be in Ht∗H^*_t (concurrent jurisdiction). The conflict resolution rules from NormConflict then determine which forum’s resolution propagates as the authoritative Ht∗H^*_t settlement.

Open questions

  • Whether invocation is itself subject to dismissal: can the forum determine that GG lacks standing before evaluating whether it has jurisdiction over φ\varphi? This requires a two-stage nucleus evaluation: first σtstanding\sigma^{\mathrm{standing}}_t (processing standing), then σtJ\sigma^{\mathcal{J}}_t (processing jurisdictional validity).
  • Whether a forum can be obligated to receive an invocation — whether there is a meta-norm that requires forums to evaluate jurisdiction when properly invoked, or whether a forum can decline to receive the invocation before evaluating it (the “door is closed” refusal, distinct from dismissal for want of jurisdiction).
