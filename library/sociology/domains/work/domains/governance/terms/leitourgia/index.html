2026-03-19 A Leitourgia is a quadruple (λ, φ, C, ι) — a designated agent in Hohfeldian liability position, a prescribed-form proposition in the fiber Heyting algebra, a witnessing community constituted by a covering family in the Grothendieck topology, and a discharge proposition. Full discharge is ι ∈ H*_t: past-settled (saturation-nucleus-fixed) and forward-stable (transfer-nucleus-fixed) simultaneously. The personal cost is the Landauer cost of the settling step: spectral mass of the kernel of the counit.

Leitourgia

What it is

A Leitourgia is a quadruple (λ,  φ,  C,  ι)(\lambda,\; \varphi,\; C,\; \iota):

Λ=(λ,  φ,  C,  ι)\Lambda = (\lambda,\; \varphi,\; C,\; \iota)

where:

  • λ\lambda is the leitourgos — an Entity in Hohfeldian liability position with respect to the community CC: designated by the appointing authority’s power, not by self-selection. Olson’s positional compulsion (The Logic of Collective Action, 1965, p.2): the obligation follows from station, not consent. The leitourgos is not an agent who has decided to serve; the station designates the service.
  • φ∈Ht\varphi \in H_t is the prescribed-form proposition — a proposition in the fiber Heyting algebra at current history tt specifying what correct enactment consists in. Austin felicity condition A.1 (How to Do Things with Words, 1962, p.14): “there must exist an accepted conventional procedure having a certain conventional effect.” The prescribed form is not a general contribution but a determinate element of HtH_t with a yes/no satisfaction condition.
  • CC is the witnessing community — not a pre-existing assembly but the entity constituted by the amalgamation of a J(t)J(t)-covering family of witnessing observations into a global section of Ht∗H^*_t. Bell (Ritual Theory, Ritual Practice, 1992, pp.69-93): “ritualization constitutes the community rather than expressing it.” CC exists as CC because and only because the enactment is collectively witnessed across a cover. Before the cover is assembled there is no community — there are only local observers.
  • ι∈Ht\iota \in H_t is the discharge proposition — the element of the fiber Heyting algebra asserting “λ\lambda enacted φ\varphi before CC.” This is the proposition about the enactment, not the prescribed form itself. φ\varphi specifies what must be enacted; ι\iota records whether the enactment has occurred.

Type note. φ\varphi and ι\iota are distinct elements of HtH_t. The prescribed form φ\varphi is a standing normative specification — it exists in HtH_t whether or not any leitourgos has acted. The discharge proposition ι\iota is a contingent claim whose truth value depends on whether λ\lambda has enacted φ\varphi before CC. What earlier formulations conflated — the normative form and the enactment record — are now distinct. This parallels the incumbent distinction between the incumbency proposition ι\iota (whether aa holds OO) and the office structure OO (which holds regardless of any occupant): see Incumbent.

Full discharge is the condition ι∈Ht∗=Fix(σt)∩Fix(Δt)\iota \in H^*_t = \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) \cap \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t): doubly stable. When ι∉Ht∗\iota \notin H^*_t, the service is partially discharged — one nucleus is satisfied but not both. See the two-stage discharge section below.

Personal cost. The enactment step ss that settles ι\iota carries the Landauer cost Ls(t)=M(δ∣Ks(t))\mathcal{L}_s(t) = M(\delta|_{\mathcal{K}_s(t)}) — the spectral mass of the kernel Ks(t)=ker⁡(εs(t):Hs⋆t→Ht)\mathcal{K}_s(t) = \ker(\varepsilon_s(t) : H_{s \star t} \to H_t): the sub-Heyting-algebra of propositions at s⋆ts \star t that vanish when ss is forgotten (Landauer, IBM Journal of Research and Development, 5:183–191, 1961). This is not a free parameter of the quadruple but a structural consequence of the step ss: the leitourgos bears a cost whose magnitude is fixed by the spectral structure of the step, not by the leitourgos’s choice. The irreversibility is not a defect but the defining mark distinguishing genuine leitourgia from a performance of leitourgia.

The Athenian structure

Leitourgia (Greek: leitos, public + ergon, work) was in classical Athens a legally required public service borne by wealthy citizens at their own expense: funding a trireme (trierarchy), equipping a dramatic chorus (choregia), provisioning a sacred embassy (theoria). Not a tax, which is distributed; not charity, which is voluntary. A positional obligation — compelled by civic standing, borne at irreversible personal cost, directed toward a specific form whose correct enactment benefited the whole community.

The structural features that persist from this civic institution:

  1. Designation by position, not choice: the liturgical class was determined by assessed wealth. A man who crossed the wealth threshold owed the service. Nomination by a rival (antidosis) was the mechanism for identifying who held the position (Davies 1971).
  2. A determinate prescribed form: not “contribute to the navy” but “provide a specific trireme, crewed and maintained, for one year.” The form had a yes/no satisfaction condition. Demosthenes Against Meidias 21.155 documents the legal content of correct trierarchic performance.
  3. A community constituted by the performance: the Athenian theatrical festivals were not performances for an audience that existed independently. The audience was constituted as the demos — the political body of Athens — by assembling in the theater and witnessing the choruses funded by the liturgy. Aristotle Politics 1280b30: the city is not a commercial alliance but is constituted by shared participation in its own institutions.
  4. Irreversible personal cost: the trierarch could not recover the expenditure. This irreversibility was the liturgy’s marker of genuine civic commitment — it distinguished it from a refundable levy or a performance for fee.

The two-stage discharge

The Incumbent spec derives two installation stages — institution (σt\sigma_t-fixed) and induction (Δt\Delta_t-fixed) — from ecclesiastical law. The leitourgia has a structurally parallel two-stage discharge:

Form-enacted (perfectus actus): The leitourgos enacts φ\varphi in the correct form before CC. The accumulation of relational history — the completed witnessing sieve — settles ι\iota under the saturation nucleus: σt(ι)=ι\sigma_t(\iota) = \iota. The past has no further demand. This corresponds to Austin’s felicity condition A.2 (complete execution) — the procedure is complete.

Forward-stable (perpetua effectus): All one-step extensions of tt carry ι\iota forward under the transfer nucleus: Δt(ι)=ι\Delta_t(\iota) = \iota. Every future history inherits the discharge as already-accomplished. No successor can be called upon to discharge what λ\lambda has already discharged. This is the structural content of “once served, always served” — the discharge is not re-litigable in any extension.

The ordering σt(ι)=ι\sigma_t(\iota) = \iota before Δt(ι)=ι\Delta_t(\iota) = \iota is the normal sequence: the past settles the enactment as correctly executed, and then the transfer nucleus carries that settled state forward. The reverse order — forward-stable before past-recognized — is structurally anomalous, as Δt\Delta_t is extensive (ι≤Δt(ι)\iota \leq \Delta_t(\iota)) and idempotent, but whether the saturation nucleus automatically settles what the transfer nucleus already carries is not yet derived; see the first open question.

Community-constitution as sheaf amalgamation

The original formulation described community-constitution as “a formal correlate in sheaf theory” — an analogy. This section states the hypothesis precisely enough to be a derived claim or a forced open question.

Hypothesis. The witnessing community CC at history tt is constituted as follows: a family of local witnessing observations {wi:1→Hti}i∈I\{w_i : 1 \to H_{t_i}\}_{i \in I} — one at each history tit_i in a covering sieve S∈J(t)S \in J(t) — forms a compatible family iff H(f)(wj)=H(f′)(wi)H(f)(w_j) = H(f')(w_i) whenever f:tk→tif : t_k \to t_i and f′:tk→tjf' : t_k \to t_j are morphisms in SS. The sheaf axiom (fiber doctrine) guarantees that every compatible family has a unique amalgamation w:1→Htw : 1 \to H_t — a global section. The community CC is the entity indexed by this global amalgamation: it exists as a unified entity iff the witnessing observations cohere across a cover.

Before the covering sieve assembles, there are local witnesses but no community. The community comes into existence at the moment the compatible family becomes a global section. This is the formal content of Bell’s claim that ritualization constitutes the community: the sheaf gluing IS the constitution.

The claim that ι∈Ht∗\iota \in H^*_t requires this community-constitution is the critical addition: not only must the enactment occur, but the witnessing sieve must cohere. An enactment witnessed by an incoherent family — one that cannot be amalgamated — does not discharge the leitourgia, because there is no community before whom discharge could be recognized. Austin’s felicity condition B.1 (How to Do Things with Words, 1962, p.15): “the particular persons and circumstances in a given case must be appropriate for the invocation of the particular procedure invoked.” Inappropriate witnesses = incomplete cover = no amalgamation = no community = no discharge.

Personal cost and the Landauer connection

The Landauer cost Ls(t)\mathcal{L}_s(t) is not a separate formal parameter but arises from the step structure. When the enactment step ss fires — settling ι\iota in Hs⋆tH_{s \star t} — the counit εs(t):Hs⋆t→Ht\varepsilon_s(t) : H_{s \star t} \to H_t restricts the new fiber back to the old. The kernel Ks(t)=ker⁡(εs(t))\mathcal{K}_s(t) = \ker(\varepsilon_s(t)) is the sub-Heyting-algebra of propositions settled by ss that do not carry back. These propositions enter the community’s fiber (at s⋆ts \star t) but are invisible from HtH_t — they are the service’s contribution to the community that the leitourgos cannot reclaim.

The Landauer bound then gives the thermodynamic cost:

ΔELandauer(t,s)  ≥  εmin⁡⋅Tthermo(t)⋅Ls(t)\Delta\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{Landauer}}(t, s) \;\geq\; \varepsilon_{\min} \cdot T_{\mathrm{thermo}}(t) \cdot \mathcal{L}_s(t)

where εmin⁡\varepsilon_{\min} is the minimum spectral drop (the relational cost constant) and Tthermo(t)=Mt(δ)T_{\mathrm{thermo}}(t) = M_t(\delta) is the spectral temperature at tt. The personal cost is bounded below by εmin⁡⋅Tthermo(t)⋅Ls(t)\varepsilon_{\min} \cdot T_{\mathrm{thermo}}(t) \cdot \mathcal{L}_s(t). An enactment step with Ls(t)=0\mathcal{L}_s(t) = 0 — one that settles nothing that does not already carry back — has zero Landauer cost and therefore zero personal cost. Such a step cannot be a genuine leitourgia: no cost, no positional discharge.

The irreversibility condition. A leitourgia with full discharge ι∈Ht∗\iota \in H^*_t and Ls(t)>0\mathcal{L}_s(t) > 0 is irreversible in two senses simultaneously: the nucleus fixed-point condition means no further normative demand can be made (σt\sigma_t and Δt\Delta_t are already satisfied), and the Landauer cost means the spectral information produced by the step is permanently deposited in the community’s fiber. The leitourgos cannot retrieve what the step cost. These two irreversibilities are structurally connected — both arise from the step ss — but whether they are formally the same irreversibility or two independent ones is the third open question below.

Theological development

The Septuagint uses leitourgia for Temple priestly service (1 Chr 23:28; 26:30; Num 8:22); the New Testament and patristic literature extend it to corporate worship. The structural transformation: the designated minister enacts a prescribed form before the assembly; the cost shifts from financial (trierarchy’s outlay) to consecrated time, purity, and submission to form; the recipient shifts from the city to the divine. Bradshaw (The Search for the Origins of Christian Worship, Oxford, 2002, pp.1–30) traces how the Eucharist inherits the positional-designation structure from both civic and Temple antecedents.

The invariant structure persists across this transformation: designated leitourgos (λ\lambda), prescribed form (φ\varphi), community constituted by the assembly’s witnessing (CC), personal cost (κ\kappa). The nuclear conditions — σt(ι)=ι\sigma_t(\iota) = \iota (recognized by the accumulated normative past) and Δt(ι)=ι\Delta_t(\iota) = \iota (inherited by all future histories of the community) — map onto the distinction between valid celebration (perfecta execitio) and the permanent effect of valid celebration (character indelebilis in sacramental theology: the priesthood, once conferred, cannot be rescinded). Whether this theological extension generates derivable claims or remains interpretive is an open question of the same kind as the institution/induction mapping in Incumbent.

Open questions

  • Whether the covering sieve JJ encodes social witnessing: The community-constitution hypothesis states that the witnessing family must form a J(t)J(t)-covering sieve for amalgamation to occur. But the Grothendieck topology JJ on TT is defined by the step-commutation and history-extension axioms of the history site — it is not defined by social witnessing. Whether social witnessing (the presence of the relevant witnesses at tt) is captured by the existing JJ, or whether a leitourgia-specific enrichment of the site is required — a second topology JwitnessJ_{\mathrm{witness}} alongside JJ, or a morphism of sites — is not yet derived. If two topologies are required, community-constitution in the leitourgia sense cannot be expressed within the existing site structure and requires a bisite or a morphism of Grothendieck sites.

  • Whether σt\sigma_t-settlement entails Δt\Delta_t-settlement for discharge propositions: The two-stage table above lists form-enacted (σt\sigma_t-fixed) and forward-stable (Δt\Delta_t-fixed) as independent conditions. But the commuting nucleus pair condition — σt∘Δt=Δt∘σt\sigma_t \circ \Delta_t = \Delta_t \circ \sigma_t — plus the extensive and idempotent properties of both nuclei, may suffice to show that for discharge propositions of a specific type (those of the form “λ enacted φ before C”), σt(ι)=ι\sigma_t(\iota) = \iota implies Δt(ι)=ι\Delta_t(\iota) = \iota. If this implication holds for the discharge proposition specifically, the two-stage distinction collapses to a single condition for leitourgia and the distinction from Incumbent is formal, not substantive. This derivation requires characterizing which propositions in HtH_t behave this way, and is not yet carried out.

  • Whether the leitourgos’s personal cost is formally identical to the Landauer cost of the step: The hypothesis identifies the personal cost κ\kappa borne by λ\lambda with Ls(t)\mathcal{L}_s(t) — the spectral mass of the kernel of the counit. This is motivated: the step ss produces information (settles propositions in Hs⋆tH_{s \star t}) that does not return to HtH_t; the leitourgos, occupying the position that fires ss, bears that irreversible production. But this identification requires a model of what it means for λ\lambda — an Entity at tt — to bear the cost of a step that produces a fiber at s⋆ts \star t. Whether the Entity λ\lambda is formally the system that undergoes the erasure (identifying λ\lambda’s resource expenditure with the kernel’s spectral mass) or merely occupies the position that triggers the step is not yet derived. Without this derivation, the Landauer cost remains a structural analog of personal cost rather than a formal identification.
