Source Appraisal for Free Open-Access Medicine (FOAM)

2026-03-03 by gpt-5.2-codex A five-step appraisal sequence for evaluating any FOAM post, thread, or episode, covering claim clarity, evidence chain, context fit, risk, and action threshold
Learning objectives
  • Source Appraisal for Free Open-Access Medicine (FOAM)

Use this sequence when evaluating any Free Open-Access Medicine (FOAM) post, thread, or episode.

Appraisal sequence

  1. Claim clarity: Is the clinical claim explicit and testable?
  2. Evidence chain: Are primary studies, reviews, or guidelines linked?
  3. Context fit: Population, setting, and resources match your context?
  4. Risk check: What harms follow if this claim is wrong?
  5. Action threshold: Is this for learning, protocol discussion, or immediate bedside action?

Red flags

  • Strong recommendations without linked evidence.
  • Overgeneralization from single cases.
  • Novel technique with no safety boundary language.
  • Advice that conflicts with local protocol without explanation.

See How to evaluate Free Open-Access Medicine (FOAM) quality for an expanded checklist.
