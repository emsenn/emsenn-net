table culture

2026-01-01
Defines table culture

Table culture is the set of shared norms, habits, and expectations that govern how players interact during a tabletop role-playing game. Every group develops its own table culture, whether consciously or not: how much crosstalk is acceptable, whether players use character voices, how phones are handled, who brings snacks, and how disagreements about rules are resolved.

Table culture shapes the play experience as much as the game system does. A group that values immersion will develop norms around staying in character and minimizing out-of-game chatter. A group that values tactical challenge will develop norms around thorough planning and optimized builds. Neither approach is better; what matters is that the group’s norms are shared rather than assumed.

Session zero is where table culture is made explicit. The game master and players discuss not just what the game will be about but how the group will play it. Unspoken norms become sources of friction when different players carry different assumptions from previous groups.
