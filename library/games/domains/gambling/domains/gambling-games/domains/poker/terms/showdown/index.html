Defines showdown

A showdown in poker is the stage at the end of a hand where the remaining players reveal their cards to determine who wins the pot [@britannica2026poker]. A hand reaches showdown only if betting does not end it earlier.

Showdown matters because it is only one of two ways a poker hand can end. The other is collective folding by all but one player. This is why poker cannot be reduced to hand-ranking alone: the threat of future showdown influences betting long before cards are actually revealed.
