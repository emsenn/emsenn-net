Website

The website for the nursery is implemented using the Hugo website generator, using my own theme.

This section has subsections for:

  • The configuration file that Hugo and my theme use.
  • Some of the pages on that website. They're exported from their Org-mode source file into Hugo-compatible Markdown using Ox-Hugo. Additional pages, like those for my writing, are created by using Ox-Hugo on the appropriate Org-mode file.
  • Local data, such as an array of my recent activities or upcoming plans.

This doc is largely just the content and configuration - to see how it is used, you'll want to look at the theme documentation.

Summary for Settlers/*: This is a /very small nursery in Chapel Hill, North Carolina focused on cultivating an indigenous relationship to the land through its soil and plants.

Request currently available inventory by emailing emsenn@emsenn.net.

Donations wanted! The nursery is looking to expand its capacity.

Continue reading for more information.

Makȟáša [mahk-hA-sha] (red earth) Workshop & Nursery is a space near the southern part of Lloyd's Branch of Bolin Creek, feeding into Little Creek that runs into Jordan Lake, in the Haw River watershed, part of the Cape Fear River Basin.

It is on land where once the people who called themselves Shoccorree lived their lives. They were displaced by colonizers, never ceding their territory. Those colonizers have had a very different relationship with the land than the Shoccorree, and it has taken a lot from the land. Animals have vanished, plants have been displaced and diseased, topsoil has been eroded, and what remains has been drained of nutrients.

This workshop is a humble gift from emsenn to the land; a labor to restore the relationship of mutual gift-giving that humans once used to ensure their long-term survival and prosperity.

Here, an effort is made to cultivate living reciprocity, from the stone beneath to the sun and sky, and everything between.

Projects

Biochar

Sustainably collected wood is turned into biochar, a soil amendment.

Clay Extraction

Clay is extracted from the indigenous topsoil and subsoil, for use in on-site constructions.

Composting

Composting is done on-site, using indigenous worms and insects.

Livestream

Sometimes, you can watch folk live and labour in the space at emsenn's Twitch channel.

Past videos are also available on Twitch.

Land Overview

Every so often emsenn does a brief walkaround the space, looking at what's changed since the last overview. These are available on Youtube.

Mycoremediation Garden

Located at the focal point for runoff from the gravel parking lot directly south, this garden is an exploration of using fungi to allow plants to more rapidly respond to environmental conditions, including toxicity.

Native Plants

Several native plant specimens are maintained within the space.

  ;; emsenn's land management elisp
  ;; (elme)
  (require 'ht)

  ;; data
  (setq elme-catalog (ht-create))

  ;; functions
  (defun elme-add-specimen! (id info)
    (ht-set! elme-catalog id info))
  (defun elme-get-specimen (id)
    (ht-get elme-catalog id))
  (defun elme-get-specimen-attribute (id attr)
    (ht-get (ht-get elme-catalog id) attr))
  (defun elme-get-specimen-species (id)
    (elme-get-specimen-attribute id 'species))

  ;; examples
  (elme-add-specimen! 1 (ht ('species "foobar")))
  (elme-get-specimen 1)
  (elme-get-specimen-attribute 1 'species)
  (elme-get-specimen-species 1)
