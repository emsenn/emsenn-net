spread

2025-12-31
Defines spread

A spread is a layout that assigns interpretive roles to card positions in a tarot reading. Each position in the spread carries a meaning — past, present, future; situation, obstacle, advice; self, other, outcome — and the card drawn into that position is read through that meaning.

Spreads range from a single card (drawn for a quick reflection or daily practice) to elaborate layouts of ten or more positions. The three-card spread (commonly read as past/present/future or situation/action/outcome) is the most widely used format for quick readings. The Celtic Cross, a ten-card layout, is the most well-known complex spread in the Western tarot tradition.

The spread acts as a scaffold for interpretation: it constrains what a given card is about without constraining what the card says. The same card in a “challenge” position means something different than in an “outcome” position, because the positional role shapes the interpretive frame. In this sense, a spread is a structured sign system — each position is a slot in a relational structure, and meaning arises from the intersection of card, position, and the reader’s interpretive context.

The choice of spread reflects the question being asked. Open-ended questions suit larger, more exploratory layouts; specific yes/no questions work better with constrained formats. Experienced readers often modify traditional spreads or design their own to fit particular situations.
