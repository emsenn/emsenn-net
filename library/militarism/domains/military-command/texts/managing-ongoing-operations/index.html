<p>Learning goal: treat operations as an ongoing management problem and understand why battle rhythm is both enabling and dangerous.</p> <h2 id="operations-are-not-a-single-plan">Operations are not a single plan</h2> <p>Even when an order is well-written, the situation changes. Operations management is the practice of keeping an organization coherent across time:</p> <ul> <li>updating shared understanding,</li> <li>making timely decisions,</li> <li>and preventing staff processes from becoming empty ritual.</li> </ul> <p>The <a href="../terms/operations-process.md" class="link-internal">operations process</a> is the high-level paradigm: plan, prepare, execute, assess, and then do it again <a href="#ref-usarmy2019adp5_0" class="citation">[usarmy2019adp5_0]</a>.</p> <h2 id="battle-rhythm-is-decision-infrastructure">Battle rhythm is decision infrastructure</h2> <p>A <a href="../terms/battle-rhythm.md" class="link-internal">battle rhythm</a> turns continuous reality into discrete decision moments <a href="#ref-usarmy2014fm6" class="citation">[usarmy2014fm6]</a>.</p> <ul> <li>If it is too slow, the organization becomes reactive and late.</li> <li>If it is too fast, the organization stops understanding what it is doing.</li> </ul> <p>The command task is to set a rhythm that matches the tempo of the environment <em>and</em> preserves enough time for actual thinking.</p> <h2 id="the-three-execution-disciplines">The three execution disciplines</h2> <p>A practical way to keep operations management real (not slide theater):</p> <ul> <li><strong>Running estimates</strong> keep every staff section accountable for what changed and what it means.</li> <li><strong>Decision support</strong> keeps key decisions visible before they arrive.</li> <li><strong>Assessment</strong> ties “what changed” to objectives and the operational approach.</li> </ul> <p>If any of these three becomes ritual, the headquarters drifts.</p> <h2 id="the-ritual-failure-mode">The ritual failure mode</h2> <p>Staff processes are attractive because they produce artifacts (slides, briefs, trackers). Over time, artifacts can become substitutes for thinking. The command task is to keep artifacts connected to decisions.</p> <h2 id="exercise">Exercise</h2> <p>Pick a historical operation or crisis (military or otherwise). Write:</p> <ul> <li>three recurring decision points,</li> <li>what information each decision point needs,</li> <li>one way the process could become ritual (producing artifacts without decisions),</li> <li>one change that would keep decisions honest.</li> </ul>
