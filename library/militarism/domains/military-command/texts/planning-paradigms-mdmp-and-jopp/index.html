Planning Paradigms: MDMP and JOPP

<p>Learning goal: treat MDMP and JOPP as compatible planning pipelines, and understand what each pipeline is trying to prevent.</p> <h2 id="design-comes-first">Design comes first</h2> <p>MDMP and JOPP are planning paradigms [@jointchiefs2011jp5; @usarmy2014fm6]. They work best when fed by a clear problem frame and an operational approach from <a href="../terms/operational-design.md" class="link-internal">operational design</a>.</p> <p>If you want a comparative view of modern doctrinal design paradigms (Army ADM, MCPP, NATO), see: <a href="./operational-design-frameworks-adm-mcpp-and-nato.md" class="link-internal">Operational design frameworks: ADM, MCPP, and NATO</a>.</p> <h2 id="the-shared-structure">The shared structure</h2> <p>Both MDMP and JOPP exist because complex operations need a repeatable way to translate ambiguity into executable coordination. Their shared structure is:</p> <ul> <li>frame the problem,</li> <li>generate options,</li> <li>wargame and compare,</li> <li>decide,</li> <li>produce an order plus synchronization products.</li> </ul> <h2 id="the-difference-is-the-integration-problem">The difference is the integration problem</h2> <ul> <li><strong>MDMP</strong> is optimized for producing detailed orders and staff products inside a service’s staff structure.</li> <li><strong>JOPP</strong> is optimized for integrating multiple services and partners into a joint operational approach.</li> </ul> <p>The practical rule: keep the same underlying artifacts (problem statement, assumptions, COAs, wargame results) and translate them into the vocabulary and products the HQ needs.</p> <h2 id="paradigm-hygiene-what-makes-these-processes-fail">Paradigm hygiene (what makes these processes fail)</h2> <p>Planning paradigms fail when they become ritual. Watch for:</p> <ul> <li>steps completed without confronting uncertainty,</li> <li>wargaming treated as false precision,</li> <li>products treated as ends rather than decision support.</li> </ul> <h2 id="exercise">Exercise</h2> <p>Pick a scenario and do a minimalist pipeline:</p> <ul> <li>write a problem statement and 5 assumptions,</li> <li>sketch 2 COAs,</li> <li>wargame each in three moves,</li> <li>write the decision you would need to make under time pressure,</li> <li>list the one synchronization rule that must hold for the plan to work.</li> </ul>

References

[usarmy2014fm6] Department of the Army. (2014). Commander and Staff Organization and Operations. Field Manual 6-0 (5 May 2014; Change 1, 26 Mar 2015).
