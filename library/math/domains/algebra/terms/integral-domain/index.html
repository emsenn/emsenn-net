Integral Domain

2026-03-05
Defines Integral Domain, integral domain

An integral domain is a commutative ring with 1≠01 \neq 0 and no zero divisors: if a⋅b=0a \cdot b = 0, then a=0a = 0 or b=0b = 0.

The absence of zero divisors means cancellation works: if a⋅b=a⋅ca \cdot b = a \cdot c and a≠0a \neq 0, then b=cb = c. The integers Z\mathbb{Z} and polynomial rings over fields are integral domains. Every field is an integral domain, but not every integral domain is a field.

An integral domain can always be embedded into a field — its field of fractions — just as Z\mathbb{Z} embeds into Q\mathbb{Q}.
