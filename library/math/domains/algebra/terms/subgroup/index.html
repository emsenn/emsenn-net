Subgroup

2026-03-05

A subgroup of a group GG is a subset H⊆GH \subseteq G that is itself a group under the same operation. This holds if and only if HH is non-empty, closed under the group operation, and closed under taking inverses.

Every group has at least two subgroups: the trivial subgroup {e}\{e\} containing only the identity, and the group GG itself. A subgroup that is neither of these is called a proper subgroup.

The subgroups of a group form a lattice ordered by inclusion, where the meet of two subgroups is their intersection and the join is the subgroup they generate together.
