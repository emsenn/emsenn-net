2026-03-30 A lattice equipped with a monoidal product and its residual implication, connected by an adjunction: a · b ≤ c iff b ≤ a → c.

A residuated lattice is a lattice (L,∧,∨)(L, \wedge, \vee) equipped with a monoidal product ⋅\cdot and a residual implication →\to satisfying the residuation law:

a⋅b≤cif and only ifb≤a→ca \cdot b \leq c \quad\text{if and only if}\quad b \leq a \to c

The product ⋅\cdot is associative with an identity element ee (making (L,⋅,e)(L, \cdot, e) a monoid). The residuation law says: the product and implication are adjoint. The product combines; the implication tests what must hold for the combination to stay below a bound.

In the non-commutative case, the product has two residuals — a left residual a\ca \backslash c and a right residual c/bc / b — because the two argument positions of ⋅\cdot are distinguishable:

a⋅b≤ciffb≤a\ciffa≤c/ba \cdot b \leq c \quad\text{iff}\quad b \leq a \backslash c \quad\text{iff}\quad a \leq c / b

In the commutative case (a⋅b=b⋅aa \cdot b = b \cdot a), the two residuals coincide into a single →\to.

Galatos, Jipsen, Kowalski, & Ono (2007) proved that the Lambek calculus — the logic of how predicates combine with their arguments — is the logic of residuated lattices. The // and \\backslash of categorial grammar are residuals in the algebraic sense.

A residuated lattice is integral if the monoidal identity ee is the top element of the lattice (e=⊤e = \top). It is commutative if a⋅b=b⋅aa \cdot b = b \cdot a. Every Heyting algebra is an integral commutative residuated lattice where the product is meet and the residual is the Heyting implication.

The twist product L⊗LL \otimes L of a commutative residuated lattice inherits a residuation (Busaniche & Cignoli 2014), giving the four-valued bilattice its algebraic operations.
