Eigenvalue

2026-03-05

An eigenvalue of a linear map T:V→VT : V \to V is a scalar λ\lambda such that T(v)=λvT(\mathbf{v}) = \lambda \mathbf{v} for some nonzero vector v\mathbf{v}, called an eigenvector corresponding to λ\lambda.

Eigenvalues are the roots of the characteristic polynomial det⁡(A−λI)=0\det(A - \lambda I) = 0. A linear map on a finite-dimensional space has at most nn eigenvalues (counted with multiplicity), where nn is the dimension.

Eigenvalues reveal the “natural scales” of a linear map: the directions along which the map acts by pure stretching or compression, without rotation.
