Divisibility

2026-03-05
Defines Divisibility, divides

An integer aa divides an integer bb (written a∣ba \mid b) if there exists an integer kk such that b=akb = ak. When a∣ba \mid b, we say aa is a divisor or factor of bb, and bb is a multiple of aa.

Divisibility defines a partial order on the positive integers: it is reflexive (a∣aa \mid a), antisymmetric (if a∣ba \mid b and b∣ab \mid a then a=ba = b for positive integers), and transitive (if a∣ba \mid b and b∣cb \mid c then a∣ca \mid c). Under this order, the positive integers form a lattice where meet is the greatest common divisor and join is the least common multiple.
