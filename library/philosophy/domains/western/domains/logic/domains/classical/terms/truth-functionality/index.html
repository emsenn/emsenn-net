Truth-Functionality

2026-03-05 The property of a connective whose compound truth value is determined entirely by the truth values of its components — the semantic backbone of classical propositional logic.
Defines Truth-functionality, truth-functional, truth-functional connective
Requires bivalence

A connective is truth-functional when the truth value of the compound it forms is determined entirely by the truth values of its components. Classical propositional logic is truth-functional throughout: knowing the truth values of A and B suffices to determine the truth values of A ∧ B, A ∨ B, A → B, and ¬A. This property is what makes truth tables possible and is the semantic backbone of classical propositional logic.

Truth-functionality depends on bivalence. If propositions can take more than two values — or if their truth value depends on context, time, possible worlds, or epistemic state — then the simple input-output picture breaks down. Modal operators (necessarily, possibly) are the paradigm of non-truth-functional connectives: knowing that A is true does not tell you whether “necessarily A” is true, because that depends on what happens at other possible worlds.

In intuitionistic logic, the connectives are not truth-functional in the classical sense. The Heyting implication A → B is not determined by the truth values of A and B alone; it encodes the existence of a constructive method for transforming proofs of A into proofs of B. The BHK interpretation replaces truth-value inputs with proof-construction inputs, and the “output” is not a truth value but a proof obligation. This is one of the deepest divergences between classical and intuitionistic logic: classical logic computes with truth values, intuitionistic logic computes with proofs.

Related terms

  • Bivalence — the semantic prerequisite for classical truth-functionality
  • Connective — the formal objects that are or are not truth-functional
  • Heyting Implication — the intuitionistic replacement that is not truth-functional
  • Modal Logic — the paradigmatic extension of classical logic with non-truth-functional operators
