E-Commerce

2026-03-29 Selling products or services directly through the web — transactions where the buyer selects, pays for, and often receives the product online.
<p>E-commerce is the sale of products or services through the web. The buyer browses a website or app, selects items, pays electronically, and receives the product — either shipped physically or delivered digitally. As of 2024, e-commerce accounts for 16% of total U.S. retail sales <a href="#ref-census2024" class="citation">[census2024]</a> and a higher share in most European and East Asian markets.</p> <p>In e-commerce, the web is the storefront. Revenue comes from selling goods at a markup over cost. The website displays the product, processes the payment, and coordinates fulfillment.</p> <p>The unit economics of e-commerce center on three numbers: <strong>average order value</strong> (AOV), <strong>cost of goods sold</strong> (COGS), and <strong>customer acquisition cost</strong> (CAC). The business pays per click or per impression and earns back the spend through resulting purchases. Paid acquisition is immediate but unprofitable without strong <a href="./conversion-rate.md" class="link-internal">conversion rates</a> and repeat purchase rates, because CAC scales linearly with volume.</p> <p><strong><a href="./organic-traffic.md" class="link-internal">Organic search</a>.</strong> E-commerce sites rank for product-related queries (&ldquo;best running shoes for flat feet,&rdquo; &ldquo;ceramic plant pots&rdquo;) through product page optimization, category page SEO, and <a href="./content-marketing.md" class="link-internal">content marketing</a> (buying guides, how-to articles that link to products). Organic acquisition is free per click but slow to build and dependent on Google.</p> <p><strong>Marketplaces.</strong> Selling through Amazon, Etsy, eBay, or Walmart Marketplace gives access to the marketplace&rsquo;s existing traffic. The tradeoff: the marketplace takes 8-45% of the sale price (depending on category and platform) and controls the customer relationship. The seller gets volume but not ownership of the customer data or the ability to build <a href="../../../terms/brand.md" class="link-internal">brand</a> recognition.</p> <p><strong>Email and retention.</strong> Once a customer has purchased, the <a href="./email-list.md" class="link-internal">email list</a> becomes the primary channel for repeat sales. Post-purchase email sequences (order confirmation → product care tips → related product recommendations → seasonal promotions) drive 20-40% of revenue for mature e-commerce businesses. The email list converts the one-time buyer into a repeat customer, which is where e-commerce margin lives: repeat customers cost nothing to acquire.</p> <p>The platforms that run e-commerce storefronts (Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Squarespace) handle product display, cart management, payment processing, and order fulfillment integration. Shopify alone powered $236 billion in gross merchandise value in 2023 across millions of stores. The platform choice determines the technical ceiling and the fee structure, but the business questions — what to sell, to whom, at what price, through which acquisition channels — are independent of the platform.</p>
