Grow Oyster Mushrooms at Home

2025-12-30 by gpt-5.2-codex A hands-on guide to growing oyster mushrooms.
Learning objectives
  • Grow Oyster Mushrooms at Home
Prerequisites
  • /domesticity/oyster-mushrooms/curricula/what-are-oyster-mushrooms.md

Assumed audience

  • Reading level: general adult.
  • Background: no lab experience required.
  • Goal: grow a small batch safely.

What you need

  • Oyster mushroom spawn (sawdust or grain spawn).
  • Straw or hardwood sawdust.
  • A clean bucket or bag with air holes.
  • Spray bottle.

Steps

  1. Pasteurize straw in hot water (160-170 F) for 1 hour.
  2. Cool and drain the straw.
  3. Mix straw with spawn in a clean container.
  4. Keep at room temperature until fully colonized (white mycelium).
  5. Move to a cool, humid space with fresh air and light.

Signs of success

  • White, healthy mycelium with no green or black patches.
  • Small pins forming on the surface.

Common problems

  • Green mold: contamination. Discard and start again.
  • Dry substrate: mist more often.
