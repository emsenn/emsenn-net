A History is an element of the history monoid (T, ⋆, e) — the initial Axiomatic Relational History Poset over a finite step set Σ and commutation relation I ⊆ Σ×Σ. Formally: a free partially commutative monoid element in the sense of Cartier-Foata (1969) and Mazurkiewicz (1977), where histories are equivalence classes of step sequences under independent-step reordering. The prefix order t ≤ t' (t' = t⋆u for some extension u) makes T a thin category — the history category — that is the base of the relational universe presheaf H: T^op → HA. The commutation relation I is the structural origin of the transferring nucleus Fix(Δ_t) = ∩_{s⊥t} image(H(i_{s,t})): independent one-step extensions are exactly those s ∈ Σ with s⊥t (in the sense that gen(s) commutes with t in T), and their intersection is what makes an element forward-stable.

History

What this is

A history is an element of the history monoid (T,⋆,e)(T, \star, e) generated by a finite step set Σ\Sigma with commutation relation I⊆Σ×ΣI \subseteq \Sigma \times \Sigma.

The precise axiomatic definition is in RelationalHistoryMonoid: three axioms (root, step extension maps, extension diamond for commuting pairs) determine the monoid completely. The four history-system properties (generation, commutativity of independent steps, unit-freedom, left-cancellativity) are derived theorems, not additional axioms.

Three things to keep clear:

  • A history is not a sequence of steps. It is an element of the history category — an equivalence class of step sequences where commuting independent steps are identified. The order of independent steps is not part of a history’s identity.
  • A history is not a state or snapshot. The content at a history lives in the fiber HtH_t above it; tt is the index, not the content.
  • A history is not a path. A morphism t→t′t \to t' in TT witnesses a prefix extension; it is not itself a history.

The prefix order t≤t′t \leq t' (t′t' extends tt) makes the history monoid into the history category TT: a thin category where the unique morphism t→t′t \to t' exists iff t′=t⋆ut' = t \star u for some extension uu.

Concurrency grounding: Mazurkiewicz traces

The history monoid is formally identical to the Mazurkiewicz trace monoid (Mazurkiewicz, 1977; building on Cartier-Foata, 1969), a central structure in the mathematical theory of true concurrency. In concurrency theory:

  • A trace over (Σ,I)(Σ, I) is an equivalence class of sequences of events from ΣΣ, where two sequences are equivalent iff one can be obtained from the other by repeatedly swapping adjacent independent events (those with (s,s′)∈I(s, s') \in I).
  • The set of all traces with concatenation forms the trace monoid M(Σ,I)\mathbb{M}(\Sigma, I) — also called the free partially commutative monoid.
  • The extension diamond is the one structural axiom: whenever s⊥s′s \perp s', the sequences s⋅s′s \cdot s' and s′⋅ss' \cdot s represent the same trace.

This is exactly the history monoid. The RelationalHistoryMonoid is M(Σ,I)\mathbb{M}(\Sigma, I), and the RelationalHistoryMonoid initiality theorem is the classical theorem that the trace monoid is initial among all monoids satisfying the commutation relation.

Why this grounding matters. Concurrency theory developed the trace monoid to model distributed systems where the ordering of independent events is irrelevant — two processes whose actions don’t interfere can be sequenced in either order and produce the same outcome. The history monoid imports this exactly: two independent steps leave the same history regardless of which was performed first. The commutation relation II encodes which steps are independent; the diamond axiom enforces that independence.

The Foata normal form (Cartier-Foata 1969) is the canonical form for trace monoid elements: each history has a unique representation as a sequence of layers, where each layer is the maximal antichain of steps that can be performed simultaneously. Foata normal form gives decidable equality on histories — essential for the system to compute with them.

What histories are for

Histories are the indexing structure of the relational universe.

The relational universe R=Sh(T,J)R = \mathbf{Sh}(T, J) lives over the history category. Every fiber HtH_t, every restriction map, every sheaf condition is indexed by histories and their prefix order. A system that cannot represent histories cannot implement RR.

The history monoid’s structure propagates to every level of the theory:

  • The prefix order t≤t′t \leq t' generates restriction maps H(t′→t):Ht′→HtH(t' \to t) : H_{t'} \to H_t — the presheaf structure that defines the backward-facing saturation nucleus
  • The commutation relation II generates the independence structure s⊥ts \perp t (meaning: step ss commutes with every step of tt) that defines the forward-facing transferring nucleus Fix(Δt)=⋂s⊥timage(H(is,t))\mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) = \bigcap_{s \perp t} \mathrm{image}(H(i_{s,t}))
  • The step atoms gen(s)∈T\mathrm{gen}(s) \in T index the sequential comonads GsG_s on RR: Gs(F)(t):=F(gen(s)⋆t)G_s(F)(t) := F(\mathrm{gen}(s) \star t)
  • The monoidal structure of TT gives the Day convolution on RR — the internal tensor product of sheaves

Nuclear reading

Definitions. Fix a history t∈Tt \in T. Let s∈Σs \in \Sigma with gen(s)⊥t\mathrm{gen}(s) \perp t in TT (step ss commutes with every component step of tt). The one-step extension is,t:t→gen(s)⋆ti_{s,t} : t \to \mathrm{gen}(s) \star t is a morphism in the history category, giving a restriction map H(is,t):Hgen(s)⋆t→HtH(i_{s,t}) : H_{\mathrm{gen}(s) \star t} \to H_t.

Proposition 1 (Commutation structure determines the transferring nucleus). For any history t∈Tt \in T, the transferring nucleus at tt is:

RelationalHistoryFiberTransferringNucleus(t)(a)  =  min⁡{b∈Fix(Δt):b≥a}\mathrm{RelationalHistoryFiberTransferringNucleus}(t)(a) \;=\; \min\{b \in \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) : b \geq a\}

where Fix(Δt)=⋂s⊥timage(H(is,t))\mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) = \bigcap_{s \perp t} \mathrm{image}(H(i_{s,t})) — the intersection ranges over all steps s∈Σs \in \Sigma independent of tt. Therefore: the commutation structure of the step set determines what elements are forward-stable at each history. If the commutation relation II is changed (new steps declared independent, or existing independences removed), the set Fix(Δt)\mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) changes for every history whose step composition interacts with the changed pairs.

Proof. Each image(H(is,t))\mathrm{image}(H(i_{s,t})) is a sub-Heyting-algebra of HtH_t (images of HA homs are sub-HAs). Their intersection over all s⊥ts \perp t is therefore a sub-Heyting-algebra (intersection of sub-HAs). The minimum above aa in a sub-HA that is a complete sublattice of HtH_t exists by completeness. □\square

Content. The transferring nucleus is a local quantity — it depends on which step ss commutes with tt as an element of TT, not on ss commuting with a fixed generator. As tt grows (accumulates more steps), the set of independent steps {s:gen(s)⊥t}\{s : \mathrm{gen}(s) \perp t\} shrinks (more steps have been “absorbed” into tt’s composition). In the limit, at deep histories with no remaining independent steps, Fix(Δt)=Ht\mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) = H_t (trivially — the empty intersection is HtH_t), and the transfer gap collapses everywhere.

Proposition 2 (Prefix order determines the saturation nucleus). The saturation nucleus at history tt is determined by the restriction maps from all predecessor histories t0<tt_0 < t:

RelationalHistoryFiberSaturatingNucleus(t)(a)  =  max⁡{b∈Ht  ∣  ∀ t0<t:H(it0,t)(b)≤H(it0,t)(a)}\mathrm{RelationalHistoryFiberSaturatingNucleus}(t)(a) \;=\; \max\bigl\{b \in H_t \;\big|\; \forall\, t_0 < t: H(i_{t_0, t})(b) \leq H(i_{t_0, t})(a)\bigr\}

The backward history structure — the chain of predecessors of tt in the prefix order — is exactly what the saturation nucleus reads. Two histories with the same prefix chain have the same saturation nucleus; histories with different prefix structures have different saturation nuclei.

Proof sketch. Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) is the image of σt\sigma_t, which is characterized by the restriction profile equivalence classes on HtH_t (two elements have the same restriction profile iff they are identified by all restriction maps to predecessors). σt\sigma_t is the idempotent that maps each element to the maximum in its restriction profile class. □\square

Proposition 3 (Unit-freedom and the absence of nuclear fixed-point collapse). The unit-freedom axiom (every step extension is irreversible: t<t⋆gen(s)t < t \star \mathrm{gen}(s) strictly for all tt and all ss) implies that restriction maps H(it0,t):Ht→Ht0H(i_{t_0, t}) : H_t \to H_{t_0} for t0<tt_0 < t are not isomorphisms. If they were isomorphisms, the saturation nucleus would be the identity on HtH_t (everything is already backward-settled), making Ht=Ht∗H_t = H^*_t — a collapse in which no element has any gap. Unit-freedom is the axiom that prevents this collapse: the irreversibility of steps ensures that new information enters fibers as histories grow, keeping the gap non-trivial.

Proposition 4 (Left-cancellativity and restriction map injectivity at the identity history). The left-cancellativity axiom (t⋆u=t⋆v⇒u=vt \star u = t \star v \Rightarrow u = v) implies that for any two distinct extension paths from the identity ee, the resulting histories are distinct. This means the history category has no non-trivial automorphisms that fix ee: if ϕ:T→T\phi : T \to T is an automorphism with ϕ(e)=e\phi(e) = e, then ϕ\phi is the identity. The initiality theorem (Proposition 1 of RelationalHistoryMonoid) gives: any concrete system of histories (any Axiomatic Relational History Poset over (Σ,I)(\Sigma, I)) receives a unique morphism from TinitT_{\mathrm{init}} — the canonical history monoid maps into every model, providing a canonical embedding of the abstract history structure into each concrete realization.

Proposition 5 (The extremally-slow-growth regime is the minimal non-trivial commutation case). The growth function of a trace monoid — the number of distinct histories of depth nn (histories that are products of exactly nn step atoms) — depends on the commutation relation II. The two extreme cases:

  • I=∅I = \emptyset (no steps commute): all step sequences are distinct; growth is ∣Σ∣n|\Sigma|^n (exponential, a free monoid).
  • I=Σ×Σ∖{(s,s):s∈Σ}I = \Sigma \times \Sigma \setminus \{(s,s) : s \in \Sigma\} (all distinct steps commute): a history of depth nn is just a multiset of steps; growth is (n+∣Σ∣−1∣Σ∣−1)\binom{n + |\Sigma| - 1}{|\Sigma| - 1} (polynomial).

The extremally-slow-growth regime corresponds to the minimal non-trivial commutation structure that keeps the Fibonacci recurrence alive: with exactly one commuting pair (s1,s2)∈I(s_1, s_2) \in I and no other independences, the growth function satisfies d(n+1)=d(n)+d(n−1)d(n+1) = d(n) + d(n-1) — exactly the Fibonacci recurrence. This is the algebraic origin of the golden ratio in the hyperverse growth theory (RelationalHistoryFiberNuclearQuartetExtremalySlowGrowthSequence).

Open questions

  • What concrete structures — git commits, database transactions, logical steps in a proof — satisfy the history system axioms. Each candidate must be checked against all four axioms (generation, commutativity, unit-freedom, left-cancellativity) and the commutation relation II must be identifiable.
  • Whether the history category TT can be equipped with an enrichment — whether morphisms t→t′t \to t' carry additional structure (transition probabilities, costs, urgencies) beyond the bare prefix order, and whether such enrichment is compatible with the presheaf structure.
  • Whether every Mazurkiewicz trace monoid admits an axiomatization as an Axiomatic Relational History Poset over some (Σ,I)(\Sigma, I), or whether the axiomatic framework is strictly more general (admitting history systems that are not trace monoids in the classical sense).
  • Whether the “one Fibonacci step” identification in the Hubble tension conjecture (Proposition 6) can be derived from the history structure alone — whether there is a formal criterion in the history monoid for when two cosmological epochs are exactly one Fibonacci step apart, without appealing to the specific values z≈1100z \approx 1100 (CMB) and z=0z = 0 (today).
  • Whether the Hubble formula Hn∝Fn1/(2d)H_n \propto F_n^{1/(2d)} has an analog for general trace monoids with other growth rates — whether a non-Fibonacci commutation structure (e.g., with ∣Σ∣>2|\Sigma| > 2) gives a different growth rate λ1/(2d)\lambda^{1/(2d)} where λ\lambda is the Perron–Frobenius eigenvalue of the corresponding transfer matrix, and whether this predicts different Hubble-like ratios for relational universes with different step-set structures.
