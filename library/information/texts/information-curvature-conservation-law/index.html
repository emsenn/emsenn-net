Information-Curvature Conservation Law

2025-10-11 by emsenn
<h2 id="abstract">Abstract</h2> <p>This paper shows that the Fisher-information geometry underlying divergence minimization across coupled systems obeys a curvature conservation law. For any collection of informational manifolds related by mutual-information-preserving maps, the sum of their Ricci curvatures equals the Laplacian of the total stability functional. This extends the asymmetric stability framework of <em>Information-Theoretic Stability as Reward Function</em> to the general multi-system case, establishing a geometric constraint linking local adaptation dynamics to global invariants.</p> <h2 id="1-introduction">1. Introduction</h2> <p>Information geometry relates the curvature of probability manifolds to statistical inference (Amari 2016). Systems that minimize divergence between successive distributions trace geodesics under the Fisher metric. Conclusion</h2> <p>Under general information-geometric conditions, the total Ricci curvature of coupled Fisher manifolds equals the Laplacian of the joint stability functional. This curvature conservation principle provides a minimal geometric constraint governing divergence-minimizing dynamics across heterogeneous systems, linking local learning behavior to a global invariant of informational geometry.</p> <h2 id="references">References</h2> <ul> <li>Amari, S.
