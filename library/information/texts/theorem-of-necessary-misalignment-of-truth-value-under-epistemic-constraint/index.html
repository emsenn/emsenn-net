This equality case is excluded by the strict mismatch assumption.</p> </li> <li> <p><strong>Scope of “necessary.”</strong> Necessity is with respect to the assumptions: finite rate, mismatch, convexity, and selection that increases reward efficiently.</p> </li> <li> <p><strong>Why decoder-level information.</strong> Semantic performance is realized through the decoded variable <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>s</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>Y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S=s(Y)</annotation></semantics></math></span>; rate–distortion bounds directly relate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>T</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D_T</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>I</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>T</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>S</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">I(T;S)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and DPI then connects <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>I</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>T</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>S</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">I(T;S)</annotation></semantics></math></span> to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>I</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>T</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>Y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">I(T;Y)</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p> </li> </ol> <h1 id="discussion">Discussion</h1> <p>The theorem identifies misalignment as a structural consequence of limited epistemic capacity. Whenever optimization intensifies for a mismatched proxy under fixed rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the system traverses the achievable frontier, sacrificing semantic information about <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>T</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T</annotation></semantics></math></span> to improve computable reward.
