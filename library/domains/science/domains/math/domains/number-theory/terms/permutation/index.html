Permutation

2026-03-05
Defines Permutation, permutation

A permutation of a set is an arrangement of its elements in a definite order. The number of permutations of nn distinct objects is n!n! (n factorial).

Permutations can also be viewed as bijective functions from a set to itself. Under composition, the permutations of a set form a group — the symmetric group SnS_n. This is one of the most important examples of a non-abelian group (for n≥3n \geq 3).

A kk-permutation of nn objects is an ordered selection of kk objects from nn, numbering n!(n−k)!\frac{n!}{(n-k)!}.
