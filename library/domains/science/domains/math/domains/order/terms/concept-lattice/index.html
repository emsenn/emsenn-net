2026-03-30 The complete lattice of formal concepts arising from a formal context — the central structure of formal concept analysis.

A concept lattice is the complete lattice of formal concepts arising from a formal context.

A formal context (Ganter & Wille 1999) is a triple (G,M,I)(G, M, I) where GG is a set of objects, MM is a set of attributes, and I⊆G×MI \subseteq G \times M is an incidence relation (I(g,m)I(g, m) holds when object gg has attribute mm). Derivation operators define a Galois connection:

A′={m∈M:∀g∈A, I(g,m)}B′={g∈G:∀m∈B, I(g,m)}A' = \{m \in M : \forall g \in A,\ I(g, m)\} \qquad B' = \{g \in G : \forall m \in B,\ I(g, m)\}

A′A' gives the attributes shared by all objects in AA. B′B' gives the objects sharing all attributes in BB. A formal concept is a pair (A,B)(A, B) with A′=BA' = B and B′=AB' = A. The set AA is the extent (the objects falling under the concept) and BB is the intent (the attributes defining the concept).

The set of all formal concepts, ordered by extent inclusion, forms a complete lattice — the concept lattice B(G,M,I)\mathfrak{B}(G, M, I).

Belohlavek (2004) generalized: when I:G×M→LI: G \times M \to L for a complete residuated lattice LL, the Galois connection uses LL’s residual implication and the concept lattice inherits LL’s residuated structure.

Wurm (2012) proved that concept lattices arising from distributional data over formal languages are residuated lattices giving complete semantics for the Lambek calculus.

Pratt (1999) proved a formal context is a Chu space over {0,1}\{0,1\}. Many-valued formal contexts are Chu spaces over the value set.

A collection of key-value records sharing attribute names is a formal context: the records are objects, the attribute names are the attributes, and the values give the incidence. The concept lattice of this context organizes the records by shared structure.
