2026-03-30 A lattice where meet distributes over join: a ∧ (b ∨ c) = (a ∧ b) ∨ (a ∧ c). The setting for Priestley duality and bilattice theory.

A distributive lattice is a lattice where meet distributes over join:

a∧(b∨c)=(a∧b)∨(a∧c)a \wedge (b \vee c) = (a \wedge b) \vee (a \wedge c)

Equivalently, join distributes over meet (either distributive law implies the other).

Distributive lattices are the setting for Priestley duality: every bounded distributive lattice is dually equivalent to a Priestley space (a compact ordered topological space). This gives every distributive lattice a geometric dual.

Every Boolean algebra is distributive. Every Heyting algebra is distributive. The converse is false in both cases.

The twist product L⊗LL \otimes L of a bounded distributive lattice LL produces a distributive bilattice (Fitting 1991). The four-valued bilattice 2⊗2\mathbf{2} \otimes \mathbf{2} is the free bounded distributive bilattice on one generator (Fitting 2002).
