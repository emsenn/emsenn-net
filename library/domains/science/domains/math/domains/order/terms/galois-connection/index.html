2026-03-30 A pair of order-reversing maps between posets satisfying f(a) ≥ b iff a ≥ g(b). The order-theoretic form of an adjunction.

A Galois connection between two partially ordered sets (A,≤)(A, \leq) and (B,≤)(B, \leq) is a pair of maps f:A→Bf: A \to B and g:B→Ag: B \to A satisfying:

f(a)≥bif and only ifa≥g(b)f(a) \geq b \quad\text{if and only if}\quad a \geq g(b)

(antitone form) or equivalently a pair of monotone maps between opposite orders. This is the order-theoretic form of an adjunction.

The central example is formal concept analysis (Ganter & Wille 1999): given a formal context (G,M,I)(G, M, I), the derivation operators A↦A′A \mapsto A' and B↦B′B \mapsto B' form a Galois connection between subsets of objects and subsets of attributes. The closed elements of this connection are the formal concepts; they form a concept lattice.

In a residuated lattice, for each fixed element aa, the maps x↦a⋅xx \mapsto a \cdot x and y↦a→yy \mapsto a \to y form a (monotone) Galois connection. This is the residuation.

On the four-valued bilattice, Jakl, Jung, & Rivieccio (2021) defined Galois biconnections: bilattice analogues of Galois connections compatible with both the truth and knowledge orderings.
