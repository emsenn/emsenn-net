2026-03-30 A lattice where meets and joins exist for all subsets, not just finite ones. The setting for the Knaster-Tarski fixpoint theorem.

A complete lattice is a lattice where every subset has a meet (greatest lower bound) and a join (least upper bound) — not just pairs, but arbitrary subsets including infinite ones.

Every complete lattice is bounded: the join of the empty set is the bottom element ⊥\bot, and the meet of the empty set is the top element ⊤\top.

The Knaster-Tarski theorem: every monotone function on a complete lattice has a least fixpoint and a greatest fixpoint. This is the foundation for closure operations in logic programming and bilattice semantics (Fitting 1991).

The power set of any set, ordered by inclusion, is a complete lattice. The set of all epistemic states {⊥,t,f,⊤}n\{\bot, t, f, \top\}^n ordered pointwise by the knowledge ordering is a complete lattice of height 2n2n.
