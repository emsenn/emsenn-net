2026-03-30 The group Z₂ × Z₂ with four elements: identity, two independent involutions, and their composition. The symmetry group of the four-valued bilattice.

The Klein four-group Z2×Z2\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2 is the group with four elements: the identity ee, two independent involutions aa and bb, and their composition ab=baab = ba. Every non-identity element has order 2 (a2=b2=(ab)2=ea^2 = b^2 = (ab)^2 = e). It is the smallest non-cyclic group.

On the four-valued bilattice {⊥,t,f,⊤}\{\bot, t, f, \top\}, the Klein four-group is generated by negation ∼\mathord{\sim} (swaps t↔ft \leftrightarrow f) and conflation −- (swaps ⊥↔⊤\bot \leftrightarrow \top). The four group elements are: identity, negation, conflation, and their composition (which swaps both pairs simultaneously). This is the symmetry group of the bilattice viewed as a square.

Neither the extending operation ⊙\odot nor the restricting operation ⇒\Rightarrow of the Busaniche-Cignoli residuation commutes with negation or conflation. The residuation is asymmetric with respect to the full symmetry group.
