2026-03-30 The property that doing something twice is the same as doing it once: a · a = a. Meet and join are idempotent; the product on a residuated lattice may or may not be.

A binary operation ∗\ast is idempotent if applying it to an element with itself changes nothing:

a∗a=aa \ast a = a

Meet and join on a lattice are always idempotent: a∧a=aa \wedge a = a and a∨a=aa \vee a = a. Taking the intersection of a set with itself gives the same set. Taking the union of a set with itself gives the same set.

In a residuated lattice, the product ⋅\cdot may or may not be idempotent. When a⋅a≤aa \cdot a \leq a for all aa, the lattice satisfies contraction — the substructural logic structural rule that says using a hypothesis twice is the same as using it once. Linear logic drops this: using a resource twice requires two copies.

On the four-valued bilattice, tt, ff, and ⊤\top are idempotent under ⊙\odot (extending by the same state twice gives the same state). ⊥\bot is not: ⊥⊙⊥=f\bot \odot \bot = f. The no-information state is the only one that changes under self-extension.
