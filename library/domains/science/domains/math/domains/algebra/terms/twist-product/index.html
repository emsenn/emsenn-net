2026-03-30 A construction that builds a De Morgan algebra (or bilattice) from a bounded distributive lattice by pairing it with itself: L ⊗ L.

The twist product of a bounded distributive lattice LL is the structure L⊗LL \otimes L built on the set L×LL \times L with operations defined by mixing the two components.

Kalman (1958) introduced the construction for De Morgan algebras. Fitting (1991) extended it to bilattices. The key example: the twist product 2⊗2\mathbf{2} \otimes \mathbf{2} of the two-element Boolean algebra with itself is Belnap’s four-valued bilattice {⊥,t,f,⊤}\{\bot, t, f, \top\}.

In L⊗LL \otimes L, each element is a pair (a,b)(a, b) where aa is the positive component and bb is the negative component. The knowledge ordering is componentwise: (a,b)≤k(c,d)(a, b) \leq_k (c, d) iff a≤ca \leq c and b≤db \leq d. The truth ordering (the twist-product order) reverses the second component: (a,b)≤t(c,d)(a, b) \leq_t (c, d) iff a≤ca \leq c and d≤bd \leq b.

When LL is an integral commutative residuated lattice, Busaniche & Cignoli (2014) proved L⊗LL \otimes L inherits a residuation:

(x,y)⊙(z,v)=(x⋅z, (x→v)∧(z→y))(x, y) \odot (z, v) = (x \cdot z,\ (x \to v) \wedge (z \to y))

(x,y)⇒(z,v)=((x→z)∧(v→y), x⋅v)(x, y) \Rightarrow (z, v) = ((x \to z) \wedge (v \to y),\ x \cdot v)

with the adjunction a⊙p≤tqa \odot p \leq_t q iff p≤t(a⇒q)p \leq_t (a \Rightarrow q) holding with respect to the twist-product order. Chajda & Langer (2021) gave explicit computation-ready formulas and established a second adjoint pair (the Nelson implication).

Jakl (2025) proved the twist construction is the Chu construction Chu(H,0)\mathrm{Chu}(H, 0) over a Heyting algebra, connecting bilattices to linear logic: tensor in linear logic is fusion (⊙\odot) in the twist product.

Fitting (2002) proved 2⊗2\mathbf{2} \otimes \mathbf{2} is the free bounded distributive bilattice on one generator — the canonical bilattice from which all others are quotients.
