2026-03-30 A bounded distributive lattice with an involution (negation) satisfying De Morgan's laws: ~(a ∧ b) = ~a ∨ ~b.

A De Morgan algebra is a bounded distributive lattice (L,∧,∨,0,1)(L, \wedge, \vee, 0, 1) equipped with an involution ∼\mathord{\sim} satisfying De Morgan’s laws:

∼(a∧b)=∼a∨∼b∼(a∨b)=∼a∧∼b\mathord{\sim}(a \wedge b) = \mathord{\sim} a \vee \mathord{\sim} b \qquad \mathord{\sim}(a \vee b) = \mathord{\sim} a \wedge \mathord{\sim} b

and ∼∼a=a\mathord{\sim}\mathord{\sim} a = a (involution).

Kalman (1958) proved every bounded distributive lattice LL generates a De Morgan algebra via the twist product L⊗LL \otimes L. The negation on the twist product swaps components: ∼(a,b)=(b,a)\mathord{\sim}(a, b) = (b, a).

Every Boolean algebra is a De Morgan algebra (with complement as negation). Every bilattice carries a De Morgan algebra structure on its truth lattice.
