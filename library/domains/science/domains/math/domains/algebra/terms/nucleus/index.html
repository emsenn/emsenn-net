Nucleus

2026-04-05 A closure operator on a Heyting algebra that preserves finite meets — the algebraic mechanism by which a sub-algebra of fixed points is canonically determined.

A nucleus on a Heyting algebra AA is an operator j:A→Aj : A \to A satisfying three conditions:

  1. Extensive: a≤j(a)a \leq j(a) for all a∈Aa \in A.
  2. Idempotent: j(j(a))=j(a)j(j(a)) = j(a) for all a∈Aa \in A.
  3. Meet-preserving: j(a∧b)=j(a)∧j(b)j(a \wedge b) = j(a) \wedge j(b) for all a,b∈Aa, b \in A.

The first two conditions make jj a closure operator. The third is the condition that distinguishes a nucleus from a general closure operator: it forces the fixed-point set Fix(j)={a∈A:j(a)=a}\mathrm{Fix}(j) = \{a \in A : j(a) = a\} to inherit the full Heyting algebra structure from AA, not merely the lattice structure.

¶The fixed-point sub-algebra

The set Fix(j)\mathrm{Fix}(j) is a Heyting sub-algebra of AA. The inclusion ι:Fix(j)↪A\iota : \mathrm{Fix}(j) \hookrightarrow A has a left adjoint given by jj itself:

j:A  ⇌  Fix(j):ι j : A \;\rightleftharpoons\; \mathrm{Fix}(j) : \iota

This adjoint pair makes Fix(j)\mathrm{Fix}(j) a reflective sub-algebra. The unit of the adjunction is the inequality a≤j(a)a \leq j(a) (extensivity). The counit is the identity, since jj restricted to Fix(j)\mathrm{Fix}(j) is the identity.

The KZ-lax idempotence condition — j∘j=jj \circ j = j, but a<j(a)a < j(a) strictly for a∉Fix(j)a \notin \mathrm{Fix}(j) — means this is a genuine reflection: elements not already fixed are strictly moved upward. The fixed-point set is reached by collapsing, not by reorganizing.

¶Origin in locale theory

The concept originates in locale theory, where a nucleus on the frame of opens O(X)\mathcal{O}(X) determines a sublocale. Each nucleus jj on O(X)\mathcal{O}(X) identifies a quotient locale whose frame of opens is Fix(j)\mathrm{Fix}(j). The correspondence between nuclei and sublocales is bijective: sublocales of a locale are in exact correspondence with nuclei on its frame.

In the Relational Universe construction, two nuclei — the saturation nucleus σt\sigma_t and the transfer nucleus Δt\Delta_t — act on each fiber Heyting algebra HtH_t. Their commutativity produces the nuclear quartet at every fiber.
