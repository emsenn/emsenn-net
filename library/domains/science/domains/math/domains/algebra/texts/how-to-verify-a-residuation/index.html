How to Verify a Residuation

2026-03-31 A how-to guide: given two operations on a finite set, check whether they form a residuated pair by testing the adjunction law on every triple.

You have two operations, ⊙\odot and ⇒\Rightarrow, on a finite ordered set. You want to know: do they form a residuated pair? That is, does the adjunction law hold?

a⊙p≤qif and only ifp≤(a⇒q)a \odot p \leq q \quad\text{if and only if}\quad p \leq (a \Rightarrow q)

Here is how to check.

¶Step 1: Write out the operation tables

List every element of your set. Compute a⊙ba \odot b and a⇒ba \Rightarrow b for every pair. Arrange the results in two tables.

For the four-valued bilattice {⊥,t,f,⊤}\{\bot, t, f, \top\} with the Busaniche-Cignoli operations, the tables are:

⊙  |  ⊥   t   f   ⊤        ⇒  |  ⊥   t   f   ⊤
---|----------------        ---|-------------
---
⊥  |  f   ⊥   f   ⊥        ⊥  |  t   t   ⊥   ⊥
t  |  ⊥   t   f   t        t  |  ⊥   t   f   f
f  |  f   f   f   f        f  |  t   t   t   t
⊤  |  ⊥   t   f   ⊤        ⊤  |  ⊥   t   f   ⊤

¶Step 2: Write out the ordering

You need to know which elements are ≤\leq which. For a residuation, you must use the correct ordering — the one the adjunction is claimed to hold on.

For the bilattice, the residuation holds on the truth ordering ≤t\leq_t (the twist-product order), not the knowledge ordering:

  • f≤t⊥≤ttf \leq_t \bot \leq_t t
  • f≤t⊤≤ttf \leq_t \top \leq_t t
  • ⊥\bot and ⊤\top are incomparable under ≤t\leq_t

Write out all pairs (x,y)(x, y) where x≤tyx \leq_t y:

(f,f)(f, f), (f,⊥)(f, \bot), (f,⊤)(f, \top), (f,t)(f, t), (⊥,⊥)(\bot, \bot), (⊥,t)(\bot, t), (⊤,⊤)(\top, \top), (⊤,t)(\top, t), (t,t)(t, t).

¶Step 3: Check every triple

For every triple (a,p,q)(a, p, q) of elements, compute both sides of the biconditional:

  • Left side: Is a⊙p≤tqa \odot p \leq_t q? Look up a⊙pa \odot p in the ⊙\odot table, then check whether the result is ≤tq\leq_t q.
  • Right side: Is p≤t(a⇒q)p \leq_t (a \Rightarrow q)? Look up a⇒qa \Rightarrow q in the ⇒\Rightarrow table, then check whether p≤tp \leq_t that result.

If the left side and right side agree (both true or both false) for every triple, the residuation holds.

¶Example: checking one triple

Take a=ta = t, p=⊥p = \bot, q=tq = t.

Left side: t⊙⊥=⊥t \odot \bot = \bot (from the table). Is ⊥≤tt\bot \leq_t t? Yes. Left side: TRUE.

Right side: t⇒t=tt \Rightarrow t = t (from the table). Is ⊥≤tt\bot \leq_t t? Yes. Right side: TRUE.

Both true. This triple checks out.

¶Example: a triple where both sides are false

Take a=ta = t, p=tp = t, q=⊥q = \bot.

Left side: t⊙t=tt \odot t = t. Is t≤t⊥t \leq_t \bot? No (tt is above ⊥\bot). Left side: FALSE.

Right side: t⇒⊥=⊥t \Rightarrow \bot = \bot. Is t≤t⊥t \leq_t \bot? No. Right side: FALSE.

Both false. Still checks out — the biconditional holds when both sides agree, regardless of which value they agree on.

¶Step 4: Count violations

On a set with nn elements, there are n3n^3 triples to check. For the four-valued bilattice, that is 43=644^3 = 64 triples.

If the violation count is 0, the residuation holds. If there are violations, the operations are not a residuated pair (at least not on this ordering — check whether a different ordering works).

The computation script at operations/science/research/2026-03-30-predication-bilattices/dynamics.py automates this for the four-valued bilattice: it checks all 64 triples on the full set and all 27 on the post-closure set {t,f,⊤}\{t, f, \top\}. Both come back with 0 violations.

¶Common mistakes

  • Wrong ordering. The Busaniche-Cignoli residuation holds on the truth ordering, not the knowledge ordering. Checking against ≤k\leq_k gives 21 violations. The ordering matters.
  • Forgetting incomparable pairs. In a partial order, some pairs are incomparable: neither x≤yx \leq y nor y≤xy \leq x. When computing “is x≤yx \leq y?”, incomparable means NO — the answer is false, not undefined.
  • Checking only one direction. The adjunction is a biconditional (if AND only if). You must check that the left side implies the right AND the right implies the left. A one-direction check is not sufficient.
