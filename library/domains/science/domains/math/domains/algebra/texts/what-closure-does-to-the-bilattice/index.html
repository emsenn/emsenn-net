What Closure Does to the Bilattice

2026-03-31 When you close a bilattice-valued system under inference, the unknown state disappears, the truth ordering becomes a chain, and the adjunction survives. Here is why.

  • What happens to the truth ordering
  • What happens to the knowledge ordering
  • Why the adjunction survives
  • What this means

The four-valued bilattice {⊥,t,f,⊤}\{\bot, t, f, \top\} has four epistemic states. When you close a system of assertions under inference — apply all derivation rules until nothing new can be produced — one of these states disappears from the active algebra.

¶Why ⊥ disappears

⊥\bot means “no information.” An assertion valued ⊥\bot is one that no source has said anything about.

Inference rules push values upward in the knowledge ordering. If assertion PP is ⊥\bot and there is an inference rule that can derive a value for PP from other assertions, the rule produces tt, ff, or ⊤\top — never ⊥\bot, because the rule adds information.

At the fixpoint, every assertion within scope has been reached by some rule. No in-scope assertion remains at ⊥\bot. The effective algebra operates on {t,f,⊤}\{t, f, \top\}.

This does not mean ⊥\bot is gone from the system. Assertions outside scope — those no rule can reach — can still be ⊥\bot. But within the closed part of the system, every assertion has been resolved to affirmed, denied, or contested.

¶What happens to the truth ordering

The truth ordering on the full bilattice is a diamond:

        t
       / \
      ⊥   ⊤
       \ /
        f

ff at the bottom, tt at the top, ⊥\bot and ⊤\top as incomparable middle elements.

Remove ⊥\bot. What remains?

    t
    |
    ⊤
    |
    f

A chain: f<t⊤<ttf <_t \top <_t t. The diamond has collapsed to a total order. Every pair of remaining values is comparable. The partial order has become total.

This is a qualitative change. In the diamond, ⊥\bot and ⊤\top were incomparable — “no information” and “contradictory information” could not be ranked against each other in truth. After closure removes ⊥\bot, ⊤\top takes a definite position: it sits between ff and tt. Contradiction is intermediate — more true than denial, less true than affirmation.

¶What happens to the knowledge ordering

The knowledge ordering on the full bilattice is also a diamond:

        ⊤
       / \
      t   f
       \ /
        ⊥

Remove ⊥\bot:

        ⊤
       / \
      t   f

A V-shape: ⊤\top above both tt and ff, with tt and ff still incomparable. This is a join-semilattice (the join t∨kf=⊤t \vee_k f = \top exists) but NOT a lattice (the meet t∧kft \wedge_k f would be ⊥\bot, which is absent).

¶Why the adjunction survives

The residuation a⊙p≤tq  ⟺  p≤t(a⇒q)a \odot p \leq_t q \iff p \leq_t (a \Rightarrow q) lives on the truth ordering, not the knowledge ordering. The truth ordering on {t,f,⊤}\{t, f, \top\} is a total order — a perfectly well-behaved chain. The adjunction has no reason to break.

Concretely: both ⊙\odot and ⇒\Rightarrow are closed on {t,f,⊤}\{t, f, \top\} (neither operation produces ⊥\bot from inputs in {t,f,⊤}\{t, f, \top\}). The identity element ⊤\top is in the set. Exhaustive computation on all 27 triples shows zero violations.

The fact that {t,f,⊤}\{t, f, \top\} is not a lattice under the knowledge ordering is irrelevant to the adjunction, because the adjunction does not live on the knowledge ordering. The two orderings are independent; the adjunction only needs one of them.

¶What this means

Closure does three things:

  1. Eliminates ignorance within scope. Every reachable assertion gets resolved.
  2. Collapses the truth ordering. The diamond becomes a chain. Contradiction (⊤\top) gets a definite position between denial and affirmation.
  3. Preserves the adjunction. Extending and restricting still work. The modal calculus remains decidable. The linear logic interpretation persists.

The system after closure is more structured than before: it has a total truth ordering instead of a partial one, and it has no unresolved assertions within scope. The algebraic operations that make reasoning possible are intact.
