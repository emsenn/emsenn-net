The tensor product in a monoidal category is a bifunctor ⊗: C × C → C that combines two objects into one. It is the categorical analogue of multiplication: just as a monoid has a binary operation on elements, a monoidal category has a binary operation on objects.

The tensor product must be associative up to coherent isomorphism: (A ⊗ B) ⊗ C ≅ A ⊗ (B ⊗ C). It must have a unit object I with I ⊗ A ≅ A ≅ A ⊗ I. The tensor product need not be commutative (A ⊗ B need not be isomorphic to B ⊗ A); when it is, the monoidal category is called symmetric.

In the category of sets, the tensor product is the Cartesian product. In the category of vector spaces, it is the classical tensor product of linear algebra. In the category of abelian groups, it is the tensor product over ℤ. In an enriched category, the tensor product of the base monoidal category is used to define composition of hom-objects: the composition map goes from Hom(B, C) ⊗ Hom(A, B) to Hom(A, C).
