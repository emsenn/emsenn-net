2026-03-30 A pair of functors F ⊣ G between categories, connected by a natural bijection Hom(F(a), b) ≅ Hom(a, G(b)).

An adjoint pair (or adjunction) is a pair of functors F:C→DF: C \to D and G:D→CG: D \to C between categories CC and DD, connected by a natural bijection:

HomD(F(a),b)≅HomC(a,G(b))\mathrm{Hom}_D(F(a), b) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_C(a, G(b))

for all objects aa in CC and bb in DD. FF is the left adjoint and GG is the right adjoint, written F⊣GF \dashv G.

The left adjoint is “free” — it generates structure. The right adjoint is “forgetful” — it constrains or tests. The adjunction says: a morphism out of the free construction F(a)F(a) corresponds exactly to a morphism into the forgetful image G(b)G(b).

Lawvere (1969) showed that existential and universal quantification are adjoint to substitution in a hyperdoctrine: ∃⊣∀\exists \dashv \forall. Existential quantification (left adjoint) extends a predicate to a larger context. Universal quantification (right adjoint) restricts to where the predicate holds everywhere.

In a residuated lattice, the product ⋅\cdot and implication →\to form an adjunction: a⋅b≤ca \cdot b \leq c iff b≤a→cb \leq a \to c. The product is the left adjoint operation (combining, extending). The implication is the right adjoint (testing, restricting).

In linear logic, tensor ⊗\otimes and linear implication ⊸\multimap form the fundamental adjunction ⊗⊣⊸\otimes \dashv \multimap.

Adjunctions are pervasive: Galois connections, free-forgetful pairs, tensor-hom adjunctions, and direct-inverse image pairs are all instances. Mac Lane’s slogan: “Adjoints arise everywhere.”
