Enriched Category

2025-12-31
Defines Enriched Category, enriched categories

An enriched category over a monoidal category V is a category-like structure whose hom-objects are objects of V rather than sets. An ordinary category is enriched over Set (with Cartesian product); replacing Set with a richer base V allows the morphisms between two objects to carry additional structure — topological, algebraic, or homotopical.

An enriched category C consists of: a collection of objects, for each pair (A, B) a hom-object Hom(A, B) in V, composition maps Hom(B, C) ⊗ Hom(A, B) → Hom(A, C) using the tensor product of V, and identity maps I → Hom(A, A) from the unit object. These must satisfy associativity and unit laws expressed as commutative diagrams in V.

A category enriched over the category of abelian groups has hom-groups (an additive category). A category enriched over the category of topological spaces has hom-spaces (a topological category). A category enriched over simplicial sets provides one model for ∞-categories. Enrichment is the mechanism by which “the morphisms themselves have structure” — a principle central to higher category theory and to the semiotic universe, where the Heyting algebra H enriches the relationships between semantic values.
