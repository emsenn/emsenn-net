2025-12-26 The do-nothing morphism on an object — neutral element for composition, required to exist by the axioms of a category.

The identity morphism idA:A→A\mathrm{id}_A : A \to A is the morphism on an object A that acts as the neutral element for composition. Composing with the identity before or after any morphism leaves it unchanged:

f∘idA=fidB∘f=ff \circ \mathrm{id}_A = f \qquad \mathrm{id}_B \circ f = f

for every morphism f:A→Bf : A \to B.

Every category is required to have an identity morphism for every object. This is one of the two axioms of a category, alongside associativity of composition. Without identity morphisms, there would be no notion of “doing nothing” — no baseline against which other morphisms can be measured. The identity morphism is to composition what zero is to addition or one is to multiplication.

What the identity morphism looks like depends on the category. In Set, the identity morphism on a set A is the identity function x↦xx \mapsto x. In a group viewed as a one-object category, the identity morphism is the group identity element. In a partial order, the identity morphism on a is the reflexivity assertion a≤aa \leq a.

Identity morphisms are what make isomorphisms definable. A morphism f:A→Bf : A \to B is an isomorphism exactly when there exists g:B→Ag : B \to A such that both composites g∘fg \circ f and f∘gf \circ g equal the respective identity morphisms. “Round trip equals doing nothing” can only be stated if “doing nothing” has a name — and the identity morphism is that name.

Identity morphisms are unique. If e:A→Ae : A \to A satisfies f∘e=ff \circ e = f for all ff out of A, then e=idAe = \mathrm{id}_A. The proof: e=idA∘e=idAe = \mathrm{id}_A \circ e = \mathrm{id}_A, using the identity law for idA\mathrm{id}_A on the left. Uniqueness means each object has exactly one identity morphism, not just at least one.
