2026-03-30 A bounded distributive lattice with an implication operation satisfying the residuation law: c ≤ (a → b) iff c ∧ a ≤ b. The algebraic semantics of intuitionistic logic.

A Heyting algebra is a bounded distributive lattice (H,∧,∨,0,1)(H, \wedge, \vee, 0, 1) with an additional operation — the Heyting implication →\to — satisfying:

c≤(a→b)if and only ifc∧a≤bc \leq (a \to b) \quad\text{if and only if}\quad c \wedge a \leq b

Implication is right adjoint to meet. This residuation law makes a Heyting algebra an integral commutative residuated lattice where the product is meet.

Heyting algebras are the algebraic semantics of intuitionistic logic, where excluded middle (a∨¬a=1a \vee \neg a = 1) does not hold in general. A Boolean algebra is a Heyting algebra where every element has a complement (¬¬a=a\neg\neg a = a). The open sets of a topological space form a complete Heyting algebra.

Jakl (2025) proved the Chu construction Chu(H,0)\mathrm{Chu}(H, 0) over a Heyting algebra HH is the twist construction producing a bilattice. This connects Heyting algebras to bilattice theory and linear logic.
