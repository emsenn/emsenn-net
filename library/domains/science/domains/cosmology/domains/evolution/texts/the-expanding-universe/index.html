The Expanding Universe

2026-03-31 In 1929, Edwin Hubble observed that distant galaxies are moving away from us, and the farther they are, the faster they recede. This was the first evidence that the universe is expanding.

In 1929, Edwin Hubble observed that distant galaxies are moving away from us, and the farther they are, the faster they recede. This was the first evidence that the universe is expanding.

¶What expansion means

The expansion of the universe is not galaxies flying apart through space like shrapnel from a bomb. It is space itself growing. The standard analogy is raisin bread rising in an oven: the raisins (galaxies) are not moving through the dough (space), but the dough is expanding, carrying the raisins along. From any raisin’s perspective, all other raisins are moving away, and the more distant ones are receding faster.

This means there is no center of expansion. Every point in the universe sees the same pattern: everything receding, recession speed proportional to distance. This is exactly what you’d expect from uniform expansion.

¶The scale factor

Cosmologists describe expansion with a single function: the scale factor a(t)a(t). It is a dimensionless number that multiplies all distances in the universe. At the present time, a=1a = 1 by convention. When a=0.5a = 0.5, the universe was half its current size. When a=2a = 2, it will be twice as large.

Its present value H0H_0 is approximately 70 km/s/Mpc. For every megaparsec (about 3.26 million light-years) of distance, the recession velocity increases by about 70 km/s.

¶Hubble’s law

The relationship v=H0dv = H_0 d – recession velocity proportional to distance – is Hubble’s law. It is not a coincidence or a selection effect. It is a mathematical consequence of uniform expansion. If all distances grow by the same percentage per unit time, then distant objects recede faster in absolute terms.

Observationally, we do not measure recession velocities directly. We measure redshifts – the stretching of light from distant sources. Redshift is a direct probe of expansion: the wavelength of a photon stretches by the same factor as space itself.

¶What governs expansion?

The Friedmann equations determine how a(t)a(t) evolves. The energy content of the universe controls the expansion rate. During the radiation-dominated era (the first ~50,000 years), radiation pressure drove the dynamics. During the matter-dominated era (from ~50,000 years to ~9 billion years), the gravitational pull of matter decelerated the expansion. For the last ~5 billion years, dark energy has dominated, and the expansion is accelerating.

¶Why this matters

Expansion is the most important single fact in cosmology. It means the universe had a beginning (the Big Bang). It means the universe was once hotter and denser than it is now. It explains the redshift of distant galaxies, the cooling of the cosmic microwave background, and the formation of all structure. Without understanding expansion, nothing else in cosmology makes sense.
