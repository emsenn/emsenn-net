Eight Principles

2026-04-02 八纲 (ba gang) — the fundamental diagnostic framework of TCM, organizing all clinical findings into four pairs of opposites: Interior/Exterior, Hot/Cold, Excess/Deficiency, and Yin/Yang

The Eight Principles (ba gang 八纲) are the most fundamental diagnostic framework in Traditional Chinese Medicine. They organize all clinical findings into four pairs of complementary opposites that locate a patient’s condition within a diagnostic coordinate system:

Pair Question it answers
Interior / Exterior (li / biao 里/表) Where is the problem?
Hot / Cold (re / han 热/寒) What is its thermal character?
Excess / Deficiency (shi / xu 实/虚) Is something present that shouldn’t be, or absent that should be?
Yin / Yang (yin / yang 阴/阳) What is the overall character?

Every TCM diagnosis begins with the Eight Principles. Before identifying which organ system is involved or which specific pathological products are present, the practitioner determines: is this interior or exterior? Hot or cold? Excess or deficient? Yin or Yang? These four determinations establish the basic treatment strategy — the wrong determination leads to the wrong treatment.

¶The four pairs

Interior / Exterior: Exterior patterns are acute, surface-level, and caused by external invasion — fever, chills, body aches, floating pulse. Interior patterns are chronic, deep, and involve the organ-function systems — organ-specific symptoms, deep pulse. Treatment of exterior patterns “releases the exterior” (inducing sweating). Treatment of interior patterns addresses the specific organ dysfunction.

Hot / Cold: Hot patterns show inflammation — redness, thirst for cold, dark urine, rapid pulse, red tongue with yellow coating. Cold patterns show insufficient warmth — pallor, preference for warm, clear urine, slow pulse, pale tongue with white coating. Hot patterns are treated with cooling methods; cold patterns with warming methods. Hu Zhang, being slightly cold, treats hot patterns.

Excess / Deficiency: Excess means a pathogenic factor is present — stagnation, accumulation, invasion. Symptoms are strong, forceful. Deficiency means a vital substance is depleted — Qi, Blood, Yin, Yang. Symptoms are weak, dull. Excess is treated by draining; deficiency by tonifying. Many presentations are mixed.

Yin / Yang: The master pair that summarizes the others. Yin patterns are interior + cold + deficient. Yang patterns are exterior + hot + excess. This pair provides the highest-level diagnostic summary.

¶Why the Eight Principles prevent harm

The Eight Principles exist to prevent the most dangerous prescribing errors:

  • Applying cold treatment to a cold pattern (deepens the cold)
  • Applying hot treatment to a hot pattern (inflames the heat)
  • Draining a deficient patient (further depletes)
  • Tonifying an excess pattern (strengthens the pathogen)

Each of these errors makes the patient worse. The Eight Principles are the minimum diagnostic framework needed to avoid them.
