Critical Appraisal

2026-03-03 A structured method for judging the trustworthiness, relevance, and applicability of evidence before using it in clinical reasoning or care decisions

Critical appraisal is a structured method for judging the trustworthiness, relevance, and applicability of evidence before using it in clinical reasoning or care decisions.

The practice originates in evidence-based medicine (EBM) and centers on a systematic set of questions applied to any piece of evidence — whether a randomized controlled trial, an observational study, a clinical guideline, or a FOAM blog post:

  1. Is the source trustworthy? Who produced this content? What is their expertise? Do they cite primary evidence? Are conflicts of interest disclosed?
  2. Is the evidence valid? What study design was used? Was the methodology sound? Are the results statistically and clinically significant? Could bias explain the findings?
  3. Is the evidence relevant? Does this apply to my patient population? Were the study participants similar to the patients I treat? Does the clinical context match?
  4. Is the evidence applicable? Can I implement these findings in my practice setting? Do I have the resources, skills, and institutional support? Does this conflict with existing local protocols?

In the context of FOAM consumption, critical appraisal takes on additional dimensions. FOAM content is typically not peer-reviewed, may not cite primary sources, and is shaped by the same platform incentives (engagement, novelty, shareability) that distort information in other domains. Red flags for FOAM content include:

  • Strong recommendations without cited evidence
  • Generalization from single cases to broad clinical populations
  • Novel techniques presented without safety language or acknowledgment of limitations
  • Recommendations that conflict with established guidelines without explicit justification

Critical appraisal is not skepticism for its own sake. It is the discipline that allows clinicians to learn from distributed sources without being misled by them. The goal is not to reject FOAM but to consume it with the same rigor applied to any other source of clinical evidence.
