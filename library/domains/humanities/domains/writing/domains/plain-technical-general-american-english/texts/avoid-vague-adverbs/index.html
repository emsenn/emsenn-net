2025-04-01 Reduce vague intensifiers and empty adverbs to improve clarity. Use when editing for precision.
  • Remove or replace vague adverbs like “clearly”, “very”, “extremely”, and “mostly”. The full list is in the style guide.
  • If a sentence feels empty without the adverb, the sentence needs rewriting, not an intensifier. Find the specific claim the adverb was trying to strengthen and state it directly [@williams2006].
  • Prefer concrete terms over intensifiers: “increased by 40%” over “increased significantly”, “every instance” over “completely”.
  • George Orwell’s rule applies: “If it is possible to cut a word out, always cut it out” [@orwell1946]. Vague adverbs are almost always cuttable.
  • Exception: adverbs that add measurable or specific meaning (“runs concurrently”, “fails silently”, “applies recursively”) are fine — they modify meaning, not intensity.
