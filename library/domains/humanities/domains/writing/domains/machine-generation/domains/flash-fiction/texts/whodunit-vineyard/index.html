Whodunit, Vineyard

2023-12-16

The sun dipped below the horizon, casting a crimson hue over the sprawling vineyard of Château Fleur. The estate buzzed with anticipation for the annual wine-tasting gala, but as guests swirled their glasses, an ear-piercing scream shattered the evening calm.

In the oak barrel room lay Bernard Lefevre, vintner and owner of Château Fleur—motionless beside his prized Merlot cask. His face was pale; his lips stained purple.

Detective Rousseau arrived as whispers snaked through clusters of onlookers. He eyed four suspects: Pierre, Bernard’s envious brother; Colette, a scorned sommelier; Lucien, a rival winemaker whose fortunes had soured like uncorked Bordeaux; and Marie, Bernard’s estranged daughter who stood to inherit everything.

Rousseau noted a broken wine glass by Bernard’s hand and traces of oleander petals—a deadly poison—on his blazer. Each suspect had been seen near the floral centerpieces decorating each table.

As he interrogated them under flickering candlelight in the tasting room, Rousseau caught sight of something peculiar: tiny fragments of crushed oleander beneath Colette’s fingernails. Her expertise with flavors would make it easy to mask such bitterness within wine’s complex notes.

With narrowed eyes meeting her startled gaze across half-empty glasses and spilled secrets—the mystery unraveled like twisted vines revealing their fruit: jealousy can be just as toxic as any plant grown wild among rows of cultivated grapes.
