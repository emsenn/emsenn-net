Writing

The craft and discipline of composing text – its forms, cognitive mechanisms, and techniques for producing meaning through language. Content covers essay forms, expository and argumentative structure, cognitive poetics, narrative identity, imagery, pacing, and the relationship between formal constraint and creative output.

Teleology: To understand how written forms produce meaning – what specific structural choices do to a reader’s cognition, and how writers can use that understanding deliberately.

Method: Cognitive poetics and formal analysis – examining how literary and expository structures engage perception, memory, and meaning-making, grounded in both cognitive science and the practical experience of writing under constraint.
