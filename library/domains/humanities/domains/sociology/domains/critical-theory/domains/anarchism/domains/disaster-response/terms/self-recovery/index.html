Self-Recovery

2026-03-08 A disaster-recovery term for affected households repairing or rebuilding through their own choices, labor, and resources, sometimes with outside support.

Self-recovery is the process by which disaster-affected households repair, build, or rebuild through their own choices, labor, and resources, sometimes with outside support [@twigg2021; @ahmedparrack2022].

The term matters because it centers household and community agency in recovery instead of treating reconstruction as something wholly delivered from outside. In shelter practice, support for self-recovery usually means helping people help themselves while preserving choice and local control [pathways2022].

Last reviewed March 8, 2026.
