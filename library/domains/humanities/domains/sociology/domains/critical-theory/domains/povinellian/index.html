Povinellian Analysis

¶Povinelli

Elizabeth Povinelli (1962–). Her work extends Foucault’s analysis of governance into the specific conditions of late liberalism — the phase of liberal governance that follows the recognition of its own colonial, racial, and environmental violence. Where Foucault analyzed how power produces knowledge and subjects, Povinelli analyzes how power operates through withdrawal: the removal of resources, recognition, and infrastructure from populations and places deemed unviable.

Her framework has three major components. Late liberalism names the political formation in which the system acknowledges its violence and manages that acknowledgment through inclusion and procedural reform rather than structural transformation. Economies of abandonment names the mode of governance in which communities are assessed, classified as unviable, and abandoned through administrative procedure — the withdrawal producing the evidence of failure that retroactively justifies it. Geontologies names the governance of the distinction between Life and Nonlife — the ontological classification that determines what can suffer and what is available for extraction, a governance more fundamental than biopolitics because it determines what counts as alive in the first place.

Endurance names the political practice of persisting under these conditions — not heroic resistance but the ongoing work of continuing to exist when the world has been organized to make your persistence irrelevant. Quasi-events name the managed disturbances that look like crises but function as maintenance — the system’s capacity to process disruption as ordinary input.

Povinelli’s fieldwork with Indigenous communities in northern Australia grounds every concept in concrete conditions of governance.

Central thesis

Povinellian analysis treats late-liberal governance as operating through administrative withdrawal — populations and places are classified, abandoned, and retroactively justified by the failures their abandonment produces — with the underlying move being ontological politics over the Life/Nonlife boundary itself.
