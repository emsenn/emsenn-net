Cybernetic Postliberalism

Cybernetic postliberalism is a school of analytical sociology that studies how late-liberal governance reproduces itself under conditions of contracting energetic surplus. Its central claim is that the system does not persuade its subjects or coerce them; it formats them. Structural contradictions that liberalism cannot resolve — universalism without individualism, individualism without universalism, progress without exhaustion — are routed through their subjects as personally manageable affect, and the self-regulation that follows is the governance mechanism itself. The process is named californication after the historical laboratory in which the apparatus was test-run, the actual coastline being not the place but what the apparatus has made of it. The mechanism is recurrent: the modern pastoral apparatus has done this work since at least the Christian church’s care of souls, with different institutional forms in different eras. What is distinctive about the present is that the energetic substrate that once funded richer institutional interiors — the seal of confession, slow planning, the centralized funding of abstraction — has collapsed, and the metabolism has redistributed onto platforms and onto the bodies of the subjects themselves.

The school’s load-bearing claim is that the circuits are exhaustive. There is no outside. Anarchist refusal, primitivist exit, accelerationist hyperengagement, abolitionist construction, off-grid retreat, melancholic critique, and spiritual transcendence are genres within the same metabolism, each producing the affective material the system requires. This is not a political verdict but a structural description: under the conjunction of declining net energy and platformal feedback architecture, no coherent subject-position constitutes an exterior, because every “outside” is itself a genre and every genre runs on the same affective infrastructure. Action that does not consent to the metabolism is possible only as practice — the unmanaged act, the care that does not wait for permission — and even practice is metabolized. The framework is honest about this. The closure is the school’s discovery, not its conclusion.

The texts below constitute the school’s monograph. Read in order, they introduce the framework, build its conceptual foundation, walk through its mechanism and the subject-formations it produces, locate its prior institutional form in the Christian pastoral apparatus, state its closure claim, position it among adjacent schools, and close by demonstrating the framework on the framework itself. The applied analyses — listed under principal cases — are the school working against particular events and figures, accessible after the monograph has supplied the vocabulary to read them. The terms in this domain are reference entries; the chapters are the readable substrate. The framework presupposes a material claim about declining net energy that lives outside this school, in the EROEI research linked under presupposes:; that claim is not part of cybernetic postliberalism but it is what makes cybernetic postliberalism necessary now, rather than one descriptive sociology among many.

Central thesis

Liberal subjectivity is reproduced not through ideology or coercion but through cybernetic feedback loops between bodies, affect, technology, and economy — made operative at planetary scale by platforms — under conditions of declining net energy that have collapsed the institutional surplus once funding centralized abstraction. The process named californication (structural contradictions formatted as personally manageable affect) is recurrent across the modern pastoral apparatus — its prior institutional form was the Christian church — and has intensified now because the energetic buffers that supported richer institutional interiors have contracted.

Featured texts

Introduction to Cybernetic Postliberalism
An orientation to the school: late-liberal governance reproduces itself by formatting structural contradictions as personally manageable affect, and the self-regulation that follows is the governance mechanism. The central concept is californication; the load-bearing structural claim is operational closure.
The Energetic Substrate
The school presupposes a material claim about declining net energy that lives in the EROEI literature outside cybernetic postliberalism proper. Useful-stage global fossil-fuel EROEI is now ~3.5:1, below Hall's 5:1 floor for complex society; the substrate's contraction is what made the apparatus's redistribution onto bodies and platforms structurally necessary.
Liberalism's Constitutive Contradictions
Liberalism carries four constitutive contradictions that no internal liberal move resolves: universalism without individualism, individualism without universalism, progress without exhaustion, and equality without sovereignty. Under contracted institutional surplus, these contradictions are now metabolized through subjects' inner lives rather than absorbed by institutional buffers — this is the input californication formats.
Genring and Ontogenring
Genring is the reduction of a living people to a fixed literary type through selection, codification, and substitution. Ontogenring extends the same logic up to the individual subject, who is addressed in a genre and expected to feel and act inside its prescribed arc. Attribution is itself an ontogenic act.
California as Laboratory
California — both the coastal territory and the imaginative screen — has served as the historical laboratory in which the californicational apparatus iterates. The Misiones, the Gold Rush, industrial agriculture, Hollywood, the Bay Area counterculture, Silicon Valley, and the wellness industry are stages of one apparatus, exporting and recombining formatting techniques on a continuous substrate of indigenous displacement and migrant labor.
Platformal Feedback Architecture
Contemporary californication operates through platforms that read each interaction, infer what the user wants, and reshape the next interaction — feedback loops at second-by-second timescales and planetary scale. Read in the lineage of Wiener, Beer, and Ashby, the platforms are Viable Systems in the strict cybernetic sense, with nested feedback layers that converge without central coordination.
Settlerism and the Cybernetic Subject
Settlerism — settler colonialism understood as a continuing structure rather than a finished event — supplies the historical-material substrate the contemporary apparatus runs on. The genres in which subjects are formatted presuppose a settler-cultural imaginary, and the temporal middle is structurally eliminated by the binary the structure offers. The cybernetic subject is a settler subject in a specific structural sense, regardless of biographical specification.
The Operational Closure of Late-Liberal Governance
The school's load-bearing structural claim: late-liberal governance is operationally closed in Niklas Luhmann's specific sense. No coherent subject-position produces an outside, because every 'outside' is itself a genre running on the same affective infrastructure. The closure is descriptive sociology, historically specific to platformal-energetic conditions, not totalizing of all human life.
The Ecclesial Recurrence
Californication is recurrent across the modern pastoral apparatus; the medieval Christian church is its most thoroughly developed prior institutional form. What is recurrent: continuous calibration of inner life through pastoral relations and specific affective genres (acedia, spes, studiositas, curiositas). What is changed: the energetic substrate is inverted, latency has collapsed, protected interiors are gone, the contradictions metabolized differ.
Cybernetic Postliberalism in Context
Cybernetic postliberalism positioned relative to adjacent postliberal critiques: right-postliberalism (Deneen, Vermeule), left-postliberalism (Brown, Streeck), affect-theoretical critique (Berlant, Hochschild), Foucauldian governmentality, settler-colonial studies, accelerationism, Frankfurt-school inheritance. The school's specific contributions are the cybernetic-systems framing, the closure claim, the energetic-substrate presupposition, and the recurrence with the church-pastoral.
Methods of Cybernetic-Postliberal Analysis
How to perform cybernetic-postliberal analysis on a specific case, organized around five operative questions: what structural contradiction is being metabolized, what genres are operating, what feedback loops are running, where the metabolism lands, what the case admits and refuses. With the reflexive corollary that the school is itself a metabolizable subject-position.

Subdomains

Who Benefits
An applied study tracing the actual beneficial parties of specific corporate-financial arrangements: who, named individually, financially benefits from MindGeek/Aylo's continued operation; from Colbeck Capital's hybrid-lending into the porn industry; from the Bruinen-ECP transition; from the Russian-organized-crime/Trump-orbit financing chains. Where the school's main analyses describe what the apparatus does, the who-benefits work names the specific people who benefit from particular configurations of the apparatus's operation. The structural concepts (beneficial ownership, nominee structures, offshore concealment, compliance theater) are tools to make the named persons traceable through the legal-jurisdictional apparatus that conceals them.
