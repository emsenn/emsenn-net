Coherent confusion

2025-04-15 The system-level governance condition in which subjects are confused but functional — unable to resolve what is happening, yet still interpreting, engaging, and producing the sensemaking labor that sustains the system. Mere confusion halts participation (people disengage and withdraw); coherent confusion sustains participation by keeping the signal ambiguous enough that interpretation never resolves but never fully fails either. The subject remains in a state of active processing: confused about the situation, but coherent in how they narrate their confusion. The interpretive labor — the takes, threads, analyses, commentary — is not a byproduct of governance; it is the governing mechanism itself, the outermost feedback loop in the cybernetic structure of late-liberal control.

Coherent confusion is the system-level governance condition in which subjects are confused but functional — unable to resolve what is happening, yet still interpreting, engaging, and producing the sensemaking labor that sustains the system. The confusion is not a failure of governance. The confusion is governance. The interpretive labor of trying to make sense of contradictory signals — the takes, the threads, the analyses, the commentary — is itself what sustains system coherence.

After the April 2025 tariff announcement, commentators produced thousands of explanations: it was a negotiating tactic, a policy error, a deliberate shock, a market test, a distraction. These explanations contradicted each other, yet each felt plausible. People remained confused — but they kept interpreting, kept producing takes, kept engaging. The system did not need them to understand. It needed them to keep trying.

¶Etymology and lineage

The compound coherent confusion foregrounds the structural paradox: the coherent names the system-level stability the condition produces; the confusion names the subject-level experience that produces it. The two are not in tension. The coherence at the system level depends on the confusion at the subject level; the confusion at the subject level is what feeds the coherence at the system level.

The conceptual lineage runs through several traditions. From cybernetics (Wiener 1948), the structural account of how systems maintain stability through feedback rather than through subjects’ understanding. From Beer (1972), the Viable System Model in which nested feedback layers operate at distinct timescales without central coordination — coherent confusion is what happens at the outermost interpretive layer. From Luhmann (1995), the systems-theoretic recognition that communication systems do not convey truth — they reproduce structure. Media does not report reality; it generates recursive readability. Each explanation adds interpretive density that stabilizes perception, allowing financial, political, and platform systems to proceed.

From Wendy Chun (2016), the temporal mechanism: modern systems preserve coherence by requiring subjects to update themselves continuously in order to remain visible and functional within systems whose logic is opaque to them. The update is the confusion made productive. From Franco Berardi (2017), the analysis of attention as both commodity and exhaust under cognitive capitalism: explanatory behavior is not friction to the system but a thermodynamic feature, a way of converting signal instability into engagement heat. From Bataille (1949), the older analysis of the accursed share — surplus that must be expended — completes the picture. In cybernetic capitalism, what must be burned is sensemaking itself.

¶Definitional structure

Three features mark a condition as coherent confusion.

Sustained ambiguity that never resolves but never fully fails. Mere confusion halts participation — people disengage, withdraw, stop processing. Coherent confusion sustains participation by keeping the signal ambiguous enough that interpretation never resolves but never fully fails either. There is always one more explanation, one more thread, one more angle. The subject remains in a state of active processing: confused about the situation, but coherent in how they narrate their confusion. The structural achievement is the maintenance of interpretive engagement at the edge of resolution without ever crossing into either resolution or disengagement.

Interpretive labor as the outermost feedback layer. Different systems respond on different timescales; the interpretive layer is the slowest of these. By the time commentators begin explaining what has happened, the underlying systems have already completed their behavioral adjustment. The explanations are not causal — they do not produce the correction. They are the final stabilizing behavior: the narrative closure that renders the correction legible and allows the system to proceed as though coherence had been maintained throughout. The interpretive layer is the apparatus’s outermost feedback loop, and coherent confusion is what it produces.

Recursive readability rather than truth. What circulates in the interpretive layer is not the truth of the situation but the readability of the situation as situation. Each explanation makes the situation more legible to itself, more available for further commentary, more usable as engagement substrate. The truth-content of any particular explanation matters less than its capacity to produce further interpretive cycles. The system rewards explanations that are interesting enough to engage with, not explanations that are correct.

¶Examples

The tariff-cycle commentary. The April 2025 tariff announcement produced thousands of explanations across newspapers, podcasts, social-media threads, and policy briefs. The explanations contradicted each other; each felt plausible; the policy was partially suspended before most of the commentary was published. The commentary kept coming anyway. This is coherent confusion in concentrated form: the system did not need the commentary to understand the tariff; the commentary was the system’s stabilization mechanism.

The pandemic-policy analysis cycle. During the COVID period, public-health, political, and economic explanations of policy decisions arrived at varying paces and contradicted one another. Subjects could not resolve what the actual policy logic was; they kept interpreting anyway. Each round of policy change produced a new round of interpretation. The interpretation was the labor the system metabolized; the underlying conditions (epidemiological, economic, political) had their own timescales the interpretation did not affect.

The election-night and post-election commentary. Election results trigger weeks-to-months of interpretive cycles: what the result meant, what voters were thinking, what the demographic shifts portend, what should have been done differently. The cycles continue past any conceivable utility for the next political action; they continue because the platform economy requires them, the news economy requires them, the subjects’ affective economy requires them. Coherent confusion is the resulting condition.

The contrast case: the practice that produces no engagement signal. A small group reading and discussing a single book over a year, refusing to summarize or post about it, allowing the book’s questions to develop slowly. This produces no interpretive substrate the apparatus can use; the apparatus’s metabolism does not register it. Such practices are candidates for what may survive metabolization (developed in The Operational Closure of Late-Liberal Governance).

¶Adjacent concepts

Coherent confusion is not the same as misinformation in the conventional public-discourse sense. Misinformation names false-content circulating against true-content; coherent confusion names a structural-systemic condition that operates regardless of whether the content circulating is true or false. True content, false content, and partial-true content all feed coherent confusion’s metabolism so long as they sustain interpretive engagement.

Coherent confusion is not the same as deliberate obfuscation by political actors. Deliberate obfuscation names an intentional move by particular actors; coherent confusion names a structural condition that operates even in the absence of any deliberate actor. The condition can be made worse by deliberate obfuscation, but it does not require it.

Coherent confusion is not the same as information overload in the popular psychological sense. Information overload names a quantitative condition (too much information for cognitive processing); coherent confusion names a qualitative-structural condition (the information’s interpretive non-resolution). Information overload produces overwhelm and withdrawal; coherent confusion produces sustained interpretive engagement.

Coherent confusion is not the same as postmodern indeterminacy in the literary-theoretical sense. Postmodern indeterminacy names a condition of meaning-instability across cultural production; coherent confusion names a specific governance-level operation by which interpretive labor is extracted as system stabilization. The two share the surface-feature of meaning-non-resolution but differ structurally in what the non-resolution produces and for whom.

¶Open questions

Can analysis exit coherent confusion without simply contributing to it? “Governing by confusion” concludes: “this piece didn’t escape the loop, it executed it.” The act of analyzing coherent confusion is itself an instance of coherent confusion. The writer who explains the mechanism is performing the interpretive labor the mechanism requires. Reading the explanation is performing it too. The loop closes when you feel it. Whether any analytical-discursive practice exits this is the question the school’s closure analysis holds open.

What is the relationship between coherent confusion and material structural change? Some structural changes do happen — regulatory action, institutional reform — and these happen even within conditions of pervasive coherent confusion. Whether the changes happen despite coherent confusion or because the confusion provides cover for them is empirically open.

How does coherent confusion interact with the affective conditions of the subjects who sustain it? The interpretive labor takes a toll: anxiety, exhaustion, the sense that something cannot be grasped. Whether sustained exposure to coherent confusion is structurally compatible with subjects’ continued participation, or whether it produces eventual disengagement that the system must then re-recruit through new content, is a live question with practical consequences.
