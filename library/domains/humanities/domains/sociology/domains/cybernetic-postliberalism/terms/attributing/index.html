Attributing

2026-04-27 The principal ontogenric act: the assignment of a property, identity, or condition to a being or situation, which installs both into the genre-formatted ontology and produces the entitlements and anticipations that follow from the installation.

To attribute is to assign a property, identity, or condition to a being or situation. The act looks descriptive — she is depressed; they are racist; this is trauma; that is collapse — and the framework’s claim is that under ontogenring the act is not descriptive but constitutive. Attributing installs the named being or situation into a genre-formatted ontology, with the ontogenric entitlements and anticipations that follow from the installation arriving with it.

¶Etymology

The verb derives from Latin attribuere — ad- (to) + tribuere (to assign, allot, give), which itself comes from tribus (tribe), originally the act of assigning citizens to their tribes for political and military purposes. The etymological residue is exact: to attribute is to assign, to allot — to do something to a person or thing rather than merely to describe it. The political-administrative root captures what the framework’s term re-foregrounds: attribution is an act of allocation, with administrative and political consequences, not a neutral predicate.

¶Definitional structure

Three features make attributing distinctive within the framework’s vocabulary.

It is unidirectional. A descriptive predicate, in the conventional view, is true or false of an independently-existing thing. Attributing is what happens when liberal ontologizing has already produced a discrete thing for predicates to be true or false of: the predicate now constitutes the thing’s place in the genre, and the thing’s subsequent behavior is read through that place. There is no neutral attributing; attributing always installs.

It is binding. Once a being or situation has been attributed, the genre’s feeling-rules and resolution-arcs apply. The trauma-attributed subject is now in the recovery genre and is entitled to (and obliged to perform) the affective arc the genre prescribes. The racism-attributed situation is now in the dialectical-overcoming genre and is entitled to (and obliged to perform) the abolitionist arc its critics expect. The binding is not psychological; it is genre-structural.

It can be self-reflexive. A subject can attribute conditions to themselves — I am exhausted; I am traumatized; I am the kind of person who — and the self-attribution operates with the same constitutive force as attribution by others. Self-attribution is the principal route by which the subject installs themselves into a genre rather than waiting to be installed.

¶Examples

Medical diagnosis as attribution. A clinician diagnoses a patient with major depressive disorder. The diagnosis appears to describe a pre-existing condition. Under ontogenring, the diagnosis installs the patient into the depression genre, with its arc (acknowledgment, treatment, response, recovery-or-management), affective register (low mood, anhedonia, slowed cognition that the patient now has vocabulary for), and resolutions (medication, therapy, lifestyle change). Hacking (1995) showed how this looping operates: the diagnosis changes who the patient is, which changes how the diagnosis applies, which changes the diagnostic category itself.

Racial categorization is attribution at the population level. The state administers race; individuals are racially attributed at birth, in legal documents, in administrative records. The attribution does not describe a pre-existing race; it installs the named person into a racial-genre with its own arcs and entitlements. Wynter’s analysis of Man-overrepresentation traces this to the constitutive moves of liberal modernity.

Expertise attribution. A person becomes a recognized expert in a domain through being treated as one — cited, invited to speak, asked for opinions. The attribution does not describe pre-existing expertise; it installs the person into the expert genre. Once installed, the genre’s arc (productivity, authoritativeness, eventual displacement by next-generation experts) shapes what the person does and how their statements are received.

¶Adjacent concepts

Attributing is not the same as describing. Description, in the conventional view, tracks the world: a description is true if it accurately captures pre-existing features. Attributing is constitutive — under ontogenring, the act of assigning the property is part of what the property’s bearer becomes.

Attributing is not the same as predicating in the logical sense. Predication is the abstract logical operation of saying something of something. Attributing is a specific kind of predication — the kind that, under conditions of liberal ontologizing and ontogenring, has installation force.

Attributing is not the same as labeling in the sociological sense (Becker, the labeling theorists). Labeling theory holds that deviance is constituted by the labeling response; attributing is broader — it covers identifications, diagnoses, recognitions, and self-categorizations whether or not the resulting position is deviant.

¶Open questions

When is attribution corrigible? Some attributions are revisable (a misdiagnosis can be corrected; a misidentification of expertise can be undone). Others appear to install with such finality that even apparent revisions leave the original installation operative. The framework does not yet specify the conditions under which attribution is corrigible vs. binding-once-made.

Are there non-installing attributions? Are there attributions that are merely descriptive, that do not install the named being into a genre? The framework’s working assumption is that all attributions made under conditions of liberal ontologizing operate with installation force, but the strong claim is contestable; weak claims (some attributions install more than others) are easier to defend.

What is the role of intention in attribution? An attribution made in good faith and an attribution made strategically (to install the target into a genre that disadvantages them) appear to operate with similar installation force. Whether the framework should distinguish them — and on what basis — is open.
