Ontogenring

2026-04-27 The process by which subjects are produced through genre-formatting — extending genring from peoples up to the level of the individual subject.

Ontogenring is the process by which subjects are produced through genre-formatting. Where genring reduces a living people to a fixed literary type — selecting, codifying, and substituting features until the type replaces the people — ontogenring extends the same operation up one level, to the production of subjects themselves. Beings are no longer merely placed in genres; their being is constituted by the genre. The genre is the ontology. Hence the onto- prefix: it is not only that experiences are generic, it is that what one is is generic.

¶Etymology

The term combines Greek ontos (being) with genre (the literary or expectational category) and the gerund -ing. The compound names a process — genre-doing-the-work-of-being — and the suffix preserves that continuous, in-progress character: ontogenring is something subjects are continuously inside, not a state they enter once.

The construction extends Lauren Berlant’s genring (Berlant 2008, 2011) from the level of experience to the level of subject. In Berlant, genre is what tells you what feelings are appropriate at each phase of an unfolding situation. In ontogenring, genre tells you what beings are inhabit-able and what each being’s arc must be. The leap from experiential expectation to ontological assignment is the step that onto- names.

¶Definitional structure

Ontogenring has four operative features.

Being-template. A genre supplies not just expectations about how a situation unfolds but a template for who one is during it. The recovered addict, the trauma survivor, the entrepreneur, the resilient activist — each is a being-template, complete with affective register, narrative arc, and recognized resolutions. Subjects do not occupy templates; templates occupy subjects.

Performative installation. Subjects are installed into being-templates through acts that look descriptive (diagnoses, identifications, attributions) but operate constitutively. Once installed, the template’s arc applies. I am depressed is, under ontogenring, less a description than an entry into the recovery genre.

Genre-ontology coextension. The genre and the subject’s being become coextensive. To exit the template is not to violate a norm; it is to fall out of intelligible existence within the genre. The subject who refuses the recovery arc is not a failed recoverer; they are illegible as a subject within the template the genre supplies.

Self-reproduction. Once installed, subjects reproduce the genre by performing it. The genre’s stability does not require ongoing institutional enforcement; the subjects do that work themselves through their continued performance of the template’s arc.

¶Examples

Trauma identity. A person identifies as having complex trauma. The identification ontogenrically installs them into a recovery genre — a template with phases (recognition, processing, integration), an affective register (vulnerability, mourning, gratitude), recognized resolutions (post-traumatic growth, advocacy, mentorship), and a vocabulary the subject is now fluent in. The identification does not merely describe a condition; it makes the subject the kind of being whose subsequent experience the genre formats.

Entrepreneur identity. A person identifies as a founder or entrepreneur. The template comes with an arc (vision, struggle, breakthrough, exit), an affective register (intensity, resilience, focus), and a corpus of inhabited postures (hustle, work-life-integration, the “operator” stance). Failure is metabolized within the genre as part of the arc; quitting is metabolized as transition to a related role.

Activist identity. A person identifies as an anti-racist activist, abolitionist, or organizer. The template has phases (radicalization, study, action, burnout, recovery), affective rules (anger that is structured, grief that is shareable), and recognized arcs (movement-building, leadership, transition to elder/educator).

In each case, what looks like personal identification is the ontogenric installation of a being-template.

¶Adjacent concepts

Ontogenring is closely related to but distinct from several adjacent operations.

Interpellation (Althusser 1971) is the moment ideology hails the subject and the subject turns: the police call you, there, and the act of turning installs you as the subject who can be addressed. Interpellation is event-shaped (a hail, a turn). Ontogenring is process-shaped: a subject is genre-formatted continuously, across many micro-installations, by the structure of expectations the surrounding apparatus produces.

Performativity (Butler 1990) is the iterated repetition of regulatory norms by which the subject is materialized. Performativity is closer to ontogenring than interpellation; both treat subject-formation as ongoing rather than punctual. The difference is emphasis: performativity foregrounds the citation of norms (gender as the iterated citation of feminine performance); ontogenring foregrounds the genre-form of the iteration (the being-template that the citations populate).

Dynamic nominalism (Hacking 2002) names the looping process by which kinds of person come into being through classification: psychiatric categories produce persons who can then be categorized, who then transform the category. Ontogenring incorporates dynamic nominalism but extends it: under ontogenring, the kind of person produced is not simply classified but ontologically installed — the kind is the being.

Strong social construction holds that some categories are produced by social processes; ontogenring is compatible with strong social construction but adds the specific claim that the construction proceeds through genre, with the structural features genres bring (phases, arcs, affective registers, resolutions) inherited as features of the constructed being.

¶Open questions

How stable are ontogenric identities under direct refusal? Subjects sometimes refuse the templates installed in them (the trauma survivor who refuses the recovery arc; the recovered addict who refuses the relapse-narrative). The framework’s claim is that such refusals are difficult to sustain because the apparatus continually re-installs the template; whether refusal can be cumulative — whether enough refusals can erode the apparatus — is contested.

What is the relationship between ontogenring and embodiment? Ontogenring is a process at the level of subject-formation; embodied experience is part of how subjects live, but is not itself ontogenric. Whether ontogenring constrains embodied experience or is constrained by it — and where the boundary falls — is open.

How do subjects formed by multiple ontogenric templates negotiate among them? A subject may be installed simultaneously into the trauma genre and the entrepreneur genre and the activist genre, with their respective arcs in tension. The negotiation among templates is an empirical-sociological question the framework gestures toward but does not resolve.
