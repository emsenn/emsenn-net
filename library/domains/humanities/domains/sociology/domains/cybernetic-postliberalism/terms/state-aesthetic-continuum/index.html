State-Aesthetic Continuum

2026-04-30 The single apparatus, varying by institutional cover, in which commercial entertainment, brand soft power, and overt psychological operations produce affectively-shaped audiences using a shared formal vocabulary.

The state-aesthetic continuum is the single apparatus, varying by institutional cover rather than by formal vocabulary, in which commercial entertainment, corporate soft power, brand-justice marketing, platform-native creator content, recruitment media, and overt psychological operations produce affectively-shaped audiences. The term names what these registers share — a common moral-physics regime, a common library of formal techniques, a common audience pool — and treats their differences as differences of position on the continuum rather than as differences of category.

The dominant analytical framing treats these registers as separate fields. Hollywood is one industry, advertising is another, the state’s information operations are a third, platform creators are a fourth. Each has its trade press, its critics, its histories, its scandals. The state-aesthetic continuum names the claim that this separation is institutional rather than analytical: at the level of what the artifacts do to audiences, the registers operate as variations on a single theme.

¶Why the term

The term is needed because the standard separations make it hard to see the work that the apparatus does as a whole. An analysis of recruitment media that does not see Hollywood is missing the audience-formation that prepared viewers to receive recruitment media as ordinary content. An analysis of platform feeds that does not see advertising is missing where the formal vocabulary of platform-native content actually came from. An analysis of advertising that does not see psychological operations is missing the doctrinal register in which the apparatus has, in recent years, openly named its capabilities.

The continuum exists because the artifacts that make it up share substantive properties:

  • A common moral physics — the same implicit rules about how harm, responsibility, and consequence operate across the registers.
  • A common formal vocabulary — rubber-hose realism, strategic infantilization, affective looping, soft surrealism, moral displacement all appear across multiple registers.
  • A common labor pool — the producers of state recruitment content, brand campaigns, and platform-native viral video are drawn from overlapping populations and trained in the same aesthetic curriculum.
  • A common distribution substrate — algorithmic platforms increasingly carry artifacts from all the registers in the same feed, presenting them to audiences as continuous.
  • A common audience — the audience for state-aesthetic recruitment, branded content, and viral creator content is largely the same audience, and increasingly self-aware of being so.

Treating the registers as separate categories misses these shared substantive properties. Naming the continuum lets analysis address them directly.

¶What the term does not claim

The continuum is not a conspiracy. It is not a coordinated apparatus. It does not require institutional cooperation between Hollywood, the Pentagon, and platform companies, though such cooperation does happen in particular cases (Disney’s WWII propaganda production, the long history of military consultation on defense-themed films, advertising agencies’ reuse of psy-ops research, platform companies’ contracts with the U.S. defense and intelligence apparatus). The continuum is real even when no specific cooperation is occurring, because the substantive properties listed above are real even when no one is coordinating them.

This matters because the conspiratorial reading of the apparatus is both attractive and analytically weak. Attractive because it offers a clear villain and a clear story. Weak because it requires inventing coordination that is not there and obscures the more important analytical fact: that the apparatus operates without coordination, through ordinary mechanisms (shared labor pools, shared distribution substrates, shared audience expectations, shared formal vocabularies), and that this is what makes it stable. A conspiratorial apparatus has central points of failure. The state-aesthetic continuum has none.

¶Positions on the continuum

The continuum is best thought of in terms of positions, each defined by a particular configuration of institutional cover, declared purpose, and audience relation. The same formal vocabulary is in use across positions; what varies is the cover under which the vocabulary is deployed.

Commercial entertainment. Declared purpose: pleasure. Institutional cover: studio system, streaming platforms, the entertainment industry’s self-narration as creative rather than persuasive work. The work the artifacts do at the level of audience formation is treated as side effect rather than purpose.

Corporate soft power. Declared purpose: brand identity, customer loyalty, market position. Institutional cover: marketing, communications, brand-management. The work the artifacts do at the level of audience formation is treated as instrumental to commercial ends but not as the substantive thing the apparatus is for.

Brand-justice marketing. Declared purpose: institutional alignment with social values. Institutional cover: corporate social responsibility, brand purpose, social-impact communications. The work the artifacts do at the level of audience formation is treated as a side effect of moral commitment.

Platform-native creator content. Declared purpose: individual creative expression. Institutional cover: the platform’s framing of creators as independent voices. The work the artifacts do at the level of audience formation is treated as authentic self-expression that happens to circulate at scale.

Recruitment and influence media. Declared purpose: institutional outreach, public information, narrative engagement. Institutional cover: communications, public affairs, recruiting commands. The work the artifacts do at the level of audience formation is treated as transparent communication of institutional purpose, even when the formal techniques deployed are designed to operate below transparent communication.

Open psychological operations. Declared purpose: information operations against adversary populations. Institutional cover: doctrinal discussion, military journals, formally-designated influence operations groups. The work the artifacts do at the level of audience formation is avowed. This position is the smallest part of the continuum but it is analytically central, because it is where the apparatus openly describes the techniques the rest of the continuum uses without acknowledgment.

The positions are not exclusive. A single artifact can occupy more than one. A recruitment video produced by a U.S. military command and circulated on platform-native channels under doctrinal influence is operating across recruitment, platform-native creator, and open-PSYOP positions simultaneously. Tracking which positions an artifact occupies, and how the institutional cover shifts depending on which audience is reading it, is one of the principal analytical operations the term enables.

¶What moves across the continuum

The continuum is not static. Several things move between its positions, and tracking the movements is part of what the term is for.

Formal techniques migrate. Rubber-hose moral physics moves from cartoons into action cinema into recruitment media. Soft-surrealist collage moves from music videos into brand campaigns into PSYOP outputs. Strategic infantilization moves from children’s media into wellness apps into platform onboarding flows. The migration is not metaphorical; the techniques are the same techniques.

Labor moves. People who learn the apparatus’s formal vocabulary in entertainment work move into advertising; people who learn it in advertising move into platform creator economies; people who learn it in platform creator economies move into military influence groups and back. The labor pool is unified. The institutional badges on individual workers do not constrain the techniques they bring with them.

Audiences move. The same audience that watches recruitment videos ironically also engages with branded content sincerely; the same audience that consumes wellness content credulously also encounters platform-native critique of wellness content. Audiences inhabit multiple positions on the continuum simultaneously and switch between modes of reception within a single feed session.

Doctrinal vocabulary moves. When the open-PSYOP position openly names a technique (memetic warfare, narrative-conflict capability), the naming becomes available to the rest of the continuum. Brands and platforms can adopt the doctrinal vocabulary as marketing copy without occupying the doctrinal position.

These movements mean the continuum is best read as a circulation system rather than a static taxonomy. What is at one position now will be at another later; what was developed under one institutional cover will be deployed under another within a generation.

¶Relation to other terms

The state-aesthetic continuum is the highest-level frame within which several other terms in cybernetic postliberalism operate.

It is the substrate on which californication is performed at scale: the continuum’s audience-formation work is what produces the population that processes structural contradiction as personal affect. The continuum makes californication a default reception, not an unusual one.

It is the field across which genring and genre calibration circulate: the genre figures and calibrations developed in one position move freely to the others.

It is what memetic warfare is the doctrinal register of: memetic warfare is not a separate apparatus but a particular position on the continuum, the position where the state operates openly inside the platformal substrate.

It is the apparatus inside which rubber-hose realism operates as moral-physics regime: rubber-hose realism is the moral physics; the state-aesthetic continuum is the apparatus that distributes the moral physics across institutional registers.

It is also the apparatus that socially ontogenic media names from a different angle: socially ontogenic media is the term for what the continuum’s outputs do to the subject positions audiences inhabit; the state-aesthetic continuum is the apparatus that produces those outputs.

¶How to identify which position an artifact occupies

A short procedure:

  1. Identify the institutional cover under which the artifact is presented to its audience (declared purpose, sponsoring institution, claimed register).
  2. Identify the formal vocabulary the artifact uses (the moral physics, the address mode, the temporal structure, the surface register).
  3. Compare. Where the formal vocabulary is farther along the continuum than the institutional cover acknowledges, you have located a useful analytical site: an artifact in (say) the brand-justice position deploying open-PSYOP vocabulary, or a platform-native creator position carrying recruitment-media moral physics.
  4. Track the audience’s relation to the gap. Sophisticated platform audiences increasingly read the gap directly — the irony with which Ghosts in the Machine was received online is exactly the audience tracking the continuum and reading position-shifts in real time.

A full procedure for this kind of analysis is given in how to trace the state-aesthetic continuum of an image.

¶What it does not name

The state-aesthetic continuum is not a claim that everything is propaganda, that audiences are dupes, or that institutional differences are illusory. The institutional differences are real and matter for many purposes. The claim of the term is narrower: that for the purposes of analyzing what these artifacts do at the level of audience formation, treating them as a continuum is more accurate than treating them as separate fields, and that this re-framing is what makes the apparatus’s work visible as a single thing rather than as a series of unrelated phenomena.
