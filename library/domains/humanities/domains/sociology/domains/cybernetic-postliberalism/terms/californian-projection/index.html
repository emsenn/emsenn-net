Californian projection

2026-04-27 The formation in which California — the actual coastal territory and the imaginative screen — has served as the historical laboratory in which the apparatus iterates: a continuous test-and-export of subject-formatting infrastructures, from the Misiones Californianas through the Gold Rush, Hollywood, Esalen, Silicon Valley, and the wellness industry, each phase a stage of one apparatus rather than separate phenomena.

Californian projection is the formation in which California — the actual coastal territory and the imaginative screen of the same name — has served as the historical laboratory in which the apparatus iterates. The Misiones Californianas, the Gold Rush, Hollywood, Esalen, Silicon Valley, the wellness industry: each is a stage of one continuous apparatus, not a separate phenomenon. The land is not California; California is what we make of it. The projection is the apparatus.

¶Etymology and twofold sense

The word projection operates in two senses simultaneously. In the cartographic sense, a projection is a mapping of one surface onto another with systematic distortion. In the psychoanalytic sense, projection is the attribution of one’s own contents to an external screen so that they can be encountered as if they were external. Californian projection is both: the actual coastal territory has been continuously mapped onto by the imaginative screen, and the screen has been used to externalize American (and increasingly global) contents that could not be encountered as internal.

The twofold sense is essential to the term. California as a place exists; the actual land, water, and inhabitants are real. California as the projection’s content is what has been made of that place — successive imaginative regimes that have conditioned what the place is for and what work the place performs. The projection is not the place and not merely the imagination; it is the relation between them, sustained over time, through which the apparatus has done its work.

¶Lineage

Carey McWilliams (1946) was first to argue, against the dominant historiography that treated Southern California as an aberrant region, that Southern California operates as a distinct civilizational laboratory — a site where new social, economic, and cultural forms are tested and from which they propagate. Kevin Starr’s multi-volume Americans and the California Dream (beginning 1973) excavated the successive nineteenth- and early twentieth-century imaginative regimes that constituted California-as-screen.

Joan Didion’s Slouching Towards Bethlehem (1968) gave the literary diagnosis of late-1960s California — Haight-Ashbury, the Manson murders, the Pentagon Papers, the broader collapse of the post-war consensus — as the place where American futurity was being projected, refined, and consumed in real time. Mike Davis’s City of Quartz (1990) extended the analysis to Los Angeles as the metropolitan engine of the projection’s twentieth-century operations: real-estate, film, military-industrial, and the spatial production of the future.

Greg Grandin (2019) places California within the longer arc of the frontier as American projection-zone: the westward extension of settler infrastructures across the continent culminated at the Pacific, and California was the terminal coast where the projection had to figure out what to do when there was no more land to extend across. Dolores Hayden (1995) and Roxanne Dunbar-Ortiz (2014) supply the structural counter-history: the projection works by erasure, and what it erases is the Indigenous, Mexican-Californian, Black, and Asian histories beneath its surfaces.

¶Definitional structure

Three features mark a phenomenon as part of the Californian projection.

Iteration of one apparatus. The Missions, the Gold Rush, Hollywood, Esalen, Silicon Valley, the wellness industry — these are not unrelated cultural phenomena that happen to share a coastline. Each is an iteration of a continuous apparatus that converts particular features of place (climate, distance from East Coast institutions, settler-colonial displacement, ocean-Pacific orientation) into the production of inhabitable subject-positions and exportable cultural infrastructures.

Test-and-export logic. The apparatus tests subject-formatting infrastructures locally and then exports them. The Missions tested the conversion of indigenous populations through managed labor, belief, and daily practice — the templates were exported across Latin America. Hollywood tested the industrial production of inhabitable fantasy — the form was exported globally. Silicon Valley tested platform infrastructures for affect-capture and engagement — the form was exported as the contemporary global digital infrastructure. The wellness industry tests the commodification of self-regulation — the form is being exported in real time.

Erasure as condition of the projection. The projection’s continuity depends on the systematic erasure of what was on the land before each iteration. The Indigenous Californian peoples (the Tongva, Chumash, Ohlone, Pomo, and many others) and their relations with the land are erased in the Mission record; Mexican-Californian dispossession is erased in the Gold Rush record; Black and Asian populations are erased in the Hollywood and post-war narratives; Indigenous and immigrant labor is erased in the Silicon Valley narrative. The projection’s iterations are continuous because the erasures are continuous.

¶Examples

The Misiones Californianas. The Franciscan mission system established along the California coast in the late eighteenth and early nineteenth centuries was, in McWilliams’s reading, the first iteration of the apparatus. It established the template the subsequent iterations would inherit: convert populations through the managed direction of labor, belief, and daily practice; produce subject-positions that the apparatus then administers; export the resulting forms.

The Gold Rush. The 1848–1855 Gold Rush converted the conditions of place (recently-annexed territory, disrupted indigenous and Mexican-Californian sovereignty, gold-bearing geology) into the production of a particular subject-position — the American who arrived, extracted, and either succeeded or moved on — and an industrial infrastructure (mining, banking, transportation) that exported the formation eastward. The Gold Rush is where extraction-as-opportunity was first formatted.

Hollywood. The early twentieth-century relocation of the U.S. film industry to Los Angeles (driven by climate, distance from East Coast patent enforcement, and the availability of varied landscapes) produced the global industry of inhabitable fantasy. Hollywood tested the formats by which mass audiences could be supplied with inhabitable subject-positions through cinematic narrative and exported the forms across the twentieth century.

Esalen, the counterculture, and the wellness industry. The 1960s establishment of Esalen Institute and the broader Bay Area counterculture tested the conversion of political contradiction into personal-spiritual-development. The forms — encounter groups, somatic practices, transpersonal psychology, the human-potential movement — were industrialized over the subsequent half-century into the global wellness industry. The wellness app, the breathwork retreat, the trauma-informed self-care framework: these are exports of the apparatus’s late-century iteration.

Silicon Valley as platform laboratory. The post-1970s convergence of military-funded computing research, venture-capital infrastructure, and counter-culture talent in the San Francisco Bay Area produced the platform-tech complex. The platforms are the apparatus’s most recent export — the global infrastructures by which engagement is captured, affect is monetized, and subject-positions are distributed.

¶Adjacent concepts

Californian projection is not the same as California-as-region. California-as-region is a geographic and political-administrative entity with measurable economic, demographic, and institutional features. Californian projection is the apparatus that operates on and through that region; the two are related but analytically distinct.

Californian projection is not the same as manifest destiny or the frontier myth, though it is continuous with them. Manifest destiny is the nineteenth-century U.S. ideology of westward continental expansion; the frontier myth is the cultural form that legitimated it. The Californian projection is what happened to the apparatus when the continental frontier closed at the Pacific; it inherited the legitimating logic but had to do its work in a place rather than across a continuum.

Californian projection is not the same as sunbelt economics or post-war suburbanization, though it has operated through both. These are economic-historical formations the projection has used; the projection itself is the longer-running apparatus, with antecedents extending back to the Missions and successors that will outlast any particular economic configuration.

¶Open questions

What happens to the projection if its terminal economic-institutional infrastructure (Silicon Valley, the wellness industry, Hollywood) is hollowed out by capital flight, climate displacement, or political reconfiguration? The projection’s continuity has so far survived multiple economic-institutional substrates; whether it can survive the contemporary period’s compounding pressures is open.

How does the projection interact with Indigenous resurgence in California? The Indigenous-Californian populations the projection erases continue to exist, organize, and assert sovereignty. The conditions under which the projection’s erasure-work fails — and what would follow from such failure — are studied in part by Indigenous-Californian scholars and organizers; the framework’s term gestures at this as a substantial open area.

Is the projection exportable as a category — does the apparatus operate elsewhere under different conditions of place? The Texas-projection, the Florida-projection, the Dubai-projection have been suggested as analogues; whether these are genuine instances of the same apparatus or different formations that share features with it is contested.
