Genre Coherence

2026-04-02 The demand that a nation's cast of folk types remain stable — so that anyone who deviates from the genre becomes a narrative disruption requiring exclusion. The mechanism connecting genring to fascist violence.

Genre coherence is the demand that a nation’s cast of folk types remain stable. Once a nation imagines itself as a collection of authentic genre figures — the Noble Indian, the Hearty Bavarian, the Mountain Kurd, the salt-of-the-earth farmer — the integrity of the national story depends on the characters remaining as assigned. Anyone who does not fit the genre becomes a problem for the story. Not necessarily an ideological enemy, but a narrative disruption: a character who breaks the fourth wall.

The mechanism connects genring to exclusion. Genring produces the characters. Genre coherence is the structural pressure to maintain them. When a Lakota person writes mathematics, a Bavarian reads Marx, or a Kurdish woman leads an anarchist revolution, they disrupt the genre. The disruption threatens the national narrative because the narrative requires its cast to be recognizable, reproducible, and emotionally stable. The feeling that something is “wrong” when a genred person deviates from type — the sense of inauthenticity, the discomfort, the refusal to engage — is genre coherence operating as social enforcement.

Roger Griffin (1991) defines fascism as palingenetic ultranationalism: a mythic narrative of national rebirth from decadence. The narrative requires a cast of authentic folk types who have been corrupted by modernity and who will be reborn through struggle. Genre coherence is what makes the cast load-bearing. Jews, Roma, queer people, disabled people, urban intellectuals — none fit the genre of the authentic Volk. Their presence threatens the coherence of the story. Whether exclusion takes the form of social marginalization, legal disenfranchisement, or physical elimination depends on circumstances. The mechanism — genre coherence demanding the removal of what does not fit — is the same at every scale.

Genre coherence is not a sufficient condition for fascism. Genred populations exist in many contexts that do not produce fascist regimes. But fascism requires genre coherence, because it requires a mythic past populated by stable, recognizable characters. The tighter the coherence, the less the story can tolerate deviation. The less it can tolerate deviation, the more it demands exclusion. The more it demands exclusion, the closer it is to violence.
