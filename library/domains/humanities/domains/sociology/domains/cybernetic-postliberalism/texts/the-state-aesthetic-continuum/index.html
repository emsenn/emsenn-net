The State-Aesthetic Continuum

2026-04-30 Why commercial entertainment, brand soft power, platform-native creator content, and overt psychological operations are best read as positions on a single continuum rather than as separate fields, and what the continuum makes visible that the field-by-field reading misses.

¶What this text is for

This text gives the long argument for treating commercial entertainment, corporate soft power, brand-justice marketing, platform-native creator content, recruitment media, and overt psychological operations as positions on a single continuum rather than as separate fields with distinct logics. The compressed argument is in the state-aesthetic continuum term. This text develops the argument: why the field-by-field reading is the default, what it costs, what the continuum reading enables, and where the continuum reading runs into limits.

The text is for readers who have read the rubber-hose-realism paper or the term file and want to understand the theoretical commitment underwriting the move from “Koko” to “PSYOP” inside a single argument. That move is doing more work than it might seem; this text shows what work.

¶The default: separate fields

The standard organization of media analysis treats the apparatus as several institutionally-discrete fields. There is a Hollywood and a body of work on its industrial structure, its labor relations, its star system, its narrative conventions. There is an advertising industry and a body of work on its persuasive techniques, its measurement methods, its relation to consumer psychology. There is a defense communications register, with its own scholarly and trade literatures, that studies recruitment, public affairs, and information operations. There is a platform economy literature, increasingly distinct, that studies algorithmic distribution, creator labor, and platformal feedback architectures. Each field has its own canon, its own conferences, its own working assumptions.

The field-by-field organization is institutionally accurate. Hollywood, advertising, defense communications, and platforms are organized as distinct industries, with distinct firms, distinct labor markets in some respects, distinct regulatory regimes, and distinct self-conceptions. A scholar working in any one field is engaging with a real object, and the field’s specialized concepts capture features of that object the other fields do not handle as well.

The field-by-field organization is also analytically inadequate to a wide range of phenomena. The phenomena it handles poorly are exactly the ones that motivate the rubber-hose realism analysis: the migrations of formal vocabulary across the institutional fields, the shared moral physics, the overlapping labor pools, the increasingly common audience. When a recruitment video uses the formal grammar of a music video, when a brand campaign deploys the address mode of a children’s medium, when a platform feed carries open psychological-operations content alongside advertising and entertainment in a single session — the field-by-field organization either misses what is happening or describes it as cross-field “borrowing” that gestures at but does not name the deeper continuity.

¶What “continuum” claims, and what it does not claim

The claim of the state-aesthetic continuum term is that the institutional differentiation of these fields is real as institutions but does not capture what the artifacts do at the level of audience formation. Across the institutional fields, the artifacts share:

  • A common moral-physics regime (rubber-hose realism being the dominant case in late liberalism).
  • A common library of formal techniques (strategic infantilization, affective looping, moral displacement, soft surrealism, consequence-free violence).
  • Overlapping labor pools, drawn from a common aesthetic curriculum.
  • An algorithmic distribution substrate that increasingly carries content from all the fields in shared feeds.
  • A largely common audience, increasingly self-aware of operating across the fields simultaneously.

These shared properties make the fields, for some analytical purposes, a single continuum. They do not abolish the institutional differences. A recruitment video and a Pixar film are still produced under different institutional covers, by different firms, under different legal regimes, for different declared purposes. The continuum claim is that these differences do not predict what the artifacts will do at the level of audience formation as cleanly as the field-by-field organization suggests.

The claim is also not a conspiratorial one. The continuum is not coordinated. There is no central directing intelligence that orchestrates the migrations. The continuum operates through ordinary mechanisms: shared labor markets, shared distribution substrates, shared audience expectations, shared formal vocabularies. This is what makes the continuum stable. A coordinated apparatus would have central points of failure. The state-aesthetic continuum has none, because it is constituted by the circulation of techniques, labor, and audiences rather than by anyone’s directive.

¶What the continuum reading makes visible

Six things the field-by-field reading tends to miss, that the continuum reading makes available.

Migrations of formal vocabulary become trackable. When the rubber-hose moral physics of 1928 Disney shows up, eight decades later, in a 2024 4th PSYOP recruitment video, the field-by-field reading has to treat this as a coincidence, as borrowing, or as a sociological curiosity. The continuum reading treats it as a traceable migration along the continuum: the moral physics was developed in the entertainment position, distributed by the soft-power position, and is now being deployed in the open-PSYOP position by labor that was trained inside all three. The migration is not a coincidence. It is a feature of the apparatus.

The labor market becomes legible as a single pool. Producers of state recruitment content, brand campaigns, and platform-native viral video are increasingly drawn from the same labor pool, often the same individual people across their careers. The skills are continuous; the institutional badges they wear at any given moment are not predictive of the techniques they bring. A continuum reading lets the labor market be analyzed as the unified thing it is, rather than as a series of disconnected fields.

The audience becomes legible as a single audience operating in different modes. The same audience consumes recruitment videos ironically, branded content sincerely, wellness content credulously, and platform-native critique cynically — sometimes within a single feed session. The audience is not divided across the fields; it inhabits a continuum and switches modes within it. Audiences are increasingly self-aware of this fact, which is itself an analytical datum.

Doctrinal acknowledgments become readable as guides to the rest of the continuum. When the open-PSYOP position openly names a technique (memetic warfare, narrative-conflict capability), the naming is doctrinal, but it makes available a vocabulary that the rest of the continuum has been deploying without acknowledgment. Reading doctrine as documentation of the continuum lets analyses of brand and entertainment artifacts borrow vocabulary that those fields have not openly named.

Wartime production lineages remain analytically active. The institutional cooperation between Hollywood and the U.S. military during World War II — Disney’s training films, the studio system’s propaganda contracts, the postwar consultation arrangements — was not a brief interruption of an otherwise commercial industry. It was the moment when the continuum’s institutional shape was clearly visible. The field-by-field reading typically files this history under “exceptional” and moves on. The continuum reading treats it as foundational: the continuum’s labor pool, distribution arrangements, and aesthetic curriculum were stabilized by these wartime cooperations and have not significantly demobilized since.

Audience self-awareness becomes available as a finding. Increasingly, audiences encountering state-aesthetic content recognize the continuum directly — the irony with which the Ghosts in the Machine series was received online is exactly the audience tracking the continuum and reading position-shifts in real time. This audience-level recognition is itself part of the apparatus’s contemporary form. The continuum is now a thing the apparatus partially announces and the audience partially expects. A field-by-field reading cannot well describe this; the continuum reading describes it directly.

¶Positions on the continuum

The compressed account is in the state-aesthetic continuum term. Briefly, the major positions:

  • Commercial entertainment. Declared purpose: pleasure. Formal vocabulary deployed without acknowledged purpose at the level of audience formation.
  • Corporate soft power. Declared purpose: brand identity. Formal vocabulary deployed for instrumental commercial ends.
  • Brand-justice marketing. Declared purpose: institutional alignment with social values. Formal vocabulary deployed in the register of moral commitment.
  • Platform-native creator content. Declared purpose: individual creative expression. Formal vocabulary developed and refined inside the platformal substrate.
  • Recruitment and influence media. Declared purpose: institutional outreach. Formal vocabulary deployed for direct audience-formation purposes the institution acknowledges only obliquely.
  • Open psychological operations. Declared purpose: information operations against adversary populations. Formal vocabulary deployed openly and with doctrinal acknowledgment.

The positions are descriptive of the institutional cover under which an artifact is presented. The same artifact can occupy more than one position simultaneously — a 4th PSYOP recruitment video circulating on platform-native channels under doctrinal influence is operating across recruitment, platform-native creator, and open-PSYOP positions at once.

¶What moves across the continuum

The continuum is not static. Several things move:

Formal techniques. Rubber-hose moral physics from 1920s cartoons; soft-surrealist collage from late-twentieth-century music video; meme-form structures from platform-native creators; strategic-infantilization address modes from children’s media; affective-looping temporal structures from advertising. Each was developed in some position, has migrated to others, and is now widely available across the continuum.

Labor. Workers move between positions across their careers. A creative director who learns the apparatus in advertising moves to a streaming service; a viral creator who learns it on platforms is hired by a defense communications contractor; a former journalist moves to a brand-justice campaign and then to a recruitment shop. The labor pool’s circulation is what stabilizes the continuum even as institutional firms come and go.

Audiences. The same audience is at multiple positions across a day. The continuum reading lets analysis describe the audience’s positional fluency — the skill of switching modes within a feed — as a feature of contemporary audience competence rather than as analytical noise.

Doctrinal vocabulary. When the open-PSYOP position names a capability, the naming becomes available to the other positions. Brand and platform actors increasingly adopt vocabulary that originated in defense communications, sometimes deliberately and sometimes without recognizing the source.

¶Limits of the continuum reading

The continuum reading is powerful but not unlimited. Several places where it is not the right tool:

Institutional analysis at the firm level. When the question is what a particular firm decided to produce, by what processes, under what regulatory and labor conditions, the field-by-field organization of media analysis is more useful. The continuum reading abstracts past these institutional specifics on purpose; the abstraction has costs when the institutional specifics are what is at stake.

Distinguishing intentional from emergent operations. The continuum reading deliberately does not require coordination, and it tends to elide the difference between artifacts produced with explicit influence intent and artifacts produced without it. For some questions — accountability, ethics, regulation — the difference matters, and the continuum reading is not the tool for those questions.

Analyses outside late-liberal contexts. The continuum is calibrated to U.S.-anchored late-liberal media production. Other state-aesthetic configurations exist (Soviet and post-Soviet, Chinese, South Korean and Japanese soft-power, post-colonial state-cultural projects), and their continuums have different shapes. The U.S. continuum is not universal; the term should not be used as if it were.

Single-artifact close reading. The continuum reading is a frame for the apparatus. Close work on one artifact’s specific scenes, dialogue, and choices needs other tools.

These limits are not failures. They are reminders that the continuum is a useful analytical frame for some questions and not for others. Like every frame in cybernetic postliberalism, it is a tool, not a theory of everything.

¶How to use the continuum frame

If you want to apply the continuum frame to a specific artifact, the procedure is in how to trace the state-aesthetic continuum of an image. The short version: identify the artifact’s institutional cover; identify its formal vocabulary; ask whether the vocabulary is farther along the continuum than the cover acknowledges; track the audience’s relation to the gap.

If you want a foundational analysis to start from, the rubber-hose-realism paper itself (Rubber-Hose Realism: From Koko the Clown and Mickey Mouse to the Aesthetic Toolkit of the U.S. 4th Psychological Operations Group) is the worked example that motivated naming the continuum.

¶What this text has not done

This text has not proposed a politics of the continuum. The analytical frame does not, by itself, prescribe what one should do about the apparatus once it has been identified. Different analysts will draw different conclusions: some will read the continuum as a target for direct opposition; some as a substrate to be navigated; some as a phenomenon to be documented; some as a context their own work necessarily operates inside. The frame is upstream of these political choices. Naming the continuum is the precondition for any of them.
