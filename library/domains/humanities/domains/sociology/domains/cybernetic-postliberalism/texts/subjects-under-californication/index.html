Subjects Under Californication: Who the System Produces

2026-03-06

A writer with a large following posts a thread about structural racism. The thread is carefully sourced, emotionally calibrated, and formatted for maximum engagement. It receives thousands of shares. The writer gains followers, credibility, and a sense of having done meaningful work. The structural racism the thread describes continues unaltered. The writer writes another thread.

This is not a story about hypocrisy. The writer’s analysis may be correct. Their engagement may be sincere. What the framework examines is the subject position they occupy — a position produced by the system they are analyzing.

This text examines the specific subject positions that californication generates. For the full conceptual treatments, see the term definitions for the neurotic platformal intellectual, the savior-slave subject, and coherent confusion.

Three positions, one circuit

The framework identifies three interlocking positions — not as moral categories (good person, bad person) but as structural positions within the feedback loop:

The savior-slave subject is the fundamental position. The liberal subject is simultaneously tasked with managing global crises (savior) and structurally incapable of transforming the conditions that produce them (slave). This is cruel optimism in cybernetic form: the fantasy that personal responsibility can address structural conditions is what keeps the subject inside the loop. Collapse is not hidden but narrativized, monetized, and aestheticized — sustained because the system’s feeling rules have made crisis-management into the primary format for a meaningful life.

The neurotic platformal intellectual is the savior-slave subject on platforms specifically. The platform industrializes Michel Foucault’s confession: it incites discourse at scale, processes it as data, and sells the data back as engagement metrics. The NPI operates under dual-audience pressure (followers want authenticity, algorithms want professionalism) and performs expertise under conditions of genuine uncertainty. Under crisis, what gets circulated is not understanding but the affect of understanding — the feeling of having learned, packaged as expertise.

Coherent confusion is the condition both positions operate within. The subject is confused but functional — unable to resolve what is happening, yet still interpreting, still engaging, still producing sensemaking labor. The confusion sustains participation because it never fully resolves and never fully fails. There is always one more thread, one more angle, one more explanation. The interpretive labor IS the governance mechanism.

In “The theory that survived the war”

The analysis of Gramsci’s American reception shows all three positions at work. The organic intellectual — Gramsci’s term for a figure who emerges from struggle to articulate its conditions — was identified and destroyed by the FBI (COINTELPRO) before Gramsci’s theory was available in English. The theory survived. The people it described did not.

Now the organic intellectual is “valuable, fundable, format-compatible.” The neurotic platformal intellectual is what the organic intellectual becomes on platforms: embedded not in class struggle but in platform logic, articulating not class interest but platform interest. The savior-slave position is visible in the organizer who is told they must make themselves legible to settlers — tasked with translating insurgent knowledge into forms the system can process. The coherent confusion is the condition of reading Gramsci in a seminar room funded by the university that contracts with ICE.

“If they let you teach it, it’s already too late.”

In “Citing for containment”

The academic who writes about Stop Cop City occupies all three positions simultaneously. They are the savior-slave subject — personally responsible for bearing witness to state violence, structurally incapable of altering it through their paper. They are the neurotic platformal intellectual — performing expertise calibrated to the abolitionist genre’s feeling rules. And they operate within coherent confusion — the paper is coherent, the situation is not, and the coherence of the paper is what allows the situation to continue unintelligible.

The people who warned against the specific actions that led to the arrests — who drew on the same theorists the paper cites, who used those theorists to navigate rather than narrate — do not appear in the paper. Their refusals are absent. The paper’s coherence requires their erasure.
