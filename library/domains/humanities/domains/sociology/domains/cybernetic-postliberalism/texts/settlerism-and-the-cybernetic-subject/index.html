Settlerism and the Cybernetic Subject

2026-04-27 Settlerism — settler colonialism understood as a continuing structure rather than a finished event — supplies the historical-material substrate the contemporary apparatus runs on. The genres in which subjects are formatted presuppose a settler-cultural imaginary, and the temporal middle is structurally eliminated by the binary the structure offers. The cybernetic subject is a settler subject in a specific structural sense, regardless of biographical specification.

The Energetic Substrate established the substrate cybernetic postliberalism presupposes; Liberalism’s Constitutive Contradictions specified the input the apparatus metabolizes; Genring and Ontogenring named the subject-forming operations; California as Laboratory traced the historical site of iteration; Platformal Feedback Architecture specified the technical-feedback structure. What follows takes up a register that runs through all of these: settlerism — the settler-colonial structure within which the contemporary apparatus has been built and continues to operate.

Three things to specify. First, what settlerism means in its operative sense, distinguished from settler colonialism understood as a finished historical event. Second, how the account of californication relies on the settler-colonial analysis as one of its load-bearing registers — not as moral framing but as historical-material substrate. Third, how the cybernetic subject is a settler subject in a specific structural sense, regardless of the individual subject’s awareness or self-identification.

¶What settlerism is

The term settlerism names settler colonialism understood as a continuing structure rather than a finished event. The foundational article is Patrick Wolfe’s “Settler Colonialism and the Elimination of the Native” (2006), which established the analytical position that has organized the field of settler colonial studies since: settler colonialism is structured by a continuing logic of elimination of the indigenous, where elimination operates through many means (removal, killing, assimilation, narrative-erasure, legal disposal of indigenous standing) and is not concluded by any historical moment but continues to operate as the structural form of governance.

The position has methodological consequences. A history that frames settler colonialism as event (“America was settled,” “the colonies became independent,” “the frontier closed”) makes the elimination operation invisible by treating it as concluded. A structure analysis frames the elimination as continuing: the settler state’s contemporary operations (legal-jurisdictional disposal, infrastructural displacement, economic incorporation, cultural-narrative formatting) are not aftermath but continuation. Eve Tuck and K. Wayne Yang’s “Decolonization Is Not a Metaphor” (2012) makes the corollary argument: decolonization, understood within the settler-colonial frame, has a specific material referent — the repatriation of indigenous land and life — and is not satisfied by metaphorical extensions to other liberatory projects.

Glen Coulthard’s Red Skin, White Masks (2014), Audra Simpson’s Mohawk Interruptus (2014), Leanne Betasamosake Simpson’s As We Have Always Done (2017), Nick Estes’s Our History Is the Future (2019), and Roxanne Dunbar-Ortiz’s An Indigenous Peoples’ History of the United States (2014) are the primary indigenous-critical literature within which settlerism is analyzed. Lorenzo Veracini’s Settler Colonialism: A Theoretical Overview (2010) supplies the comparative-theoretical synthesis. Sylvia Wynter’s analysis of the overrepresentation of Man (2003) is upstream-adjacent: settlerism operates within the conditions of the overrepresentation, and the overrepresentation operates in part through the settler-colonial register.

¶How settlerism bears on the analysis

The reading of californication depends on the settler-colonial substrate in three structurally specific ways.

The land that the apparatus operates on. California, the historical laboratory of the apparatus, is settler-colonial territory: the indigenous peoples of that coast (Ohlone, Tongva, Chumash, Miwok, Pomo, Yurok, and others) were not vacated by the apparatus’s arrival; they were continuously displaced and continue to be displaced. The reading in California as Laboratory — Misiones, Gold Rush, agriculture, Hollywood, Esalen, Silicon Valley, wellness — as one apparatus requires reading them on this substrate. A reading that treated the laboratory’s work as taking place on neutral ground would be missing the substrate that made the rapid iteration possible. That the apparatus could test rapidly because its substrate could be ignored (the Indigenous peoples whose displacement underwrote the rapidity) is a settler-colonial claim about the laboratory’s conditions. Crisis settlerism names how the structure intensifies its frontier-projection when its prior settlements destabilize.

The subjects that the apparatus produces. Genring is structurally adjacent to the settler-colonial operation of producing the indigenous as a fixed type: the Noble Indian, the Mountain Kurd, the Tribal-Authentic figure who provides the settler-cultural imaginary with its access to authenticity precisely by remaining frozen in the position the imaginary requires. The settler-colonial structure produces, as its constitutive outside, the indigenous-as-genre-figure; the settler subject’s self-image as modern, mobile, and futural depends on that figure’s stability. This is what genre coherence names: the settler-narrative integrity requires the indigenous figure to remain in the genre, and any deviation (the indigenous mathematician, the indigenous urban-cosmopolitan, the indigenous filmmaker) registers as a problem for the narrative — the rhetorical structure within which the deviation is then disposed of is what fascist grammar names.

The temporal middle that the apparatus eliminates. The temporal middle — the position of inheriting, continuing, and transforming a tradition without either renewing it wholesale or abandoning it — is the structural position settlerism systematically eliminates. The settler-colonial apparatus offers indigenous subjects two positions: traditional-authentic (frozen in pre-contact form, eliminable as residue) or assimilated-modern (severed from indigenous tradition, eliminable as continuance). The temporal middle — the actual position every living indigenous subject occupies, of inheriting and continuing a tradition that is alive precisely because it changes — is structurally absent from the settler-colonial frame. This is one of the apparatus’s signature operations, recognizable across registers from settler-colonial dispossession to platformal genre-coherence enforcement.

¶The cybernetic subject as settler subject

The cybernetic subject — the subject who self-regulates through the californication-circuit, who routes structural contradiction through personal affect, who participates in the platform’s feedback loops as the loops require — is a settler subject in a specific structural sense, regardless of whether the individual subject is descended from settlers, recently arrived as an immigrant, racially marked as inheritor of the settler position, or any other autobiographical specification.

The claim is structural, not biographical. Several features mark the subject-position as settler-structural.

Operation on land whose prior relations are not registered. The cybernetic subject lives on territory that has been subjected to the elimination operation; the territory’s prior relations (indigenous land-tenure, kinship-place practices, ceremonial obligations, ecological knowledges) are not part of the subject’s relation to the territory. Even subjects who consciously acknowledge the prior relations participate in an everyday infrastructure (housing, transit, food production, water provision) that operates as if the prior relations did not exist. The structural settlerism does not require ideological endorsement; the everyday operation is sufficient.

Subject-formation by genres that presuppose settler coherence. The ontogenric genres in which the cybernetic subject is formatted (the wellness-self, the activist-self, the productive-self, the resilient-self) presuppose a settler-cultural imaginary. The wellness-self, in particular, draws on appropriations of indigenous and non-Western practices (yoga, mindfulness, plant-medicine ceremony) reformatted as personal-developmental tools, with the original relations stripped. This is a continuation of the settler-colonial apparatus’s logic of elimination: the practice is incorporated; the relation that gave the practice its meaning is eliminated. Curiositas, in the Thomist diagnostic, names the disordered cognitive appetite that drives the consumption-incorporation loop.

Affective metabolism that prefers personal management to structural confrontation. The subject’s response to structural contradiction is the californication-circuit: route the contradiction through personal affect, manage the affect, return to functional participation. This is structurally consistent with the settler-cultural reflex of treating structural injustice as a personal-developmental opportunity (the white-fragility workbook, the anti-racism reading group, the personal-acknowledgment land statement) rather than as a demand for structural action that the subject is not in a position to refuse without consequence. Harm governance names the specific institutional form this metabolism takes when it scales: care collapsed into risk management, performed by people who have lost faith in the state but reproduce its administrative logic with each other.

The claim is precise about what it is and is not. It is not the claim that all subjects who participate in the californication-circuit are personally responsible for settler-colonial dispossession in any morally direct sense; that claim would be a moral verdict cybernetic postliberalism rejects. It is the claim that the subject-position the apparatus produces is structurally a settler subject-position in the sense specified above, and that an honest analysis must register this rather than treating the cybernetic subject as a generic late-modern self.

¶What is drawn from indigenous critical thought

Indigenous critical thought is not supplementary material but analytically constitutive. Several specific contributions:

The structural reading of settler colonialism. Wolfe’s article and the broader settler-colonial-studies literature provide the analytical apparatus that makes settlerism a usable category. Without the structure-not-event distinction, the reading of californication’s continuity from the Misiones to the platform would not be available.

The temporal-middle analysis. The temporal middle is drawn directly from Wolfe, Simpson, Coulthard, and Vizenor’s analyses of the position settler colonialism eliminates. The account of how californication operates across temporal registers (the fixed-traditional / assimilated-modern binary that the apparatus offers indigenous and non-indigenous subjects alike) is built on this analysis.

The practice of refusal. Audra Simpson’s analysis of refusal — the practice of declining settler-state recognition in order to preserve the prior political relation — is the most important resource for thinking about what subjects can do when the apparatus’s coherent subject-positions have been metabolized. The closure claim is that no coherent subject-position produces an outside; refusal, as Simpson develops it, is not a coherent subject-position but a relational-political practice that operates otherwise than the subject-position framework. Refusal is one of the practices that may survive metabolization; the closure-text holds open the question without claiming to systematize the answer.

The distinction between metaphor and material. Tuck and Yang’s “Decolonization Is Not a Metaphor” is the foundational corrective for keeping the analysis honest about what its own conceptual moves do and do not produce. Cybernetic postliberalism is a sociology, and its moves are conceptual; the conceptual moves do not, on their own, perform decolonization. Tuck and Yang’s article is a discipline against potential drift into theoretical satisfaction.

¶What follows

With settlerism specified as a continuing structure rather than a finished event, the apparatus’s historical-material substrate is in place. The Operational Closure of Late-Liberal Governance states the structural claim that no coherent subject-position produces an outside under contemporary platformal-energetic conditions. The Ecclesial Recurrence reads californication as recurrent with the medieval Christian pastoral apparatus of which the Misiones are the California-specific instance. Cybernetic Postliberalism in Context positions the analysis relative to adjacent schools of postliberal critique. The settler-colonial register continues to bear on each, even where it is not the explicit topic.
