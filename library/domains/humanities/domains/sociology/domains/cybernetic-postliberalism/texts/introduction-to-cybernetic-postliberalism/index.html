Introduction to Cybernetic Postliberalism

2026-04-27 An orientation to the school: late-liberal governance reproduces itself by formatting structural contradictions as personally manageable affect, and the self-regulation that follows is the governance mechanism. The central concept is californication; the load-bearing structural claim is operational closure.

Cybernetic postliberalism is a school of analytical sociology. It studies how late-liberal governance reproduces itself under conditions of contracting energetic surplus. Its claim is that the apparatus does not persuade its subjects and does not, principally, coerce them — it formats them. Structural contradictions that liberalism cannot resolve are routed through subjects as personally manageable affect, and the self-regulation that follows is itself the governance mechanism. The interpretive labor of those who try to understand or escape the apparatus is the labor that keeps the apparatus stable.

Cybernetic postliberalism asks a third question. The classical questions are how do those in power persuade their subjects to consent (the ideology question) and how does the system coerce subjects when persuasion fails (the violence question). Cybernetic postliberalism asks: how does a system reproduce itself when it can neither persuade nor coerce — when its constitutive promises have become structurally insoluble and the energetic surplus that once funded their partial fulfillment has collapsed? Its central concept, californication, is the answer.

¶What cybernetic postliberalism analyzes

The central object of analysis is the circuit by which structural contradiction becomes personal affect, personal affect becomes content, content becomes engagement, and engagement becomes the substrate of governance. The components of this circuit:

  • Genring and ontogenring, developed at length in Genring and Ontogenring — how genres of affect calibrate what counts as crisis, what counts as resolution, and what feelings are appropriate at each stage; how attribution itself is an ontogenic act that produces both entitlement and anticipation.
  • Feeling rules and emotional labor — the institutional prescriptions governing which emotions count as legitimate responses to which conditions, extended from Hochschild’s workplace finding to the whole of liberal subjectivity.
  • Coherent confusion — the system-level condition in which subjects are confused but functional, producing interpretive labor that sustains the system precisely because it never resolves.
  • The savior-slave subject — the dual subject-position the system produces: personally responsible for managing structural crises, structurally incapable of transforming the conditions that produce them.
  • Zen fascism, harm governance, the neurotic platformal intellectual, and socially ontogenic media — particular formations the system rewards: detachment as ethical credential, care collapsed into risk management, expertise performed under conditions of genuine uncertainty, and media artifacts that distribute survivable templates of selfhood.

The structural conditions that govern the apparatus’s operation — the temporal middle it consumes, the industrial intellectualism it absorbs, and the settlerism that supplies its historical-material substrate (analyzed in Settlerism and the Cybernetic Subject) — frame the circuit’s operation in conditions cybernetic postliberalism takes seriously.

¶Lineage

Cybernetic postliberalism synthesizes traditions that each describe part of the circuit. Foucault on governmentality and confession — how power produces self-governing subjects who incite themselves into subjection. Berlant on cruel optimism, genre, and crisis ordinariness — how subjects maintain attachment to conditions that harm them. Hochschild on feeling rules and emotional labor. Wiener, Beer, and Ashby on cybernetics, the viable system model, requisite variety, operational closure, and ultrastability — how systems maintain stability through distributed feedback without central control. Povinelli on late liberalism and the quasi-event — how governance demands endurance rather than resolution.

Beyond these: Wynter, Mbembe, and Fanon on the racial-colonial register of liberal subject-formation; Han, Brown, Berardi, and Fisher on adjacent left critiques of late-modern subjectivity; Luhmann on systems theory and recursive readability; Benjamin on weak messianic power; and Aquinas on the pre-modern pastoral apparatus that cybernetic postliberalism treats as californication’s prior institutional form (see The Ecclesial Recurrence). The full key-figures list is in the school’s _index.

The school’s distinctive contribution is showing how these threads connect into a single circuit, how platforms made the circuit operative at planetary scale, and how the energetic substrate that funded richer institutional interiors in the prior pastoral apparatus has collapsed — leaving the metabolism distributed onto bodies and platforms with no protected interior.

¶The closure claim

The school’s load-bearing claim is that the circuits are exhaustive. No coherent subject-position produces an outside. Anarchist grammar, primitivist exit, accelerationist hyperengagement, abolitionist construction, melancholic critique, off-grid retreat, and spiritual transcendence are genres within the same metabolism, each producing the affective material the apparatus requires. Late-liberal governance is operationally closed in Niklas Luhmann’s sense — described, not prescribed. Under the conjunction of declining net energy and platformal feedback architecture, no subject-formation produces an exterior, because every “outside” is itself a genre and every genre runs on the same affective infrastructure. The Operational Closure of Late-Liberal Governance develops the claim in detail.

The closure is structural, not pessimistic. Action is possible only as practice — the unmanaged act, the care that does not wait for permission — and even practice is metabolized. Cybernetic postliberalism is honest about this. The analyst writing it is inside the metabolism. So is the reader.

¶Three things cybernetic postliberalism is not

It is not a conspiracy theory. No one is steering. The mechanism is distributed; feedback loops between bodies, platforms, economy, and affect converge without coordination from a center. Platformal Feedback Architecture develops the technical specifics.

It is not a moral verdict on the people inside it. The writer who threads about structural racism, the activist who burns out, the worker who meditates through their housing crisis, the parent who scrolls during dinner — these are subject-positions the system produces, not failures of conscience. Analysis is not indictment.

It is not a political program. Cybernetic postliberalism does not prescribe what should replace late-liberal governance. It is descriptive sociology with a closure claim. What anyone does with the description is their own work.

¶Scope

The empirical scope is contemporary living — late-modern, post-platform — going back into history as far as the analysis of any specific phenomenon requires. It is not bound to any one nation, but its sharpest cases are American, because the United States is where the platform infrastructure was built and where the laboratory California operates. Cybernetic postliberalism presupposes a material claim about declining net energy that lives outside this school, in the EROEI research the school’s _index links under presupposes:. That claim is not part of cybernetic postliberalism; it is what makes cybernetic postliberalism necessary now rather than one descriptive sociology among many. The Energetic Substrate develops the presupposition’s specifics.

¶Where to read next

Each text in this school is written to stand on its own and the texts can be assembled into a monograph by reading in the order given by the school’s featured-texts list in its _index. After this orientation: The Energetic Substrate (the material precondition), Liberalism’s Constitutive Contradictions (the input being metabolized), Genring and Ontogenring (the subject-formation operations), California as Laboratory (the historical site of iteration), Platformal Feedback Architecture (the technical infrastructure), Settlerism and the Cybernetic Subject (the colonial-historical substrate), The Operational Closure of Late-Liberal Governance (the load-bearing structural claim), The Ecclesial Recurrence (the pre-modern pastoral apparatus), Cybernetic Postliberalism in Context (the position relative to adjacent schools), and Methods of Cybernetic-Postliberal Analysis (how to perform the analysis on specific cases).
