Duty of Progressive Encoding

2026-04-07 The obligation on every office-holder to encode completed work so that future holders — or less capable agents — can perform it at lower cost. A general governance duty grounded in the settlement path: every discharge of a work duty should produce an artifact that moves the work one step toward process.

The duty of progressive encoding is the obligation to encode completed work so that it can be reproduced at lower cost. It is a duty in the Hohfeldian sense: the bearer is any office-holder, the correlative party is the institution and its future members, and the normative system is any governance framework that values institutional learning.

¶The five-tuple

Component Value
Bearer Any office-holder who completes work
Position The bearer’s office
Normative system The institution’s governance framework
Required act After completing work, produce an artifact that encodes the method
Correlative party The institution and future holders of the same office

¶Grounding in the settlement path

The settlement path describes how work moves from open inquiry to deterministic process. Each step along the path reduces the judgment required:

Work completed at… Encoding produces… Work settles to…
Inquiry A finding or term definition Derivation
Derivation A procedure or runbook Procedure
Procedure A script or tool Process

The duty of progressive encoding is the obligation to take one step. Not to reach the end — some work is irreducibly inquiry — but to move what CAN be encoded one position along the path.

¶Why this is a duty and not a preference

A preference can be overridden without justification. A duty has a correlative party with a claim. The correlative parties here are:

  • Future office-holders who will inherit a richer or poorer set of encoded methods
  • The institution which accumulates or loses organizational knowledge across successions
  • Less capable agents who could perform the work IF it were encoded, freeing more capable agents for unsettled work

This is the formal content of what Nonaka and Takeuchi (The Knowledge-Creating Company, 1995) call the externalization of tacit knowledge: converting personal knowledge into organizational artifacts. The duty makes this conversion obligatory rather than optional.

¶Connection to closure operators

Each encoding step applies a closure operator that makes implicit knowledge explicit. The closure of an inquiry under its resolution rules is a derivation. The closure of a derivation under procedural encoding is a procedure. The settlement path IS the iterated closure of work under progressively stronger completion rules. The duty of progressive encoding is the obligation to apply the next closure.

¶Discharge

The duty is discharged when the encoding artifact exists and the work it encodes can be performed at the next formality level. Discharge is not the production of the artifact alone (that is execution settlement) but its recognition as useful — meaning settlement requires that the encoding actually captures the method, not merely that a file was created.

¶Failure modes

  • Fire-and-forget — work is completed but no artifact is produced. The next holder starts from scratch.
  • Premature formalization — encoding attempted before the work is understood. Produces brittle artifacts.
  • Encoding without verification — an artifact is produced but never tested against actual work.
