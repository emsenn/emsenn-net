2026-03-20 JurisdictionalVires is a quality Q_vires(φ, A, t) ∈ {0,1} — the quality of an act φ relative to authority A at history t — where Q_vires = 1 (intra vires) iff JV(A, φ, t) ∈ H*_t: all four jurisdiction conditions hold simultaneously. Q_vires = 0 (ultra vires) whenever any condition fails. The defining structure: vires is the natural transformation that evaluates whether φ has a settled path through A's jurisdictional nucleus. It is a quality of the act, not of the authority — the same authority A may be intra vires on one act and ultra vires on another at the same history.

JurisdictionalVires

Formal definition

JurisdictionalVires is the quality Qvires:ActClass×Authority×T→{0,1}Q_{\mathrm{vires}} : \mathrm{ActClass} \times \mathrm{Authority} \times T \to \{0, 1\} defined by:

Qvires(φ,A,t)={1if JV(A,φ,t)∈Ht∗J0otherwiseQ_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A, t) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \in H^{*\mathcal{J}}_t \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}

where JV(A,φ,t)=RJ(pos(A,t),φ,V(t,φ))\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) = R_{\mathcal{J}}(\mathrm{pos}(A, t), \varphi, V(t, \varphi)) is the jurisdictional validity proposition from Jurisdiction.

Qvires=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}} = 1 is called intra vires (“within the powers”). Qvires=0Q_{\mathrm{vires}} = 0 is called ultra vires (“beyond the powers”).

Intra vires iff all four hold simultaneously:

  1. t∈TJt \in T_J — territorial/temporal scope: the act occurs within the jurisdiction’s history sub-site
  2. φ∈HJ\varphi \in H_J — subject-matter scope: the act is of a type within the jurisdiction’s sub-fiber
  3. V(t,φ)=⊤V(t, \varphi) = \top — validity condition: the act satisfies the jurisdiction’s specific validity predicate
  4. pos(A,t)∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) — positional authority: AA actually holds the relevant Hohfeldian Power at tt

Four ultra vires failure modes. Each corresponds to one condition’s failure:

Failure Formal condition Name
t∉TJt \notin T_J Territorial/temporal scope fails Ultra vires by territory
φ∉HJ\varphi \notin H_J Subject-matter scope fails Ultra vires by subject-matter
V(t,φ)=⊥V(t, \varphi) = \bot Validity condition fails Ultra vires by validity
pos(A,t)∉Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A, t) \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) Positional authority unsettled Ultra vires by want of authority

These are not mutually exclusive: an act can fail on multiple conditions simultaneously.

JurisdictionalVires vs. excess

JurisdictionalVires measures whether an act is within AA’s full jurisdiction J\mathcal{J}. JurisdictionalExcess is the condition of being intra vires within J\mathcal{J} but outside an officer’s personal sub-jurisdiction Z=J∣BZ = \mathcal{J}|_B:

JurisdictionalExcess(B,φ,t)  ⟺  Qvires(φ,A,t)=1  ∧  Qvires(φ,B,t)=0\mathrm{JurisdictionalExcess}(B, \varphi, t) \iff Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A, t) = 1 \;\wedge\; Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, B, t) = 0

JurisdictionalExcess is insubordination, not invalidity. An act in excess of sub-jurisdiction creates valid duties within J\mathcal{J} but BB violated their standing orders in issuing it. An ultra vires act creates no duties at all — it is a dead letter.

Nuclear reading

Using the nuclear axioms for σt\sigma_t and Δt\Delta_t on HtH_t, and the definition JV(A,φ,t):=pos(A,t)∧φ\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) := \mathrm{pos}(A, t) \wedge \varphi from Jurisdiction:

Definition (intra vires and ultra vires). Qvires(φ,A,t)=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A, t) = 1 iff JV(A,φ,t)∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t). Qvires(φ,A,t)=0Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A, t) = 0 otherwise. This is a definition, naming the fixed-point condition.

Proposition 1 (ultra vires acts remain in H_t but not in Fix(σ_t)). For any φ∈Ht\varphi \in H_t, the extensiveness axiom φ≤σt(φ)\varphi \leq \sigma_t(\varphi) (see Saturation Nucleus) gives σt(φ)≥φ\sigma_t(\varphi) \geq \varphi, so φ\varphi is always a valid element of HtH_t. The condition Qvires=0Q_{\mathrm{vires}} = 0 does not remove φ\varphi from HtH_t; it asserts σt(φ)>φ\sigma_t(\varphi) > \varphi, i.e., φ\varphi is not yet saturated. The act exists in the fiber as an unsettled element.

Proof. Extensiveness: a≤σt(a)a \leq \sigma_t(a) for all a∈Hta \in H_t. So φ∈Ht\varphi \in H_t regardless of the value of σt(φ)\sigma_t(\varphi). The membership condition φ∈Fix(σt)\varphi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) is the additional requirement σt(φ)=φ\sigma_t(\varphi) = \varphi, which may or may not hold. □\square

Proposition 2 (intra vires conjunction). If Qvires(φ1,A,t)=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi_1, A, t) = 1 and Qvires(φ2,A,t)=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi_2, A, t) = 1, then Qvires(φ1∧φ2,A,t)=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi_1 \wedge \varphi_2, A, t) = 1, provided pos(A,t)∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t).

Proof. By hypothesis and definition, JV(A,φ1,t)=pos(A,t)∧φ1∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi_1, t) = \mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi_1 \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) and JV(A,φ2,t)=pos(A,t)∧φ2∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi_2, t) = \mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi_2 \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t). By meet-preservation of σt\sigma_t (see Meet Preservation):

σt((pos(A,t)∧φ1)∧(pos(A,t)∧φ2))=σt(pos(A,t)∧φ1)∧σt(pos(A,t)∧φ2).\sigma_t((\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi_1) \wedge (\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi_2)) = \sigma_t(\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi_1) \wedge \sigma_t(\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi_2).

Since both terms on the right equal themselves, the meet equals (pos(A,t)∧φ1)∧(pos(A,t)∧φ2)=pos(A,t)∧(φ1∧φ2)=JV(A,φ1∧φ2,t)(\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi_1) \wedge (\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi_2) = \mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge (\varphi_1 \wedge \varphi_2) = \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi_1 \wedge \varphi_2, t). So JV(A,φ1∧φ2,t)∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi_1 \wedge \varphi_2, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t), giving Qvires(φ1∧φ2,A,t)=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi_1 \wedge \varphi_2, A, t) = 1. □\square

Proposition 3 (idempotence: vires quality does not iterate). For any φ∈Ht\varphi \in H_t, σt(σt(φ))=σt(φ)\sigma_t(\sigma_t(\varphi)) = \sigma_t(\varphi) (see Idempotence). Consequently: if Qvires(φ,A,t)=0Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A, t) = 0, applying σt\sigma_t once produces σt(φ)\sigma_t(\varphi); applying it again produces the same element. The nucleus does not “creep” toward φ\varphi over multiple applications — the gap between φ\varphi and σt(φ)\sigma_t(\varphi) is fully resolved in one step, and the result is stable.

Proof. Direct application of the idempotence axiom to σt(φ)∈Ht\sigma_t(\varphi) \in H_t: σt(σt(φ))=σt(φ)\sigma_t(\sigma_t(\varphi)) = \sigma_t(\varphi). □\square

What is not derivable from the nuclear axioms alone. The scope boundary for authority AA — which elements of HtH_t are in HtJH^J_t (within AA’s subject-matter and territorial scope) and which are outside it — is not determined by the axioms for σt\sigma_t and Δt\Delta_t. Those axioms apply uniformly to all elements of HtH_t. The claim that AA’s acts can produce settled elements only within HtJH^J_t requires an additional scope-boundary axiom specifying the structural domain of AA’s constitutive rule. Without that axiom, the nuclear axioms alone do not establish the ultra vires barrier. Any nuclear reading that treats “outside scope” as a derivable consequence of σt\sigma_t alone is incorrect.

Remark (transfer-stability of vires quality). Whether JV(A,φ,t)∈Fix(Δt)\mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) holds — that is, whether the intra vires condition is transfer-stable — depends on whether for every s⊥ts \perp t there exists b∈Hs⋆tb \in H_{s \star t} with H(is,t)(b)=JV(A,φ,t)H(i_{s,t})(b) = \mathrm{JV}(A, \varphi, t) (see Transfer Nucleus). The commutation axiom σt∘Δt=Δt∘σt\sigma_t \circ \Delta_t = \Delta_t \circ \sigma_t (see Commutation) ensures these conditions are compatible. But whether transfer-stability holds for a specific JV\mathrm{JV} requires knowing the extension structure of the fiber bundle, which is not a consequence of the nuclear axioms alone.

JurisdictionalVires and normative consequences

Intra vires, good outcome: the duty is created; the target is bound; accountability attaches to AA for the instruction.

Intra vires, bad outcome (excess): the duty is created (valid within J\mathcal{J}) but BB has exceeded ZZ; BB acquires a duty to the principal for the excess. The third party (duty-target) is still bound.

Ultra vires: no duty is created; the instruction is a dead letter; AA may have breached a duty (to not issue ultra vires instructions); depending on the normative system, the target party may be under no obligation to obey and may have a privilege to disregard.

Open questions

  • Whether QviresQ_{\mathrm{vires}} is monotone in authority hierarchy: if A1≺A2A_1 \prec A_2 (A_1 is subordinate to A_2) and Qvires(φ,A1,t)=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A_1, t) = 1, does Qvires(φ,A2,t)=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A_2, t) = 1? This would mean superior authorities are always at least as broadly empowered as subordinates — not obviously true if subject-matter jurisdiction is partitioned rather than nested.
  • Whether a partially ultra vires act can be severed: if φ=φ1∧φ2\varphi = \varphi_1 \wedge \varphi_2 with Qvires(φ1,A,t)=1Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi_1, A, t) = 1 and Qvires(φ2,A,t)=0Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi_2, A, t) = 0, is φ1\varphi_1 a valid intra vires instruction independently? This is the severability doctrine; it requires the Heyting algebra to support decomposition of instructions.
