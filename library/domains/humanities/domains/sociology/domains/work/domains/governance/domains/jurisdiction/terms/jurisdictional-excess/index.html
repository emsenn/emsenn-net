2026-03-20 JurisdictionalExcess is a triple (B, J, φ) — an officer B, a full jurisdiction J = (A, T_J, H_J, V), and an act φ — where Q_vires(φ, A, t) = 1 (φ is intra vires within J) but Q_vires(φ, B, t) = 0 (φ falls outside B's personal sub-jurisdiction Z = J|_B). The defining structure: excess is the gap between the full jurisdiction's nuclear domain and the officer's delegated sub-domain. The act is valid — it creates duties — but B has exceeded the scope of their authority, acquiring a duty to the principal for the transgression.

JurisdictionalExcess

Formal definition

JurisdictionalExcess is the triple (B,J,φ)(B, \mathcal{J}, \varphi):

(B:Officer,  J=(A,TJ,HJ,V):Jurisdiction,  φ∈Ht)(B : \mathrm{Officer},\; \mathcal{J} = (A, T_J, H_J, V) : \mathrm{Jurisdiction},\; \varphi \in H_t)

where the excess condition holds:

Qvires(φ,A,t)=1  ∧  Qvires(φ,B,t)=0Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A, t) = 1 \;\wedge\; Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, B, t) = 0

φ\varphi is intra vires within the full jurisdiction J\mathcal{J} but outside BB’s personal sub-jurisdiction Z=J∣BZ = \mathcal{J}|_B.

Sub-jurisdiction. Z=J∣B=(B,TJ∩TB,HJ∩HB,V∣TB×HB)Z = \mathcal{J}|_B = (B, T_J \cap T_B, H_J \cap H_B, V|_{T_B \times H_B}) is the restriction of J\mathcal{J} to BB’s temporal scope TBT_B and fiber scope HBH_B, from Jurisdiction §Delegation. BB holds second-quartet Hohfeldian Powers within ZZ and lacks them outside it, even within J\mathcal{J}.

Three ways BB acts in excess:

  1. Territorial excess: t∈TJt \in T_J but t∉TBt \notin T_B — BB issues instructions outside their temporal or geographic window (e.g., a duty officer who acts during another officer’s watch period)
  2. Subject-matter excess: φ∈HJ\varphi \in H_J but φ∉HB\varphi \notin H_B — BB acts on a type of matter outside their delegated subject-matter scope (e.g., a logistics officer issuing operational orders)
  3. Validity excess: V(t,φ)=⊤V(t, \varphi) = \top but V∣TB×HB(t,φ)=⊥V|_{T_B \times H_B}(t, \varphi) = \bot — φ\varphi satisfies J\mathcal{J}’s validity predicate but fails BB’s sub-jurisdiction validity predicate (e.g., an act that requires principal’s countersignature)

JurisdictionalExcess vs. ultra vires

Qvires(φ,A,t)Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A, t) Qvires(φ,B,t)Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, B, t) Duty created? Violation
Intra vires 1 1 Yes None
JurisdictionalExcess 1 0 Yes (within J\mathcal{J}) Insubordination
Ultra vires 0 0 No Void act

The key asymmetry: excess creates valid duties. The third party receiving BB’s instruction is bound — their duty arises from J\mathcal{J}, not from ZZ. What BB has violated is BB’s duty to the principal AA: BB is unauthorized, not invalid.

This is why “I was just following orders” and “the order was unauthorized” are different normative situations: if BB’s order was in excess, it creates a duty in the target; if it was ultra vires, it creates no duty. The target who obeys an excess order is not acting wrongly toward J\mathcal{J}; the target who obeys an ultra vires order may be.

Nuclear reading

The excess condition involves two fiber elements in HtH_t: pos(A,t)\mathrm{pos}(A, t) (the positional authority of the full jurisdiction holder AA) and pos(B,t)\mathrm{pos}(B, t) (the positional authority of the delegate BB within sub-jurisdiction ZZ). Both are propositions in the fiber Heyting algebra.

Definition (excess in terms of Fix(σ_t)). The excess condition Qvires(φ,A,t)=1∧Qvires(φ,B,t)=0Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, A, t) = 1 \wedge Q_{\mathrm{vires}}(\varphi, B, t) = 0 translates, using the definition from JurisdictionalVires, to:

pos(A,t)∧φ∈Fix(σt)andpos(B,t)∧φ∉Fix(σt).\mathrm{pos}(A, t) \wedge \varphi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) \quad \text{and} \quad \mathrm{pos}(B, t) \wedge \varphi \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t).

This is a definition of the excess condition at the nuclear level; it does not require additional axioms to state.

Proposition 1 (excess is compatible with σ_t axioms). The simultaneous conditions pos(A,t)∧φ∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) and pos(B,t)∧φ∉Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(B,t) \wedge \varphi \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) are logically consistent with the nuclear axioms for σt\sigma_t, provided pos(B,t)∧φ<pos(A,t)∧φ\mathrm{pos}(B, t) \wedge \varphi < \mathrm{pos}(A, t) \wedge \varphi in HtH_t.

Proof. The meet-preservation axiom says σt(a∧b)=σt(a)∧σt(b)\sigma_t(a \wedge b) = \sigma_t(a) \wedge \sigma_t(b) for all a,b∈Hta, b \in H_t (see Meet Preservation). Suppose pos(A,t)∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) and φ∈Fix(σt)\varphi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t); then pos(A,t)∧φ∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) (by meet-preservation). Now suppose pos(B,t)<pos(A,t)\mathrm{pos}(B, t) < \mathrm{pos}(A, t) (the delegate’s positional authority is strictly weaker) and pos(B,t)∉Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(B, t) \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t), so σt(pos(B,t))>pos(B,t)\sigma_t(\mathrm{pos}(B, t)) > \mathrm{pos}(B, t). Then:

σt(pos(B,t)∧φ)=σt(pos(B,t))∧σt(φ)=σt(pos(B,t))∧φ>pos(B,t)∧φ,\sigma_t(\mathrm{pos}(B,t) \wedge \varphi) = \sigma_t(\mathrm{pos}(B,t)) \wedge \sigma_t(\varphi) = \sigma_t(\mathrm{pos}(B,t)) \wedge \varphi > \mathrm{pos}(B,t) \wedge \varphi,

so pos(B,t)∧φ∉Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(B,t) \wedge \varphi \notin \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t). The two conditions are simultaneously satisfiable. □\square

Proposition 2 (Fix(σ_t) is closed under the meet of valid elements). If pos(A,t)∧φ∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t), then for any ψ∈Fix(σt)\psi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t), (pos(A,t)∧φ)∧ψ∈Fix(σt)(\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi) \wedge \psi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t).

Proof. Direct application of meet-preservation: σt((pos(A,t)∧φ)∧ψ)=σt(pos(A,t)∧φ)∧σt(ψ)=(pos(A,t)∧φ)∧ψ\sigma_t((\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi) \wedge \psi) = \sigma_t(\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi) \wedge \sigma_t(\psi) = (\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi) \wedge \psi. □\square

This means: valid acts within J\mathcal{J} remain valid when conjoined with other settled propositions. The excess act’s settled status within J\mathcal{J} is robust under conjunction with settled facts.

What is not derivable from the nuclear axioms alone. The claim that an excess act “creates a valid duty” — i.e., that pos(A,t)∧φ∈Fix(σt)\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) implies a duty for the target party — is not a consequence of the nuclear axioms. The nuclear axioms tell us about the closure structure of Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t); they do not interpret elements of Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) as duties. The duty-creation interpretation requires an additional axiom connecting settled propositions in particular sub-fibers to Hohfeldian normative positions. This is not derivable and must be stipulated.

Similarly, the claim that BB acquires a new duty to AA for the excess is not derivable from the nuclear axioms. It requires the additional normative structure specifying that violation of a delegated scope constraint generates accountability obligations. The nuclear axioms establish only the fixed-point structure.

Remark (the excess fact in H_t requires scope-boundary axiom).* The expression Exc(B,φ,t)\mathrm{Exc}(B, \varphi, t) — the excess as a settled fiber element — can be defined as pos(A,t)∧φ∧¬(pos(B,t)∧φ)\mathrm{pos}(A,t) \wedge \varphi \wedge \neg(\mathrm{pos}(B,t) \wedge \varphi) in the Heyting algebra (using Heyting negation ¬\neg). That this element belongs to Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) given the hypotheses of Proposition 1 would follow from meet-preservation only if ¬(pos(B,t)∧φ)∈Fix(σt)\neg(\mathrm{pos}(B,t) \wedge \varphi) \in \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t). Whether the nucleus commutes with Heyting negation in this way is not guaranteed by the three nuclear axioms (extensiveness, idempotence, meet-preservation); it requires an additional property. This is an open point.

JurisdictionalExcess and judgment

In the Judgment structure, the officer’s zone ZZ is the sub-jurisdiction within which discretionary authority is exercised. An act α∉Z\alpha \notin Z but α∈J\alpha \in \mathcal{J} is excess: the officer made a judgment call outside their zone. The act may have been correct (the right outcome for the situation) while still being insubordinate (outside the zone). Correctness of outcome and authorization of process are separate assessments.

Open questions

  • Whether excess is a continuous quality rather than binary: whether the degree to which φ\varphi falls outside ZZ (how far outside TBT_B or HBH_B) constitutes a graded violation, or whether all excess is equally insubordinate regardless of degree.
  • Whether ratification — a principal AA subsequently endorsing BB’s excess act — retroactively converts the excess into an intra vires act: whether σtZ\sigma^Z_t can be retroactively applied with a new domain that includes φ\varphi, or whether the ratification creates a new act rather than validating the old one.
