Custody

2026-03-19 A Custody is a triple (H, O, [t₀,t₁)) — a holder H who possesses an object O during a bounded interval [t₀,t₁) with a duty of care for O but without ownership of O. The defining structure: possession without title; the holder may use O only as necessary to fulfill the custodial purpose; accountability for O's condition runs from t₀ to t₁. The duty of care is preservation of O's settlement: the holder maintains O's condition through the interval and returns it at t₁ with the same settlement profile it had at t₀.

¶Formal definition

A Custody is a triple C=(H,O,W)\mathcal{C} = (H, O, W):

C=(H:Holder,  O:Object,  W=[t0,t1):Interval)\mathcal{C} = (H : \mathrm{Holder},\; O : \mathrm{Object},\; W = [t_0, t_1) : \mathrm{Interval})

with conditions:

  • HH has possession of OO during WW: OO is physically or constructively within HH’s control
  • HH does not have ownership of OO: title to OO remains with the owner Ω≠H\Omega \neq H
  • HH has a duty of care for OO during WW: HH must preserve OO’s condition and return or deliver it at t1t_1
  • HH’s use of OO is purpose-bounded: HH may use OO only to the extent required by the custodial purpose

Five invariants. C\mathcal{C} is a custody iff it satisfies:

  1. Possession without title: HH controls OO during WW; title stays with Ω\Omega. This is the constitutive condition. If HH held title, the relationship would be ownership or sale. If HH had no control, there would be no custody.

  2. Duty of care: HH owes Ω\Omega (or the eventual recipient) a duty to maintain OO’s condition during WW. The standard of care varies: a bailee for hire is held to a higher standard than a gratuitous bailee; a professional custodian (carrier, warehouseman) is held to the standard of their trade. Formally: the predication cell recording OO’s condition must reach the same affirming settlement at t1t_1 as it had at t0t_0 — the object returned must be in the condition received (identity preservation).

  3. Purpose boundedness: HH’s use of OO during WW is limited to the custodial purpose. A carrier may stow cargo; they may not sell it. A trustee may invest trust property; they may not use it for personal benefit. A bailee may store an item; they may not consume it. Any use exceeding the custodial purpose is conversion.

  4. Temporal boundedness: custody is defined over W=[t0,t1)W = [t_0, t_1). At t0t_0, custody begins (typically by formal transfer of possession). At t1t_1, custody ends (by delivery, return, or legal discharge). During WW, accountability for OO’s condition is HH’s; outside WW, it is not.

  5. Return or delivery obligation: HH is obligated to return OO to Ω\Omega or deliver it to a designated third party at or before t1t_1. The obligation is not merely to preserve OO but to complete the custody by relinquishing it.

¶The bailment structure

In common law, custody is formally the bailment — the delivery of personal property by one person (bailor) to another (bailee) for a specific purpose, with a contract (express or implied) that the purpose will be fulfilled and the property returned or disposed of according to the bailor’s directions.

The bailment taxonomy:

Type For whose benefit Duty of care Example
Gratuitous bailment for bailor Bailor only Slight care Leaving item with friend
Gratuitous bailment for bailee Bailee only Great care Borrowing a tool
Mutual benefit bailment Both Ordinary/reasonable care Carrier, warehouseman

All three share the custody triple (H,O,W)(H, O, W) with the same five invariants. The duty of care standard varies; the structural relationship does not.

¶Custody vs. ownership vs. adjacent relations

Relation Title with Possession with Use bounded? Return obligation?
Custody/Bailment Ω\Omega (owner) HH (holder) Yes — purpose Yes
Ownership HH HH No No
Lease/Hire Ω\Omega HH Partially — term Yes
Pledge/Pawn Ω\Omega HH (creditor) No use Yes (on redemption)
Lien Ω\Omega HH (lien holder) No use Yes (on satisfaction)
Trust HH (trustee — legal) HH Yes — beneficiaries Yes (to beneficiaries)

The trust is the deepest form of custody: the trustee holds legal title and possession, but the beneficial interest belongs to others. The trustee’s custody is permanent (not merely transient) and involves a fiduciary duty more stringent than ordinary bailment.

¶In the cargo context

The cargo custody during transit is a mutual-benefit bailment:

  • HH = the carrier (vessel operator)
  • OO = the cargo
  • WW = the voyage (from loading to discharge)
  • Ω\Omega = the shipper/consignee

The carrier’s duty of care is the maritime law standard: “properly and carefully load, handle, stow, carry, keep, care for, and discharge the goods” (Hague-Visby Rules, Art. III Rule 2). Any shortfall is a breach of the custodial duty of care. The cargo manifest is the formal record of what entered custody (t0t_0) and what must exit (t1t_1).

¶Settlement reading: possession as preservation

Custody is the institutional encoding of settlement preservation at the object level: the holder maintains OO’s settlement through the custodial interval, returning it at t1t_1 in the same settlement state in which it was received at t0t_0.

Possession as fiber-algebra-level preservation. During W=[t0,t1)W = [t_0, t_1), the holder HH maintains OO’s value in the predication fiber algebra at the same position it occupied at t0t_0. The duty of care is the obligation to keep the witnessings about OO’s condition affirming on the relevant predicates, not to introduce new witnessings that would shift OO’s settlement (toward damage, depletion, alteration).

Ownership vs. custody. The distinction between the owner and the holder is a distinction of authority over the framework’s structure:

  • The owner holds authority over the structural conditions defining what OO’s settlement means — what counts as the object being “in good condition,” what counts as a use rather than a destruction, what counts as a return. The owner can alter these structural conditions; this is the power that title confers.
  • The holder has only preservation authority: the holder can maintain OO’s settlement under the structural conditions as already defined, but cannot alter those conditions. Possession without title is settlement-preservation without structure-defining authority.

A transfer of title is a transfer of structure-defining authority — the new owner can alter what counts. A custodial arrangement transfers only preservation capability. This is why conversion (using the object beyond the custodial purpose) is not a failure of settlement-preservation but an exercise of structure-defining authority without title: the holder is acting as if they had the power to change what OO’s use counts as, which is the owner’s exclusive power.

Return condition as settlement restoration. The return obligation — that OO must be returned at t1t_1 with the same condition as received at t0t_0 — is the requirement that OO’s settlement at t1t_1 equals its settlement at t0t_0. The witnessings about OO must yield the same affirming-settlement profile at the end of custody as they did at the beginning. The holder cannot return OO in a degraded settlement state (for instance, with new witnessings of damage that the original owner’s settlement did not include). Such a degradation would be a breach of the duty of care — the holder failed to maintain OO’s settlement.

The identity preservation condition from Invariant 2 is the formal expression of this: the predication cell recording OO’s condition must reach the same affirming settlement at the return as at the entry.

¶Open questions

  • Whether custody admits delegation — whether the holder HH can sub-bail OO to a third party H′H' without the bailor’s consent, and under what conditions this transfers the duty of care from HH to H′H' or creates joint and several liability.
  • Whether data (digital objects) admit a coherent custody structure — whether possessing a copy of a file is “possession” in the custodial sense, or whether custody of digital objects requires a different formal treatment.
  • The formal relationship between custody and the on-call officer’s reachability obligation — whether “being available” is a form of custody of one’s own attention and professional capacity for the benefit of the activating party.
