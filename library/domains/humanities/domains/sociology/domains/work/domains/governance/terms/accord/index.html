Accord

2026-03-19 An Accord is a sheaf F: T^op → Set over the history site — the type of observations. A section F(t) at history t is an Observation. The sheaf condition says local observations at different histories cohere into global ones.

¶Formal definition

An Accord is a sheaf over the history site:

F:Top→SetF : T^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}

satisfying the sheaf condition for the commutation topology JJ: for every covering sieve S∈J(t)S \in J(t), every matching family {xs∈F(s)}s∈S\{x_s \in F(s)\}_{s \in S} has a unique amalgamation x∈F(t)x \in F(t) with F(i)(x)=xsF(i)(x) = x_s for all i:s→ti : s \to t in SS.

The sections of FF:

  • A local section at tt is an element x∈F(t)x \in F(t) — an Observation of type FF at history tt
  • A global section is an element of Γ(F)=F(1)=lim⁡TopF\Gamma(F) = F(\mathbf{1}) = \lim_{T^{\mathrm{op}}} F — a compatible family {xt∈F(t)}t∈T\{x_t \in F(t)\}_{t \in T} cohere across all restriction maps

Restriction maps. For each morphism f:s→tf : s \to t in TT (prefix extension s≤ts \leq t), the restriction F(f):F(t)→F(s)F(f) : F(t) \to F(s) sends a section at tt to its restriction at ss. An Observation at tt restricts backward consistently.

Accord as type. An Accord FF is the type of which Observations are terms. Multiple observations of the same Accord at the same history tt are multiple elements of F(t)F(t). The Accord defines what kind of evidence is being accumulated; the observations are its instances.

¶What this is

Every claim in a relational system — every Proposition in HtH_t — is grounded by looking for an Observation of the appropriate Accord type that backs it. The nucleus σt\sigma_t evaluates a Proposition by checking whether it is consistent with the accumulated sections across all histories s≤ts \leq t. Settling (σt(P)=P\sigma_t(P) = P) means the Proposition is already as large as its Accord-backed evidence forces it to be.

The name: an accord is an agreement — a coherent arrangement in which local data at different histories are in agreement (satisfy the matching family condition). When observations “accord” with one another, they cohere into a global picture.

¶In a rule-based normative system

In a rule-based normative system, Accords are the typed observation spaces from which evidence is drawn:

  • A version-controlled history is an Accord over the history monoid: each revision is a section recording a snapshot of the system state
  • A specification file is an Accord section: an observation of type “spec content” at a particular history
  • A session log entry is a section of the memory Accord at the session history

Different Accords are different types of evidence. The grounding claim evaluation procedure checks, for each claim, which Accord it appeals to and whether a section of that Accord at the relevant history backs the claim.

¶Open questions

  • Whether the correct target category for Accords is Set\mathbf{Set}, HA\mathbf{HA}, or the category of nuclear algebras — the answer determines whether Accord sections are raw data or already-structured propositions.
  • Whether every HtH_t is itself the sections of some Accord — whether the fiber doctrine is a functor Top→HAT^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{HA} that is a sheaf.
