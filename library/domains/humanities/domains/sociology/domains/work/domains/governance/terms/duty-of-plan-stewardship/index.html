Duty of Plan Stewardship

2026-04-07 The obligation on the owner of a plan to ensure it converges toward its goal — maintaining tasks, tracking dependencies, surfacing blockers, and reporting progress. Distinct from the duty of execution: the steward ensures the work gets done, not that any specific task is performed.

The duty of plan stewardship is the obligation on the owner of a plan to ensure it converges toward its goal. The steward does not necessarily DO the work — the steward ensures the work gets done, by maintaining the plan’s task list, tracking dependencies, surfacing blockers, and reporting progress.

¶The five-tuple

Component Value
Bearer The office designated as the plan’s owner
Position The owning office
Normative system The governance framework governing the plan
Required act Maintain tasks, track dependencies, surface blockers, converge toward definition-of-done
Correlative party The containing project and the goal it serves

¶Distinction from the duty of execution

The duty of execution applies to individual tasks. Plan stewardship applies to the plan as a whole. A plan steward may delegate every task to other offices and execute none personally — the duty is to ensure convergence, not to perform work. This is the distinction between operational accountability (did the plan succeed?) and task accountability (did this specific task get done?).

¶Discharge

Discharged when the plan’s definition-of-done is met, or the plan is explicitly abandoned with recorded reasons. A plan that lingers with no events is evidence of non-discharge — the steward is not maintaining it.
