2026-03-20 An Estoppel is a quadruple (Q, R, ρ, δ) — a party Q whose position is being estopped, a relying party R who changed their position based on Q's prior representation ρ, and the detriment δ R would suffer if Q were now permitted to assert their actual position. The defining structure: estoppel does not extinguish Q's right (contrast with waiver) but blocks the path from Q's right C ∈ H_{t*} to the settled layer H*_{t*} within this forum — the forum's σ-nucleus refuses to process Q's claim because the contextual condition (R's reliance to their detriment) makes settlement of C inequitable. Formally: a nucleus-blocking condition imposed by the forum's site topology J — estoppel is a local sieve that prevents Q's claim from covering, not a retraction of the claim from the fiber.

Estoppel

Formal definition

An Estoppel is a quadruple E=(Q,R,ρ,δ)\mathcal{E} = (Q, R, \rho, \delta):

E=(Q:EstoppedParty,  R:RelyingParty,  ρ∈Htρ,  δ∈Htδ)\mathcal{E} = (Q : \mathrm{EstoppedParty},\; R : \mathrm{RelyingParty},\; \rho \in H_{t_\rho},\; \delta \in H_{t_\delta})

where:

  • QQ is the estopped party — the party whose representation or conduct gave rise to the estoppel; after estoppel is established, QQ cannot assert a position inconsistent with ρ\rho in the current forum
  • RR is the relying party — the party who relied on QQ’s representation ρ\rho and changed their position as a result; RR is the equitable beneficiary of the estoppel
  • ρ∈Htρ\rho \in H_{t_\rho} is the representation — the proposition that QQ asserted, represented, or stood by at time tρt_\rho; may be an explicit statement, a course of conduct, or a silence where there was a duty to speak
  • δ∈Htδ\delta \in H_{t_\delta} is the detriment — the harm that RR would suffer if QQ were now permitted to assert a position inconsistent with ρ\rho; the detriment must be real and caused by RR’s reliance on ρ\rho

The estoppel condition: the forum JJ’s σ-nucleus refuses to settle QQ’s inconsistent claim CC while the estoppel holds. In formal terms: the forum’s site topology JJ does not contain any covering sieve S∈J(t)S \in J(t) that would make CC locally valid — the local sieve generated by QQ’s inconsistent claim fails to cover, because JJ treats the combination of ρ\rho and CC as incompatible.

Four invariants. E\mathcal{E} is a valid estoppel iff:

  1. Representation: QQ made a representation ρ\rho — a statement of fact, a course of conduct, a silence where there was a duty to speak, or a taking of a position in prior proceedings. The representation is a proposition ρ∈Htρ\rho \in H_{t_\rho} that QQ effectively placed in the relational context of the interaction with RR.

  2. Reliance: RR acted on ρ\rho — RR changed their position (entered a contract, incurred expenses, abstained from acquiring alternate protection) in reasonable reliance on ρ\rho. The reliance must be reasonable: RR cannot claim estoppel if RR knew or should have known that ρ\rho was incorrect.

  3. Detriment: δ≠0\delta \neq 0 — if QQ is now permitted to contradict ρ\rho, RR will suffer a detriment that would not have occurred but for RR’s reliance on ρ\rho. Detriment is the causal link between reliance and harm: RR relied, and because of that reliance, RR would be worse off if QQ’s contradiction is allowed.

  4. Inequity: it would be inequitable — inconsistent with the forum’s JJ-topology — to permit QQ to assert a position inconsistent with ρ\rho given RR’s reliance and detriment. The inequity condition is the forum-relative judgment: whether JJ recognizes this combination of representation, reliance, and detriment as sufficient to block QQ’s claim.

Estoppel vs. waiver: the key distinction

Estoppel and waiver both result in a party being unable to assert a right, but they are structurally different:

Dimension Waiver Estoppel
What happens to the right CC is driven to ⊥\bot (extinguished) CC remains in Ht∗H_{t^*}; only the path to Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*} is blocked
Initiator QQ (right-holder) voluntarily relinquishes Forum blocks QQ’s assertion on equitable grounds
Third-party reliance Not required Required
Revocability Generally irrevocable once made Lapses if RR’s detriment is remedied
Intent QQ must intend to relinquish QQ’s intent is irrelevant; focus is on RR’s reliance
Math Section retraction (C→⊥C \to \bot) Nucleus blocking (JJ refuses to cover CC)

The formal difference: waiver retracts CC from the fiber entirely. Estoppel does not touch CC’s position in the fiber — CC remains in Ht∗H_{t^*}; it is the forum’s JJ-topology that refuses to process CC’s claim as a covering event in this context. In another forum, CC might still be settled. Estoppel is forum-relative; waiver is not.

This distinction matters: if an estoppel is later remedied (the detriment is compensated), the estoppel may be lifted and QQ can once again assert CC. A waiver cannot typically be reversed. This asymmetry follows from the formal structure: restoring JJ-coverage of CC is possible (the topology can be updated); restoring a retracted section requires a new act of creation.

The three main forms

Promissory estoppel

QQ makes a promise ρ\rho (a future-directed proposition: “I will do X”) that RR reasonably relies upon. Even without consideration (making it unenforceable as a contract), QQ is estopped from denying the promise. Restatement (Second) of Contracts §90 (ALI, 1981):

“A promise which the promisor should reasonably expect to induce action or forbearance on the part of the promisee or a third person and which does induce such action or forbearance is binding if injustice can be avoided only by enforcement of the promise.”

In the formal framework: the promise ρ\rho is placed in the relational context (ρ∈Htρ\rho \in H_{t_\rho} as a forward-directed proposition); RR’s reliance changes RR’s section (moves RR from one fiber position to another based on ρ\rho); the forum’s JJ-topology refuses to allow QQ to contradict ρ\rho because doing so would cause δ\delta.

Equitable estoppel (estoppel by representation)

QQ makes a representation of present fact ρ\rho (not a promise but a factual statement); RR relies; QQ is estopped from asserting the true facts. Classic case: QQ represents that XX belongs to QQ; RR purchases XX from QQ in reliance; QQ is estopped from asserting that XX actually belongs to a third party (Freeman v. Cooke, 2 Ex. 654, 1848).

In the formal framework: ρ\rho is a proposition about the present state of the fiber (ρ∈Htρ\rho \in H_{t_\rho}, not a prospective section); RR settles their own fiber position based on ρ\rho; the forum blocks QQ’s assertion of ¬ρ\neg\rho because that would collapse RR’s settled position.

Estoppel by conduct (estoppel in pais)

QQ’s conduct (not explicit statement) constitutes the representation ρ\rho. Silence where there was a duty to speak is a classic case: if QQ watches RR make expenditures on QQ’s land believing it to be RR’s own, and QQ says nothing, QQ may be estopped from asserting ownership. Pickard v. Sears, 6 A&E 469 (1837).

In the formal framework: ρ\rho is not a verbal proposition but a section of QQ’s behavioral fiber — the aggregate of QQ’s acts and omissions at tρt_\rho constitutes a proposition that RR reasonably reads as settling a certain state. The forum’s JJ-topology treats this behavioral section as equivalent to a verbal representation.

Estoppel by record (res judicata)

A prior judicial determination binds the parties in subsequent proceedings. QQ cannot re-litigate an issue already decided against QQ in a prior proceeding where QQ had a full and fair opportunity to be heard. This is estoppel by the record — the prior judgment ϕ∈Htϕ∗(k)\phi \in H^{*(k)}_{t_\phi} blocks re-settlement of the same issue.

In the formal framework: the prior judgment ϕ\phi is already in H∗(k)H^{*(k)} (doubly settled at depth kk in the prior proceeding). The current forum’s JJ-topology treats the prior settled judgment as a sieve that covers the current dispute — any attempt by QQ to re-litigate the same issue fails to produce new coverage because the issue is already covered by ϕ\phi. The prior nucleus-settled proposition acts as a blocking sieve in the current forum.

Math grounding

Estoppel operates at the level of the site topology JJ, not the fiber Ht∗H_{t^*}. The forum’s Grothendieck topology JJ determines which covering families are recognized. An estoppel is a constraint on JJ at the specific context of QQ’s claim:

For the estopped claim CC: the local sieve SQ(C)S_Q(C) generated by QQ’s assertion of CC does not cover — there is no JJ-covering refinement of SQ(C)S_Q(C) that is in J(t)J(t) — because the cultural/equitable topology JJ treats the combination {ρ,δ,C}\{\rho, \delta, C\} (prior representation + detriment + inconsistent claim) as a non-covering triple.

Formally: estoppel adds a condition to JJ. The normal site topology JJ might allow QQ’s claim CC to cover. The estoppel-modified topology JεJ_\varepsilon excludes CC-covering when QQ’s prior representation ρ\rho is in the context:

Jε(t)=J(t)∖{S∈J(t)∣S includes Q’s C-assertion and (ρ,δ) are established}J_\varepsilon(t) = J(t) \setminus \{S \in J(t) \mid S \text{ includes } Q\text{'s } C\text{-assertion and } (\rho, \delta) \text{ are established}\}

This is a Lawvere–Tierney topology modification: the estoppel acts as a local nucleus jε:Ω→Ωj_\varepsilon : \Omega \to \Omega that closes off the subobject corresponding to QQ’s inconsistent claim. The modified nucleus jεj_\varepsilon is exactly what blocks QQ’s claim from propagating to Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*} in this forum.

The contrast with waiver: waiver retracts CC from the fiber (uses the Δt∗\Delta_{t^*}-retraction morphism). Estoppel modifies the topology (uses the Lawvere–Tierney nucleus on the subobject classifier Ω\Omega). Different morphisms; same practical effect for QQ in the current forum.

Open questions

  • Whether estoppel-by-record (res judicata) has the structure of a Grothendieck topology condition: whether the prior judgment ϕ\phi generates a covering sieve that explicitly includes all attempts to re-litigate the same issue, making those attempts non-covering by the maximality axiom of the topology (the maximal sieve covers everything, so a sieve that includes the prior judgment covers the current dispute).
  • Whether promissory estoppel (the promise is binding without consideration) corresponds to the cultural topology JJ treating forward-directed propositions (promises) as binding sieve-generators — whether the culture’s background topology includes promise-as-covering as a standard sieve, bypassing the contractual consideration requirement.
  • Whether the four forms of estoppel (promissory, equitable, conduct, record) correspond to the four sources of the sieve that blocks QQ: future-directed proposition (ρ\rho as promise), present-fact proposition (ρ\rho as representation), behavioral section (ρ\rho as conduct), and prior settled proposition (ρ\rho as prior judgment) — and whether these four sources exhaust the morphisms that can generate a blocking sieve in JJ.
