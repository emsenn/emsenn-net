Law

A LawfulClaim is a knowledge claim in the determined mode of the inquiry trajectory and the truth-only corner of settlement — fully witnessed, affirmingly established, with no further witnessing capable of moving it. The strongest epistemic position the framework recognizes.

¶Formal definition

A Law is a predication cell value that has reached the joint condition: the knowledge-order is at its top (⊤\top, full evidence accumulated) and the truth-order is at its top (t\mathbf{t}, the affirming pole). The value occupies the true-only corner of settlement — the position where adding further witnessings of the kind the inquiry can perform does not change the value.

Equivalently, a Law is a claim in the determined mode of the inquiry trajectory, tilted toward the affirming truth-axis pole. The absurd → possible → necessary → determined trajectory has been traversed for the claim, and the determination is affirming.

¶What this is

A Law is a knowledge claim that has been settled by inquiry: the relations in the system that bear on the claim have all been witnessed, the witnessing has tilted toward affirmation, and adding more witnessings of the same kind does not change the position. This is what law names in the epistemology of science: an exceptionless universal that the historical record has established and that every forward case continues to confirm. Newton’s laws, conservation laws, the laws of logic — all exhibit this kind of settlement.

In mathematics, the equivalent is a theorem: derived from axioms and shown to hold across every model the theory admits.

The distinction between a Law and a Corroborated claim is one of trajectory position: a Corroborated claim is in the necessary mode (the relations force its current shape but the inquiry has not closed) rather than the determined mode (further witnessings cannot move it). A Law has reached the local fixed point; a Corroborated claim is structurally bound but not yet at the fixed point.

¶What the determination condition means for a knowledge claim

Knowledge axis at top: evidence is full. The witnessings the inquiry can perform have all been performed, in both affirming and denying directions, and they do not contradict — the affirming evidence outweighs or has settled the denying side. There is no further witnessing of the kind available to the inquiry that would shift the claim’s settlement. This is not “we have checked enough evidence” but “there is no relevant evidence within the inquiry’s reach that has not been considered.”

Truth axis at top: the determination is affirming. The witnessings, taken together, tilt the claim toward the affirming pole rather than the denying pole or the both-directions pole. A Law is not just settled — it is settled toward the claim being true.

Together: the claim sits at the t\mathbf{t} corner of settlement: true-only, fully determined.

¶The four epistemic positions

The framework distinguishes four epistemic positions a knowledge claim can occupy, corresponding to the four modes of the inquiry trajectory:

Position Trajectory mode Description
Law Determined Knowledge axis at top, truth axis affirming. Claim is settled affirmingly; further witnessings within reach do not move it.
Corroborated Necessary Relations bound the claim’s shape, but the inquiry’s witnessing has not closed. Some further witnessings could move the position.
Conjecture Possible Witnessing has begun. The claim has been related to the rest of the system but the relations are not tight enough to force its shape.
Open Absurd No witnessing has been performed; no relations constrain the claim. The claim is held bare or not held at all.

The four positions partition the inquiry’s claims by how far along the trajectory they have been moved. A claim in the Open position is at the start of inquiry; a claim at Law is at the local terminus.

¶Local stability versus global truth

Settlement is local and revisable. A Law is a claim whose settlement has reached the determined corner under the witnessings the inquiry has so far performed and given the absurdities the inquiry has accepted. A different inquiry, with different starting absurdities or witnessings the first inquiry could not perform, may produce a different settlement.

This is not a softening of the framework’s commitments. A Law within an inquiry is genuinely settled by the relations the inquiry has built up; its shape is not negotiable while those relations hold. What is relativized is the frame — different inquiries can produce different Laws, all internally rigorous, none globally arbitrating.

The framework treats this as honest description of what knowledge production actually does. Newton’s laws were Laws within Newtonian mechanics; they remain locally settled within the framework’s classical regime, even after relativistic and quantum mechanics introduced different starting absurdities and produced different Laws of their own.

¶Open questions

  • Whether every claim that reaches the determined corner of settlement corresponds to a knowable proposition, or whether some Laws are settled by structure but unformulable in any finite language.
  • The relation between the epistemic quartet (Law / Corroborated / Conjecture / Open) and the work-trajectory quartet (Process / Procedure / Derivation / Inquiry): both are instantiations of the four-mode trajectory, but under different interpretations of what witnessing and settlement mean in each domain.
  • Whether the claim that no further witnessing could move a Law is itself a Law — that is, whether the meta-claim about settlement stability has the same epistemic structure as the object-level claims.
  • Whether lawful-claim should reference a base type for epistemic status; currently no such base definition exists.
