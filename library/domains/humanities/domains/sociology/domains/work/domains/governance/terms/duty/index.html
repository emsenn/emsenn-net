Duty

Formal definition

A Duty is a five-tuple D=(a,p,N,φ,r)D = (a, p, N, \varphi, r):

D=(a,  p∈Pos,  N,  φ:T→{0,1},  r)D = (a,\; p \in \mathrm{Pos},\; N,\; \varphi : T \to \{0,1\},\; r)

where:

  • aa is the duty-bearer — the agent who holds the duty
  • p∈Posp \in \mathrm{Pos} is the position grounding the duty — the office, role, or standing that generates it
  • NN is the normative system — the authority (law, regulation, custom, contract) that imposes the duty
  • φ\varphi is the required act or state — the act whose performance discharges the duty; formally a predicate φ:Acts(a)→{0,1}\varphi : \mathrm{Acts}(a) \to \{0,1\}
  • rr is the correlative party — the entity whose claim φ\varphi satisfies; rr may be an individual, an institution, or the class of all others

Seven invariants. DD is a duty if and only if it satisfies:

  1. Correlativity (Hohfeld): every duty D(a,φ,r)D(a, \varphi, r) entails a claim C(r,φ,a)C(r, \varphi, a). If aa has a duty to φ\varphi toward rr, then rr has a claim against aa that aa shall φ\varphi.

  2. Consistency (SDL axiom D): O(φ)⇒¬O(¬φ)\mathrm{O}(\varphi) \Rightarrow \neg\mathrm{O}(\neg\varphi). A duty cannot obligate both φ\varphi and ¬φ\neg\varphi within the same normative system NN.

  3. Positional derivation: the duty derives from position pp, not from the personal characteristics of bearer aa. If a′∈pa' \in p (another agent holds the same position), the duty transfers: D(a′,p,N,φ,r)D(a', p, N, \varphi, r) holds by the same normative system.

  4. Categorical force: the duty holds independently of whether φ\varphi serves aa’s interest. It is not a hypothetical imperative (if you want G, then φ)(\text{if you want } G, \text{ then } \varphi) but categorical (you shall φ)(\text{you shall } \varphi).

  5. Discharge condition: duty DD is discharged when φ\varphi is at the nuclear fixed point:

discharged(D)  ⟺  σt(φ(a))=φ(a)  ∧  Δt(φ(a))=φ(a)\mathrm{discharged}(D) \iff \sigma_t(\varphi(a)) = \varphi(a) \;\wedge\; \Delta_t(\varphi(a)) = \varphi(a)

The performance of φ\varphi by aa at tt must be doubly-settled — both meaning-closed and execution-closed — to constitute discharge. Performance that is routine (Δt\Delta_t-settled) but not yet recognized (σt\sigma_t-unsettled) is incomplete discharge.

  1. Non-delegability: the duty D(a,p,N,φ,r)D(a, p, N, \varphi, r) cannot be transferred to another bearer while aa holds position pp. aa may authorize another agent a′a' to perform acts that contribute to φ\varphi, but accountability for φ\varphi remains with aa. The required act φ\varphi is not itself delegable unless NN explicitly permits substitution.

  2. Relational predicate: duty is a three-place relation D(a,φ,r)\mathbf{D}(a, \varphi, r) in the normative system NN. This distinguishes duty from mere obligation-to-act: a duty is always a duty to someone.

The Hohfeldian position

Duty occupies one of four Hohfeldian fundamental legal positions:

Position Relational form Correlative
Duty D(a,φ,r)\mathbf{D}(a, \varphi, r) Claim: C(r,φ,a)\mathbf{C}(r, \varphi, a)
Privilege ¬D(a,¬φ,r)\neg\mathbf{D}(a, \neg\varphi, r) No-claim: ¬C(r,¬φ,a)\neg\mathbf{C}(r, \neg\varphi, a)
Power P(a,R→R′,r)\mathbf{P}(a, R \to R', r) Liability: L(r,R→R′,a)\mathbf{L}(r, R \to R', a)
Immunity ¬P(r,R→R′,a)\neg\mathbf{P}(r, R \to R', a) Disability: ¬P(r,R→R′,a)\neg\mathbf{P}(r, R \to R', a)

A duty is not a power (which creates new normative relations) and not a privilege (which is the absence of a duty). The correlative of a duty is always a claim held by rr against aa.

Standard Deontic Logic reading

In SDL, the duty D(a,φ,r)D(a, \varphi, r) is expressed as Oa(φ)\mathrm{O}_a(\varphi): it is obligatory for aa to bring about φ\varphi.

The consistency axiom D: O(φ)⇒P(φ)\mathrm{O}(\varphi) \Rightarrow \mathrm{P}(\varphi) (obligation implies permission), equivalently O(φ)⇒¬O(¬φ)\mathrm{O}(\varphi) \Rightarrow \neg\mathrm{O}(\neg\varphi).

The transmission principle: if O(φ)\mathrm{O}(\varphi) and φ⇒ψ\varphi \Rightarrow \psi, then O(ψ)\mathrm{O}(\psi) — obligation transmits along entailment.

Discharge and the nuclear fixed-point

The discharge condition (invariant 5) uses the nuclei σt\sigma_t and Δt\Delta_t of the fiber HtH_t at the current history tt:

  • Δt(φ(a))=φ(a)\Delta_t(\varphi(a)) = \varphi(a): the performance of φ\varphi is execution-settled — the procedure for counting it as performance is fixed and has been followed
  • σt(φ(a))=φ(a)\sigma_t(\varphi(a)) = \varphi(a): the performance is meaning-settled — the act is recognized by NN and by rr as constituting discharge

Both must hold. A duty can be executed but unrecognized (execution-settled, meaning-open), or recognized in principle but not yet executed (meaning-settled, execution-open). Full discharge requires φ(a)∈Ht∗=Fix(σt)∩Fix(Δt)\varphi(a) \in H^*_t = \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) \cap \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t).

Duty and position

The positional derivation invariant (3) gives the relation between duty and Office:

p⊢D  ⟺  N⊨D(holder(p),φ,r)p \vdash D \iff N \models \mathbf{D}(\mathrm{holder}(p), \varphi, r)

The position pp is the source of DD. When the holder changes — when aa is succeeded by a′a' — the duty DD transfers to a′a'. The duty outlasts any particular bearer; it attaches to the position, not the person.

Duties from offices are categorically different from personal obligations: personal obligations dissolve with the person, positional duties transfer.

Duty and Norm

A duty is the instantiation of a Norm at a specific bearer aa and correlative rr. The Norm triple (φ,O,N)(\varphi, O, N) — propositional content, deontic operator, normative system — is the general form; a duty is that triple indexed to aa and rr:

D(a,p,N,φ,r)=Norm(φ,O,N)  applied to  (a,p,r)D(a, p, N, \varphi, r) = \mathrm{Norm}(\varphi, \mathrm{O}, N)\;\text{applied to}\;(a, p, r)

The deontic operator OO in the Norm spec is the obligation-modality opcode — the same opcode that appears in this spec’s AST under required-act. The distinction between Norm (the general deontic proposition) and Duty (the position-indexed instance) mirrors the distinction between a type and a term: the Norm is the type; the Duty (a,p,N,φ,r)(a, p, N, \varphi, r) is a term of that type, with bearer and correlative filled in.

The HohfeldianPosition spec places Duty in the first quartet: Duty is the jural correlative of Claim-Right. The hohfeldian-jural-correlative opcode in the AST above names this correlative relation explicitly — whenever a duty D(a,φ,r)D(a, \varphi, r) holds, a claim C(r,φ,a)C(r, \varphi, a) holds in the same NormativeSystem, by the jural correlativity axiom.

Open questions

  • Whether the discharge condition must require φ(a)∈Ht∗\varphi(a) \in H^*_t or whether Δt\Delta_t-settlement alone constitutes sufficient discharge in legal contexts (execution without recognition).
  • Whether a NormativeSystem can generate contradictory duties and, if so, what the resolution operator is — whether the nuclear quartet provides a natural conflict-resolution structure (the Norm spec’s consistency-predicate opcode names this requirement formally without resolving it).
  • Whether Contract’s bilateral norm-binding (P1,P2,Φ12,Φ21,V)(P_1, P_2, \Phi_{12}, \Phi_{21}, V) is exactly a pair of duties — D(P1,⋅,Ncontract,Φ12,P2)D(P_1, \cdot, N_\mathrm{contract}, \Phi_{12}, P_2) and D(P2,⋅,Ncontract,Φ21,P1)D(P_2, \cdot, N_\mathrm{contract}, \Phi_{21}, P_1) — or whether the exchange structure (Φ12,Φ21)(\Phi_{12}, \Phi_{21}) adds something that two bare duties do not contain (consideration, simultaneity of commitment).
