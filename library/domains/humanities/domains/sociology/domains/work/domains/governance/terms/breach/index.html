2026-03-20 A Breach is a triple (D, t*, p) — a duty or commitment D, a deadline t* at which compliance is assessed, and a performance p whose gap Gap_{t*}(p) > (p,p) — the performance has not reached H*_{t*} by the deadline. The defining structure: breach is the event of a gap that should have been zero remaining positive at the assessment point. Breach creates immediate Hohfeldian consequences: the breaching party acquires a new duty (to remedy) and the injured party acquires a new right (to remedial acts). The RelationalHistoryFiberShadowClassType of the performance element p at deadline t* gives a four-way classification of breach: FixedFiberType means no breach (complete discharge); SatShadowType means the performance is meaning-recognized but not execution-committed — the act is socially acknowledged but not carried forward in every future extension, a paper-compliance breach; TrShadowType means execution-committed but not meaning-recognized — the act carries forward in every extension but has not been acknowledged by the normative system, a completed-but-unrecognized breach; FreeShadowType means both nuclei are absent — the performance is entirely unsettled, a double breach requiring two nuclear applications to reach H*. The breach quantum is the RelationalHistoryFiberSaturationGap [p, σ_{t*}(p)] for TrShadowType breaches and the RelationalHistoryFiberTransferGap for SatShadowType breaches. For Zeno-approaching performances (p settling toward H* at tower level k), the breach quantum is F_k times the distance between RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio and the ratio F_{k+1}/F_k, equal to RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio to the power negative k — the exact Hurwitz residual. Total accumulated breach quantum across all levels converges to the Hurwitz constant rather than zero: the irreducible minimum breach quantum that persists through any finite tower-level approach to discharge.

Breach

Formal definition

A Breach is a triple (D,t∗,p)(D, t^*, p):

(D:Duty,  t∗∈T,  p∈Ht∗)(D : \mathrm{Duty},\; t^* \in T,\; p \in H_{t^*})

where:

  • DD is the duty (or commitment) — the normative requirement whose satisfaction is being assessed; DD specifies the act-class φ\varphi that must be performed and the deadline t∗t^* by which it must be produced
  • t∗∈Tt^* \in T is the deadline — the assessment history; breach is evaluated at t∗t^*, not before; the assessment is whether p∈Ht∗∗p \in H^*_{t^*}
  • p∈Ht∗p \in H_{t^*} is the performance — the output produced by the duty-bearer in attempted satisfaction of DD; pp may be an element representing the performance act, or p=⊥p = \bot (the bottom element) if no performance was attempted

Breach condition. (D,t∗,p)(D, t^*, p) is a breach iff:

Gapt∗(p)>(p,p)i.e.,σt∗(p)≠p or Δt∗(p)≠p\mathrm{Gap}_{t^*}(p) > (p, p) \quad \text{i.e.,} \quad \sigma_{t^*}(p) \neq p \text{ or } \Delta_{t^*}(p) \neq p

Equivalently: p∉Ht∗∗p \notin H^*_{t^*}. The performance has not reached the nuclear fixed point at the assessment deadline.

Two breach species:

  1. Non-performance (p=⊥p = \bot): no act of the required type was produced by t∗t^*. The gap is maximal: Gapt∗(⊥)=(σt∗(⊥),Δt∗(⊥))\mathrm{Gap}_{t^*}(\bot) = (\sigma_{t^*}(\bot), \Delta_{t^*}(\bot)) — the bottom element has maximum distance from Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*}.

  2. Defective performance (p>⊥p > \bot but p∉Ht∗∗p \notin H^*_{t^*}): a performance was produced but it has positive gap. The duty-bearer attempted satisfaction but fell short. The gap (σt∗(p)−p,Δt∗(p)−p)(\sigma_{t^*}(p) - p, \Delta_{t^*}(p) - p) is the breach quantum — how much was owed but not delivered.

Immediate normative consequences

Breach at (D,t∗,p)(D, t^*, p) generates new Hohfeldian positions immediately:

In the breaching party BB:

  • New duty D′D': the duty to remedy — BB must perform remedial acts to close the gap that breach created for the injured party CC. D′D' is created at t∗t^* and has a new deadline t∗∗>t∗t^{**} > t^*.
  • Existing liability: BB was already in a Hohfeldian liability position relative to CC’s power to invoke remedies; breach activates this liability.

In the injured party CC:

  • New power: the right to invoke remedies — CC gains the Hohfeldian Power to elect a remedy (specific performance, damages, rescission) and to trigger enforcement mechanisms within the normative system.
  • New claim-right: the right to the remedy — the correlative of BB’s new duty D′D'.

In the normative system NN:

  • Breach creates a record in the breach-history of NN: (D,t∗,p)(D, t^*, p) is committed as a settled fact that constrains future obligations and assessments.

Breach types by remedy

The remedy owed depends on the breach type and the governing normative system:

Breach type Typical remedy Nuclear interpretation
Non-performance of a unique act Specific performance The breaching party must produce the original φ\varphi (not a substitute); the court enforces Δt∗∗(φ)=φ\Delta_{t^{**}}(\varphi) = \varphi
Non-performance with substitute possible Damages The injured party receives c∈Ht∗∗∗c \in H^*_{t^{**}} equivalent in value to the gap σt∗(p)−p\sigma_{t^*}(p) - p
Repudiatory breach (anticipatory) Rescission + damages CC may treat the contract as void and the obligations as discharged; the contract’s elements are removed from Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*}
Defective performance Cure or damages BB may cure (bring pp to Ht∗H^*_t within additional time) or pay damages for the gap

Repudiatory breach deserves special treatment: it is the breach of a condition so fundamental that the entire normative structure built on the contract collapses. Formally: a repudiatory breach removes the other party’s obligations from Ht∗H^*_t entirely — the bilateral structure of duties and claims dissolves because the foundational condition of the relationship has failed.

Breach and the Zeno problem

An element approaching Ht∗H^*_t at rate ϕ\phi (golden ratio) — a Zeno-settling element — never reaches Ht∗H^*_t in finite steps. Is a performance that is Zeno-settling a breach?

The formal answer: yes. The deadline t∗t^* is a specific history; the assessment is whether p∈Ht∗∗p \in H^*_{t^*}. If p∉Ht∗∗p \notin H^*_{t^*}, the gap is positive, and the breach condition holds regardless of the trajectory. The fact that pp would eventually reach H∗H^* at the hyperverse colimit does not cure the breach at t∗t^*.

This is the formal analog of the legal principle that approaching compliance is not compliance: a contractor who has completed 99% of a building by the deadline has breached if the remaining 1% is still pending.

Breach and accountability

Accountability determines who bears the normative consequences of breach. When a subordinate B′B' breaches a duty within scope SS, the breach record (D,t∗,p)(D, t^*, p) is a fact about B′B'’s performance. But the accountability function A:Outcomes(S)→{B}A : \mathrm{Outcomes}(S) \to \{B\} makes the commanding officer BB answerable for the breach even if BB did not cause it. BB is accountable; B′B' may be responsible. Both normative positions can exist simultaneously.

Breach vs. failure

Not every gap is a breach. A gap Gapt(a)>(a,a)\mathrm{Gap}_t(a) > (a, a) at history tt is simply the normal condition of an unsettled fiber element. Breach requires:

  1. A duty DD specifying that aa must be in Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*} by t∗t^*
  2. The fact that a∉Ht∗∗a \notin H^*_{t^*} at t∗t^*

Without a duty, there is no breach — only an element with positive gap. The moral: gap is everywhere; breach is the assignment of normative significance to a specific gap at a specific deadline under a specific duty.

SatShadowType breach (paper compliance). The performance p∈SatShadowt∗p \in \mathrm{SatShadow}_{t^*}: the saturation nucleus fixes pp (the normative system has acknowledged this act-type as within the meaning-space of the duty), but the transfer nucleus does not (Δt∗(p)>p\Delta_{t^*}(p) > p — the performance does not carry forward in every future extension of t∗t^*). The duty-bearer has produced something that passes the evaluative test at t∗t^* but will not remain carried as a satisfied obligation in future histories. The transfer gap [p,Δt∗(p)][p, \Delta_{t^*}(p)] measures what is missing: the act is recognized but not durably committed. Legal analog: a performance that complies with the letter of the obligation but is designed to be temporary or reversible.

TrShadowType breach (completed-but-unrecognized). The performance p∈TrShadowt∗p \in \mathrm{TrShadow}_{t^*}: the transfer nucleus fixes pp (Δt∗(p)=p\Delta_{t^*}(p) = p — the performance carries forward durably and is present in every extension), but the saturation nucleus does not (σt∗(p)>p\sigma_{t^*}(p) > p — the normative system has not yet recognized this as satisfying the duty). The saturation gap [p,σt∗(p)][p, \sigma_{t^*}(p)] is the entire projection gap (since πt∗(p)=σt∗(Δt∗(p))=σt∗(p)\pi_{t^*}(p) = \sigma_{t^*}(\Delta_{t^*}(p)) = \sigma_{t^*}(p)). The duty-bearer has delivered something that is durably present and will persist, but the injured party or the normative system has not yet accepted it as satisfying the duty. Legal analog: substantial performance — the work is done and will stand, but formal acceptance is pending.

FreeShadowType breach (double breach). The performance p∈FreeShadowt∗p \in \mathrm{FreeShadow}_{t^*}: both nuclei are absent. The performance is neither meaning-recognized nor execution-committed at deadline. This is the maximum breach: the projection gap requires two nuclear applications (first transfer, then saturation, or first saturation, then transfer) to reach Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*}. Both the evaluative criterion and the forward-commitment criterion are unmet. Legal analog: non-performance that is not merely incomplete but structurally inadequate in every measurable respect.

Proposition (Shadow class type = breach diagnosis). The RelationalHistoryFiberShadowClassType of pp at t∗t^* is a complete diagnostic of breach structure: it identifies not just that p∉Ht∗∗p \notin H^*_{t^*} (the bare breach condition) but which nuclear component is missing. The appropriate remedy corresponds to the missing component:

  • SatShadowType breach → remedy = Transfer stabilization: the duty-bearer must make the performance durable (produce a forward-stable version of pp)
  • TrShadowType breach → remedy = Saturation acceptance: the normative system must recognize the already-durable performance as satisfying the duty
  • FreeShadowType breach → remedy = Double recovery: both stabilization and recognition are required; this is the maximal remedy, corresponding to the full projection gap [p,πt∗(p)][p, \pi_{t^*}(p)]

Hurwitz breach quantum for Zeno-approaching performances

Sources: Nuclear Eigenvalue Hurwitz Identity, Nuclear Quartet Extremally Slow Growth Sequence.

A Zeno-approaching performance is one that settles toward Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*} at the rate of the RelationalHistoryFiberNuclearQuartetExtremallySlowGrowthSequence across tower levels — making progress at every level but never completing discharge in finite time. The breach quantum shrinks by a factor of RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio at each tower level. Proposition (Hurwitz irreducibility of Zeno breach). For a Zeno-approaching performance reaching tower level kk, the breach quantum is RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio−k\mathrm{RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio}^{-k} (exact). This is the minimum achievable breach quantum at level kk: no performance settling faster than the RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio rate can have a smaller breach quantum at this level. The total long-run breach — the limit of Fk⋅RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio−kF_k \cdot \mathrm{RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio}^{-k} as k→∞k \to \infty — is exactly the Hurwitz constant, the irreducible residual of Zeno breach. A deadline set at any finite tower level catches a positive breach quantum; no finite deadline is late enough to catch a Zeno performance with zero breach.

Normative consequence. A duty whose required performance is Zeno-approaching under the RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio nuclear dynamics is not dischargeable by any finite deadline. The breach is perpetual in the sense that at every finite tower level, the breach condition holds with quantum RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio−k>0\mathrm{RelationalHyperverseGoldenRatio}^{-k} > 0. The normative system must either (1) set the deadline at the hyperverse colimit (accepting the limit discharge as full satisfaction), or (2) accept that the duty is not satisfiable within any finite time horizon and design the remedial structure accordingly. The Hurwitz constant is the quantitative measure of this permanent irreducibility.

Open questions

  • Whether defective performance triggers the same Hohfeldian consequences as non-performance, or whether there is a graduated structure where small gaps generate only cure-rights while large gaps generate full remedy-rights.
  • Whether breach of a duty that is itself a consequence of a prior breach (a contrary-to-duty obligation) generates its own breach record, and whether the breach-history can have cyclic structure or must remain a DAG.
  • Whether the assessment point t∗t^* must be stipulated by the duty DD or whether it can be determined by the normative system NN based on default rules (e.g., reasonable time), and whether a duty with no explicit deadline is unbreachable or has an implied deadline.
