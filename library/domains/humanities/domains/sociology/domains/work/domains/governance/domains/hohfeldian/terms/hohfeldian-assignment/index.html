Assignment

2026-03-20 An Assignment is a triple (Q, C, B) — an assignor Q who holds a Hohfeldian claim C in H*_{t*}, an assignee B who receives C, and an assignment act α that moves C's section from Q's authority domain to B's authority domain. The defining structure: assignment transfers the right (the Hohfeldian claim — Q's right to receive) without changing the correlative duty (the obligor's duty to perform). The obligor P's duty is unchanged; only the identity of the party to whom P owes performance changes from Q to B. Formally: assignment is a natural transformation α : H_Q → H_B between Q's and B's authority presheaves that carries C's settled section from Q's fiber to B's fiber, leaving the duty-fibration unchanged.

¶Formal definition

An Assignment is a triple A=(Q,C,B)\mathcal{A} = (Q, C, B):

A=(Q:Assignor,  C∈Ht∗∗(Q),  B:Assignee)\mathcal{A} = (Q : \mathrm{Assignor},\; C \in H^*_{t^*}(Q),\; B : \mathrm{Assignee})

together with an assignment act α:Ht∗∗(Q)→Ht∗∗(B)\alpha : H^*_{t^*}(Q) \to H^*_{t^*}(B):

α(C)=C′∈Ht∗∗(B)\alpha(C) = C' \in H^*_{t^*}(B)

where C′C' is the transferred claim — the same normative relationship (right to performance by the obligor PP) now held by BB rather than QQ.

  • QQ is the assignor — the original holder of CC; after α\alpha, QQ no longer holds CC
  • C∈Ht∗∗(Q)C \in H^*_{t^*}(Q) is the assigned claim — a Hohfeldian claim (right to receive something from PP); the settled right held by QQ that is being transferred to BB
  • BB is the assignee — the party who receives CC; after α\alpha, BB holds the right to demand performance from PP that QQ previously held
  • α\alpha is the assignment act — the notification, writing, or formal act that constitutes the transfer; the assignment is effective against the obligor PP when PP has notice of α\alpha

Three invariants. A\mathcal{A} is a valid assignment iff:

  1. Assignability: CC is assignable — not every right can be assigned. A right is non-assignable iff it is personal to QQ (its value depends essentially on QQ’s identity — a contract for QQ’s personal services, a right grounded in trust of QQ specifically) or is made non-assignable by the governing normative system. The test: can the obligation be performed identically for BB as for QQ? If yes, the right is assignable.

  2. Obligor’s duty preserved: the assignment does not change PP’s correlative duty. PP still owes performance; only the recipient of performance changes from QQ to BB. PP cannot resist performance to BB on the grounds that PP preferred QQ as the obligee (except where the obligee’s identity is material to performance — the personal-right exception).

  3. Notice of assignment: assignment is effective against PP when PP has notice of α\alpha. Before notice: PP can discharge their duty by performing to QQ (the original obligee, who must then account to BB). After notice: PP must perform to BB to discharge the duty.

¶The Hohfeldian analysis

An assignment moves a Hohfeldian claim from one holder to another. Hohfeldian analysis (Wesley Newcomb Hohfeld, “Some Fundamental Legal Conceptions as Applied in Judicial Reasoning,” Yale Law Journal 23:16, 1913) identifies four types of legal positions:

Position Correlative Opposite
Right (claim) Duty No-right
Privilege (liberty) No-right Duty
Power Liability Disability
Immunity Disability Liability

Assignment transfers a claim (right) from QQ to BB: the correlative duty on PP is unchanged. This is the key structural asymmetry: assignment operates entirely on QQ’s side of the Hohfeldian relation without touching PP’s side.

Contrast with:

  • Delegation: transfers a power (authority to act on behalf of QQ) while QQ retains the correlative accountability
  • Novation: extinguishes both the claim and the correlative duty and replaces them with a new set
  • Waiver: relinquishes the claim entirely without transferring it to anyone

¶Types of assignment

Legal assignment: a formal assignment that transfers the right absolutely and enables BB to sue PP directly in BB’s own name. Requirements: written form, signed by the assignor, notice to the obligor. Under the Law of Property Act 1925 (UK) §136 and UCC Article 9 (US), a legal assignment transfers complete ownership of the claim.

Equitable assignment: an informal assignment that creates obligations between QQ and BB but may require QQ to join as a party in proceedings against PP. No particular form is required; words of present transfer suffice. A party who says “I assign to you my right to receive the debt” has made an equitable assignment even without writing.

Partial assignment: assignment of a portion of a claim — “QQ assigns to BB half of the right to receive XX.” This creates a fractional interest: BB holds C′=C/2C' = C/2 and QQ retains C/2C/2. In formal terms: α\alpha moves a sub-section of Ht∗∗(Q)H^*_{t^*}(Q) to Ht∗∗(B)H^*_{t^*}(B) while the remaining sub-section stays with QQ.

Future assignment (assignment of future rights): an assignment of a right not yet in existence — “when I earn the right to X, I hereby assign that right to B.” In formal terms: this is an assignment of a prospective section — a section that will be in Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*} when the generative condition is satisfied. The assignment creates a priority right: when CC arrives in Ht∗∗(Q)H^*_{t^*}(Q), α\alpha is applied immediately.

¶Math grounding

The assignment act α\alpha is a natural transformation α:HQ⇒HB\alpha : H_Q \Rightarrow H_B between QQ’s and BB’s authority presheaves (Natural Transformation):

αt:Ht(Q)→Ht(B)for each t∈T\alpha_t : H_t(Q) \to H_t(B) \quad \text{for each } t \in T

commuting with the restriction maps: αt∘H(f)B=H(f)Q∘αt′\alpha_{t} \circ H(f)_B = H(f)_Q \circ \alpha_{t'} for all f:t→t′f : t \to t'.

The assignment preserves the settled layer: αt(Ht∗(Q))⊆Ht∗(B)\alpha_t(H^*_t(Q)) \subseteq H^*_t(B) — settled claims transfer as settled claims. The natural transformation condition ensures that BB’s claim C′C' is coherent across all histories in TT: if CC was globally recognized in QQ’s fiber, C′=α(C)C' = \alpha(C) is globally recognized in BB’s fiber.

The assignment does not act on the obligor PP’s fiber: Ht(P)H_t(P) is unchanged. The duty DP∈Ht∗(P)D_P \in H^*_t(P) — PP’s correlative duty — remains settled regardless of the assignment. The only change is in the target of PP’s performance: after notice of α\alpha, PP’s duty is discharged by performing to BB rather than to QQ.

In Relational Universe Morphism terms: the assignment is a morphism (ϕ,α):UQ→UB(\phi, \alpha) : \mathbf{U}_Q \to \mathbf{U}_B where ϕ=idT\phi = \mathrm{id}_T (same history structure) and α:HQ→∼HB\alpha : H_Q \xrightarrow{\sim} H_B is the natural isomorphism restricted to the claim CC.

¶Open questions

  • Whether partial assignment (assignment of a fractional interest in a claim) has the structure of a sub-presheaf assignment: whether BB’s partial claim C/2C/2 is a section of the sub-Heyting-algebra Ht∗∗(Q)/2H^*_{t^*}(Q)/2 and whether the meet structure of the Heyting algebra provides the natural definition of “half a claim.”
  • Whether future rights assignment (assignment of prospective sections) corresponds to a morphism in the pro-category of presheaves — a filtered inverse limit of sections that converges to the settled right when the generative condition fires.
  • Whether an assignment chain Q→B→B′Q \to B \to B' (successive assignments of the same claim) is a composition of natural transformations, and whether the resulting composite natural transformation HQ⇒HB′H_Q \Rightarrow H_{B'} factors through any intermediate presheaf or composes directly.
