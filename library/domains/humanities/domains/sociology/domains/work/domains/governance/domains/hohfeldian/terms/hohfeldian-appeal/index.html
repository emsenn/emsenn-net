Appeal

2026-03-20 An Appeal is a quadruple (J₁, J₂, φ, λ) — a lower jurisdiction J₁, an appellate jurisdiction J₂ at a higher depth level, a judgment φ ∈ H*_{t*}^(n) settled at depth n, and an appeal standing λ held by the appellant. The appeal carries φ from J₁'s fiber at depth n to J₂'s fiber at depth k ≥ n via the depth-filtration fiber level transition map ι_{n,k}(t). The defining structure: the appellate fiber at depth k is richer than J₁'s fiber at depth n — it contains more distinguished propositions, more fine-grained normative distinctions, and higher institutional authority. The transferring nucleus asymmetry (ι_{n,k} preserves σ-fixed points exactly but not Δ-fixed points) means that on appeal, propositions settled at the lower level remain provisionally present but propositions that transferred at the lower level may be re-examined. Formally: the appeal is the composition of ι_{n,k} with the appellate forum's adjudication act.

¶Formal definition

An Appeal is a quadruple P=(J1,J2,ϕ,λ)\mathcal{P} = (J_1, J_2, \phi, \lambda):

P=(J1:Jurisdiction,  J2:Jurisdiction,  ϕ∈Ht∗∗(n),  λ:Standing)\mathcal{P} = (J_1 : \mathrm{Jurisdiction},\; J_2 : \mathrm{Jurisdiction},\; \phi \in H^{*(n)}_{t^*},\; \lambda : \mathrm{Standing})

where:

  • J1J_1 is the originating jurisdiction — the forum that issued the judgment ϕ\phi; J1J_1 operates at depth nn in the filtration tower; its fiber Ht∗(n)H^{(n)}_{t^*} is the domain from which the appeal originates
  • J2J_2 is the appellate jurisdiction — the forum with authority to review ϕ\phi; J2J_2 operates at depth k≥nk \geq n; its fiber Ht∗(k)H^{(k)}_{t^*} is richer, containing the full depth-nn context plus additional normative distinctions
  • ϕ∈Ht∗∗(n)\phi \in H^{*(n)}_{t^*} is the judgment being appealed — a proposition that is σ\sigma-settled at depth nn (meaning-settled by J1J_1’s nucleus) but whose Δ\Delta-status at depth kk is not yet determined
  • λ\lambda is the appeal standing — the appellant’s Hohfeldian privilege plus correlative claim-recognition in J2J_2; without λ\lambda, J2J_2’s σ\sigma-nucleus does not process the appellant’s submissions

The appeal morphism is the composition:

P:Ht∗∗(n)→ιn,k(t∗)Ht∗(k)→J2-adjudicationHt∗∗(k)\mathcal{P} : H^{*(n)}_{t^*} \xrightarrow{\iota_{n,k}(t^*)} H^{(k)}_{t^*} \xrightarrow{J_2\text{-adjudication}} H^{*(k)}_{t^*}

where ιn,k(t∗)\iota_{n,k}(t^*) is the depth-filtration fiber level transition map (Relational History Depth Filtration Fiber Level Transition Map) that carries propositions from depth-nn to depth-kk.

Four invariants. P\mathcal{P} is a valid appeal iff:

  1. Depth ordering: k≥nk \geq n — the appellate jurisdiction is at least as deep (at least as authoritative) as the originating jurisdiction. An appeal to a lower-depth forum is not an appeal but a collateral challenge. The depth ordering is the institutional hierarchy: higher courts are higher in the filtration tower.

  2. Judgment existence: ϕ∈Ht∗∗(n)\phi \in H^{*(n)}_{t^*} — the judgment must be settled by J1J_1’s nucleus before the appeal is available. Appealing an unsettled matter (a proceeding that has not reached judgment) is either premature (interlocutory appeal, a special case) or a request for original jurisdiction in J2J_2 (not an appeal).

  3. Standing: λ\lambda — the appellant must have standing in J2J_2; specifically: (a) the appellant was a party to the J1J_1 proceeding or has a legally cognizable interest in the judgment ϕ\phi; (b) the appellant has a claim that J1J_1 erred; (c) the error, if established, would produce a different judgment. In formal terms: λ\lambda is the appellant’s section in J2J_2’s standing fibration — the appellant is in J2J_2’s recognized actor class.

  4. Transition coherence: ιn,k(t∗)\iota_{n,k}(t^*) carries ϕ\phi coherently — the transition map preserves ϕ\phi’s σ\sigma-fixed-point status: σt∗(k)(ιn,k(ϕ))=ιn,k(ϕ)\sigma^{(k)}_{t^*}(\iota_{n,k}(\phi)) = \iota_{n,k}(\phi) (the proposition that was settled at depth nn remains provisionally present at depth kk). The appellate court receives the judgment as a starting point; the question is whether it is also Δ\Delta-fixed at depth kk — whether it transfers to the settled-and-transferred layer of the higher fiber.

¶The transferring nucleus asymmetry

The key mathematical fact (Relational History Depth Filtration Fiber Level Transition Map):

“It preserves RelationalHistoryFiberSaturatingNucleus-fixed points exactly, while RelationalHistoryFiberTransferringNucleus-fixed points are NOT preserved in general.”

In institutional terms:

  • σ-fixed points (meaning-settled propositions) are preserved by ιn,k\iota_{n,k}: a judgment that was given settled meaning at depth nn retains that meaning-content when viewed from depth kk. The appellate court sees the same proposition the lower court saw.

  • Δ-fixed points (transfer-settled propositions — those that have completed their normative transfer and are in the doubly-quiescent layer H∗H^*) are not preserved: a judgment that was in H∗(n)H^{*(n)} (doubly settled at depth nn) may not be in H∗(k)H^{*(k)} after the transition. The appellate fiber has a different Δ-nucleus, and what transferred at depth nn may not be fully transferred at depth kk.

This asymmetry is the formal expression of appellate review: the appellate court accepts the lower court’s factual findings (σ\sigma-settlements preserved) but may re-examine the legal conclusions (Δ\Delta-fixed points not preserved). “Questions of law” are the Δ-layer of the lower court’s judgment; “questions of fact” are the σ\sigma-layer. De novo review = ιn,k\iota_{n,k} is applied without any deference to the lower court’s Δ-settlements. Clear error / abuse of discretion review = the Δ-settlements from depth nn are given some weight in the depth-kk adjudication (a qualified deference to the lower fiber’s nuclear determinations).

¶Standards of review

The standard of appellate review is the formal parameter that determines how much weight J2J_2 gives to J1J_1’s Δ-settlements:

Standard Formal meaning
De novo J2J_2 applies ιn,k\iota_{n,k} and then reapplies Δ(k)\Delta^{(k)} from scratch; J1J_1’s Δ-fixed points have no deference weight
Clear error J2J_2 accepts J1J_1’s Δ-fixed points unless they are outside the range that Δ(k)\Delta^{(k)} could produce; a strong prior toward the lower fiber’s Δ-settlements
Abuse of discretion J2J_2 accepts J1J_1’s judgment ϕ\phi unless J1J_1’s selection of ϕ\phi from the admissible range fails the minimum nucleus condition; the strongest deference
Manifest weight J2J_2 overturns only if J1J_1’s σ\sigma-fixed points are clearly wrong on the record; near-complete deference to the lower fiber

In the formal framework: the standard of review is a weighting morphism w:Ht∗∗(n)→Ht∗(k)w : H^{*(n)}_{t^*} \to H^{(k)}_{t^*} applied before J2J_2’s adjudication; more deferential standards weight J1J_1’s Δ-fixed points more heavily in J2J_2’s adjudication.

¶Historical ground: appellate jurisdiction

Roman law: the provocatio (appeal from magistrate to the popular assembly or emperor), developed into the appellatio under the Principate — a formal request to a higher magistrate to review a lower magistrate’s judgment. Ulpian (Dig. 49.1.1): “It is permitted to appeal from the sentences of all judges; whether they are sitting judges or arbitrators or those who have jurisdiction… from the emperor alone there is no appeal, nor from those who judge with his authority.”

The depth-filtration structure: from the local judge (depth 1) to the praetor (depth 2) to the emperor (depth ∞\infty — the terminal appellate authority). The emperor’s judgment settles at the deepest available fiber and is therefore Δ\Delta-fixed at all depths — not appealable because there is no depth k>nemperork > n_{\mathrm{emperor}}.

Common law: writ of error (direct challenge to the record — σ\sigma-layer review), certiorari (discretionary appellate review — J2J_2 selects which Δ\Delta-questions to examine), habeas corpus (collateral attack — a new proceeding, not an appeal in the strict sense; uses a different fiber entirely, not a transition map).

US federal courts: three-tier hierarchy (District Court → Court of Appeals → Supreme Court) is a three-level filtration tower: depth n=1n = 1 (trial), depth k=2k = 2 (circuit review), depth k=3k = 3 (Supreme Court review of federal questions). Marbury v. Madison, 5 U.S. 137 (1803): the Supreme Court’s review of constitutional questions is depth-3 adjudication — the Court’s Δ-fixed points on constitutional questions cannot be further appealed within the domestic system.

¶Appeal vs. collateral attack

Mechanism Jurisdiction relation Uses transition map Judgment effect
Appeal J2J_2 reviews J1J_1; k≥nk \geq n ιn,k\iota_{n,k} (depth transition) Judgment affirmed, modified, or reversed
Collateral attack J3J_3 independently adjudicates same facts in a separate proceeding No transition map; new forum Original judgment stands; new judgment created in parallel
Habeas corpus J4J_4 reviews legality of J1J_1’s jurisdiction No depth transition; addresses J1J_1’s authority (its standing fiber), not its judgment content Original judgment may be voided for J1J_1’s jurisdictional defect

A collateral attack does not use ιn,k\iota_{n,k}: it initiates a new proceeding in J3J_3 with a fresh section in J3J_3’s jurisdiction. The depth-filtration transition map is specific to the appeal relationship (vertical review within a hierarchy); collateral attacks are horizontal — they originate in a separate position in the topology of jurisdictions.

¶Open questions

  • Whether the terminal appellate authority (no further appeal available) corresponds to a maximum-depth fiber in the tower — a fiber Ht∗∗(N)H^{*(N)}_{t^*} above which no further ιN,k\iota_{N,k} exists — and whether the maximum depth is reached at the point where σ(N)=Δ(N)\sigma^{(N)} = \Delta^{(N)} (the nuclei coincide: there is no more transfer to do, only settlement).
  • Whether the three standards of review (de novo, clear error, abuse of discretion) can be derived from a single formal parameter (a deference weight w∈[0,1]w \in [0,1]) applied to the depth-transition map, and whether the special case w=0w = 0 (de novo) gives a functorial relationship between the depth-nn and depth-kk fibers.
  • Whether a chain of appeals J1→J2→J3J_1 \to J_2 \to J_3 (appeal, then appeal of the appellate judgment) is the composition ιn,k∘ιk,m\iota_{n,k} \circ \iota_{k,m} of depth-transition maps, and whether the composite ιn,m\iota_{n,m} produces the same result as appealing directly from J1J_1 to J3J_3 — i.e., whether the depth-filtration transition maps satisfy the transitivity ιk,m∘ιn,k=ιn,m\iota_{k,m} \circ \iota_{n,k} = \iota_{n,m}.
