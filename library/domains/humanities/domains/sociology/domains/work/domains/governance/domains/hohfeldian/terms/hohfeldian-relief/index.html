Relief

2026-03-20 A Relief is a triple (O, h, R) — an office O, a holder h currently in H*_{t*} (the settled binding), and a reason R (for cause, by request, end of tour, emergency) — together with a dissolution act that drives h's role-binding from H*_{t*} to ⊥. The defining structure: relief is the retraction of the section installed by investiture; after relief, the office O exists with its position vacant (the section s_r → h is collapsed to s_r → ⊥). Relief is the inverse morphism of the investiture's I⁺ act: it applies σ_{t*} to dissolve rather than settle the role-binding. Three cases have distinct formal structures: mutual relief (full investiture I⁻ with μ = 1), relief for cause (unilateral dissolution without incoming holder acknowledgement), and emergency succession (the relief is simultaneous with assumption by the successor).

Formal definition

A Relief is a triple R=(O,h,R)\mathfrak{R} = (O, h, R):

R=(O:Office,  h:Ht∗∗,  R:ReasonClass)\mathfrak{R} = (O : \mathrm{Office},\; h : H^*_{t^*},\; R : \mathrm{ReasonClass})

together with a dissolution act ε:Ht∗∗→⊥t∗\varepsilon : H^*_{t^*} \to \bot_{t^*}:

ε(h)=⊥Ht∗\varepsilon(h) = \bot_{H_{t^*}}

where:

  • OO is the office — the position being vacated; OO carries its full Hohfeldian deontic profile (authority, accountability, non-delegable duties) independently of who holds it
  • h∈Ht∗∗h \in H^*_{t^*} is the current holder’s binding — the role-assignment proposition that is currently meaning-settled: σt∗(h)=h\sigma_{t^*}(h) = h and Δt∗(h)=h\Delta_{t^*}(h) = h; the holder is in the doubly-quiescent layer
  • RR is the reason class — one of the structurally distinct cases (see below); the reason determines whether mutual acknowledgement μ\mu is required
  • ε\varepsilon is the dissolution act — the formal event that drives hh from Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*} to ⊥Ht∗\bot_{H_{t^*}}: the binding is dissolved, the section sO↦hs_O \mapsto h is replaced by sO↦⊥s_O \mapsto \bot, and the office is vacant

Three invariants. R\mathfrak{R} is a valid relief iff:

  1. Settled binding: h∈Ht∗∗h \in H^*_{t^*} before ε\varepsilon — the holder must be properly installed (in the doubly-settled layer) for relief to be meaningful. Relief of a person who was never invested is void: h∉Ht∗∗h \notin H^*_{t^*} means there is no binding to dissolve.

  2. Vacancy after dissolution: ε(h)=⊥Ht∗\varepsilon(h) = \bot_{H_{t^*}} — after the relief act, the office’s section position is genuinely vacant: sO↦⊥s_O \mapsto \bot. No residual binding persists; the holder’s accountability and authority within OO’s scope return to zero. If relief is immediately followed by a new investiture, the vacancy is instantaneous (a limit point between the two bounded intervals).

  3. Documentary record: R\mathfrak{R} is normatively effective only when recorded — in a log entry, orders, or official announcement. The record is the institutional expression that makes the vacancy legible to third parties. An unrecorded relief may be physically complete but institutionally invisible.

The three cases

Mutual relief (normal rotation)

The full investiture acts I+I^+ and I−I^- with mutual acknowledgement μ=1\mu = 1 (Investiture). The outgoing holder says “I stand relieved” (or equivalent); the incoming holder says “I relieve you.” Both acknowledgements are required for ε\varepsilon to be effective. The relief and the next investiture are not separate events but two halves of a single handover morphism:

I+∘R=handover(O,hout,hin)I^+ \circ \mathfrak{R} = \mathrm{handover}(O, h_{\mathrm{out}}, h_{\mathrm{in}})

The vacancy between ε(hout)=⊥\varepsilon(h_{\mathrm{out}}) = \bot and σt∗(hin)=hin\sigma_{t^*}(h_{\mathrm{in}}) = h_{\mathrm{in}} is instantaneous: continuity invariant 3 of Investiture ensures no gap and no overlap.

Relief for cause

Unilateral dissolution by a superior authority, without incoming holder acknowledgement. The superior applies ε\varepsilon without μ=1\mu = 1; the outgoing holder’s consent is not required. The consequence: the outgoing holder’s binding is dissolved and the office is vacant until the next investiture. Relief for cause may leave the office without a ready replacement — the vacancy is real and may persist.

The formal difference from mutual relief: μ=0\mu = 0 but ε\varepsilon is still valid because the superior holds the authority to dissolve the binding unilaterally. UCMJ Art. 71 (removal of a commissioned officer), 33 CFR Part 164.82 (maritime: owner or charterer can relieve master), SOLAS Chapter XI-2/6 (port state can relieve master for safety violations): all provide this unilateral dissolution authority.

Emergency succession

Relief is simultaneous with assumption by the successor: ε(hout)\varepsilon(h_{\mathrm{out}}) and σt∗(hin)\sigma_{t^*}(h_{\mathrm{in}}) occur at the same instant, triggered by an activation predicate (death, incapacity, capture). There is no mutual acknowledgement from the outgoing holder because they cannot give it. The documentation is performed by the incoming holder: the assumption is documented in writing, recording the time of assumption and the triggering condition.

Emergency succession is the relief case studied in Succession: the ordered list of candidates activates when the primary holder’s binding cannot be maintained.

Math grounding

The relief act is the nuclear dissolution of the settled binding h∈Ht∗∗h \in H^*_{t^*}:

  • Before ε\varepsilon: h∈Ht∗∗=Fix(σt∗)∩Fix(Δt∗)h \in H^*_{t^*} = \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_{t^*}) \cap \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_{t^*}) — the binding is doubly quiescent
  • ε\varepsilon applies σt∗\sigma_{t^*} in the dissolving direction: σt∗\sigma_{t^*} is applied to retract hh from Ht∗∗H^*_{t^*} back into Ht∗∖Ht∗∗H_{t^*} \setminus H^*_{t^*}, and then the restriction map H(f):Ht∗→Ht∗−H(f) : H_{t^*} \to H_{t^*-} propagates the vacancy backward
  • After ε\varepsilon: h↦⊥Ht∗h \mapsto \bot_{H_{t^*}} — the binding is at the bottom element; the office position is unfilled

The dissolution act ε\varepsilon is a restriction map H(f)H(f) in the contravariant direction of the presheaf (Passage, backward pole): it restricts the settled binding at t∗t^* to its predecessor history, removing the forward-settled content. In section terms (Section): ε\varepsilon is the retraction of the section sO:{O}→Ht∗∗s_O : \{O\} \to H^*_{t^*} — it collapses the right inverse back to the zero element.

Relief vs. resignation vs. expiry

Mechanism Initiator Mutual ack. Holder’s role Math
Normal relief Superior + holder Required (μ=1\mu = 1) Outgoing holder acknowledges release Section retraction with counit
Relief for cause Superior only Not required Holder’s consent irrelevant Unilateral nucleus dissolution
Resignation Holder only Accepted by superior Holder requests dissolution Holder-initiated section retraction
Expiry of tour Institutional schedule Automatic at TT’s end Section lapses by period end Temporal boundary of enrollment

Resignation is the holder-initiated relief: hh requests ε\varepsilon and the institutional authority accepts. The formal structure is the same as mutual relief but the direction of initiation is reversed.

Expiry is the automatic lapse of enrollment at TT’s end (Enrollment, invariant 3): not a relief act but the natural termination of the bounded-period section. An officer whose tour ends is not “relieved” — their section lapses.

Open questions

  • Whether relief for cause (unilateral ε\varepsilon without μ\mu) has a distinct categorical structure from mutual relief — whether the two are different morphisms or the same morphism with different generators (μ=1\mu = 1 vs. μ=0\mu = 0 as a parameter of the dissolution act, not a separate type).
  • Whether the vacancy state (after ε\varepsilon, before the next I+I^+) has a formal object in the category — an empty section object ⊥O\bot_O — and whether vacancy is a valid state of the fibration or only a transitional non-state.
  • Whether a chain of reliefs and investments can be represented as a path in the category of sections over the office fibration, and whether this path has a normal form (relief–vacancy–investment is the 3-step normal form, collapsing to a single handover morphism when vacancy is instantaneous).
