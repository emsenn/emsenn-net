The Office as Normative Presheaf: Fiber Doctrine, Roman Law, and Constitutional Opposition

2026-04-03 The formal model of office as a normative presheaf with nuclear doctrine — fiber Heyting algebras, the munus/honor/dignitas trinity from Roman law, Kantorowicz's body politic, and Madison's separation of powers as nuclear insulation.

An office is a normative position that persists independently of any particular holder. This text develops the formal model: the office as a presheaf of fiber Heyting algebras with nuclear doctrine, the correspondence with Roman legal categories (munus, honor, dignitas), Kantorowicz’s body politic as the presheaf structure, and Madison’s separation of powers as nuclear insulation between offices.

¶Formal tuple

An Office O is a quadruple (H,A,DO,I+)(H, A, D_O, I^+):

O=(H:Top→HAnucl,  A,  DO:A→{power,duty,privilege,immunity},  I+:Investiture)O = (H : T^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{HA_{nucl}},\; A,\; D_O : A \to \{\mathrm{power}, \mathrm{duty}, \mathrm{privilege}, \mathrm{immunity}\},\; I^+ : \mathrm{Investiture})

where:

  • HH is the fiber nuclear doctrine — the presheaf of fiber Heyting algebras HtH_t with saturation nucleus σt\sigma_t and transfer nucleus Δt\Delta_t commuting at each t∈Tt \in T; this is the body politic — the normative presheaf whose structure governs the fibers and exists for all t∈Tt \in T whether or not any element inhabits it
  • AA is the action domain — the set of acts and relations the office governs
  • DO:A→{power,duty,privilege,immunity}D_O : A \to \{\mathrm{power}, \mathrm{duty}, \mathrm{privilege}, \mathrm{immunity}\} is the deontic profile — the function assigning each action a Hohfeldian position independently of any holder
  • I+:InvestitureI^+ : \mathrm{Investiture} is the installation morphism — the constitutive speech act that moves an incumbency proposition ι∈Ht\iota \in H_t into Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t)

Compliance space. The compliance space of the office at history tt is:

O.Fixt=Ht∗=Fix(σt)∩Fix(Δt)O.\mathrm{Fix}_t = H^*_t = \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) \cap \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t)

An element ι∈Ht\iota \in H_t is in the office as soon as it is submitted to the nuclei. It is fully compliant iff ι∈Ht∗\iota \in H^*_t — both nuclear demands satisfied simultaneously. The obligation gap of a candidate ι\iota is (σt(ι), Δt(ι))(\sigma_t(\iota),\, \Delta_t(\iota)): how far ι\iota must move to satisfy each nucleus.

Persistence. The nuclear structure (H,σt,Δt)t∈T(H, \sigma_t, \Delta_t)_{t \in T} is a natural transformation of presheaves — not an element at any particular tt. Vacating the office means no element is currently being evaluated; the nuclei persist. This is the formal content of dignitas non moritur: the dignity does not die because the dignity IS the nuclear structure, not any element in any fiber.

¶The munus/honor/dignitas trinity

The Roman law of public service encoded in Digest Title 50.4 ("De muneribus et honoribus") provides the purest external grounding for the nuclear structure.

Munus: Callistratus (Dig. 50.4.14) defines munus as administration of public affairs with expense but without title of dignity (“cum sumptu sine titulo dignitatis”). The nuclear reading: ι∈Fix(Δt)∖Ht∗\iota \in \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) \setminus H^*_t — forward-committed but not backward-recognized. The leitourgos of Athens was compelled to fund the trireme (munus) without thereby acquiring the honor of holding office.

Honor: The honor municipalis is administration with a grade of dignity (“cum dignitatis gradu”). The key addition: σt(ι)=ι\sigma_t(\iota) = \iota — the saturation nucleus has settled ι\iota as recognized, dignitary-grade. Honor carries recognition backward; munus carries obligation forward.

Dignitas: The qualifier distinguishing honor from munus — the constitutional grade of standing. Rilinger’s analysis (Ordo und Dignitas, 2007): dignitas is inherently comparative and relational — position within a ranked order, constituted by relation to the rest of the system. The nuclear reading: ι∈Ht∗=Fix(σt)∩Fix(Δt)\iota \in H^*_t = \mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) \cap \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) — recognized backward AND committed forward simultaneously.

Modestinus’s priority rule (Dig. 50.4.10): “Honorem sustinenti munus imponi non potest: munus sustinenti honor deferri potest.” One holding an honor cannot have a munus imposed; one holding a munus may be given an honor. The nuclear reading: if ι∈Ht∗\iota \in H^*_t (full honor), then ι∈Fix(Δt)\iota \in \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) already — no new munus can impose an additional demand. But if ι∈Fix(Δt)∖Ht∗\iota \in \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) \setminus H^*_t (munus-only), the saturation nucleus may subsequently settle it into full honor.

The cursus honorum — quaestor → praetor → consul — is a chain of nuclear inclusions: Ht∗,consul⊇Ht∗,praetor⊇Ht∗,quaestorH^{*,\mathrm{consul}}_t \supseteq H^{*,\mathrm{praetor}}_t \supseteq H^{*,\mathrm{quaestor}}_t. Each higher office’s fixed fiber contains more settled obligations and confers more settled authority.

¶The King’s Two Bodies

Kantorowicz’s reconstruction of Tudor legal theory (Plowden’s Commentaries, c.1571):

“The King has in him two Bodies, viz., a Body natural, and a Body politic… his Body politic is a Body that cannot be seen or handled, consisting of Policy and Government… utterly void of Infancy, and old Age, and other natural Defects.”

Body politic IS the normative presheaf (H,σt,Δt)(H, \sigma_t, \Delta_t) over all of TT. It is not an element of any fiber. It “cannot be seen or handled” because it is not a fiber element — it is a global structure.

Body natural is the current occupant — an element ι∈Ht\iota \in H_t, indexed to tnowt_{\mathrm{now}}, mortal in the precise sense of being a fiber element that does not automatically persist to t′≠tnowt' \neq t_{\mathrm{now}} without an explicit stepping map.

Dignitas non moritur: the nuclei (σt,Δt)(\sigma_t, \Delta_t) are natural transformations — defined over all of TT. They are not consumable by any act of an incumbent. No element’s departure from HtH_t can modify them. The formal proof: the nuclei are structure on the fiber functor HH, not elements of any HtH_t; they exist for all tt by the definition of the fiber nuclear doctrine.

Kantorowicz’s genealogy: the phrase is medieval canonist formulation (not classical Roman). Baldus de Ubaldis and Johannes Andreae coined it to solve the succession problem. The answer: the dignitas never died; the authority passed instantaneously to the successor. Because the nuclear structure is not an element, succession is instantaneous — no waiting for a new element, because the nuclei were never attached to the old element.

¶The interregnum and vacancy

An office is vacant at tt when Ht∗H^*_t is structurally defined but contains no filling proposition:

O is vacant at t  ⟺  Ht∗≠∅  ∧  ∄ ιfilling∈Ht∗O \text{ is vacant at } t \iff H^*_t \neq \varnothing \;\wedge\; \nexists\, \iota_{\mathrm{filling}} \in H^*_t

The nuclear structure persists; no element currently satisfies both nucleus conditions.

The Roman interrex held ιinterrex∈Fix(Δt)∖Ht∗\iota_{\mathrm{interrex}} \in \mathrm{Fix}(\Delta_t) \setminus H^*_t — a partial section sufficient to advance the process but not constituting full incumbency. The interrex’s authority was structurally a munus without honor — pure forward obligation to reconstitute the full office.

The patrum auctoritas (Senate’s collective authority during interregnum) corresponds to the Grothendieck topology JJ on TT itself — the covering condition that determines which families of facts count as valid. The topology persists regardless of which elements inhabit any HtH_t; it is the “rule of recognition” (Hart) at the base of the site.

¶The structural opposition of offices

Madison (Federalist No. 51):

“Ambition must be made to counteract ambition. The interest of the man must be connected with the constitutional rights of the place… the private interest of every individual may be a sentinel over the public rights.”

Constitutional opposition between offices O1O_1 and O2O_2 is the condition that their nuclear structures are mutually insulated:

  1. No act of the holder of O1O_1 can unilaterally advance a proposition into Fix(σtO2)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma^{O_2}_t)
  2. No act of the holder of O2O_2 can unilaterally advance a proposition into Fix(σtO1)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma^{O_1}_t)

This is the nuclear formulation of separation of powers: each branch’s fixed fiber is closed under its own nuclear operations but not under the other’s. Joint settlement requires a constitutional procedure that traverses both nuclear structures simultaneously — the analog of a covering sieve requiring both branches’ witnessing before amalgamation.

Madison’s additional point — “each department should have a will of its own” — corresponds to each office having its own nuclear pair (σO,ΔO)(\sigma^O, \Delta^O), not shared with other offices. A system where all branches operated under the same nuclei would not constitute separation of powers; it would be a single office with multiple occupants.

The connection of personal interest to the office’s prerogatives: the obligation gap (σtO(ι)−ι,ΔtO(ι)−ι)(\sigma^O_t(\iota) - \iota, \Delta^O_t(\iota) - \iota) motivates the incumbent to reduce it. Madison’s mechanism converts personal ambition into structural self-defense.

¶Installation: the constitutive speech act

Installation has two stages corresponding to the two nuclei:

  1. The installing authority issues a status function declaration (Searle 1995, pp. 43–51): “X counts as holder of O in institution N.” This moves ι\iota from HtH_t into Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) — first-stage installation (backward recognition).
  2. Forward-stability: Δt(ι)=ι\Delta_t(\iota) = \iota — the incumbency is stable in all one-step extensions. This completes installation.
  3. Full installation: ι∈Ht∗\iota \in H^*_t — both stages complete.

The declaration must satisfy felicity conditions (Austin 1962, pp. 14–15): appropriate authority, appropriate procedure, and completeness. An attempted installation by an entity lacking the power fails the felicity condition — the declaration does not move ι\iota into Fix(σt)\mathrm{Fix}(\sigma_t) regardless of the words used.
