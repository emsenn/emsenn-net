Completed

2026-04-06 A unit of work whose goal has been achieved — the terminal state of successful work.

Completed is the terminal state of successful work. The task’s goal has been achieved. No further action is needed or possible on this unit of work.

When a task reaches completed, the part-of relation entails consequences for the whole: a completed part contributes complete to its container. When ALL parts of a plan are completed, the plan’s state is constituted as completed — not by notification but by the mereological fact that the whole is constituted by its parts.

Completed follows in-progress via an act of finishing. It is terminal — can-become is empty. A completed task cannot reopen. If the work needs to be redone, a NEW task is created (per Policy 005: accrete, don’t replace).
