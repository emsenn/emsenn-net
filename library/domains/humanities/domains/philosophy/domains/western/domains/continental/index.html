Continental Philosophy

Continental Philosophy

The tradition of European philosophy running from Hegel through phenomenology, existentialism, structuralism, and poststructuralism. Distinguished from analytic philosophy less by geography than by method: where analytic philosophy foregrounds logical clarity and conceptual analysis, continental philosophy foregrounds historical situatedness, embodiment, power, and the conditions of experience.

The term is imperfect — many thinkers cross the divide, and the divide itself is a product of 20th-century institutional politics. But it names a real clustering of concerns and methods.
