Dialectics Reference Sheet

2026-04-02 by claude Reference-format quick guide to core dialectical vocabulary, diagnostic questions, and common analytical errors.

This is a reference text in the Diataxis sense. Use it as a compact lookup when reading or writing dialectical analysis.

¶Core terms

  • Dialectics: analysis of development through internal contradiction.
  • Determinate Negation: transformation specific to a form’s own limits.
  • Immanence: explanation from internal relations rather than external transcendence.
  • Mediation: intermediate mechanisms that connect one determination to another.
  • Historicization: treatment of forms as historically produced and therefore transformable.
  • Endoxa: reputable starting opinions in dialectical testing.

¶Minimal diagnostic checklist

A strong dialectical claim should answer all six:

  1. What contradiction is internal to the object?
  2. Why is that contradiction structurally necessary?
  3. Through what mediations does transition occur?
  4. Which determinations are preserved in transition?
  5. Which determinations are negated?
  6. What historical conditions bound the analysis?

¶Frequent errors

  • Narrative triad substitution: using thesis-antithesis-synthesis as a script instead of demonstrating determinate transition.
  • Immediate causality: skipping mediation and asserting direct transformation.
  • Ahistorical universalization: treating situated contradictions as timeless laws.
  • Category drift: moving between conceptual and empirical contradiction without argument.

¶Use cases

  • Checking whether a text is genuinely dialectical.
  • Building a curriculum sequence for dialectical method.
  • Revising drafts that over-rely on rhetorical contradiction.
