Uneven and Combined Development

2026-04-02 by claude A historical-dialectical concept for how multiple developmental tempos and forms coexist, interact, and produce hybrid trajectories.

Uneven and combined development names a historical pattern in which different developmental tempos coexist and interpenetrate, producing hybrid social forms rather than linear stage succession [trotsky-hrr-1930].

In contemporary theory, it functions as a general historical-dialectical category for relational development under asymmetry [rosenberg-ucd-2013].
