Ontologization

2026-03-02 The process of treating something contingent — a social arrangement, a conceptual distinction — as if it were a fixed feature of reality, removing it from the domain of question and critique.
Defines Ontologization, ontologize

Ontologization is the process of treating something contingent — a social arrangement, a conceptual distinction, a historical product — as if it were a fixed feature of reality. To ontologize is to convert what is made into what simply is, removing it from the domain of question and critique. The operation is powerful because it is invisible: once an arrangement has been ontologized, the question of how it came to be becomes nonsensical. It did not come to be. It simply is.

The concept appears across critical theory, postcolonial thought, and philosophy of science. When a social category — race, gender, disability, nationality — is ontologized, the category is treated as naming a natural kind rather than a product of specific historical practices. Race becomes a biological fact rather than a colonial classification system. Gender becomes a natural binary rather than a historically specific organization of bodies and roles. Disability becomes an intrinsic deficit rather than a relation between a body and an environment designed without it in mind. In each case, ontologization closes the political question by converting “this arrangement could be otherwise” into “this is how things are.”

Ontologization is distinct from reification and naturalization, though the three often work together. Reification treats an abstraction as a concrete thing — treating “the economy” as an agent that acts rather than as a set of relations that people maintain. Naturalization presents the cultural as natural — treating gender norms as expressions of biological sex. Ontologization goes deeper than either: it produces the claim that the entity in question has a fixed nature that exists independently of the relations, practices, and histories that constitute it. Reification and naturalization are techniques. Ontologization is the metaphysical commitment they serve.

The relational critique of ontologization holds that entities do not have fixed natures prior to the relations that constitute them. What something is depends on the relational field in which it participates, and ontologization is the operation that denies this relational constitution by positing independent essences. Contingency is the inverse: the recognition that what exists could have been otherwise, keeping open the space of political imagination that ontologization closes.
