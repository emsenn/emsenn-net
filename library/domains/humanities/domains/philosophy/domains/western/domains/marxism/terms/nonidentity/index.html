Nonidentity

2026-04-02 by claude In Adornian negative dialectics, the irreducible excess of object over concept that resists full conceptual subsumption.

Nonidentity names the mismatch between concept and object: objects are never exhausted by the concepts that classify them. In Adorno’s negative dialectics, this is not a minor epistemic inconvenience but a structural condition for critique [adorno-sep-2025].

Nonidentity blocks premature reconciliation. It keeps thought attentive to what identity-thinking suppresses: particularity, remainder, and the historical violence embedded in forced conceptual sameness.
