Process Thinking

2025-12-31 The basic shift from substance to process: becoming is more fundamental than being, relations come first, and things emerge as temporary stabilities from ongoing happenings.

Audience: readers new to process philosophy.

Learning goal: explain the basic shift from substance thinking to process thinking and why it matters for relational ontology.

Process philosophy begins with a reversal: becoming is more fundamental than being. Where substance metaphysics treats the world as composed of things that persist through change, process philosophy treats the world as composed of happenings from which things emerge as temporary stabilities. A river is not a thing that flows but a flowing that we identify as a river. An organism is not a substance that metabolizes but a metabolizing that we identify as an organism.

This is not merely a restatement of the obvious fact that things change. Substance metaphysics acknowledges change. The difference is about what is ontologically primary. For a substance thinker, the rock exists first and then enters into relations — with gravity, with the hillside, with the observer. For a process thinker, the relations come first, and the rock is a pattern that emerges from those relations and persists only so long as they sustain it.

Alfred North Whitehead diagnosed the tendency to treat abstractions as concrete realities as the fallacy of misplaced concreteness. When we say “the rock,” we are abstracting a stable pattern from an ongoing process and treating the abstraction as though it were the fundamental reality. This move is useful — we need stable names for stable patterns — but mistaking the abstraction for the reality leads to philosophical problems that process thinking dissolves.

The shift to process thinking matters because it makes relations constitutive rather than accidental. If things are achievements of relational processes rather than substances that happen to stand in relations, then changing the relations changes the things. This is the core insight of relational ontology, and it connects process philosophy to Indigenous ontologies that have long understood beings as constituted through relations.

Check for understanding: What does it mean to say that becoming is prior to being?
