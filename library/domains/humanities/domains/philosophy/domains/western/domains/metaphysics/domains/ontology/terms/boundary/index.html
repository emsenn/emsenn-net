Boundary

2026-04-04 The locus where interior complexity meets exterior relation — the surface through which a system is known. In topology, category theory, physics, and process philosophy, boundaries are not passive edges but the structurally active sites where interaction happens.

A boundary is the surface where the interior of a thing meets what is outside it. It is not a wall or a line drawn after the fact. It is the structurally necessary interface through which a thing relates to what it is not.

¶Topology

In point-set topology, the boundary of a set S is the set of points that belong to the closure of S but not to its interior: ∂S = cl(S) \ int(S). A boundary point is one where every neighborhood contains both points in S and points not in S. The boundary is where membership is undecidable by proximity alone. Open sets exclude their boundary; closed sets include it. The distinction matters: a set’s topological character — what it can contain, what limits it can reach — is determined by how it relates to its boundary.

¶Category theory

In categorical terms, a boundary is an adjunction between bulk and surface. The inclusion of a boundary into a space and the restriction of structure from a space onto its boundary form an adjoint pair. The bulk contains more information; the boundary compresses it. What survives the compression is what is externally visible — the relational content. Sheaf theory makes this precise: sections over an open set restrict to sections over its boundary, and the obstruction to extending boundary data back to the bulk is measured by cohomology.

¶Physics: the holographic principle

The holographic principle (due to ’t Hooft and Susskind, made precise in Maldacena’s AdS/CFT correspondence) states that the information content of a volume of space is encoded on its boundary. A bulk gravitational theory in d+1 dimensions is equivalent to a boundary field theory in d dimensions. The boundary is not a summary or approximation. It is a complete encoding — everything true in the bulk is already determined by the boundary.

¶Process philosophy

In Whitehead’s process philosophy, every actual entity has a boundary in a temporal sense: the transition from its private becoming (concrescence) to its public availability for other entities (objective immortality). The boundary is where subjective experience becomes objective datum — where the entity passes from process into relation. The boundary is the locus of experience not because experience happens at edges, but because experience is the meeting of interior process with exterior world.

¶In the relational system

The Holographic Boundary Identity conjecture in this repository’s mathematical framework proposes that the subobject classifier of the bulk topos is isomorphic to the double-negation subtopos of the boundary. In plain terms: the boundary is the fixed point where the logic of the universe becomes classical. The bulk carries intuitionistic, many-valued, unsettled logic. The boundary carries Boolean, decided, stable logic. Everything true in the bulk is already fully encoded in the stable logic of the boundary.

This gives boundary a precise role in relational ontology. An entity’s boundary is not its skin or its edge. It is the projection of its full relational complexity onto the interface through which other entities encounter it. A human relating to a computer system is not modeled in full — the system knows them through their boundary: credentials, roles, attention, history. The boundary is complete for the purposes of the relation, just as the holographic boundary is complete for the physics of the bulk.
