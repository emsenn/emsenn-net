Tutorial: Tracing Dialectical Development

2026-04-02 by claude Tutorial-format walkthrough for beginners learning to trace contradiction, mediation, and transition in an argument.

This is a tutorial text. It teaches one beginner workflow through a small, guided exercise.

Prerequisites

  • Dialectics
  • Determinate Negation
  • Mediation
  • Historicization

Learning objective

By the end, you should be able to produce a five-line dialectical map of a short argument.

Step 1: Start with a tiny claim

Use this toy claim:

“A labor market that treats labor as a commodity depends on stable workers, yet competitive pressure destabilizes those workers’ lives.”

Do not debate whether the claim is true yet. First map its structure.

Step 2: Mark contradiction

Write two commitments that cannot be jointly stabilized:

  • Commitment A:
  • Commitment B:

In the toy claim:

  • A: the market needs reliable labor capacity.
  • B: market competition undermines that reliability.

Step 3: Add mediations

Now list mediating mechanisms (minimum two):

  • wage pressure,
  • housing precarity,
  • health stress,
  • turnover,
  • productivity decline.

Your list should connect contradiction to outcomes without logical leaps.

Step 4: State the transition

Write one sentence beginning with:

“Given these mediations, the prior form tends to…”

Example:

“Given these mediations, the prior market form tends to generate policy, organizational, or collective counter-measures that partially re-stabilize labor while reproducing new tensions.”

Step 5: Historicize

Add time/place bounds in one sentence:

“This map describes [period], [region], under [institutional conditions].”

Without this sentence, your map is too abstract.

Final output format

Produce exactly five lines:

  1. Object
  2. Contradiction
  3. Mediations
  4. Transition
  5. Historical bounds

If you can do this clearly, you have performed a basic dialectical analysis workflow.
