2026-03-30 Possible-world semantics for modal logic: a set of worlds, an accessibility relation, and a valuation. □p holds at w iff p holds at every accessible world.

Kripke semantics (Kripke 1963) interprets modal logic over relational structures called Kripke frames.

A Kripke frame is a pair (W,R)(W, R) where WW is a set of worlds and R⊆W×WR \subseteq W \times W is an accessibility relation. A Kripke model adds a valuation VV assigning truth values to propositions at each world.

The modal operators are defined by the accessibility relation:

  • □p\Box p holds at world ww iff pp holds at every world RR-accessible from ww.
  • ◊p\Diamond p holds at ww iff pp holds at some RR-accessible world.

Different conditions on RR yield different modal logics: reflexive gives T, reflexive and transitive gives S4, equivalence relation gives S5.

Rivieccio, Jung, & Jansana (2017) extended Kripke semantics to the four-valued bilattice: truth at each world is four-valued (⊥\bot, tt, ff, ⊤\top), and modal operators decompose into pairs of classical operators on the twist product components. The resulting four-valued modal logic is decidable.
