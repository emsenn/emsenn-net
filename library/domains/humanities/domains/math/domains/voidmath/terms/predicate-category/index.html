Predicate category

2026-04-28 The small category whose objects are predicates and whose morphisms are generated by exactly four canonical classes — specialization, converse, subject-projection, object-projection — that exhaust the natural maps between predication fiber algebras under entity-set equivariance.

  • See also

A predicate category, denoted C\mathcal{C}, is a small category whose objects are predicates and whose morphisms record the structural relationships between them. The morphisms are not freely chosen: under a Sym(E)\mathrm{Sym}(E)-equivariance hypothesis on the entity set EE (with ∣E∣≥3|E| \geq 3), every natural map between predication fiber algebras is generated by exactly four canonical classes. That the generator set is exactly four classes is a theorem about equivariance, not a stipulation.

The four-generator structure is the mathematical articulation of the structural lopsidedness that the relationality framework commits to philosophically. It is where the four-cornered shape of predication — the four positions a settlement can occupy across the knowledge and truth orders — gets its canonical content.

¶The four canonical generator classes

Each generator class is induced by a specific manipulation of the entity-pair index E×EE \times E that underlies the predication cell:

  • Specialization f:q→pf : q \to p — a sub-support inclusion dom(q)↪dom(p)\mathrm{dom}(q) \hookrightarrow \mathrm{dom}(p). The morphism encodes that qq is a sub-predicate of pp, with qq’s values inheriting from pp’s on the smaller domain and extending by ⊥\bot outside. The induced fiber-algebra map f∗:Ap→Aqf^* : A_p \to A_q is restriction along the inclusion.
  • Converse f:pc→pf : p^c \to p — induced by the swap (s,o)↦(o,s)(s, o) \mapsto (o, s) on the entity-pair index. Encodes the relational converse: “pp holds of (s,o)(s, o)” becomes “pcp^c holds of (o,s)(o, s).” Asymmetry is what allows the converse to produce a distinct predicate; in the symmetric case where a predicate equals its own converse, the generator collapses.
  • Subject-projection f:q→pf : q \to p — induced by the diagonal collapse (s,o)↦(s,s)(s, o) \mapsto (s, s). The resulting predicate qq depends on pp only through its subject-diagonal content: qq’s value at (s,o)(s, o) is pp’s value at (s,s)(s, s). This is the formal source of unary predicates as derived structures.
  • Object-projection f:q→pf : q \to p — the dual of subject-projection, induced by (s,o)↦(o,o)(s, o) \mapsto (o, o).

These four classes are residuation-natural: the twist-product fusion and right-residual on the bilattice fiber algebras are preserved by each reindexing.

¶What the structure means

The predicate category is the indexing base of the predication fibration. Reindexing along its morphisms is how predicate-relating-to-predicate translates into fiber-algebra-relating-to-fiber-algebra. Specialization gives sub-predicates; converse gives directional inversion; the projections give unary derivations. These four are the ways predicates structurally relate to one another, and the category is the structure that holds them together.

¶Why exactly four

The four-generator exhaustiveness theorem is structurally important because it explains why the framework’s four-cornered logic and four-position settlement taxonomy are not coincidences. The four corners of a bilattice (true-only, false-only, both, neither), the four positions of settlement (settled-affirmed, settled-denied, both, neither), and the four generator classes of the predicate category all share a common origin: they reflect, at different layers, the structural ways a directed triadic predication can stand. None of them is a free parameter; each is a structural consequence of taking the directed triple as primitive.
