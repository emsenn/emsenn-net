Predicate object-projection

2026-04-29 A morphism class in the predicate category, induced by the diagonal collapse (s, o) ↦ (o, o) on the entity-pair index. Builds a binary predicate q whose value at (s, o) depends only on p's value at (o, o), broadcasting the object-diagonal content. The dual of subject-projection. One of the four canonical generators of the predicate category.

A predicate object-projection is a morphism in the predicate category, denoted f:q→pf : q \to p, induced by the collapse of the entity-pair index to the object diagonal:

(s,o)↦(o,o).(s, o) \mapsto (o, o).

The induced fiber-algebra map f∗:Ap→Aqf^* : A_p \to A_q acts cellwise:

f∗(v)(s,o)=v(o,o).f^*(v)(s, o) = v(o, o).

The resulting predicate qq has values that depend only on pp’s object-diagonal content: at every cell (s,o)(s, o), qq inherits whatever value pp takes at (o,o)(o, o). The subject coordinate ss becomes irrelevant — it doesn’t enter the definition of qq’s value at all. Object-projection is the dual of subject-projection, and one of the four canonical generator classes of the predicate category.

¶What object-projection encodes

Object-projection is the formal source of unary predicates that report on the object of a binary relation. While subject-projection extracts subject-diagonal content (the predicate becomes effectively a property of the subject alone), object-projection extracts object-diagonal content (the predicate becomes effectively a property of the object alone).

Examples:

  • A predicate target-is-employed (effectively unary on the object) appears as the object-projection of employs, where the diagonal content of employs records the employment status when the object coincides with itself.
  • A predicate recipient-is-eligible appears as the object-projection of eligible-for-receiving-from-self, broadcast across all subject coordinates.

¶Subject vs. object projection

The two projections capture the two ways of deriving a unary predicate from a binary one:

Projection Diagonal Resulting unary content
Subject-projection (s,o)↦(s,s)(s, o) \mapsto (s, s) depends on the subject only
Object-projection (s,o)↦(o,o)(s, o) \mapsto (o, o) depends on the object only

Both projections collapse one of the cell’s two entity coordinates onto the other. Subject-projection collapses object onto subject; object-projection collapses subject onto object. The resulting binary predicate is constant in the collapsed coordinate.

¶Composition with converse

Object-projection and subject-projection are interconvertible via converse: composing object-projection with converse yields subject-projection, and vice versa. The swap (s,o)↦(o,s)(s, o) \mapsto (o, s) converts collapse-onto-object-diagonal into collapse-onto-subject-diagonal:

fconv∘fop=fspf_{\mathrm{conv}} \circ f_{\mathrm{op}} = f_{\mathrm{sp}}

at the generator-class level. The four canonical generators are not entirely independent — they admit composition relations of this kind, which together encode the predicate category’s structure under Sym(E)\mathrm{Sym}(E)-equivariance.
