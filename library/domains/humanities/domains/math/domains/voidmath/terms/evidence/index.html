Evidence

2026-04-29 The role borne by the two components of a twist-product value: positive evidence (first coordinate) tracks information for an assertion; negative evidence (second coordinate) tracks information against. The two evidence kinds behave dually under the bilattice's two parallel orders.

Evidence is the role borne by the two coordinates of a twist-product value ⟨a+,a−⟩∈L×L\langle a_+, a_- \rangle \in L \times L. The first coordinate a+a_+ tracks positive evidence — information for the assertion. The second coordinate a−a_- tracks negative evidence — information against. Together they record the two-sided evidential situation a value reports.

The two evidence kinds are not just two lattice components used as bookkeeping. They behave differently under the two parallel orders of the bilattice, and the difference is what makes the framework’s settlement structure four-cornered rather than bivalent.

¶Behavior under the two orders

  • The truth order ≤t\leq_t raises positive evidence and lowers negative evidence. A value tilts toward affirmation when its positive component grows and its negative component shrinks. The truth order’s directionality is the asymmetry: it is monotone in a+a_+ and antitone in a−a_-.
  • The knowledge order ≤k\leq_k raises both components. A value rises in the knowledge order whenever any evidence accumulates, regardless of direction. The knowledge order does not distinguish affirming from denying evidence; it counts amount.

This dual behavior is what makes the bilattice non-trivial. A value with much positive and much negative evidence (high ⊤\top) is high on the knowledge axis (lots of evidence) but mid on the truth axis (the evidence cancels). A value with no evidence in either direction (low ⊥\bot) is low on both axes. These two cases are structurally different — both-witnessed-paraconsistently versus un-witnessed — and the bilattice records the difference.

¶Negation as evidence-swap

Negation on the twist product is exactly the swap of evidence components:

∼⟨a+,a−⟩=⟨a−,a+⟩.\sim \langle a_+, a_- \rangle = \langle a_-, a_+ \rangle.

The positive evidence of the negated value is the negative evidence of the original; the negative is the positive. This is consistent with the truth-axis behavior of negation (antitone — flips direction) and the knowledge-axis behavior (monotone — preserves amount): swapping components reverses the truth-axis tilt while preserving the total information.

¶Four canonical configurations

The four-element bilattice FOUR\mathbf{FOUR} instantiates the four canonical evidence configurations:

Value Positive evidence Negative evidence Reading
⊥=⟨0,0⟩\bot = \langle 0, 0 \rangle none none no evidence at all
t=⟨1,0⟩\mathbf{t} = \langle 1, 0 \rangle full none only positive — told true
f=⟨0,1⟩\mathbf{f} = \langle 0, 1 \rangle none full only negative — told false
⊤=⟨1,1⟩\top = \langle 1, 1 \rangle full full both — told both (paraconsistent)

These four corners are the bilattice’s extremal positions; everything else is interior — partial evidence in one or both directions, intermediate truth-axis tilt and intermediate knowledge-axis position.

¶Evidence and settlement

A value’s settlement position is its joint coordinate on the two axes — what evidence it carries and in what direction. Settlement is not an abstract truth-value attached to the value; it is the joint shape of the value’s evidence. Reporting a value’s settlement and reporting its evidence are the same act, viewed at the joint and component levels.
