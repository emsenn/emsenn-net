Pre-bilattice

2026-04-29 An algebra carrying two parallel lattice structures on a common carrier — one for the truth order, one for the knowledge order — without yet the negation that makes a bilattice. The structural skeleton on which the voidmath bilattice is built.

A pre-bilattice is an algebra B=⟨B,∧t,∨t,∧k,∨k⟩\mathbf{B} = \langle B, \wedge_t, \vee_t, \wedge_k, \vee_k \rangle in which both ⟨B,∧t,∨t⟩\langle B, \wedge_t, \vee_t \rangle and ⟨B,∧k,∨k⟩\langle B, \wedge_k, \vee_k \rangle are lattices on the common carrier BB.

The order associated with the first lattice is the truth order ≤t\leq_t; the order associated with the second is the knowledge order ≤k\leq_k. When both lattices are bounded, their extremes are denoted f,t\mathbf{f}, \mathbf{t} (bottom and top in ≤t\leq_t) and ⊥,⊤\bot, \top (bottom and top in ≤k\leq_k).

¶Interlacing and distributivity

A pre-bilattice is interlaced if each of the four lattice operations is monotone with respect to both orders: e.g., ∧t\wedge_t is monotone in ≤k\leq_k as well as in ≤t\leq_t. Interlacing is what couples the two lattice structures into a single coherent algebra rather than two independent lattices on the same carrier.

A pre-bilattice is distributive if all twelve distributive laws

x∘(y∙z)≈(x∘y)∙(x∘z)x \circ (y \bullet z) \approx (x \circ y) \bullet (x \circ z)

hold for distinct ∘,∙∈{∧t,∨t,∧k,∨k}\circ, \bullet \in \{\wedge_t, \vee_t, \wedge_k, \vee_k\}.

¶Pre-bilattice vs. bilattice

A pre-bilattice equipped with a unary negation operation — antitone in ≤t\leq_t, monotone in ≤k\leq_k, and involutive — is a bilattice. The pre-bilattice is the structure before negation is supplied; the bilattice is the structure with negation.

The framework’s underlying value algebras are interlaced distributive pre-bilattices that admit negation: the twist product L⋈\mathbf{L}^\bowtie over a residuated lattice produces an interlaced distributive pre-bilattice as its (∧t,∨t,∧k,∨k)(\wedge_t, \vee_t, \wedge_k, \vee_k)-reduct, and the swap-of-components negation upgrades it to a bilattice.
