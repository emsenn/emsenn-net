Predicate specialization

2026-04-29 A morphism class in the predicate category, given by a sub-support inclusion dom(q) ↪ dom(p) between predicates. Encodes the relation 'q is a sub-predicate of p': q's cells are a structurally designated subset of p's cells, with q's values inherited from p's on that subset and extended by ⊥ outside. One of the four canonical generators of the predicate category.

A predicate specialization is a morphism in the predicate category, denoted f:q→pf : q \to p, given by a sub-support inclusion

ιf:dom(q)↪dom(p)\iota_f : \mathrm{dom}(q) \hookrightarrow \mathrm{dom}(p)

where dom(p),dom(q)⊆E×E\mathrm{dom}(p), \mathrm{dom}(q) \subseteq E \times E are structurally designated sub-supports attached to pp and qq within the entity set EE.

The induced fiber-algebra map f∗:Ap→Aqf^* : A_p \to A_q is restriction along ιf\iota_f, extending by ⊥\bot outside the smaller domain:

f∗(v)(s,o)={v(s,o)if (s,o)∈dom(q)⊥otherwise.f^*(v)(s, o) = \begin{cases} v(s, o) & \text{if } (s, o) \in \mathrm{dom}(q) \\ \bot & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases}

A specialization encodes that qq is a sub-predicate of pp: qq’s cells are a structurally designated subset of pp’s cells, with qq’s values inherited from pp’s on that subset and extended by ⊥\bot outside. Specialization is one of the four canonical generator classes of the predicate category.

¶What specialization encodes

Specialization captures the inheritance-by-restriction relationship between predicates whose domains differ:

  • Domain narrowing: a predicate parent-of-a-minor specializes parent-of: its cells are exactly the parent-of cells where the object is a minor.
  • Sortedness: when the entity set is sorted, predicates may apply only on certain sort combinations; specialization is the morphism from the sorted predicate to the broader one.
  • Subkind relations: in any taxonomic structure, a more specific predicate is a specialization of a more general one.

The specialization morphism is order-preserving on cells but only partially defined on values — values extend by ⊥\bot outside the smaller domain, which is the formal expression of “the more specific predicate doesn’t apply here, so we have no information about it.”

¶Specialization as the fibration’s substitution

In the predication fibration, specialization is the prototypical case of substitution: the reindexing functor f∗:Ep→Eqf^* : \mathcal{E}_p \to \mathcal{E}_q along a specialization morphism is restriction-and-extend-by-bottom. This is the cleanest structural connection between the predicate category and the fiber algebras: specialization at the predicate level becomes restriction at the fiber-algebra level.

¶Composition

Specialization morphisms compose by composition of inclusions: f:q→pf : q \to p and g:p→rg : p \to r compose to g∘f:q→rg \circ f : q \to r if dom(q)⊆dom(p)⊆dom(r)\mathrm{dom}(q) \subseteq \mathrm{dom}(p) \subseteq \mathrm{dom}(r). Composition with the other three generator classes — converse, subject-projection, object-projection — yields morphisms that may not themselves be specializations: the composition rules are part of the predicate category’s structure.
