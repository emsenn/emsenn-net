Predication fiber algebra

2026-04-28 The algebra of values held at the cells of a single predicate — the pointwise twist product of a residuated lattice over the entity-pair index. Bilattice-residuated, with two parallel orders (knowledge and truth) and the operations that lift from the underlying lattice.

The predication fiber algebra at a predicate pp, denoted ApA_p, is the algebra of values that can be held at every predication cell with predicate coordinate fixed at pp. Concretely, it is the pointwise twist product of a residuated lattice L\mathbf{L} over the entity-pair index E×EE \times E:

Ap:=L⋈ E×E.A_p := \mathbf{L}^{\bowtie\, E \times E}.

An element of ApA_p is a function E×E→L×LE \times E \to L \times L — at each subject–object pair (s,o)(s, o), a value v(s,o)∈L⋈v(s, o) \in \mathbf{L}^\bowtie in the bilattice. As an abstract algebra, ApA_p is the same for every predicate; the subscript pp records which predicate’s values are being held, not a difference in the algebra’s structure.

¶Two parallel orders

The fiber algebra is a bilattice at every cell: a structure with two distinct partial orders, the knowledge order ≤k\leq_k and the truth order ≤t\leq_t. Each value v(s,o)∈L⋈v(s, o) \in \mathbf{L}^\bowtie sits in the joint space of these two axes — its position is its settlement at that cell.

  • ≤k\leq_k measures how much evidence has accumulated. Bottom ⊥\bot is no information; top ⊤\top is full evidence in both directions. This is the math-side counterpart of the absurd ↔ determination axis.
  • ≤t\leq_t measures which direction the evidence tilts. Bottom f\mathbf{f} is denial; top t\mathbf{t} is affirmation. This is the math-side counterpart of the false ↔ true axis.

The two orders are independent. A value’s joint position is its settlement; the four extremal positions t,f,⊤,⊥\mathbf{t}, \mathbf{f}, \top, \bot are the four corners of the joint structure.

¶Operations

The bilattice operations (∧t,∨t,∧k,∨k,∼)(\wedge_t, \vee_t, \wedge_k, \vee_k, \sim) lift pointwise from L⋈\mathbf{L}^\bowtie to ApA_p: for example, (v∧kv′)(s,o):=v(s,o)∧kv′(s,o)(v \wedge_k v')(s, o) := v(s, o) \wedge_k v'(s, o). When L\mathbf{L} is distributive, so is ApA_p.

Two further operations from the residuation structure are central:

  • Twist-product fusion ⊙\odot — the monoidal multiplication on L⋈\mathbf{L}^\bowtie, lifted pointwise. Combines values at the same cell.
  • Twist-product right residual ⇒\Rightarrow — the right adjoint of fusion, giving the implication structure on the bilattice.

These are what make ApA_p a residuated bilattice rather than just a bilattice, and what give the predicate category’s four generator classes the residuation-natural property: each reindexing preserves ⊙\odot and ⇒\Rightarrow pointwise.

¶Why one algebra per predicate

Because ApA_p is structurally the same for every predicate, the differences between predicates live entirely in the morphisms of the predicate category, not in the value algebras themselves. Specialization, converse, and the projections give the structural relationships between predicates; the fiber algebra gives the value structure that those relationships preserve. This separation — uniform fibers, structured base — is what makes the predication fibration cleanly fibrational.
