Entity

2026-04-29 An element of an entity set — the kind of thing predicates discuss. An entity occupies the subject or object position of a predication cell; it is the relatum-side of relating, the underlying individual that predication makes claims about.

An entity is an element of an entity set, denoted ee. Entities are the individuals or nodes that predicates discuss. Each entity is just an element of a set; the entity itself carries no required structure beyond set membership.

In the predication framework, every entity may occupy the subject or object position of a predication cell. The first and third coordinates of a cell (s,p,o)(s, p, o) are entities; the cell records that predicate pp holds of subject ss in relation to object oo. The same entity may occupy the subject coordinate of one cell and the object coordinate of another; being an entity is not specific to any role within a particular cell.

¶Entities are the relata-side of predication

The framework’s primitive is the directed triadic relating that constitutes its terms. Entities are what those terms are once produced: the relata-side, the structural slots that the directed act fills. Without entities, there are no positions for the predicate to run between; without the predicate’s directional run, the entities have no determinate position. Entities and predicates are co-defined by the predication structure.

Entities should not be conflated with substances. An entity in this framework is just an element — a position in the entity set — not a thing-with-essential-properties that pre-exists its relations. What the entity is is determined by its role within the predication cells it occupies, not by intrinsic content the entity carries on its own.

¶Sortedness

Specific applications may equip the entity set with sorts (types), so that some entities are of one kind and some another, and some predicates apply only to entities of certain sorts. Sortedness is an additional structure on top of bare set-hood; the framework treats sorted entity sets as a refinement, not a different primitive.
