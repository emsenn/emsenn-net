Settlement

2026-04-28 The four-cornered joint structure of values in the voidmath bilattice — composed of two independent axes (truth and knowledge) whose extremal positions give the four corners a predication value can occupy. The mathematical articulation of the relationality framework's settlement concept.

In voidmath, settlement names the four-cornered joint structure within which every predication value sits. It is not a single position but a position in a two-dimensional space — the space whose two axes are the truth order (≤t\leq_t, running from f\mathbf{f} to t\mathbf{t}) and the knowledge order (≤k\leq_k, running from ⊥\bot to ⊤\top). The four corners of this space are the extremal positions a value can occupy in the bilattice; the interior is the mass of partial settlements that constitute most actual valuations.

Settlement is the math-side articulation of the relationality framework’s settlement concept. The two domains describe the same structure in different vocabularies: where the philosophical voice speaks of absurd, determination, true, and false as positions, the math voice speaks of ⊥\bot, ⊤\top, t\mathbf{t}, and f\mathbf{f} as the corresponding bilattice extremes.

¶The two axes

Axis Math notation Pole bottom Pole top Philosophical pair
Truth ≤t\leq_t f\mathbf{f} t\mathbf{t} false ↔ true
Knowledge ≤k\leq_k ⊥\bot ⊤\top absurd ↔ determination

The two orders are independent. A value can be high in one and low in the other; combinations of axis-position give the joint settlement.

¶The four corners

The bilattice has four extremal elements, each occupying a corner of the joint space:

Corner Math Truth-axis Knowledge-axis Reading
true-only t\mathbf{t} top mid “Affirmed and structurally witnessed”
false-only f\mathbf{f} bottom mid “Denied and structurally witnessed”
both ⊤\top mid top “Witnessed in both directions; paraconsistent determination”
neither ⊥\bot mid bottom “Un-witnessed; absurd”

Classical bivalent logic recognizes only t\mathbf{t} and f\mathbf{f}. Three-valued logics (Kleene, intuitionistic) admit ⊥\bot as a third value. Bilattice logic admits all four corners as positive structural data — none of them is a defective version of another, and every value in the bilattice is a position in this joint space.

¶Settlement as accumulation

In a temporal setting — where predication valuations evolve as evidence accumulates — settlement is the dynamic at the joint level: each step adds witnessing, raising the knowledge-axis position and possibly tilting the truth-axis position. The settling monotonicity condition formalizes this: knowledge values rise monotonically in ≤k\leq_k over time, never retracting.

A value is settled (locally) when it has reached a fixed point: further evidence does not change its position. This is the math-side counterpart of determination in the relationality framework. Settlement does not require reaching the ⊤\top corner; a value can settle at any position the available evidence supports.

¶Settlement and the predication fiber algebra

Settlement is not a separate construction added to the bilattice; it is the joint-axis structure already present in the predication fiber algebra ApA_p. Each value v(s,o)∈L⋈v(s, o) \in \mathbf{L}^\bowtie at a predication cell sits somewhere in the four-corner space; the fiber algebra holds an entire field of such positions over E×EE \times E. Operations on the fiber algebra (the bilattice operations ∧t,∨t,∧k,∨k,∼\wedge_t, \vee_t, \wedge_k, \vee_k, \sim and the residuated operations ⊙,⇒\odot, \Rightarrow) are the structural moves that combine and transform settlements.
