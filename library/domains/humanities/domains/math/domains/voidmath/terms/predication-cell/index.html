Predication cell

2026-04-28 The minimum writable unit of predication: an ordered triple (subject, predicate, object) at which a value can be assigned. The directed triadic structure that relationality's primitive paradox spells out into when fully articulated.

A predication cell is an ordered triple ϕ=(s,p,o)\phi = (s, p, o) where ss is a subject (an entity), pp is a predicate, and oo is an object (also an entity). The triple records the proposition predicate pp holds of subject ss in relation to object oo. Predication cells are the elements of the predication cell set Φ=E×Π×E\Phi = E \times \Pi \times E, where EE is a set of entities and Π\Pi is a set of predicates.

The cell is the smallest unit at which a predication valuation can assign a value. The value lives in a residuated bilattice L⋈\mathbf{L}^\bowtie associated to the predicate; the cell is where the value is assigned, the value is what is assigned. Predication cells are not relations in their own right — they are the directed triadic shapes that relations take when written out.

¶The directed triadic structure

The cell has three coordinates, and the three are not interchangeable. The first and third (subject and object) are entities and could in principle be swapped, but doing so produces a different cell, related to the original by the predicate converse generator rather than equal to it. The second coordinate (the predicate) is the directional element: it holds of ss in relation to oo, not of oo in relation to ss. The cell is directed by construction.

This directionality is the structural form of what the relationality framework calls lopsidedness in relating, and is what survives of the relationlessness paradox once the paradox has been spelled out: the would-be symmetric absence resolves into a directed triple, not into nothing. The cell is the minimum writable form in which the paradox can be expressed; there is no shorter form.

¶Coordinates

  • Subject (ss) — the first entity coordinate. The predicate is asserted of the subject.
  • Predicate (pp) — the directed element. Holds from subject toward object.
  • Object (oo) — the second entity coordinate. The predicate is asserted in relation to the object.

Unary predicates (those that depend only on one entity, not on a pair) appear as the special case where the predicate has been pre-projected onto the subject or object diagonal — formally, where one of the projection generators of the predicate category has been applied. The cell shape itself never collapses below three coordinates; the binary base E×EE \times E of the entity index is the minimal index, and unary predicates are derived structurally rather than admitted as a separate primitive.
