Bilattice homomorphism

2026-04-29 A function between bilattices preserving all four lattice operations and negation (and conflation, when present). The structure-preserving morphism class for bilattices; the natural bottom-preserving such homomorphisms between predication fiber algebras are exactly the maps induced by the four predicate-category generators.

A bilattice homomorphism is a function h:B1→B2h : \mathbf{B}_1 \to \mathbf{B}_2 between bilattices preserving all four lattice operations and the negation:

  • h(a∧tb)=h(a)∧th(b)h(a \wedge_t b) = h(a) \wedge_t h(b) and h(a∨tb)=h(a)∨th(b)h(a \vee_t b) = h(a) \vee_t h(b);
  • h(a∧kb)=h(a)∧kh(b)h(a \wedge_k b) = h(a) \wedge_k h(b) and h(a∨kb)=h(a)∨kh(b)h(a \vee_k b) = h(a) \vee_k h(b);
  • h(∼a)=∼h(a)h(\sim a) = \sim h(a).

When the bilattices carry a conflation, a bilattice homomorphism is additionally required to preserve it: h(−a)=−h(a)h(-a) = -h(a).

A bilattice homomorphism need not preserve bounds. A homomorphism that additionally preserves the knowledge-order bottom — h(⊥)=⊥h(\bot) = \bot — is called bottom-preserving.

¶Bottom-preserving homomorphisms and the predicate category

The natural bottom-preserving bilattice homomorphisms between predication fiber algebras are exactly the maps induced by the four predicate category generator classes: specialization, converse, subject-projection, and object-projection. Each generator f:q→pf : q \to p induces a fiber-algebra map f∗:Ap→Aqf^* : A_p \to A_q that is a bottom-preserving bilattice homomorphism.

This is what makes the predicate category’s structure cleanly fibrational: the morphisms in the base correspond to the structure-preserving maps in the fibers. The fiber algebras form a strict diagram of bilattices indexed by the predicate category, with the four generator classes giving the four kinds of structural relationship between predicates that the framework recognizes.
