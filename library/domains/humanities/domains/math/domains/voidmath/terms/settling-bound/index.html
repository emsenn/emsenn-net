Settling bound

2026-04-29 An ordinal λ that bounds the settling index of a family of predication valuations: settling steps τ range over τ < λ. Typically ω (countable settlings) but may be any fixed ordinal, supporting transfinite settlings while avoiding the size-theoretic issues of indexing by the class of all ordinals.

A settling bound is an ordinal λ\lambda that bounds the settling index of a family of predication valuations. Settling steps τ\tau range over τ<λ\tau < \lambda; the family is (vτ)τ<λ(v_\tau)_{\tau < \lambda}.

The settling bound is typically ω\omega — the regime of countable settlings, in which finite-convergence theorems live — but may be any fixed ordinal. Bounding by a fixed λ\lambda avoids the size-theoretic issues of indexing by the class of all ordinals while retaining the flexibility to support transfinite settlings, in which closure operators take strictly more than ω\omega-many iterations to reach quiescence.

¶Regimes by ordinal size

The choice of λ\lambda determines the regime of settling:

  • λ=ω\lambda = \omega — Scott-continuous closures reach quiescence in at most ω\omega steps; the strict and Scott-continuous fragments of the framework live here.
  • ω<λ≤\omega < \lambda \leq countable — transfinite-but-bounded regime; non-Scott-continuous closures may require iteration past ω\omega but stabilize within a fixed countable ordinal.
  • larger λ\lambda — aperiodic regime; settlings whose quiescence ordinal is not bounded by any small ordinal.

The framework’s structural results adapt to the regime; the bound λ\lambda is the parameter that selects which regime is in scope.
