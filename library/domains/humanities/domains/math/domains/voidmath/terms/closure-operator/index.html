Closure operator

2026-04-29 A family of monotone, inflationary, idempotent, and naturally-coherent maps on the predication fiber algebras — one per predicate — implementing consequence-closure under inference rules. The operator-theoretic engine of settling: each settling step takes a valuation to its closure.

A closure operator on the predication fiber functor F:Cop→DBiLat(L)F : \mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{DBiLat}(\mathbf{L}) is a family of maps cl=(clp)p∈C\mathrm{cl} = (\mathrm{cl}_p)_{p \in \mathcal{C}}, one per predicate, with clp:Ap→Ap\mathrm{cl}_p : A_p \to A_p on each predication fiber algebra, satisfying four axioms:

  1. Monotone in ≤k\leq_k: v≤kv′⇒clp(v)≤kclp(v′)v \leq_k v' \Rightarrow \mathrm{cl}_p(v) \leq_k \mathrm{cl}_p(v') in the knowledge order.
  2. Inflationary: v≤kclp(v)v \leq_k \mathrm{cl}_p(v).
  3. Idempotent: clp∘clp=clp\mathrm{cl}_p \circ \mathrm{cl}_p = \mathrm{cl}_p.
  4. Natural in C\mathcal{C}: for every morphism f:q→pf : q \to p in the predicate category C\mathcal{C}, f∗∘clp=clq∘f∗.f^* \circ \mathrm{cl}_p = \mathrm{cl}_q \circ f^*.

Conditions (1)–(3) are the classical lattice-theoretic closure axioms applied per fiber: each clp\mathrm{cl}_p is a closure operator on the knowledge-order lattice (Ap,≤k)(A_p, \leq_k) in the standard sense. Condition (4) is the additional content imposed by the family structure: the closures at distinct predicates are coherent under reindexing — closing then reindexing equals reindexing then closing.

¶Interpretation

The closure clp(v)\mathrm{cl}_p(v) is the consequence closure of the valuation vv under the inference rules encoded in cl\mathrm{cl}. Different applications choose different closures:

  • For a logic-programming-style settling, cl\mathrm{cl} implements Fitting’s fixed-point operator per fiber.
  • For a description-logic-style settling, cl\mathrm{cl} implements TBox-driven inference.
  • For the framework’s general settling regime, any closure operator satisfying the four axioms is admissible.

The spatiotemporal knowledge presheaf definition is closure-agnostic; applications choose cl\mathrm{cl} to suit.

¶Generators

A closure operator can be specified syntactically by a closure-rule system — a family of positive Π1\Pi_1-schemas in the inherited signature on (L,C,E)(\mathbf{L}, \mathcal{C}, E). The generated closure is the least operator satisfying every rule consequent whenever the corresponding antecedent holds. The rule-level syntactic structure is what makes the operator’s Scott-continuity (and hence the settling regime it produces) visible: rules with quantifier-free antecedents generate Scott-continuous closures; rules with ∀\forall-blocks over infinite domains generate non-Scott-continuous closures.

¶Role in settling

Settling, as the framework’s evolution mechanism, is iteration of cl\mathrm{cl}. Starting from a base valuation v0v_0, the family vτ:=clτ(v0)v_\tau := \mathrm{cl}^\tau(v_0) traces the inquiry’s progress through the knowledge order; settling monotonicity is automatic from axioms (1)–(2). The closure determines what counts as inference at each step; the framework supplies the rest of the structure (the bilattice, the predicate category, the fibration) within which that inference operates.
