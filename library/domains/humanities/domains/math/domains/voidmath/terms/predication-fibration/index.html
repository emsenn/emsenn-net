Predication fibration

2026-04-28 The cloven Grothendieck fibration whose fibers are predication fiber algebras and whose reindexing along each predicate-category generator acts cellwise by the manipulation of the entity-pair index that generator specifies. The fibrational layer of the predication framework.

A predication fibration is a cloven Grothendieck fibration

p:E↠Cp : \mathcal{E} \twoheadrightarrow \mathcal{C}

over a predicate category C\mathcal{C}, whose fibers are predication fiber algebras and whose reindexing along each of the four generator classes acts cellwise by the manipulation of the entity-pair index E×EE \times E specified by that generator. It is the fibrational layer of the framework — the structure that ties predicates (objects of C\mathcal{C}) to the values held at their cells (elements of the fibers Ep≅Ap\mathcal{E}_p \cong A_p) in a way that makes substitution and reindexing well-behaved.

Three conditions characterize a predication fibration over base parameters (L,C,E)(\mathbf{L}, \mathcal{C}, E) — a residuated lattice, a small predicate category with finite limits, and an entity set:

(A1) Fiber uniformity. For every q∈Cq \in \mathcal{C}, the fiber Eq:=p−1(q)\mathcal{E}_q := p^{-1}(q) is canonically isomorphic to the predication fiber algebra Aq:=L⋈ E×EA_q := \mathbf{L}^{\bowtie\,E\times E}, viewed as a thin category via its ≤k\leq_k-poset.

(A2) Generator action. For each of the four predicate-category generator classes — specialization, converse, subject-projection, object-projection — the reindexing functor f∗:Ep→Eqf^* : \mathcal{E}_p \to \mathcal{E}_q for f:q→pf : q \to p acts on fiber elements by the cellwise reindexing specified by that generator’s manipulation of the entity-pair index.

(A3) Fiber structure preservation. For every ff in C\mathcal{C}, the reindexing f∗f^* is a bilattice-residuated homomorphism: it preserves the bilattice operations ∧t,∨t,∧k,∨k,∼\wedge_t, \vee_t, \wedge_k, \vee_k, \sim and the residuated operations ⊙\odot (twist-product fusion) and ⇒\Rightarrow (right residual), pointwise.

¶Equivalent formulations

A cloven fibration over C\mathcal{C} corresponds bijectively (by Grothendieck transposition) to a pseudo-functor Cop→Cat\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Cat}. For predication fibrations, this pseudo-functor lands in DBiLat(L)\mathbf{DBiLat}(\mathbf{L}), the 2-category of distributive bilattices over L\mathbf{L}. The transpose of a predication fibration is a predication fiber functor.

By the Emmenegger fibration 2-comonad result, cloven fibrations over C\mathcal{C} are exactly pseudo-coalgebras for the lax-idempotent 2-comonad N\mathbb{N} on Cat/C\mathbf{Cat}/\mathcal{C}. A predication fibration is therefore equivalently a pseudo-N\mathbb{N}-coalgebra satisfying (A1)–(A3). Pseudo-coalgebra rather than strict coalgebra reflects the non-strict composition of generators in the predicate category.

¶The 2-category of predication fibrations

The full sub-2-category of Cat/C\mathbf{Cat}/\mathcal{C} on predication fibrations, denoted PrFib(L,C,E)\mathbf{PrFib}(\mathbf{L}, \mathcal{C}, E), has cloven-fibration morphisms respecting (A1)–(A3) as 1-cells and natural transformations of fibration morphisms as 2-cells. This 2-category is the ambient structure for the closure 2-monad whose pseudo-algebras are predication hyperdoctrines.

¶In the layer stack

The predication fibration is one layer in a property-like stack:

  1. The predicate category gives the indexing base.
  2. The predication fibration adds the cloven-fibration structure that ties predicates to their fiber algebras with well-behaved reindexing.
  3. The predication hyperdoctrine adds parallel adjoint quantifier systems.

Each layer is property-like (Kelly–Lack): present-or-not, no choice involved, unique up to unique iso when present.
