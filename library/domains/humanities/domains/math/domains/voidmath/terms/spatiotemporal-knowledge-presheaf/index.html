Spatiotemporal knowledge presheaf

2026-04-29 A settling-monotone family of predication valuations indexed by a settling bound. The full sheaf-theoretic object encoding the framework's evolution: a settle-indexed family of valuations whose pointwise values rise monotonically in the knowledge order. The bridge between static settlement structure and dynamic settlement structure.

Fix a residuated lattice L\mathbf{L}, a predicate category C\mathcal{C}, an entity set EE, and a settling bound λ\lambda.

A spatiotemporal knowledge presheaf over (L,C,E,λ)(\mathbf{L}, \mathcal{C}, E, \lambda) is a family v=(vτ)τ<λv = (v_\tau)_{\tau < \lambda} in which:

  • each vτv_\tau is a predication valuation, assigning to every predicate p∈Cp \in \mathcal{C} an element vτ(p)∈Apv_\tau(p) \in A_p of the predication fiber algebra at pp;
  • the family satisfies settling monotonicity — for all σ≤τ<λ\sigma \leq \tau < \lambda and every pp,
vσ(p)≤kvτ(p)v_\sigma(p) \leq_k v_\tau(p)

pointwise in ApA_p, where ≤k\leq_k is the knowledge order.

¶What it encodes

A spatiotemporal knowledge presheaf is the framework’s central temporal-evidence object. It packages, into a single coherent structure:

  • the spatial dimension — the predication-cell layout E×Π×EE \times \Pi \times E over which vτv_\tau assigns values, with the predicate category coordinating across predicates;
  • the temporal dimension — the settling index τ<λ\tau < \lambda, ordering the steps of inquiry;
  • the evidential dimension — the bilattice values vτ(p)(s,o)∈L⋈v_\tau(p)(s, o) \in \mathbf{L}^\bowtie recording how much affirming and denying evidence has accumulated, at each cell, by step τ\tau;
  • the honesty dimension — the monotonicity condition, which forbids the loss of evidence under settling.

The four together are what the framework calls spatiotemporal knowledge: knowledge as positioned in space and time, accumulating monotonically, structured by the predicate category and valued in the bilattice.

¶Bridge between static and dynamic

The static settlement structure — the bilattice fiber algebra at one moment τ\tau — describes a snapshot of the inquiry. The dynamic settlement structure — a family vv evolving over λ\lambda — describes the trajectory. Without monotonicity, the dynamic structure would be just a sequence of snapshots; with monotonicity, it is a presheaf with the knowledge-order-monotone evolution that the framework’s commitments require.

The presheaf is the structural object that supports the framework’s higher constructions: settling cohomology, settling-associated sheaves, paraconsistent settling, and the various moduli structures all live over the spatiotemporal knowledge presheaf as their base data.

¶Lean mechanization scope

The Lean structure SpatiotemporalKnowledgePresheaf L P E covers a restricted parameter range:

  • Settling bound: Nat (= ω\omega) instead of arbitrary λ\lambda. The strict and Scott-continuous regimes (κ≤ω\kappa \leq \omega) are in scope; transfinite-bounded and aperiodic regimes (ω<κ\omega < \kappa) require additional ordinal infrastructure and live in a separate module.
  • Predicate index: a bare type P instead of a predicate category C\mathcal{C} with the four-generator structure. The categorical structure on predicates — specialization, converse, projections — is deferred.
  • Lattice strength: [Lattice L] instead of a residuated lattice. The residuated structure is needed for fusion and residual on the predication fiber algebra, not for the bare presheaf definition; the basic structure is genuinely independent of residuation.

The Lean type therefore mechanizes the strict / Scott-continuous fragment of the prose definition’s parameter space. The full transfinite, categorical, and residuated structure is available in the prose corpus and pending mechanization in dedicated modules.
