Residuated lattice

2026-04-29 The underlying value algebra of the framework: a lattice equipped with a commutative monoid product (fusion) and its residual implication, related by the residuation adjunction. The chosen residuated lattice is what gets pair-doubled into the twist product to produce the voidmath bilattice.

A commutative residuated lattice is an algebra L=⟨L,∨,∧,⋅,→,1⟩\mathbf{L} = \langle L, \vee, \wedge, \cdot, \to, 1 \rangle in which ⟨L,∨,∧⟩\langle L, \vee, \wedge \rangle is a lattice, ⟨L,⋅,1⟩\langle L, \cdot, 1 \rangle is a commutative monoid, and the binary operations ⋅\cdot (the fusion product) and →\to (the residual) satisfy the residuation adjunction:

x⋅y≤z  ⟺  x≤y→zx \cdot y \leq z \iff x \leq y \to z

for all x,y,z∈Lx, y, z \in L.

A residuated lattice is integral when 11 is the top element of the lattice, and bounded when a bottom element 00 exists.

¶Examples

  • The Boolean lattice 2\mathbf{2} with ⋅=∧\cdot = \wedge and classical implication x→y:=¬x∨yx \to y := \neg x \vee y is an integral commutative residuated lattice.
  • Any Heyting algebra is an integral commutative residuated lattice (with ⋅=∧\cdot = \wedge).
  • MV-algebras are integral commutative residuated lattices in which ⋅\cdot is the Łukasiewicz strong conjunction.

¶Role in voidmath

The residuated lattice L\mathbf{L} is the underlying value structure that voidmath builds on: the twist product construction L⋈\mathbf{L}^\bowtie pairs L\mathbf{L} with itself to produce the voidmath bilattice, and the pointwise lift of L⋈\mathbf{L}^\bowtie over the entity-pair index is the predication fiber algebra ApA_p at each predicate. The residuated structure is what gives the twist product its fusion ⊙\odot and right residual ⇒\Rightarrow — the bilattice operations that lift the residuation adjunction to pairs.

The framework is parametric in L\mathbf{L}. Choosing L=2\mathbf{L} = \mathbf{2} yields the classical four-valued Belnap–Ginsberg–Fitting bilattice 4\mathbf{4}. Choosing L\mathbf{L} a Heyting algebra yields an intuitionistic-flavored bilattice; choosing L\mathbf{L} an MV-algebra yields a many-valued/fuzzy bilattice. The framework’s structural results are uniform over the choice of L\mathbf{L}.
