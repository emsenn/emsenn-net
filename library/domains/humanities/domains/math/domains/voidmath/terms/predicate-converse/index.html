Predicate converse

2026-04-29 A morphism class in the predicate category, induced by the swap (s, o) ↦ (o, s) on the entity-pair index. Encodes the relational converse: the predicate p^c holds at the pair (s, o) exactly where p holds at (o, s). One of the four canonical generators of the predicate category.

A predicate converse is a morphism in the predicate category, denoted f:pc→pf : p^c \to p, induced by the swap of the entity-pair index:

(s,o)↦(o,s).(s, o) \mapsto (o, s).

The induced fiber-algebra map f∗:Ap→Apcf^* : A_p \to A_{p^c} acts cellwise:

f∗(v)(s,o)=v(o,s).f^*(v)(s, o) = v(o, s).

A predicate converse encodes the relational converse of pp: the predicate pcp^c holds at the pair (s,o)(s, o) exactly where pp holds at (o,s)(o, s). It is one of the four canonical generator classes of the predicate category under Sym(E)\mathrm{Sym}(E)-equivariance.

¶What converse encodes

A relating like parent of has a converse — child of — that is structurally distinct but related by exactly this swap. The converse is not a separate predicate invented from scratch; it is the same content read from the opposite direction. The predicate category’s converse generator gives this duality a precise structural form: the converse is a specific morphism between predicates, not an arbitrary association.

A predicate’s relationship to its own converse is informative:

  • A symmetric predicate is one with p=pcp = p^c: the swap fixes it. Examples: equal to, adjacent to, married to (in symmetric jurisdictions). For symmetric predicates, the converse generator collapses to the identity on pp, and the directional asymmetry of the cell becomes vacuous on this predicate’s content.
  • An asymmetric predicate has p≠pcp \neq p^c. Most relations are asymmetric in this sense: parent of, taller than, manager of. The converse generator produces a structurally distinct predicate, and the framework recognizes both as separate objects of C\mathcal{C}.

¶Composition with other generators

Predicate converse can compose with the other three generator classes — specialization, subject-projection, object-projection — but the composition is not always another converse. The composition rules are part of the predicate category’s structure; they encode how directional flips interact with sub-support inclusion and diagonal collapses.

For instance, applying converse twice yields the identity on pp: fconv∘fconv=idf_{\mathrm{conv}} \circ f_{\mathrm{conv}} = \mathrm{id}. Applying converse to a subject-projection gives an object-projection (and vice versa): the swap of the entity-pair index converts collapse-onto-subject-diagonal into collapse-onto-object-diagonal.
