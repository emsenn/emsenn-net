Twist-product fusion

2026-04-29 The monoidal multiplication ⊙ on the twist product L^⋈, lifting the residuated lattice's fusion product to evidence pairs. Combines values at the same cell: positive components multiply directly; negative components arise from cross-residuation.

The twist-product fusion is a binary operation ⊙\odot on the carrier L×LL \times L of the twist product L⋈\mathbf{L}^\bowtie over an integral commutative residuated lattice L=⟨L,∨,∧,⋅,→,1⟩\mathbf{L} = \langle L, \vee, \wedge, \cdot, \to, 1 \rangle:

⟨x,y⟩⊙⟨z,v⟩:=⟨x⋅z, (x→v)∧(z→y)⟩.\langle x, y \rangle \odot \langle z, v \rangle := \langle x \cdot z,\ (x \to v) \wedge (z \to y) \rangle.

The first component is the monoid product of positive-evidence components: affirming evidence multiplies directly. The second component cross-tests each side’s positive evidence against the other side’s negative evidence: the x→vx \to v term measures how much the first side’s affirmation is challenged by the second side’s denial, and the z→yz \to y term symmetrically.

The monoidal unit is ⟨1,1⟩\langle 1, 1 \rangle.

¶Adjoint pair

Together with the twist-product right residual ⇒\Rightarrow, the fusion forms an adjoint pair under the truth order ≤t\leq_t:

a⊙b≤tc  ⟺  a≤tb⇒c.a \odot b \leq_t c \iff a \leq_t b \Rightarrow c.

This residuation adjunction is the lift of the underlying residuated lattice’s x⋅y≤z  ⟺  x≤y→zx \cdot y \leq z \iff x \leq y \to z from LL to the pair carrier L×LL \times L, performed by the Busaniche–Cignoli construction. It is what makes the twist product a residuated bilattice rather than just a bilattice.

¶Role in the predication fiber algebra

The twist-product fusion lifts pointwise from L⋈\mathbf{L}^\bowtie to the predication fiber algebra ApA_p: (v⊙v′)(s,o):=v(s,o)⊙v′(s,o)(v \odot v')(s, o) := v(s, o) \odot v'(s, o). The predicate category’s four canonical generator classes preserve ⊙\odot pointwise — the residuation-natural property — which is what makes the residuation structure compatible with reindexing across predicates.
