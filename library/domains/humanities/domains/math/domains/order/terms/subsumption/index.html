2026-03-30 An ordering by specificity: a subsumes b if b is at least as specific as a. The natural ordering on types and feature structures.

Subsumption is an ordering by specificity. aa subsumes bb (written a⊑ba \sqsubseteq b or b≤ab \leq a) if bb is at least as specific as aa — bb has all the information aa has and possibly more.

In typed feature structures (Carpenter 1992), F1⊑F2F_1 \sqsubseteq F_2 iff every feature-value pair in F1F_1 is present and at least as specific in F2F_2. A feature structure with more specified attributes is more specific.

In type systems, a supertype subsumes a subtype. In ontologies, a general concept subsumes a specific one.

The set of all typed feature structures over a given signature forms a bounded lattice under subsumption. The meet (unification) is the most general structure at least as specific as both inputs. The join (generalization) is the most specific structure subsuming both.
