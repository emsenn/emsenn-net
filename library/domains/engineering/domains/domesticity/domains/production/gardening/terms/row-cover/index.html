Row Cover

2026-04-02 Lightweight spunbond polypropylene fabric draped over crops to provide frost protection, pest exclusion, or microclimate modification while transmitting light

A row cover is a sheet of lightweight, spunbond polypropylene fabric (typically 0.5–1.5 oz per square yard) laid directly over growing plants or supported on low hoops. It functions as a flexible microclimate modifier — controlling temperature, excluding pests, and reducing wind stress — while allowing most sunlight and water to reach the plants below.

Material Properties

Commercial row covers are 100% polypropylene spun-bonded with UV stabilizers for durability. The fabric is permeable to air and water, allowing gas exchange and rain penetration while trapping a thin boundary layer of warm air above the plants. Light transmission varies by weight: lightweight covers (AG-15) transmit about 85–90% of incident light, while heavier grades (AG-50) transmit around 50%.

Frost Protection

The primary mechanism of frost protection is simple: the fabric reduces radiative heat loss from the soil surface to the clear night sky and moderates wind chill. Different weight classes provide different levels of protection:

  • Lightweight (AG-15): 85% light transmission, 2–4°F frost protection. Used mainly for pest exclusion; frost protection is incidental.
  • Medium (AG-19, AG-30): 70–75% light transmission, 4–6°F frost protection. Balances light access and frost protection.
  • Heavy (AG-50): 50% light transmission, up to 8°F frost protection. For maximum cold protection when light is less critical (late fall, early spring).

Uses and Management

Row covers excel at three distinct tasks:

Pest Exclusion: Lightweight covers prevent airborne insects from reaching crops. Cabbage white butterflies, flea beetles, and other external pests cannot penetrate the fabric. The cover must be installed before pest flight season and removed during the main growing season, or pests will accumulate trapped inside.

Early-Season Warming: Medium-weight covers warm the soil and air around tender transplants (peppers, eggplant, melons), accelerating growth. They add 2–4 weeks to the effective growing season.

Fall/Winter Greens: Heavy covers protect cool-season crops (kale, mâche, spinach, chard) through hard frosts, allowing harvest into winter in temperate zones.

Critical Limitation

Row covers must be removed during bloom for insect-pollinated crops (squash, beans, cucumbers, tomato), or fruit set fails. They are most effective for leaf crops, seed crops, and crops pollinated by wind or managed by hand.
