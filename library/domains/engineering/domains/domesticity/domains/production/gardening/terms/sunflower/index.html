Sunflower

2026-04-02 Annual cultivar of Helianthus annuus, 60-110 days to maturity. Tall cultivars (2-3.5 m) require staking; dwarf ornamental cultivars (0.6-1 m) do not. Full sun, well-drained soil, consistent moisture. Prolific pollinator attractor. Large-headed giants mature in 90-110 days; rapid confection types in 65-90 days.

Sunflower cultivation spans diverse cultivar groups selected from Helianthus annuus for ornament, oil seed, or confection. Cultivars range from dwarf ornamentals (0.6 m, compact capitulum, double flowers) to tall giants (3 m, single stem, 30+ cm capitula).

¶Site Selection and Soil Preparation

Sunflowers require full sun (minimum 6-8 hours) and well-drained soil with pH 6.0-7.5. Soil preparation is modest: the deep taproot accesses subsoil moisture and nutrients, so cultivation works in marginally fertile soils where other crops fail. Work 2-3 inches of compost into the top 6-8 inches if soil is very poor. Avoid heavily manured sites (excess nitrogen promotes leafy growth at the expense of flowering). Tall cultivars need protection from wind; consider windbreaks or staking sites in exposed areas.

¶Spacing and Planting

Direct-seed after frost danger passes and soil reaches 50°F. Spacing depends on cultivar: dwarf ornamentals 12-18 inches apart; medium cultivars (1-1.5 m) 18-24 inches; tall cultivars (2+ m) 24-36 inches. Thin seedlings to final spacing at 2-3 true leaves. Successive sowings every 2 weeks extend bloom from midsummer through early fall. In cool zones, start seeds indoors 3-4 weeks before transplant to maximize maturity window.

¶Water and Fertility

Water deeply but infrequently: 1-1.5 inches per week combined rain and irrigation. The robust taproot makes sunflowers drought-tolerant once established (after 4-6 weeks). Overwatering promotes fungal diseases and weakens stems. Fertilize sparingly—excess nitrogen produces lush foliage and weak stems. A light side-dress at the 6-leaf stage (balanced NPK such as 10-10-10) and again at bud formation is sufficient.

¶Support and Pest Management

Tall cultivars (>2 m) require staking: drive a 2-inch diameter wood stake 18-24 inches into the ground adjacent to the plant at the 4-6 leaf stage. Loosely tie the stem (as it enlarges) with soft twine or cloth strips, avoiding stem girdling. Wind damage to tall cultivars often occurs at flowering when the heavy capitulum catches wind.

Sunflower maggot (Strauzia longipennis) larvae tunnel in stems; handpick affected stems or use preventive row covers. Sunflower beetle (Zygogramma exclamationis) skeletonizes leaves but rarely warrants treatment in home gardens. Fungal diseases (powdery mildew, rust, Sclerotinia) appear in humid summers; improve air circulation, avoid overhead watering, and select resistant cultivars if disease is persistent.

¶Harvest and Use

For cut flowers, harvest when outer ray florets fully unfurl but before pollen presentation (to extend vase life). For seed (confection or oil), allow capitula to mature fully: outer florets dry and reflexed, disc florets brown and seeds hard. Hang mature heads in a warm, dry location for 2-3 weeks to finish drying. Thresh seeds by rubbing the dried head or rub seeds free with gloved hands. Store in cool, dry conditions.

¶Cultivar Recommendations

  • Tall ornamental giants (90-110 days): ‘Teddy Bear’ (pompom double, 1.5 m, bright yellow); ‘Moulin Rouge’ (single, deep burgundy, 2.5 m); ‘Velvet Queen’ (maroon, 1.8 m).
  • Rapid confection (65-90 days): ‘Chocolate Cherry’ (dark brown disc, rapid); ‘Zebu’ (striped).
  • Oil-type compact (70-90 days): ‘Sunrich Orange’ (compact, single-stem, uniform maturity).
  • Medium ornamental (60-75 days): ‘Lemon Queen’ (pale yellow).
