Leek cultivation

2026-04-02 Growing protocol for leek cultivars (Allium ampeloprasum Porrum Group), optimized for white shank development through soil blanching and cold-hardy overwintering into winter harvest.

Leek cultivation focuses on developing long, tender white shanks through progressive soil blanching. Early-season cultivars mature in 90-110 days; late-season types extend the harvest into winter, taking 130+ days but offering cold hardiness and extended shelf life.

Planting and Shank Development

Start seed indoors 8-10 weeks before last spring frost, in trays at 65-70°F. Transplant seedlings when 4-6 inches tall (pencil-thin) outdoors after frost danger passes and soil warms to 50°F. Space 6 inches apart in rows 18 inches apart, or 4-6 inches apart for closer spacing that encourages thinner, more tender shanks.

Shank blanching begins immediately after transplanting. Create shallow furrows 3-4 inches wide and mound soil incrementally over the developing white base as the plant grows. With each cultivation or weeding, mound additional soil around the base, covering any emerging green tissue. By mid-summer, shanks will have developed 4-6 inches of white blanched tissue. Continue mounding through late summer for extended blanching.

Growing Season and Moisture

Leeks require consistent moisture and moderate nitrogen fertilization to sustain large shank growth. Apply balanced fertilizer (10-10-10) at planting and side-dress every 4-6 weeks with higher-nitrogen formula (20-10-10) to encourage leaf and shank expansion. Inadequate moisture or nitrogen results in thin, tough shanks and early bolting.

Harvest Timing by Maturity Type

Early-season leeks (90-110 days): Bulgarian Giant, American Flag, Varna. Harvest in late summer or early fall when shanks are 1-2 inches diameter and 6-8 inches of white blanching. These cultivars do not winter-hardy well and should be used fresh or stored short-term.

Late-season leeks (130+ days): Autumn Giant, Winter Giant, King Richard. Plant same time as early types but do not harvest until mid-fall or even winter. These cultivars tolerate hard freezes (to -10°F) and can be harvested through January in mild zones, or mulched heavily for winter storage in colder regions.

Cold Hardiness and Winter Harvest

Late-season leeks require 4-6 weeks of chilling below 50°F to develop full cold tolerance. In zones 5-8, unmulched beds can be harvested throughout winter by lifting as needed. In zones 3-4, apply 6-8 inches of straw mulch after ground freezes to protect overwintering plants; lift a section of mulch to harvest as needed, then restore it.

Storage and Shelf Life

Freshly harvested leeks store 2-3 weeks in refrigerator in plastic bags. For longer storage, blanch briefly in boiling water, cool, and freeze in freezer bags for up to 6 months; frozen leeks work well in soups and braises.
