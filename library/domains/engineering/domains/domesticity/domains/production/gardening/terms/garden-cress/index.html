Garden cress cultivation

2026-04-02 Growing protocol for *Lepidium sativum*, the rapid-cycling salad and microgreen crop. Germination to maturity in 15–20 days under optimal cool conditions. Multiple succession plantings (5–8 crops per season) from early spring through fall. Excellent introductory crop for children and rapid microgreen production.

Lepidium sativum is one of the fastest-maturing vegetables and requires minimal space and resources; perfect for succession planting to ensure continuous supply of peppery, tender greens.

¶Direct Sowing and Rapid Germination

Sow directly in garden beds or containers. Scatter seeds densely, barely cover with 0.25 inch soil (seeds are tiny). Keep consistently moist. Germination occurs at 15–20°C in 2–4 days. Seedlings emerge by day 5–7.

For successive harvests, sow every 10–14 days from early spring (after soil can be worked) through fall (6–8 weeks before first freeze).

¶Spacing and Density

For salad use, thin seedlings to 4–6 inches apart to allow foliage development. For microgreens, leave dense; no thinning required.

¶Growing Season Management

Minimal fertilization required; moderate fertility is adequate. Water consistently (0.5–1 inch per week); stress from irregular watering hastens bolting. No significant pest pressure; occasional flea beetle damage on seedlings is negligible.

¶Harvesting

Microgreen stage (7–10 days): Harvest seedlings at 2–3 inches tall, just above the soil surface. Snip with scissors. Most tender and mildest peppery flavor at this stage. Used for garnish and in microgreen mixes.

Baby greens (14–21 days): Cut entire plant at 4–6 inches tall by cutting 1 inch above soil line. Use fresh in salads. Peppery flavor is pronounced but not overwhelming.

Mature leaf harvest (25–35 days): Allow plants to grow to full size (8–12 inches) before bolting; pick individual outer leaves or cut entire plant. Mature leaves are peppery and best used as a garnish or lightly cooked.

Once bolting occurs, leaves are too coarse for salad use; allow plants to flower and set seed if saving seeds for next season.

¶Succession Planning

The extraordinary speed of Lepidium sativum permits 5–8 crops per growing season in temperate climates:

  • Early spring: Sow mid-March for April harvest
  • Late spring: Successive sowings through May
  • Early summer: Last useful plantings in June (if temperatures remain <25°C); bolting is rapid in heat
  • Fall: Resume sowings in August through September; excellent fall production through first frost

This allows year-round supply with minimal garden space—a 2-foot by 2-foot bed can produce 40–50 cress harvests per season.

¶Environmental Preferences

Cress prefers cool to moderate temperatures (15–20°C optimal); above 25°C, bolting occurs within 15–20 days, drastically shortening productive window. Afternoon shade in late spring/early summer moderates soil temperature and extends harvest.

Tolerates partial shade (50% incident light) but produces more robust plants in full sun.

¶Storage and Use

Harvest in cool morning hours. Use immediately for best flavor and texture. Store in high-humidity refrigerator (35–40°F) for 2–3 days; microgreens are best used same-day.

Flavor characteristics: Peppery, with mustard-like pungency that intensifies with age. Seedlings are mildest; mature plants are sharply peppery. Use as a garnish, in salads, or wilted into warm dishes.

¶Cultivation without a Garden

Lepidium sativum is ideal for container and indoor cultivation:

  • Trays: Sow directly in shallow seed trays, microgreen flats, or repurposed containers. Drainage is less critical for the short 10–20 day cycle.
  • Windowsill: Grows in bright windowsill light without supplemental grow lights.
  • Hydroponics/hydroton: Highly suitable for home hydroponic systems; very fast turnover.

¶Seed Saving

Allow final fall planting to mature through bolting (25–35 days). Collect silicles (seed pods) as they dry and turn brown; thresh to extract seeds. Store in cool, dry conditions for the following year. Seeds retain viability for 3–4 years.

¶Children and Beginner Gardeners

Lepidium sativum is an excellent introduction to gardening for children:

  • Fast results (visible growth within days)
  • Minimal maintenance
  • High success rate
  • Usable harvest in <3 weeks
  • Multiple cycles extend engagement
