AHAM Verifide Certification

2026-04-04 Third-party independent verification that an air purifier's Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR) claims meet the ANSI/AHAM AC-1 standard without relying on manufacturer testing.

AHAM Verifide certification indicates that an air purifier has been tested by an independent laboratory and verified to deliver its advertised CADR performance. The program reduces the risk of inflated manufacturer claims.

¶Certification Process

The Association of Home Appliance Manufacturers (AHAM) administers a certification program where:

  1. Manufacturers submit their air purifier models for third-party laboratory testing.
  2. The laboratory tests the device using the ANSI/AHAM AC-1 standard in a 1,008 cubic foot test chamber.
  3. Three separate CADR values (smoke, dust, pollen) are measured and reported.
  4. If results pass, the manufacturer receives the AHAM Verifide seal and certificate.
  5. The certified unit and its CADR values are listed in the AHAM directory.

¶What It Means vs. Manufacturer Claims

  • Certified (AHAM Verifide): CADR ratings have been independently verified. The device displays an AHAM seal on packaging listing the three CADR numbers.
  • Uncertified (manufacturer-claimed): CADR values are based on in-house or contracted testing, without third-party verification. These claims may be inflated.

Studies show that some uncertified products claim significantly higher CADR values than similar certified products, suggesting manufacturer testing can overstate performance.

¶Recognition by Regulators

The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and Federal Trade Commission (FTC) recognize AHAM Verifide certification as a reliable performance indicator. Some government procurement and indoor air quality guidelines specify AHAM-certified units.

¶Directory and Selection

AHAM maintains a searchable directory (AHAM Directory 2.0) listing all certified air cleaning devices with their verified CADR values. This allows consumers and professionals to compare products objectively and filter by specific requirements (e.g., “CADR ≥ 300 for smoke”).

¶Cost and Availability

Certification involves testing fees, so AHAM Verifide units are typically more expensive than uncertified alternatives. However, the premium is usually modest relative to the device cost and reflects genuine third-party verification.

¶Limitations

  • Certification covers only CADR performance; it does not evaluate noise, energy consumption, durability, or filter replacement cost.
  • AHAM does not certify activated carbon performance or VOC removal.
  • A certified device may still be poorly suited for a specific application (e.g., high noise for a bedroom).
  • Recertification is not required if the manufacturer changes a model; older units in the database may no longer be in production.

¶See Also

  • CADR — the performance metric that AHAM Verifide certifies
  • CARB Certification — a separate certification focused on safety and ozone emissions
