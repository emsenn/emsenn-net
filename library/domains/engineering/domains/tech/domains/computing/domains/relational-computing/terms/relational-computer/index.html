Relational Computer

2026-04-27 A computer whose primary substrate is the relation: data is encoded as associations between entities, and computation is the addition, removal, and traversal of those associations in a graph.

¶rasts-with: boolean-computer date-created: 2026-04-27

A relational computer is a computer whose primary substrate is the relation. Its data is encoded as associations between entities, and its computation is the addition, removal, and traversal of those associations in a graph. The relational computer takes relationality as its organizing ontology.

¶Substrate and identity

In a relational computer, an entity is constituted by the relations it participates in, not by its location in memory or the value stored at that location. Identity is positional in the graph rather than locational. Two entities are equal when their relational structure is the same; an entity persists when its relations persist; an entity is observed by traversing the edges incident to it.

¶Operations

Three primitives are sufficient: insertion of a relation, retraction of a relation, and traversal along relations. A query is a graph traversal beginning at an entity and following edges of named predicates to reach related entities. There is no fetch-from-address primitive: the closest analog is “what is related to entity e by predicate p?”, a question the graph answers by enumeration. A write is the addition or retraction of a relation; there is no value-mutation primitive, because there are no stored values to mutate.
