OpenClaw DM Policy

2026-03-09 The configuration that determines how OpenClaw handles inbound direct messages before they reach an agent.

The OpenClaw DM policy is the configuration that determines how OpenClaw handles inbound direct messages before they reach an agent. It is a channel-level gate on message processing, not only a cosmetic notification preference.

¶Modes

OpenClaw documents four DM policy modes:

  • pairing: unknown senders receive a short code and their message is ignored until approval
  • allowlist: only approved senders can reach the bot
  • open: any sender can reach the bot if the channel allowlist explicitly opts in
  • disabled: inbound DMs are ignored

¶Function

This policy is one of OpenClaw’s primary safety controls for consumer messaging surfaces. It constrains who can steer a tool-using assistant before prompts, tool calls, or session routing become relevant.
