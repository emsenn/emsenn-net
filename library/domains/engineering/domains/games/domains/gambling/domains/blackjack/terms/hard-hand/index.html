A hard hand in blackjack is a hand whose total does not include an ace counted as 11 [@thorp1966]. Either the hand has no ace at all, or any ace it contains must count as 1 to avoid a bust. A 10-6 is a hard 16. Ace-6-10 is also a hard 17, because the ace can no longer remain 11.

Hard hands matter because they have less flexibility than soft hands. A hard 16 cannot absorb another high card without busting, while a soft 16 can. This is why blackjack strategy distinguishes sharply between hard and soft totals even when the visible number is the same [@thorp1966].
