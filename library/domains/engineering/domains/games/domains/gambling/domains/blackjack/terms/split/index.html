To split in blackjack is to divide a starting pair into two separate hands, each matched by an additional wager equal to the original bet [@thorp1966]. If the player is dealt two 8s, for example, they may split them and play each 8 as the base of a new hand.

Splitting matters because it can turn one weak composite hand into two stronger strategic positions. A pair of 8s totals 16, which is awkward as a single hard hand, but splitting creates two fresh 8-start hands with new decision paths. Rules about whether players may resplit, split aces, or double after splitting materially change blackjack strategy and expected value [@shackleford2025blackjackcalculator].
