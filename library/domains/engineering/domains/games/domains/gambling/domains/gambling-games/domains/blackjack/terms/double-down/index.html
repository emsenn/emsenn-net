double down

2026-03-09
Defines double down

To double down in blackjack is to double the original wager in exchange for committing to receive exactly one additional card [@thorp1966]. The action increases both risk and reward: the player invests more precisely when the expected value of one more card is favorable enough to justify the larger stake.

Double-down rules vary by ruleset. Some tables allow doubling on any first two cards. Others restrict it to totals such as 9, 10, or 11, or prohibit it after a split. Because doubling changes how much money enters favorable situations, its availability materially affects the house edge [@shackleford2025blackjackcalculator].
