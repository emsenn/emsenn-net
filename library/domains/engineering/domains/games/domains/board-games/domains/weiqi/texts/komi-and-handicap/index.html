Komi and Handicap

2026-03-09 Audience: readers who understand basic weiqi structure and want to know how even games and uneven games are balanced.

Audience: readers who understand basic weiqi structure and want to know how even games and uneven games are balanced.

Learning goal: explain the difference between komi and handicap, and show why weiqi uses both.

¶Worked example: even game versus teaching game

In an even game, Black moves first and White receives komi, a fixed score bonus that offsets the first-move advantage [@britgo2025default; @britgo2026whatisgo]. In a teaching game between players of unequal strength, the weaker player may instead receive handicap stones before play begins [@britgo2026whatisgo]. These two adjustments solve different problems. Komi balances the initiative inside an otherwise equal game. Handicap balances the starting position between unequal players.

¶Why komi exists

Weiqi is not fully symmetrical at the start because Black moves first [@britannica2026go; @britgo2026whatisgo]. Komi compensates for that structural advantage by adding points to White’s final score. This means the core rules of play stay the same while the scoring system absorbs the asymmetry.

Komi therefore belongs to scoring, not to local tactics. It matters when the final territory and related points are counted, not when players are reading captures or life-and-death fights.

¶Why handicap exists

Handicap solves a different problem. It lets players of unequal strength compete in a game that still produces meaningful decisions for both sides [@britgo2026whatisgo]. Instead of changing how stones move or how groups live, handicap changes the starting position by placing extra Black stones on the board before the first move.

This matters pedagogically as well as competitively. A weaker player can experience middle-game and endgame decisions from a stronger position without needing the rules themselves to change.

¶Komi and handicap are not interchangeable

Komi is a scoring offset in an even game. Handicap is a positional offset in an uneven game. They should not be collapsed into one idea just because both are balancing devices [@britgo2025default; @britgo2026whatisgo].

The difference matters because each adjustment shapes play differently. Komi leaves the opening empty and symmetrical except for move order. Handicap creates immediate whole-board structure before ordinary play begins.

¶Exercises

  1. Why does komi affect the end of the game rather than the start of the board position?
Answer

Because komi is a score adjustment, not an extra stone or changed move rule. It offsets Black’s first move by changing the final count, not by altering local tactical play.

  1. Why is handicap useful even though komi already exists?
Answer

Because komi balances even games, while handicap balances games between players of different strength. They address different asymmetries.
