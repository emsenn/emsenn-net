Actual play is the public presentation of a role-playing session as media: a livestream, podcast, video series, transcript, or edited recording. In older hobby usage, the phrase could also mean play as it actually happened at the table, especially when contrasted with theory or preparation. In current TTRPG discourse, it usually names a published form of play.

Actual play matters because it turns a private table event into a teachable and watchable artifact. Viewers learn systems, observe table culture, and absorb norms about pacing, consent, humor, and game master technique by watching other groups play. It is therefore both entertainment and infrastructure for the hobby.
