Relational Worlding Grammar

RWG v1.0 — Relational Worlding Grammar A field-based ontology for live, frictional, unresolved world systems. (Built from REL: Relational Emission Logic) — ## 0. SYSTEM OVERVIEW Relational Worlding is not the construction of lore, but the design of pressure-conditioned systems in which meaning emerges through emission, trace, and alignment under field strain. Worlds are not maps or timelines. They are built from fields, actors, traces, and methods. Narrative is not central. Friction is. — ## 1. BASE ENTITIES ### 1.1 Field (F) A relational pressure environment composed of multiple, coexisting forces: ecological, emotional, infrastructural, political, historical, spiritual. - Fields shape behavior. - Fields overlap and may contradict. - Every moment in-world occurs within at least one field. Design Rule: Start from force interactions, not landmasses. — ### 1.2 Actor (A) Any presence that registers and/or emits pressure within a field. May be human, nonhuman, architectural, symbolic, procedural, or spectral. - Actors do not require agency. - Defined by what they affect and are affected by. Design Rule: Define actors by consequence, not biography. — ### 1.3 Alignment (α) An actor’s tension-shaped position within a given field at a given moment. - Dynamic, situational, historically conditioned. - Governs what emissions can be received, resolved, or rejected. Design Rule: Alignment is what you can hold—not what you believe. — ### 1.4 Emission (E) Any act, symbol, gesture, trace, utterance, or rupture that exerts pressure into the field. - May be visible or invisible. - Emissions have aftereffects regardless of actor intent. Design Rule: Don’t write events. Write emissions and let fields respond. — ### 1.5 Method (M) A repeatable practice by which actors respond to pressure under their current alignment. - May include magic, ritual, sabotage, silence, recordkeeping, deflection. - Methods produce trace. Not always reliably. Design Rule: Build method as frictional practice, not systematized power. — ### 1.6 Trace (T) What remains after emission. Memory, residue, wound, object, belief, distortion. - Trace shapes future fields. - Contradictory traces are not errors—they are structure. Design Rule: Don’t hide worldbuilding in lore. Surface it in trace. — ## 2. WORLD LOGIC ### 2.1 Signal Resolution E + A(α, M) ⇒ S → When an emission is resolved by an actor’s alignment and method. ### 2.2 Noise Generation E ⧧ A(α, M) ⇒ N → When an emission fails to resolve. May distort or linger. ### 2.3 Irresolved Noise N (persistent) ⇒ IRN → Active contradiction that alters field behavior without resolving. ### 2.4 Friction Event E received by A₁…Aₙ | α₁ ≠ α₂… ⇒ Fᵣ → Multi-actor misalignment over same emission. Produces drag. — ## 3. DESIGN CONDITIONS - Coherence is emergent. Not designed. - Narrative is local. Not world-determining. - Drag is realism. Let it slow you down. - Canon is recursive. Contradiction is not failure—it’s history. — ## 4. APPLICATION MODES - Narrative fiction systems - TTRPGs and LARP - Speculative history and memory fields - Collaborative world laboratories - Simulation engines and AI logic protocols — ## 5. ETHICAL CLAUSE > Worlding is a form of care. > Do not resolve what the field has not allowed. > Do not erase trace. > If something won’t stabilize, don’t fix it—carry it.

All information can only be grounded in other information. Nothing here or anywhere should be understood as True, or False.
